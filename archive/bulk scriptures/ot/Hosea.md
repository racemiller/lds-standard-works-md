Chapter 1

Hosea and his family are a sign unto Israel—In the day of gathering, the people of Israel will become the sons of the living God.

1 The word of the Lord that came unto Hosea, the son of Beeri, in the days of [aUzziah](#note1a), Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, kings of Judah, and in the days of Jeroboam the son of Joash, king of Israel.

2 The beginning of the word of the Lord by Hosea. And the Lord said to Hosea, Go, take unto thee a [awife](#note2a) of whoredoms and children of whoredoms: for the land hath committed great [bwhoredom](#note2b), departing from the Lord.

3 So he went and took Gomer the daughter of Diblaim; which conceived, and bare him a son.

4 And the Lord said unto him, Call his name Jezreel; for yet a little while, and I will avenge the blood of Jezreel upon the house of [aJehu](#note4a), and will cause to [bcease](#note4b) the kingdom of the house of Israel.

5 And it shall come to pass at that day, that I will [abreak](#note5a) the bow of Israel in the valley of Jezreel.

6 ¶ And she conceived again, and bare a daughter. And God said unto him, Call her name [aLo-ruhamah](#note6a): for I will [bno more](#note6b) have mercy upon the house of Israel; but I will utterly take them [caway](#note6c).

7 But I will have [amercy](#note7a) upon the house of [bJudah](#note7b), and will save them by the Lord their God, and will not save them by bow, nor by sword, nor by battle, by [chorses](#note7c), nor by horsemen.

8 ¶ Now when she had weaned Lo-ruhamah, she conceived, and bare a son.

9 Then said God, Call his name [aLo-ammi](#note9a): for ye are not my people, and I will not be your God.

10 ¶ Yet the [anumber](#note10a) of the children of Israel shall be as the [bsand](#note10b) of the sea, which cannot be measured nor numbered; and it shall come to pass, that in the place where it was said unto them, Ye are not my people, there it shall be said unto them, Ye are the [csons](#note10c) of the [dliving God](#note10d).

11 Then shall the children of Judah and the children of Israel be [agathered](#note11a) together, and appoint themselves one head, and they shall come up out of the land: for great shall be the day of Jezreel.

Chapter 2

Worshipping false gods brings severe judgments upon Israel—In the last days, Israel will be reconciled to God and become His people.

1 Say ye unto your brethren, [aAmmi](#note1a); and to your sisters, [bRuhamah](#note1b).

2 Plead with your [amother](#note2a), plead: for she is not my wife, neither am I her husband: let her therefore put away her whoredoms out of her sight, and her adulteries from between her breasts;

3 Lest I [astrip](#note3a) her [bnaked](#note3b), and set her as in the day that she was [cborn](#note3c), and make her as a wilderness, and set her like a dry land, and slay her with [dthirst](#note3d).

4 And I will not have mercy upon her children; for they be the children of whoredoms.

5 For their mother hath played the harlot: she that conceived them hath done [ashamefully](#note5a): for she said, I will go after my lovers, that give me my [bbread](#note5b) and my water, my wool and my flax, mine oil and my drink.

6 ¶ Therefore, behold, I will [ahedge](#note6a) up thy way with thorns, and make a wall, that she shall not find her paths.

7 And she shall follow after her lovers, but she shall not overtake them; and she shall seek them, but shall not find them: then shall she say, I will go and return to my first [ahusband](#note7a); for then was it better with me than now.

8 For she did not know that I gave her [acorn](#note8a), and wine, and oil, and multiplied her silver and gold, which they prepared for Baal.

9 Therefore will I return, and take away my corn in the time thereof, and my wine in the season thereof, and will recover my wool and my flax given to cover her nakedness.

10 And now will I discover her [alewdness](#note10a) in the sight of her lovers, and none shall deliver her out of mine hand.

11 I will also cause all her [amirth](#note11a) to [bcease](#note11b), her feast days, her new [cmoons](#note11c), and her [dsabbaths](#note11d), and all her [esolemn](#note11e) feasts.

12 And I will destroy her vines and her fig trees, whereof she hath said, These are my rewards that my lovers have given me: and I will make them a forest, and the beasts of the field shall eat them.

13 And I will visit upon her the days of [aBaalim](#note13a), wherein she burned incense to them, and she decked herself with her [bearrings](#note13b) and her jewels, and she went after her lovers, and forgat me, saith the Lord.

14 ¶ Therefore, behold, I will allure her, and bring her into the [awilderness](#note14a), and speak comfortably unto her.

15 And I will give her her vineyards from thence, and the valley of [aAchor](#note15a) for a door of hope: and she shall [bsing](#note15b) there, as in the days of her youth, and as in the day when she came up out of the land of Egypt.

16 And it shall be at that [aday](#note16a), saith the Lord, that thou shalt call me [bIshi](#note16b); and shalt call me no more [cBaali](#note16c).

17 For I will take away the names of [aBaalim](#note17a) out of her mouth, and they shall no more be remembered by their name.

18 And in that day will I make a [acovenant](#note18a) for them with the [bbeasts](#note18b) of the field, and with the fowls of heaven, and with the creeping things of the ground: and I will break the bow and the sword and the battle out of the earth, and will make them to lie down [csafely](#note18c).

19 And I will [abetroth](#note19a) thee unto me for ever; yea, I will betroth thee unto me in righteousness, and in judgment, and in lovingkindness, and in mercies.

20 I will even betroth thee unto me in faithfulness: and thou shalt [aknow](#note20a) the Lord.

21 And it shall come to pass in that day, I will hear, saith the Lord, I will hear the heavens, and they shall hear the earth;

22 And the earth shall hear the corn, and the wine, and the oil; and they shall hear Jezreel.

23 And I will sow her unto me in the earth; and I will have mercy upon [aher](#note23a) that had not obtained mercy; and I will say to [bthem](#note23b) which were not my [cpeople](#note23c), Thou art my [dpeople](#note23d); and they shall say, Thou art my God.

Chapter 3

Israel will seek the Lord, return to the Lord, and receive of His goodness in the latter days.

1 Then said the Lord unto me, Go yet, love a woman beloved of her friend, yet an [aadulteress](#note1a), according to the love of the Lord toward the children of Israel, who look to other gods, and love [bflagons of wine](#note1b).

2 So I bought her to me for fifteen pieces of silver, and for an homer of barley, and an half homer of barley:

3 And I said unto her, Thou shalt abide for me many days; thou shalt not play the harlot, and thou shalt not be for another man: so will I also be for thee.

4 For the children of Israel shall abide many days without a king, and without a prince, and without a sacrifice, and without an image, and without an ephod, and without teraphim:

5 Afterward shall the children of Israel return, and [aseek](#note5a) the Lord their God, and [bDavid](#note5b) their king; and shall fear the Lord and his goodness in the [clatter days](#note5c).

Chapter 4

Israel loses all truth, mercy, and knowledge of God and goes whoring after false gods.

1 Hear the word of the Lord, ye children of Israel: for the Lord hath a [acontroversy](#note1a) with the inhabitants of the land, because there is no [btruth](#note1b), nor mercy, nor [cknowledge](#note1c) of God in the land.

2 By [aswearing](#note2a), and [blying](#note2b), and killing, and [cstealing](#note2c), and committing adultery, they [dbreak out](#note2d), and [eblood toucheth blood](#note2e).

3 Therefore shall the [aland](#note3a) mourn, and every one that dwelleth therein shall languish, with the beasts of the field, and with the fowls of heaven; yea, the fishes of the sea also shall be taken away.

4 Yet let no man [astrive](#note4a), nor reprove another: for thy people are as they that strive with the priest.

5 Therefore shalt thou fall in the day, and the [aprophet](#note5a) also shall fall with thee in the night, and I will destroy thy mother.

6 ¶ My people are destroyed for lack of [aknowledge](#note6a): because thou hast [brejected](#note6b) [cknowledge](#note6c), I will also reject thee, that thou shalt be no priest to me: seeing thou hast forgotten the [dlaw](#note6d) of thy God, I will also forget thy children.

7 As they were increased, so they sinned against me: therefore will I change their glory into [ashame](#note7a).

8 They eat up the sin of my people, and they set their heart on their iniquity.

9 And there shall be, like people, like priest: and I will punish them for their ways, and reward them their doings.

10 For they shall eat, and not have enough: they shall commit whoredom, and shall not increase: because they have left off to take heed to the Lord.

11 [aWhoredom](#note11a) and wine and new wine take away the heart.

12 ¶ My people ask [acounsel](#note12a) at their stocks, and their staff declareth unto them: for the [bspirit](#note12b) of [cwhoredoms](#note12c) hath caused them to err, and they have gone a whoring from under their God.

13 They sacrifice upon the tops of the mountains, and burn incense upon the hills, under oaks and poplars and elms, because the shadow thereof is good: therefore your daughters shall commit whoredom, and your spouses shall commit [aadultery](#note13a).

14 I will not punish your daughters when they commit whoredom, nor your spouses when they commit adultery: for themselves are separated with whores, and they sacrifice with harlots: therefore the people that doth not understand shall fall.

15 ¶ Though thou, Israel, play the [aharlot](#note15a), yet let not Judah offend; and come not ye unto Gilgal, neither go ye up to [bBeth-aven](#note15b), nor swear, The Lord liveth.

16 For Israel slideth back as a backsliding heifer: now the Lord will feed them as a lamb in a large place.

17 Ephraim is joined to [aidols](#note17a): let him alone.

18 Their drink is sour: they have committed whoredom continually: her rulers [awith shame do love](#note18a), Give ye.

19 The wind hath bound her up in her wings, and they shall be ashamed because of their sacrifices.

Chapter 5

The kingdoms of Judah and Israel will both fall because of their iniquities.

1 Hear ye this, O priests; and hearken, ye house of Israel; and give ye ear, O house of the king; for judgment is toward you, because ye have been a snare on Mizpah, and a net spread upon Tabor.

2 And the revolters are profound to make slaughter, though I have been a rebuker of them all.

3 I know Ephraim, and Israel is not hid from me: for now, O Ephraim, thou committest [awhoredom](#note3a), and Israel is defiled.

4 [aThey](#note4a) will not frame their doings to turn unto their God: for the spirit of whoredoms is in the midst of them, and they have not known the Lord.

5 And the pride of Israel doth testify to his face: therefore shall Israel and Ephraim fall in their iniquity; Judah also shall fall with them.

6 They shall go with their flocks and with their herds to seek the Lord; but they shall not find him; he hath [awithdrawn](#note6a) himself from them.

7 They have dealt treacherously against the Lord: for they have begotten strange children: now shall a [amonth](#note7a) devour them with their portions.

8 Blow ye the [acornet](#note8a) in Gibeah, and the trumpet in Ramah: cry aloud at Beth-aven, after thee, O Benjamin.

9 Ephraim shall be desolate in the day of rebuke: among the tribes of Israel have I made known that which shall surely be.

10 The princes of Judah were like them that remove the [abound](#note10a): therefore I will pour out my wrath upon them like water.

11 Ephraim is oppressed and broken in judgment, because he willingly walked [aafter the commandment](#note11a).

12 Therefore will I be unto Ephraim as a moth, and to the house of Judah as rottenness.

13 When Ephraim saw his sickness, and Judah saw his [awound](#note13a), then went [bEphraim](#note13b) to the Assyrian, and sent to king [cJareb](#note13c): yet could he not [dheal](#note13d) you, nor cure you of your wound.

14 For I will be unto Ephraim as a lion, and as a young lion to the house of Judah: I, even I, will tear and go away; I will take away, and none shall rescue him.

15 ¶ I will go and return to my place, till they [aacknowledge](#note15a) their offence, and [bseek](#note15b) my face: in their [caffliction](#note15c) they will seek me early.

Chapter 6

Hosea calls Israel to return and serve the Lord—The mercy and knowledge of God are more important than ritualistic sacrifices.

1 Come, and let us return unto the Lord: for he hath torn, and he will [aheal](#note1a) us; he hath smitten, and he will bind us up.

2 After two days will he [arevive us](#note2a): in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his [bsight](#note2b).

3 Then shall we know, if we follow on to know the Lord: his going forth is prepared as the morning; and he shall come unto us as the rain, as the [alatter and former rain](#note3a) unto the earth.

4 ¶ O Ephraim, what shall I do unto thee? O Judah, what shall I do unto thee? for your goodness is as a morning cloud, and as the early [adew](#note4a) it goeth away.

5 Therefore have I hewed them by the prophets; I have [aslain](#note5a) them by the words of my mouth: and thy judgments are as the light that goeth forth.

6 For [aI](#note6a) desired [bmercy](#note6b), and not sacrifice; and the [cknowledge](#note6c) of God more than burnt [dofferings](#note6d).

Chapter 12

The Lord uses prophets, visions, and similitudes to guide His people, but they become rich and will not wait on the Lord—Ephraim provokes Him most bitterly.

1 [aEphraim](#note1a) feedeth on wind, and followeth after the east wind: he daily increaseth lies and desolation; and they do make a [bcovenant](#note1b) with the Assyrians, and oil is carried into Egypt.

2 The Lord hath also a [acontroversy](#note2a) with Judah, and will punish Jacob according to his ways; according to his doings will he recompense him.

3 ¶ He took his brother by the [aheel](#note3a) in the womb, and by his strength he had power with God:

4 Yea, he had power over the [aangel](#note4a), and [bprevailed](#note4b): he wept, and made supplication unto him: he found him in [cBeth-el](#note4c), and there he spake with [dus](#note4d);

5 Even the Lord God of hosts; the Lord is his [amemorial](#note5a).

6 Therefore [aturn](#note6a) thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually.

7 ¶ He is a merchant, the [abalances](#note7a) of deceit are in his hand: he loveth to oppress.

8 And Ephraim said, Yet I am become rich, I have found me out substance: in all my labours they shall find none iniquity in me that were sin.

9 And I that am the Lord thy God from the land of Egypt will yet make thee to dwell in [atabernacles](#note9a), as in the days of the solemn feast.

10 I have also spoken by the prophets, and I have multiplied visions, and used similitudes, by the ministry of the prophets.

11 Is there iniquity in Gilead? surely they are vanity: they sacrifice bullocks in [aGilgal](#note11a); yea, their altars are as heaps in the furrows of the fields.

12 And Jacob fled into the country of [aSyria](#note12a), and Israel served for a [bwife](#note12b), and for a wife he kept sheep.

13 And by a [aprophet](#note13a) the [bLord](#note13b) brought Israel out of Egypt, and by a prophet was he preserved.

14 Ephraim provoked him to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his [ablood](#note14a) upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.

Chapter 13

Ephraim’s sins provoke the Lord—There is no Savior beside the Lord—He ransoms from the grave and redeems from death.

1 When Ephraim spake trembling, he exalted himself in Israel; but when he [aoffended](#note1a) in Baal, he died.

2 And now they sin more and more, and have made them molten [aimages](#note2a) of their silver, and idols according to their own understanding, all of it the work of the craftsmen: they say of them, Let the men that sacrifice kiss the calves.

3 Therefore they shall be as the morning cloud, and as the early dew that passeth away, as the [achaff](#note3a) that is driven with the whirlwind out of the floor, and as the smoke out of the chimney.

4 Yet I am the Lord thy God from the land of Egypt, and thou shalt know no [agod](#note4a) but me: for there is no [bsaviour](#note4b) [cbeside](#note4c) me.

5 ¶ I did [aknow](#note5a) thee in the wilderness, in the land of great [bdrought](#note5b).

6 According to their pasture, so were they [afilled](#note6a); they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore have they [bforgotten](#note6b) me.

7 Therefore I will be unto them as a lion: as a leopard by the way will I observe them:

8 I will meet them as a [abear](#note8a) that is bereaved of her whelps, and will rend the [bcaul](#note8b) of their heart, and there will I devour them like a lion: the wild beast shall tear them.

9 ¶ O Israel, thou hast [adestroyed](#note9a) thyself; but in me is thine [bhelp](#note9b).

10 I will be thy [aking](#note10a): where is any other that may save thee in all thy cities? and thy judges of whom thou saidst, Give me a [bking](#note10b) and princes?

11 I gave thee a king in mine [aanger](#note11a), and [btook](#note11b) him away in my wrath.

12 The iniquity of Ephraim is bound up; his sin is hid.

13 The [asorrows](#note13a) of a [btravailing](#note13b) woman shall come upon him: he is an unwise son; for he should not stay long in the place of the [cbreaking](#note13c) forth of children.

14 I will [aransom](#note14a) them from the power of the [bgrave](#note14b); I will [credeem](#note14c) them from death: O [ddeath](#note14d), I will be thy [eplagues](#note14e); O grave, I will be thy destruction: [frepentance](#note14f) shall be hid from mine eyes.

15 ¶ Though he be fruitful among his brethren, an east [awind](#note15a) shall come, the wind of the Lord shall come up from the wilderness, and his spring shall become dry, and his fountain shall be dried up: he shall spoil the treasure of all pleasant vessels.

16 [aSamaria](#note16a) shall become desolate; for she hath rebelled against her God: they shall fall by the sword: their infants shall be [bdashed](#note16b) in pieces, and their women with child shall be ripped up.

Chapter 14

In the last days, Ephraim will repent and return unto the Lord.

1 O Israel, return unto the Lord thy God; for thou hast [afallen](#note1a) by thine iniquity.

2 Take with you words, and turn to the Lord: say unto him, Take away all iniquity, and receive us graciously: so will we [arender](#note2a) the [bcalves](#note2b) of our lips.

3 [aAsshur](#note3a) shall not [bsave](#note3b) us; we will not ride upon [chorses](#note3c): neither will we say any more to the work of our hands, Ye are our gods: for in thee the [dfatherless](#note3d) findeth mercy.

4 ¶ I will heal their backsliding, I will [alove](#note4a) them freely: for mine anger is turned away from him.

5 I will be as the [adew](#note5a) unto Israel: he shall grow as the lily, and cast forth his roots as Lebanon.

6 His branches shall spread, and his beauty shall be as the [aolive tree](#note6a), and his smell as Lebanon.

7 They that dwell under his shadow shall return; they shall revive as the corn, and grow as the vine: the scent thereof shall be as the wine of Lebanon.

8 Ephraim shall say, What have I to do any more with idols? I have heard him, and observed him: I am like a green fir tree. From me is thy fruit found.

9 Who is [awise](#note9a), and he shall understand these things? [bprudent](#note9b), and he shall know them? for the [cways](#note9c) of the Lord are right, and the just shall [dwalk](#note9d) in them: but the transgressors shall fall therein.
