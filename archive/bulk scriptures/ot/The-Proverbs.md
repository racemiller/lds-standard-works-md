Chapter 1

The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge—If sinners entice you, do not consent—Those who hearken to wisdom will dwell safely.

1 The [aproverbs](#note1a) of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel;

2 To know wisdom and instruction; to perceive the words of understanding;

3 To receive the [ainstruction](#note3a) of wisdom, justice, and judgment, and equity;

4 To give subtilty to the [asimple](#note4a), to the young man knowledge and discretion.

5 A wise man will hear, and will increase [alearning](#note5a); and a man of understanding shall attain unto wise counsels:

6 To understand a proverb, and the interpretation; the words of the wise, and their [adark sayings](#note6a).

7 ¶ The [afear of the Lord](#note7a) is the beginning of [bknowledge](#note7b): but fools despise [cwisdom](#note7c) and [dinstruction](#note7d).

8 My son, hear the [ainstruction](#note8a) of thy father, and forsake not the law of thy [bmother](#note8b):

9 For they shall be an [aornament](#note9a) of [bgrace](#note9b) unto thy head, and chains about thy neck.

10 ¶ My son, if sinners [aentice](#note10a) thee, [bconsent](#note10b) thou not.

11 If they say, Come with us, let us lay wait for blood, let us [alurk privily](#note11a) for the innocent without cause:

12 Let us [aswallow](#note12a) them up alive as the grave; and whole, as those that go down into the [bpit](#note12b):

13 We shall find all precious substance, we shall fill our houses with spoil:

14 Cast in thy lot among us; let us all have one purse:

15 My son, walk not thou in the way with them; [arefrain](#note15a) thy foot from their path:

16 For their feet run to [aevil](#note16a), and make haste to shed blood.

17 Surely in vain the net is spread in the sight of any bird.

18 And they lay wait for their own blood; they lurk privily for their own lives.

19 So are the ways of every one that is [agreedy](#note19a) of gain; which taketh away the life of the owners thereof.

20 ¶ Wisdom crieth without; she uttereth her voice in the streets:

21 She crieth in the chief place of concourse, in the openings of the gates: in the city she uttereth her words, saying,

22 How long, ye simple ones, will ye love simplicity? and the scorners delight in their [ascorning](#note22a), and fools [bhate](#note22b) [cknowledge](#note22c)?

23 Turn you at my reproof: behold, I will [apour out](#note23a) my spirit unto you, I will make known my words unto you.

24 ¶ Because I have called, and ye [arefused](#note24a); I have stretched out my hand, and no man regarded;

25 But ye have set at [anought](#note25a) all my [bcounsel](#note25b), and would none of my reproof:

26 I also will laugh at your calamity; I will mock when your fear cometh;

27 When your fear cometh as desolation, and your destruction cometh as a whirlwind; when distress and anguish cometh upon you.

28 Then shall they [acall](#note28a) upon me, but I will not [banswer](#note28b); they shall seek me early, but they shall not find me:

29 For that they hated knowledge, and did not [achoose](#note29a) the fear of the Lord:

30 They would none of my counsel: they despised all my [areproof](#note30a).

31 Therefore shall they eat of the [afruit](#note31a) of their own way, and be filled with their own devices.

32 For the turning away of the simple shall slay them, and the prosperity of fools shall destroy them.

33 But whoso hearkeneth unto me shall dwell safely, and shall be quiet from fear of evil.

Chapter 2

The Lord gives wisdom, knowledge, and understanding—Walk in the way of good men.

1 My son, if thou wilt receive my words, and [ahide](#note1a) my commandments with thee;

2 So that thou incline thine ear unto wisdom, and apply thine [aheart](#note2a) to understanding;

3 Yea, if thou criest after knowledge, and liftest up thy voice for understanding;

4 If thou seekest her as silver, and searchest for her as for hid treasures;

5 Then shalt thou understand the [afear](#note5a) of the Lord, and find the [bknowledge](#note5b) of God.

6 For the Lord giveth [awisdom](#note6a): out of his mouth cometh knowledge and [bunderstanding](#note6b).

7 He layeth up sound wisdom for the righteous: he is a [abuckler](#note7a) to them that [bwalk](#note7b) uprightly.

8 He keepeth the paths of judgment, and preserveth the way of his saints.

9 Then shalt thou [aunderstand](#note9a) righteousness, and judgment, and equity; yea, every good path.

10 ¶ When wisdom entereth into thine heart, and knowledge is pleasant unto thy soul;

11 [aDiscretion](#note11a) shall preserve thee, understanding shall keep thee:

12 To deliver thee from the way of the evil man, from the man that speaketh froward things;

13 Who leave the paths of uprightness, to [awalk](#note13a) in the ways of darkness;

14 Who rejoice to do evil, and delight in the [afrowardness](#note14a) of the wicked;

15 Whose ways are crooked, and they froward in their paths:

16 To deliver thee from the [astrange woman](#note16a), even from the stranger which flattereth with her words;

17 Which forsaketh the guide of her youth, and forgetteth the covenant of her God.

18 For her house inclineth unto death, and her paths unto the dead.

19 None that go unto her [areturn](#note19a) again, neither take they hold of the paths of life.

20 That thou mayest walk in the way of good men, and keep the paths of the righteous.

21 For the [aupright](#note21a) shall dwell in the land, and the perfect shall remain in it.

22 But the wicked shall be [acut off](#note22a) from the earth, and the transgressors shall be rooted out of it.

Chapter 3

Write mercy and truth upon the tablet of your heart—Trust in the Lord—Honor Him with your substance—Whom the Lord loves He corrects—Happy is the man who finds wisdom.

1 My son, forget not my law; but let thine heart keep my commandments:

2 For length of days, and [along life](#note2a), and [bpeace](#note2b), shall they add to thee.

3 Let not mercy and [atruth](#note3a) forsake thee: [bbind](#note3b) them about thy neck; write them upon the [ctable](#note3c) of thine [dheart](#note3d):

4 So shalt thou find [afavour](#note4a) and good understanding in the sight of God and man.

5 ¶ [aTrust](#note5a) in the Lord with all thine [bheart](#note5b); and lean not unto thine [cown](#note5c) [dunderstanding](#note5d).

6 In all thy ways [aacknowledge](#note6a) him, and he shall [bdirect](#note6b) thy [cpaths](#note6c).

7 ¶ Be not [awise](#note7a) in thine own eyes: [bfear](#note7b) the Lord, and depart from evil.

8 It shall be [ahealth](#note8a) to thy navel, and marrow to thy bones.

9 [aHonour](#note9a) the Lord with thy [bsubstance](#note9b), and with the [cfirstfruits](#note9c) of all thine increase:

10 So shall thy barns be filled with [aplenty](#note10a), and thy presses shall burst out with new wine.

11 ¶ My son, despise not the [achastening](#note11a) of the Lord; neither be weary of his correction:

12 For whom the Lord loveth he [acorrecteth](#note12a); even as a [bfather](#note12b) the son in whom he delighteth.

13 ¶ [aHappy](#note13a) is the man that findeth [bwisdom](#note13b), and the man that getteth [cunderstanding](#note13c).

14 For the merchandise of it is better than the merchandise of silver, and the gain thereof than fine gold.

15 She is more precious than rubies: and all the things thou canst desire are not to be compared unto her.

16 Length of days is in her right hand; and in her left hand [ariches](#note16a) and honour.

17 Her ways are ways of pleasantness, and all her paths are peace.

18 She is a tree of [alife](#note18a) to them that lay hold upon her: and happy is every one that retaineth her.

19 The Lord by wisdom hath [afounded](#note19a) the earth; by [bunderstanding](#note19b) hath he established the heavens.

20 By his knowledge the depths are [abroken up](#note20a), and the clouds drop down the dew.

21 ¶ My son, let not them depart from thine eyes: keep sound wisdom and discretion:

22 So shall they be life unto thy soul, and [agrace](#note22a) to thy neck.

23 Then shalt thou walk in thy way safely, and thy foot shall not stumble.

24 When thou liest down, thou shalt not be afraid: yea, thou shalt lie down, and thy sleep shall be sweet.

25 Be not afraid of sudden fear, neither of the desolation of the wicked, when it cometh.

26 For the Lord shall be thy [aconfidence](#note26a), and shall keep thy foot from being taken.

27 ¶ [aWithhold](#note27a) not [bgood](#note27b) from them to whom it is due, when it is in the power of thine hand to do it.

28 Say not unto thy neighbour, Go, and come again, and to morrow I will give; when thou hast it by thee.

29 [aDevise](#note29a) not evil against thy [bneighbour](#note29b), seeing he dwelleth securely by thee.

30 ¶ [aStrive](#note30a) not with a man without cause, if he have done thee no harm.

31 ¶ Envy thou not the [aoppressor](#note31a), and choose none of his ways.

32 For the [afroward](#note32a) is abomination to the Lord: but his secret is with the righteous.

33 ¶ The [acurse](#note33a) of the Lord is in the house of the wicked: but he blesseth the habitation of the just.

34 Surely he scorneth the scorners: but he giveth [agrace](#note34a) unto the [blowly](#note34b).

35 The [awise](#note35a) shall inherit glory: but [bshame](#note35b) shall be the promotion of fools.

Chapter 4

Keep the commandments and live—With all your getting, get understanding—Go not in the way of evil men.

1 Hear, ye children, the [ainstruction](#note1a) of a father, and attend to know understanding.

2 For I give you good doctrine, forsake ye not my law.

3 For I was my father’s son, tender and only beloved in the sight of my mother.

4 He taught me also, and said unto me, Let thine heart retain my words: keep my commandments, and live.

5 Get wisdom, get understanding: forget it not; neither decline from the words of my mouth.

6 Forsake her not, and she shall preserve thee: love her, and she shall keep thee.

7 [aWisdom](#note7a) is the principal thing; therefore get wisdom: and with all thy getting get [bunderstanding](#note7b).

8 Exalt her, and she shall promote thee: she shall bring thee to honour, when thou dost embrace her.

9 She shall give to thine head an [aornament](#note9a) of grace: a crown of [bglory](#note9b) shall she deliver to thee.

10 Hear, O my son, and receive my sayings; and the [ayears](#note10a) of thy life shall be many.

11 I have taught thee in the way of wisdom; I have [aled](#note11a) thee in right paths.

12 When thou goest, thy steps shall not be [astraitened](#note12a); and when thou [brunnest](#note12b), thou shalt not stumble.

13 Take fast [ahold](#note13a) of [binstruction](#note13b); let her not go: keep her; for she is thy life.

14 ¶ Enter not into the [apath](#note14a) of the wicked, and go not in the way of evil men.

15 Avoid it, pass not by it, turn from it, and pass away.

16 For they sleep not, except they have done mischief; and their sleep is taken away, unless they cause some to fall.

17 For they eat the bread of wickedness, and drink the wine of violence.

18 But the [apath](#note18a) of the [bjust](#note18b) is as the shining [clight](#note18c), that [dshineth](#note18d) more and more unto the perfect day.

19 The way of the wicked is as [adarkness](#note19a): they know not at what they stumble.

20 ¶ My son, attend to my words; incline thine ear unto my sayings.

21 Let them not depart from thine [aeyes](#note21a); [bkeep](#note21b) them in the midst of thine heart.

22 For they are [alife](#note22a) unto those that find them, and health to all their flesh.

23 ¶ Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it are the issues of life.

24 Put away from thee a [afroward](#note24a) mouth, and perverse lips put far from thee.

25 Let thine [aeyes](#note25a) look right on, and let thine eyelids look straight before thee.

26 [aPonder](#note26a) the path of thy feet, and let all thy ways be established.

27 Turn not to the right hand nor to the left: remove thy foot from evil.

Chapter 11

The state and rewards of the righteous and the wicked are contrasted—When a wicked man dies, his expectations perish—He who wins souls is wise.

1 [aA](#note1a) false [bbalance](#note1b) is abomination to the Lord: but a just weight is his delight.

2 When [apride](#note2a) cometh, then cometh [bshame](#note2b): but with the lowly is wisdom.

3 The [aintegrity](#note3a) of the upright shall guide them: but the perverseness of transgressors shall destroy them.

4 [aRiches](#note4a) profit not in the day of [bwrath](#note4b): but righteousness delivereth from death.

5 The [arighteousness](#note5a) of the perfect shall direct his way: but the wicked shall [bfall](#note5b) by his own wickedness.

6 The righteousness of the upright shall deliver them: but transgressors shall be taken in their own naughtiness.

7 When a wicked man dieth, his expectation shall perish: and the hope of unjust men perisheth.

8 The righteous is delivered out of trouble, and the wicked cometh in his stead.

9 An [ahypocrite](#note9a) with his mouth destroyeth his neighbour: but through knowledge shall the [bjust](#note9b) be delivered.

10 When it goeth well with the [arighteous](#note10a), the city rejoiceth: and when the wicked perish, there is shouting.

11 By the blessing of the upright the city is exalted: but it is overthrown by the mouth of the wicked.

12 He that is void of wisdom despiseth his [aneighbour](#note12a): but a man of understanding holdeth his peace.

13 A [atalebearer](#note13a) revealeth secrets: but he that is of a faithful spirit concealeth the matter.

14 Where no [acounsel](#note14a) is, the people fall: but in the [bmultitude](#note14b) of [ccounsellors](#note14c) there is safety.

15 He that is [asurety](#note15a) for a stranger shall smart for it: and he that hateth suretiship is sure.

16 A gracious [awoman](#note16a) retaineth honour: and strong men retain riches.

17 The merciful man doeth good to his own soul: but he that is [acruel](#note17a) troubleth his own flesh.

18 The wicked worketh a [adeceitful](#note18a) work: but to him that soweth [brighteousness](#note18b) shall be a sure [creward](#note18c).

19 As righteousness tendeth to [alife](#note19a): so he that pursueth evil pursueth it to his own death.

20 They that are [aof](#note20a) a [bfroward](#note20b) heart are abomination to the Lord: but such as are upright in their way are his delight.

21 Though hand join in hand, the wicked shall not be unpunished: but the seed of the righteous shall be delivered.

22 As a jewel of gold in a swine’s snout, so is a fair woman which is without discretion.

23 The desire of the righteous is only good: but the expectation of the wicked is wrath.

24 There is that scattereth, and yet [aincreaseth](#note24a); and there is that withholdeth more than is meet, but it tendeth to poverty.

25 The liberal soul shall be made fat: and he that [awatereth](#note25a) shall be watered also himself.

26 He that withholdeth corn, the people shall curse him: but blessing shall be upon the head of him that selleth it.

27 He that diligently seeketh good procureth favour: but he that seeketh mischief, it shall come unto him.

28 He that [atrusteth](#note28a) in his riches shall fall: but the righteous shall flourish as a branch.

29 He that troubleth his own [ahouse](#note29a) shall inherit the wind: and the fool shall be servant to the wise of heart.

30 The fruit of the righteous is a [atree of life](#note30a); and he that winneth souls is wise.

31 Behold, the righteous shall be [arecompensed](#note31a) in the earth: much more the wicked and the sinner.

3 A man shall not be established by wickedness: but the root of the righteous shall not be moved.

4 A [avirtuous](#note4a) [bwoman](#note4b) is a crown to her [chusband](#note4c): but she that maketh ashamed is as rottenness in his bones.

10 A righteous man regardeth the life of his [abeast](#note10a): but the tender mercies of the wicked are [bcruel](#note10b).

11 He that tilleth his land shall be satisfied with bread: but he that followeth vain persons is void of understanding.

12 The wicked desireth the net of evil men: but the root of the righteous yieldeth fruit.

13 The wicked is snared by the [atransgression](#note13a) of his [blips](#note13b): but the just shall come out of trouble.

18 There is that speaketh like the piercings of a sword: but the tongue of the wise is health.

21 There shall no evil happen to the just: but the wicked shall be filled with mischief.

22 [aLying](#note22a) lips are abomination to the Lord: but they that deal [btruly](#note22b) are his [cdelight](#note22c).

24 The hand of the [adiligent](#note24a) shall bear rule: but the [bslothful](#note24b) shall be under tribute.

25 Heaviness in the heart of man maketh it stoop: but a good word maketh it glad.

26 The righteous is more excellent than his neighbour: but the way of the wicked seduceth them.

27 The [aslothful](#note27a) man roasteth not that which he took in hunting: but the substance of a [bdiligent](#note27b) man is precious.

28 In the way of [arighteousness](#note28a) is life; and in the pathway thereof there is no death.

2 A man shall eat good by the fruit of his mouth: but the soul of the transgressors shall eat violence.

13 Whoso [adespiseth](#note13a) the word shall be destroyed: but he that feareth the commandment shall be [brewarded](#note13b).

14 The law of the wise is a [afountain](#note14a) of life, to depart from the snares of death.

15 Good understanding giveth favour: but the way of transgressors is hard.

16 Every [aprudent](#note16a) man dealeth with knowledge: but a fool layeth open his folly.

17 A wicked messenger falleth into mischief: but a faithful ambassador is health.

18 Poverty and [ashame](#note18a) shall be to him that refuseth [binstruction](#note18b): but he that regardeth [creproof](#note18c) shall be honoured.

Chapter 14

Go from the presence of a foolish man—A true witness delivers souls—Righteousness exalts a nation.

1 Every wise [awoman](#note1a) buildeth her house: but the foolish plucketh it down with her hands.

2 He that [awalketh](#note2a) in his uprightness feareth the Lord: but he that is perverse in his ways despiseth him.

3 In the mouth of the foolish is a rod of pride: but the lips of the wise shall preserve them.

4 Where no oxen are, the crib is clean: but much increase is by the strength of the ox.

5 A faithful witness will not lie: but a false witness will utter lies.

6 A scorner seeketh wisdom, and findeth it not: but [aknowledge](#note6a) is easy unto him that understandeth.

It is better to get wisdom than gold—Pride goes before destruction—The gray hair of the righteous person is a crown of glory.

10 A divine sentence is in the lips of the king: his mouth transgresseth not in judgment.

11 A just weight and balance are the Lord’s: all the weights of the bag are his work.

12 It is an abomination to kings to commit [awickedness](#note12a): for the throne is established by righteousness.

13 [aRighteous](#note13a) [blips](#note13b) are the delight of kings; and they love him that speaketh right.

18 [aPride](#note18a) goeth before destruction, and an [bhaughty](#note18b) spirit before a fall.

28 A froward man soweth [astrife](#note28a): and a whisperer separateth chief friends.

29 A violent man enticeth his neighbour, and leadeth him into the way that is not good.

30 He shutteth his eyes to devise froward things: moving his lips he bringeth evil to pass.

31 [aThe hoary head](#note31a) is a crown of glory, if it be found in the way of righteousness.

32 He that is slow to [aanger](#note32a) is better than the mighty; and he that ruleth his spirit than he that taketh a city.

33 The [alot](#note33a) is cast into the lap; but the whole disposing thereof is of the Lord.

Chapter 17

He who is glad at calamities will be punished—A friend loves at all times—Even a fool, when he holds his peace, is counted wise.

1 Better is a dry morsel, and [aquietness](#note1a) therewith, than an house full of sacrifices with [bstrife](#note1b).

2 A wise [aservant](#note2a) shall have rule over a son that causeth shame, and shall have part of the inheritance among the brethren.

3 The [afining pot](#note3a) is for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the Lord [btrieth](#note3b) the [chearts](#note3c).

4 A wicked doer giveth heed to false lips; and a liar giveth ear to a naughty tongue.

5 Whoso [amocketh](#note5a) the [bpoor](#note5b) reproacheth his Maker: and he that is [cglad](#note5c) at calamities shall not be unpunished.

6 [aChildren’s](#note6a) children are the [bcrown](#note6b) of old men; and the [cglory](#note6c) of [dchildren](#note6d) are their fathers.

7 Excellent [aspeech](#note7a) becometh not a fool: much less do lying lips a prince.

8 A gift is as a precious stone in the eyes of him that hath it: whithersoever it turneth, it prospereth.

9 He that [acovereth a transgression](#note9a) [bseeketh](#note9b) [clove](#note9c); but he that repeateth a matter separateth very friends.

10 A [areproof](#note10a) entereth more into a wise man than an hundred stripes into a fool.

11 An evil man seeketh only [arebellion](#note11a): therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him.

12 Let a [abear](#note12a) robbed of her whelps meet a man, rather than a fool in his folly.

13 Whoso [arewardeth](#note13a) [bevil](#note13b) for good, evil shall not depart from his house.

14 The beginning of [astrife](#note14a) is as when one letteth out water: therefore leave off [bcontention](#note14b), before it be meddled with.

15 He that [ajustifieth](#note15a) the [bwicked](#note15b), and he that condemneth the just, even they both are abomination to the Lord.

16 Wherefore is there a price in the hand of a fool to get wisdom, seeing he hath no heart to it?

17 A [afriend](#note17a) loveth at all times, and a [bbrother](#note17b) is born for adversity.

18 A man void of understanding striketh hands, and becometh surety in the presence of his friend.

19 He loveth transgression that loveth strife: and he that [aexalteth](#note19a) his gate seeketh destruction.

20 He that hath a [afroward](#note20a) heart findeth no good: and he that hath a perverse tongue falleth into mischief.

21 He that begetteth a [afool](#note21a) doeth it to his sorrow: and the father of a fool hath no joy.

22 A [amerry](#note22a) [bheart](#note22b) doeth good like a medicine: but a broken spirit drieth the bones.

23 A wicked man taketh a [agift](#note23a) out of the bosom to pervert the ways of judgment.

24 Wisdom is before him that hath [aunderstanding](#note24a); but the [beyes](#note24b) of a fool are in the ends of the earth.

25 A [afoolish](#note25a) son is a grief to his father, and bitterness to her that bare him.

24 A [aslothful](#note24a) man hideth his hand in his bosom, and will not so much as bring it to his mouth again.

26 He that wasteth his [afather](#note26a), and chaseth away his mother, is a son that causeth shame, and bringeth reproach.

27 Cease, my son, to [ahear](#note27a) the instruction that causeth to err from the words of knowledge.

28 An ungodly witness scorneth judgment: and the mouth of the wicked devoureth iniquity.

29 Judgments are prepared for scorners, and [astripes](#note29a) for the back of fools.

11 He that loveth pureness of heart, for the grace of his lips the king shall be his friend.

Chapter 23

Labor not to be rich—As a man thinks in his heart, so is he—Withhold not correction from a child—Be not among drunkards.

16 Yea, my reins shall rejoice, when thy lips speak right things.

18 For surely there is an end; and thine expectation shall not be cut off.

19 Hear thou, my son, and be wise, and guide thine heart in the way.

20 Be not among [awinebibbers](#note20a); among [briotous](#note20b) eaters of flesh:

Chapter 24

In a multitude of counselors there is safety—Fret not yourself because of evil men—It is not good to show partiality in judgment.

1 Be not thou [aenvious](#note1a) against [bevil](#note1b) men, neither desire to be with them.

2 For their heart studieth destruction, and their lips talk of mischief.

3 Through wisdom is an house builded; and by understanding it is established:

4 And by knowledge shall the chambers be filled with all precious and pleasant riches.

5 A wise man is [astrong](#note5a); yea, a man of knowledge increaseth strength.

6 For by wise counsel thou shalt make thy [awar](#note6a): and in [bmultitude](#note6b) of [ccounsellors](#note6c) there is safety.

7 Wisdom is too high for a [afool](#note7a): he openeth not his mouth in the gate.

8 He that deviseth to do evil shall be called a mischievous person.

9 The thought of [afoolishness](#note9a) is sin: and the scorner is an abomination to men.

10 If thou [afaint](#note10a) in the day of adversity, thy strength is small.

11 If thou forbear to deliver them that are drawn unto death, and those that are ready to be slain;

12 If thou sayest, Behold, we knew it not; doth not he that [apondereth](#note12a) the heart consider it? and he that keepeth thy soul, doth not he know it? and shall not he render to every man according to his [bworks](#note12b)?

13 My son, eat thou [ahoney](#note13a), because it is good; and the honeycomb, which is sweet to thy taste:

14 So shall the knowledge of wisdom be unto thy soul: when thou hast found it, then there shall be a reward, and thy expectation shall not be cut off.

15 Lay not wait, O wicked man, against the dwelling of the righteous; spoil not his resting place:

16 For a just man falleth seven times, and [ariseth](#note16a) up again: but the wicked shall fall into mischief.

17 Rejoice not when thine [aenemy](#note17a) falleth, and let not thine heart be glad when he stumbleth:

18 Lest the Lord see it, and it displease him, and he turn away his wrath from him.

19 Fret not thyself because of evil men, neither be thou [aenvious](#note19a) at the wicked;

20 For there shall be no reward to the evil man; the [acandle](#note20a) of the wicked shall be put out.

21 My son, [afear](#note21a) thou the Lord and the king: and meddle not with them that are given to change:

22 For their calamity shall rise suddenly; and who knoweth the ruin of them both?

23 These things also belong to the wise. It is not good to have [arespect](#note23a) of persons in judgment.

24 He that saith unto the [awicked](#note24a), Thou art righteous; him shall the people curse, nations shall abhor him:

25 But to them that rebuke him shall be delight, and a good blessing shall come upon them.

26 Every man shall kiss his lips that giveth a right answer.

27 [aPrepare](#note27a) thy work without, and make it fit for thyself in the field; and afterwards build thine house.

28 Be not a [awitness](#note28a) against thy neighbour without cause; and [bdeceive](#note28b) not with thy lips.

29 Say not, I will [ado](#note29a) so to him as he hath done to me: I will render to the man according to his work.

33 Yet a little [asleep](#note33a), a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep:

34 So shall thy poverty come as one [athat travelleth](#note34a); and thy want as an armed man.

Chapter 25

Boast not of false gifts—Give food and drink to your enemy.

1 These are also [aproverbs](#note1a) of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out.

2 It is the glory of God to [aconceal](#note2a) a thing: but the honour of kings is to search out a matter.

3 The heaven for height, and the earth for depth, and the heart of kings is unsearchable.

4 Take away the [adross](#note4a) from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer.

5 [aTake](#note5a) away the wicked from before the [bking](#note5b), and his throne shall be established in righteousness.

6 Put not forth thyself in the presence of the king, and stand not in the place of great men:

7 For better it is that it be said unto thee, [aCome](#note7a) up hither; than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the prince whom thine eyes have seen.

8 Go not forth hastily to [astrive](#note8a), lest thou know not what to do in the end thereof, when thy neighbour hath put thee to shame.

9 Debate thy cause with thy neighbour himself; and [adiscover not](#note9a) a secret to another:

10 Lest he that heareth it put thee to shame, and thine infamy turn not away.

11 A [aword](#note11a) fitly spoken is like apples of gold in pictures of silver.

12 As an earring of gold, and an [aornament](#note12a) of fine gold, so is a wise [breprover](#note12b) upon an obedient ear.

13 As the cold of snow in the time of harvest, so is a faithful messenger to them that send him: for he refresheth the soul of his masters.

14 Whoso [aboasteth](#note14a) himself of a false gift is like clouds and wind without rain.

15 By long [aforbearing](#note15a) is a prince [bpersuaded](#note15b), and a soft tongue breaketh the bone.

16 Hast thou found [ahoney](#note16a)? eat so much as is sufficient for thee, lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it.

17 Withdraw thy foot from thy neighbour’s house; lest he be weary of thee, and so hate thee.

18 A man that [abeareth](#note18a) [bfalse witness](#note18b) against his neighbour is a [cmaul](#note18c), and a [dsword](#note18d), and a sharp arrow.

19 Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble is like a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint.

20 As he that taketh away a garment in cold weather, and as vinegar upon nitre, so is he that singeth songs to an heavy heart.

21 If thine [aenemy](#note21a) be [bhungry](#note21b), give him bread to eat; and if he be thirsty, give him water to drink:

22 For thou shalt heap coals of fire upon his head, and the Lord shall [areward](#note22a) thee.

23 The north wind driveth away rain: so doth an angry countenance a [abackbiting](#note23a) tongue.

24 It is better to [adwell](#note24a) in the corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman and in a wide house.

25 As cold waters to a thirsty soul, so is good news from a far country.

26 A righteous man falling down before the wicked is as [aa troubled fountain](#note26a), and a corrupt spring.

27 It is not good to eat much honey: so for men to search their own [aglory](#note27a) is not glory.

28 He that hath no [arule](#note28a) over his own spirit is like a city that is broken down, and without walls.

Chapter 26

Honor is not fitting for a fool—Answer not a fool according to his folly—Where there is no talebearer, the strife ceases.

1 As snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, so [ahonour](#note1a) is not seemly for a fool.

2 As the bird by wandering, as the swallow by flying, so the curse causeless shall not come.

3 A whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, and a rod for the fool’s back.

4 Answer not a fool according to his folly, lest thou also be like unto him.

5 [aAnswer](#note5a) a fool according to his folly, lest he be wise in his [bown conceit](#note5b).

6 He that sendeth a message by the hand of a fool cutteth off the feet, and drinketh [adamage](#note6a).

7 The legs of the lame are not equal: so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

8 As he that bindeth a stone in a sling, so is he that giveth honour to a fool.

9 As a thorn goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, so is a parable in the mouth of fools.

10 The great God that formed all things both [arewardeth](#note10a) the fool, and rewardeth transgressors.

11 As a dog returneth to his [avomit](#note11a), so a fool returneth to his folly.

12 Seest thou a man wise in his own [aconceit](#note12a)? there is more hope of a [bfool](#note12b) than of him.

13 The [aslothful](#note13a) man saith, There is a lion in the way; a lion is in the streets.

14 As the door turneth upon his hinges, so doth the slothful upon his bed.

15 The slothful hideth his hand in his bosom; it grieveth him to bring it again to his mouth.

16 The sluggard is wiser in his own conceit than seven men that can render a reason.

17 He that passeth by, and meddleth with strife belonging not to him, is like one that [ataketh](#note17a) a dog by the ears.

18 As a mad man who casteth firebrands, arrows, and death,

19 So is the man that [adeceiveth](#note19a) his neighbour, and saith, Am not I in sport?

20 Where no wood is, there the fire goeth out: so where there is no [atalebearer](#note20a), the [bstrife](#note20b) ceaseth.

21 As coals are to burning coals, and wood to fire; so is a [acontentious](#note21a) man to kindle strife.

22 The words of a talebearer are as wounds, and they go down into the innermost parts of the belly.

23 Burning lips and a wicked heart are like a [apotsherd](#note23a) covered with silver dross.

24 He that [ahateth](#note24a) dissembleth with his lips, and layeth up [bdeceit](#note24b) within him;

25 When he [aspeaketh](#note25a) fair, believe him not: for there are seven abominations in his heart.

26 Whose hatred is covered by deceit, his wickedness shall be [ashewed](#note26a) before the whole congregation.

27 Whoso diggeth a [apit](#note27a) shall fall therein: and he that rolleth a stone, it will return upon him.

28 A [alying](#note28a) tongue hateth those that are afflicted by it; and a [bflattering](#note28b) mouth worketh ruin.

Chapter 27

Let another man praise you—A prudent man foresees evil—Hell and destruction are never full.

1 [aBoast](#note1a) not thyself of to [bmorrow](#note1b); for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.

2 Let another man [apraise](#note2a) thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips.

3 A stone is heavy, and the sand weighty; but a fool’s [awrath](#note3a) is heavier than them both.

4 Wrath is [acruel](#note4a), and [banger](#note4b) is outrageous; but who is able to stand before envy?

5 Open [arebuke](#note5a) is better than secret love.

6 Faithful are the [awounds](#note6a) of a [bfriend](#note6b); but the [ckisses](#note6c) of an enemy are [ddeceitful](#note6d).

7 The full soul loatheth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet.

8 As a bird that wandereth from her nest, so is a man that wandereth from his place.

9 Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man’s friend by hearty counsel.

10 Thine own [afriend](#note10a), and thy father’s friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother’s house in the day of thy calamity: for better is a [bneighbour](#note10b) that is near than a brother far off.

11 My son, be wise, and make my heart glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me.

12 A prudent man foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself; but the simple pass on, and are punished.

13 Take his [agarment](#note13a) that is surety for a stranger, and take a pledge of him for a [bstrange woman](#note13b).

14 He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a [acurse](#note14a) to him.

15 A [acontinual](#note15a) dropping in a very rainy day and a [bcontentious](#note15b) woman are alike.

16 Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the ointment of his right hand, which bewrayeth itself.

17 Iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his [afriend](#note17a).

18 Whoso keepeth the fig tree shall [aeat](#note18a) the fruit thereof: so he that [bwaiteth](#note18b) on his master shall be honoured.

19 As in water face answereth to face, so the heart of man to man.

20 [aHell](#note20a) and destruction are never full; so the eyes of man are never [bsatisfied](#note20b).

21 As the fining pot for silver, and the furnace for gold; so is a man to his praise.

22 Though thou shouldest [abray](#note22a) a fool in a mortar among wheat with a pestle, yet will not his foolishness depart from him.

23 Be thou diligent to know the state of thy [aflocks](#note23a), and look well to thy herds.

24 For [ariches](#note24a) are not for ever: and doth the crown endure to every generation?

25 The hay appeareth, and the tender grass sheweth itself, and herbs of the mountains are gathered.

26 The lambs are for thy clothing, and the goats are the price of the field.

27 And thou shalt have goats’ milk enough for thy food, for the food of thy household, and for the maintenance for thy maidens.

Chapter 28

The wicked flee when no man pursues—Whoever walks uprightly will be saved—A faithful man will abound with blessings.

1 The wicked [aflee](#note1a) when no man pursueth: but the [brighteous](#note1b) are bold as a lion.

2 For the transgression of a land many are the princes thereof: but by a man of understanding and knowledge the state thereof shall be prolonged.

3 A poor man that [aoppresseth](#note3a) the poor is like a sweeping rain which leaveth no food.

4 They that forsake the law praise the wicked: but such as keep the law contend with them.

5 Evil men [aunderstand](#note5a) not judgment: but they that seek the Lord understand all things.

6 Better is the poor that walketh in his [auprightness](#note6a), than he that is perverse in his ways, though he be rich.

7 Whoso keepeth the law is a wise son: but he that is a companion of [ariotous](#note7a) men [bshameth](#note7b) his father.

8 He that by [ausury](#note8a) and unjust gain [bincreaseth](#note8b) his substance, he shall gather it for him that will pity the poor.

9 He that turneth away his ear from hearing the [alaw](#note9a), even his [bprayer](#note9b) shall be abomination.

10 Whoso causeth the righteous to go astray in an evil way, he shall [afall](#note10a) himself into his own [bpit](#note10b): but the upright shall have good [cthings](#note10c) in possession.

11 The rich man is wise in his own conceit; but the [apoor](#note11a) that hath understanding searcheth him out.

12 When [arighteous](#note12a) men do rejoice, there is great glory: but when the wicked rise, a man is hidden.

13 He that [acovereth](#note13a) his sins shall not prosper: but whoso [bconfesseth](#note13b) and [cforsaketh](#note13c) them shall have mercy.

14 Happy is the man that [afeareth](#note14a) alway: but he that [bhardeneth](#note14b) his heart shall fall into mischief.

15 As a [aroaring lion](#note15a), and a ranging bear; so is a wicked ruler over the poor people.

16 The prince that wanteth understanding is also a great oppressor: but he that hateth [acovetousness](#note16a) shall prolong his days.

17 A man that doeth violence to the [ablood](#note17a) of any person shall [bflee to the pit](#note17b); let no man stay him.

18 Whoso [awalketh](#note18a) uprightly shall be saved: but he that is perverse in his ways shall fall at once.

19 He that tilleth his land shall have plenty of bread: but he that followeth after vain persons shall have poverty enough.

20 A faithful man shall abound with blessings: but he that maketh [ahaste](#note20a) to be [brich](#note20b) shall not be innocent.

21 To have [arespect](#note21a) of persons is not good: for for a [bpiece](#note21b) of [cbread](#note21c) that man will transgress.

22 He that hasteth to be rich hath an evil eye, and considereth not that poverty shall come upon him.

23 He that [arebuketh](#note23a) a man afterwards shall find more favour than he that [bflattereth](#note23b) with the tongue.

24 Whoso robbeth his father or his mother, and saith, It is no transgression; the same is the companion of a destroyer.

25 He that is of a [aproud](#note25a) heart stirreth up [bstrife](#note25b): but he that putteth his [ctrust](#note25c) in the Lord shall be [dmade fat](#note25d).

26 He that [atrusteth](#note26a) in his own heart is a fool: but whoso walketh wisely, he shall be delivered.

27 He that giveth unto the [apoor](#note27a) shall not lack: but he that hideth his eyes shall have many a curse.

28 When the [awicked](#note28a) rise, men hide themselves: but when they perish, the righteous increase.

Chapter 29

When the wicked rule, the people mourn—The righteous consider the cause of the poor—A fool speaks all that is in his mind—Where there is no vision, the people perish.

1 He, that being often reproved hardeneth his neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without [aremedy](#note1a).

2 When the [arighteous](#note2a) are in [bauthority](#note2b), the people [crejoice](#note2c): but when the [dwicked](#note2d) beareth [erule](#note2e), the people [fmourn](#note2f).

3 Whoso loveth wisdom rejoiceth his father: but he that keepeth company with harlots [aspendeth](#note3a) his substance.

4 The king by judgment establisheth the land: but he that receiveth gifts overthroweth it.

5 A man that [aflattereth](#note5a) his neighbour spreadeth a net for his feet.

6 In the [atransgression](#note6a) of an evil man there is a [bsnare](#note6b): but the righteous doth sing and rejoice.

7 The righteous considereth the cause of the poor: but the wicked regardeth not to know it.

8 Scornful men bring a city into a snare: but wise men turn away wrath.

9 If a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, there is no rest.

10 The [abloodthirsty](#note10a) hate the upright: but the just seek his soul.

11 A fool [auttereth](#note11a) all his [bmind](#note11b): but a [cwise](#note11c) man keepeth it in till afterwards.

12 If a ruler hearken to lies, all his servants are wicked.

13 The poor and the deceitful man meet together: the Lord lighteneth both their eyes.

14 The [aking](#note14a) that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne shall be established for ever.

15 The [arod](#note15a) and [breproof](#note15b) give wisdom: but a [cchild](#note15c) left to himself bringeth his [dmother](#note15d) to [eshame](#note15e).

16 When the wicked are multiplied, transgression increaseth: but the righteous shall see their fall.

17 Correct thy son, and he shall give thee rest; yea, he shall give delight unto thy soul.

18 Where there is no [avision](#note18a), the people [bperish](#note18b): but he that [ckeepeth](#note18c) the [dlaw](#note18d), [ehappy](#note18e) is he.

19 A servant will not be corrected by words: for though he understand he will not answer.

20 Seest thou a man that is [ahasty](#note20a) in his words? there is more hope of a [bfool](#note20b) than of him.

21 He that delicately bringeth up his servant from a child shall have him become his son at the length.

22 An [aangry](#note22a) man stirreth up [bstrife](#note22b), and a furious man aboundeth in transgression.

23 A man’s [apride](#note23a) shall bring him low: but honour shall uphold the [bhumble](#note23b) in spirit.

24 Whoso is partner with a thief [ahateth](#note24a) his own soul: he heareth cursing, and bewrayeth it not.

25 The [afear](#note25a) of man bringeth a snare: but whoso putteth his [btrust](#note25b) in the Lord shall be safe.

26 Many seek the ruler’s [afavour](#note26a); but every man’s judgment cometh from the Lord.

27 An unjust man is an abomination to the just: and he that is upright in the way is abomination to the [awicked](#note27a).

Chapter 30

Every word of God is pure—Give me neither poverty nor riches.

1 The words of Agur the son of Jakeh, even the prophecy: the man spake unto Ithiel, even unto Ithiel and Ucal,

2 Surely I am more brutish than any man, and have not the understanding of a man.

3 I neither learned wisdom, nor have the [aknowledge](#note3a) of the holy.

4 Who hath ascended up into heaven, or descended? who hath gathered the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what is his name, and what is his son’s name, if thou canst tell?

5 Every word of God is [apure](#note5a): he is a [bshield](#note5b) unto them that put their trust in him.

6 [aAdd](#note6a) thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar.

7 Two things have I required of thee; deny me them not before I die:

8 Remove far from me [avanity](#note8a) and lies: give me neither poverty nor [briches](#note8b); feed me with [cfood convenient for me](#note8c):

9 Lest I be full, and [adeny](#note9a) thee, and say, Who is the Lord? or lest I be poor, and steal, and take the name of my God in vain.

10 Accuse not a servant unto his master, lest he curse thee, and thou be found guilty.

11 There is a generation that curseth their father, and doth not bless their mother.

12 There is a [ageneration](#note12a) that are [bpure](#note12b) in their own eyes, and yet is not washed from their [cfilthiness](#note12c).

13 There is a generation, O how lofty are their [aeyes](#note13a)! and their eyelids are lifted up.

14 There is a generation, whose [ateeth](#note14a) are as swords, and their jaw teeth as knives, to devour the poor from off the earth, and the needy from among men.

15 The [ahorseleach](#note15a) hath two daughters, crying, Give, give. There are three things that are never satisfied, yea, four things say not, It is enough:

16 The grave; and the barren womb; the earth that is not filled with water; and the fire that saith not, It is enough.

17 The eye that mocketh at his father, and despiseth to obey his mother, the ravens of the valley shall pick it out, and the young eagles shall eat it.

18 There be three things which are too wonderful for me, yea, four which I know not:

19 The way of an eagle in the air; the way of a serpent upon a rock; the way of a ship in the midst of the sea; and the way of a man with a maid.

20 Such is the way of an adulterous woman; she eateth, and wipeth her [amouth](#note20a), and saith, I have done no wickedness.

21 For three things the earth is disquieted, and for four which it cannot bear:

22 For a [aservant](#note22a) when he reigneth; and a fool when he is filled with meat;

23 For an odious woman when she is married; and an handmaid that is heir to her mistress.

24 There be four things which are little upon the earth, but they are exceeding wise:

25 The [aants](#note25a) are a people not strong, yet they prepare their meat in the summer;

26 The [aconies](#note26a) are but a feeble folk, yet make they their houses in the rocks;

27 The locusts have no king, yet go they forth all of them by bands;

28 The spider taketh hold with her hands, and is in kings’ palaces.

29 There be three things which go well, yea, four are comely in going:

30 A lion which is strongest among beasts, and turneth not away for any;

31 A [agreyhound](#note31a); an he goat also; and a king, against whom there is no rising up.

32 If thou hast done foolishly in lifting up thyself, or if thou hast thought evil, lay thine hand upon thy mouth.

33 Surely the churning of milk bringeth forth butter, and the wringing of the nose bringeth forth blood: so the forcing of wrath bringeth forth [astrife](#note33a).

Chapter 31

Wine and strong drink are condemned—Plead the cause of the poor and needy—A virtuous woman is more precious than rubies.

1 The words of king Lemuel, the prophecy that his [amother](#note1a) taught him.

2 What, my son? and what, the son of my womb? and what, the son of my vows?

3 Give not thy strength unto [awomen](#note3a), nor thy ways to that which destroyeth kings.

4 It is not for kings, O Lemuel, it is not for kings to drink [awine](#note4a); nor for princes strong drink:

5 Lest they drink, and forget the law, and pervert the judgment of any of the afflicted.

6 Give strong drink unto him that is ready to perish, and wine unto those that be of heavy hearts.

7 Let him drink, and forget his poverty, and remember his misery no more.

8 Open thy mouth for the dumb in the cause of all such as are appointed to destruction.

9 Open thy mouth, judge righteously, and plead the [acause](#note9a) of the poor and needy.

10 ¶ Who can find a [avirtuous](#note10a) [bwoman](#note10b)? for her price is far above rubies.

11 The heart of her husband doth safely [atrust](#note11a) in her, so that he shall have no need of spoil.

12 She will do him good and not evil all the days of her life.

13 She seeketh wool, and flax, and worketh willingly with her [ahands](#note13a).

14 She is like the merchants’ ships; she bringeth her food from afar.

15 She [ariseth](#note15a) also while it is yet night, and giveth meat to her household, and a portion to her maidens.

16 She considereth a field, and buyeth it: with the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard.

17 She girdeth her loins with strength, and strengtheneth her arms.

18 She perceiveth that her merchandise is good: her candle goeth not out by night.

19 She layeth her hands to the spindle, and her hands hold the [adistaff](#note19a).

20 She stretcheth out her hand to the [apoor](#note20a); yea, she reacheth forth her hands to the needy.

21 She is not afraid of the snow for her household: for all her household are clothed with scarlet.

22 She maketh herself coverings of tapestry; her [aclothing](#note22a) is silk and purple.

23 Her husband is known in the gates, when he sitteth among the elders of the land.

24 She maketh fine linen, and selleth it; and delivereth girdles unto the merchant.

25 Strength and honour are her [aclothing](#note25a); and she shall rejoice in time to come.

26 She openeth her mouth with wisdom; and in her tongue is the law of [akindness](#note26a).

27 She looketh well to the ways of her household, and eateth not the bread of [aidleness](#note27a).

28 Her children arise up, and call her blessed; her husband also, and he praiseth her.

29 Many daughters have done virtuously, but thou excellest them all.

30 Favour is deceitful, and [abeauty](#note30a) is vain: but a woman that [bfeareth the Lord](#note30b), she shall be praised.

31 Give her of the fruit of her hands; and let her own works praise her in the gates.
