Chapter 1

The Jews despise the Lord by offering polluted bread upon the altar and by sacrificing animals with blemishes—The Lord’s name will be great among the Gentiles.

1 The burden of the [aword](#note1a) of the Lord to Israel by Malachi.

2 I have [aloved](#note2a) you, saith the Lord. Yet ye say, Wherein hast thou loved us? Was not Esau Jacob’s brother? saith the Lord: yet I [bloved](#note2b) Jacob,

3 And I hated Esau, and laid his mountains and his [aheritage](#note3a) [bwaste](#note3b) for the [cdragons](#note3c) of the wilderness.

4 Whereas Edom saith, We are impoverished, but we will return and build the desolate places; thus saith the Lord of hosts, They shall build, but I will throw down; and they shall call them, The border of wickedness, and, The people against whom the Lord hath indignation for ever.

5 And your eyes shall see, and ye shall say, The Lord will be magnified from the border of Israel.

6 ¶ A son [ahonoureth](#note6a) his father, and a servant his master: if then I be a father, where is mine [bhonour](#note6b)? and if I be a master, where is my fear? saith the Lord of hosts unto you, O priests, that despise my name. And ye say, Wherein have we despised thy name?

7 Ye offer polluted bread upon mine altar; and ye say, Wherein have we polluted thee? In that ye say, The table of the Lord is contemptible.

8 And if ye offer the [ablind](#note8a) for [bsacrifice](#note8b), is it not evil? and if ye offer [cthe lame and sick](#note8c), is it not evil? offer it now unto thy governor; will he be pleased with thee, or accept thy person? saith the Lord of hosts.

9 And now, I pray you, beseech God that he will be gracious unto us: this hath been by your means: will he regard your persons? saith the Lord of hosts.

10 Who is there even among you that would shut the doors for nought? neither do ye kindle fire on mine altar for nought. I have no pleasure in you, saith the Lord of hosts, neither will I [aaccept](#note10a) an offering at your hand.

11 For from the [arising](#note11a) of the sun even unto the going down of the same my [bname](#note11b) shall be great among the [cGentiles](#note11c); and in every place incense shall be offered unto my name, and a pure offering: for my name shall be great among the heathen, saith the Lord of hosts.

12 ¶ But ye have profaned it, in that ye say, The [atable](#note12a) of the Lord is polluted; and the fruit thereof, even his meat, is contemptible.

13 Ye said also, Behold, what a weariness is it! and ye have [asnuffed at it](#note13a), saith the Lord of hosts; and ye brought that which was torn, and the [blame](#note13b), and the sick; thus ye brought an offering: should I [caccept](#note13c) this of your hand? saith the Lord.

14 But [acursed](#note14a) be the deceiver, which hath in his flock a male, and voweth, and sacrificeth unto the Lord a corrupt thing: for I am a great King, saith the Lord of hosts, and my name is [bdreadful](#note14b) among the heathen.

Chapter 2

The priests are reproved for not keeping their covenants and not teaching the people—The Jews are condemned for dealing treacherously with one another and with their wives.

1 And now, O ye priests, this commandment is for you.

2 If ye will not [ahear](#note2a), and if ye will not lay it to heart, to give glory unto my name, saith the Lord of hosts, I will even send a [bcurse](#note2b) upon you, and I will curse your blessings: yea, I have cursed them already, because ye do not lay it to heart.

3 Behold, I will [acorrupt](#note3a) your seed, and spread dung upon your faces, even the dung of your solemn feasts; and one shall take you away with it.

4 And ye shall know that I have sent this commandment unto you, that my [acovenant](#note4a) might be with Levi, saith the Lord of hosts.

5 My [acovenant](#note5a) was with him of life and peace; and I gave them to him for the fear wherewith he feared me, and was afraid before my name.

6 The law of [atruth](#note6a) was in his mouth, and iniquity was not found in his lips: he walked with me in peace and equity, and did [bturn](#note6b) many away from [ciniquity](#note6c).

7 For the priest’s lips should keep [aknowledge](#note7a), and they should seek the [blaw](#note7b) at his mouth: for he is the [cmessenger](#note7c) of the Lord of hosts.

8 But ye are [adeparted](#note8a) out of the way; ye have caused many to [bstumble](#note8b) at the law; ye have [ccorrupted](#note8c) the [dcovenant](#note8d) of Levi, saith the Lord of hosts.

9 Therefore have I also made you [acontemptible](#note9a) and base before all the people, according as ye have not kept my ways, but have been partial in the law.

10 Have we not all one [afather](#note10a)? hath not one God [bcreated](#note10b) us? why do we deal treacherously every man against his brother, by [cprofaning](#note10c) the covenant of our fathers?

11 ¶ Judah hath dealt treacherously, and an abomination is committed in Israel and in Jerusalem; for Judah hath profaned the holiness of the Lord which he loved, and hath [amarried](#note11a) the daughter of a [bstrange god](#note11b).

12 The Lord will [acut off](#note12a) the man that doeth this, the master and the scholar, out of the tabernacles of Jacob, and him that offereth an offering unto the Lord of hosts.

13 And this have ye done again, covering the altar of the Lord with tears, with weeping, and with crying out, insomuch that he regardeth not the offering any more, or receiveth it with good will at your hand.

14 ¶ Yet ye say, Wherefore? Because the Lord hath been witness between thee and the [awife](#note14a) of thy youth, against whom thou hast dealt treacherously: yet is she thy companion, and the wife of thy covenant.

15 And did not he make [aone](#note15a)? Yet had he the residue of the spirit. And wherefore one? That he might seek a [bgodly](#note15b) [cseed](#note15c). Therefore take heed to your spirit, and let none deal treacherously against the [dwife](#note15d) of his youth.

16 For the Lord, the God of Israel, saith that he hateth [aputting away](#note16a): for one covereth violence with his garment, saith the Lord of hosts: therefore take heed to your spirit, that ye deal not treacherously.

17 ¶ Ye have wearied the Lord with your words. Yet ye say, Wherein have we wearied him? When ye say, Every one that doeth [aevil](#note17a) is good in the sight of the Lord, and he delighteth in them; or, Where is the God of [bjudgment](#note17b)?

Chapter 3

The Lord’s messenger will prepare the way for the Second Coming—The Lord will sit in judgment—The people of Israel are commanded to pay tithes and offerings—They keep a book of remembrance.

1 Behold, I will [asend](#note1a) my [bmessenger](#note1b), and he shall prepare the way before me: and the Lord, whom ye seek, shall suddenly [ccome](#note1c) to his [dtemple](#note1d), even the [emessenger](#note1e) of the covenant, whom ye delight in: behold, he shall come, saith the Lord of hosts.

2 But who may [aabide](#note2a) the [bday](#note2b) of his [ccoming](#note2c)? and who shall [dstand](#note2d) when he appeareth? for he is like a [erefiner’s](#note2e) [ffire](#note2f), and like fullers’ [gsoap](#note2g):

3 And he shall sit as a [arefiner](#note3a) and purifier of silver: and he shall [bpurify](#note3b) the [csons](#note3c) of [dLevi](#note3d), and purge them as gold and silver, that they may offer unto the Lord an [eoffering](#note3e) in righteousness.

4 Then shall the offering of [aJudah](#note4a) and Jerusalem be [bpleasant](#note4b) unto the Lord, as in the days of old, and as in former years.

5 And I will come near to you to [ajudgment](#note5a); and I will be a swift witness against the [bsorcerers](#note5b), and against the [cadulterers](#note5c), and against [dfalse](#note5d) swearers, and against those that [eoppress](#note5e) the hireling in his [fwages](#note5f), the [gwidow](#note5g), and the fatherless, and that turn aside the [hstranger](#note5h) from his right, and fear not me, saith the Lord of hosts.

6 For I am the Lord, I [achange not](#note6a); therefore ye sons of [bJacob](#note6b) are not [cconsumed](#note6c).

7 ¶ Even from the days of your [afathers](#note7a) ye are gone away from mine [bordinances](#note7b), and have not kept them. [cReturn](#note7c) unto me, and I will return unto you, saith the Lord of hosts. But ye said, Wherein shall we return?

8 ¶ [aWill](#note8a) a man [brob](#note8b) God? Yet ye have robbed me. But ye say, Wherein have we robbed thee? In [ctithes](#note8c) and offerings.

9 Ye are [acursed](#note9a) with a curse: for ye have robbed me, even this whole nation.

10 Bring ye all the [atithes](#note10a) into the storehouse, that there may be [bmeat](#note10b) in mine house, and [cprove](#note10c) me now herewith, saith the Lord of hosts, if I will not [dopen](#note10d) you the [ewindows of heaven](#note10e), and pour you out a [fblessing](#note10f), that there shall not be room enough to receive it.

11 And I will [arebuke](#note11a) the [bdevourer](#note11b) for your sakes, and he shall not destroy the fruits of your ground; neither shall your vine cast her fruit before the time in the field, saith the Lord of hosts.

12 And all nations shall call you blessed: for ye shall be a delightsome land, saith the Lord of hosts.

13 ¶ Your words have been [astout](#note13a) against me, saith the Lord. Yet ye say, What have we spoken so much against thee?

14 Ye have said, It is [avain](#note14a) to serve God: and what [bprofit](#note14b) is it that we have kept his ordinance, and that we have walked mournfully before the Lord of hosts?

15 And now we call the [aproud](#note15a) happy; yea, they that work [bwickedness](#note15b) are set up; yea, they that tempt God are even delivered.

16 ¶ Then they that feared the Lord spake often one to another: and the Lord hearkened, and heard it, and a [abook](#note16a) of [bremembrance](#note16b) was written before him for them that feared the Lord, and that thought upon his name.

17 And they shall be [amine](#note17a), saith the Lord of hosts, in that day when I make up my [bjewels](#note17b); and I will [cspare](#note17c) them, as a man spareth his own son that serveth him.

18 Then shall ye return, and [adiscern](#note18a) between the righteous and the wicked, between him that serveth God and him that serveth him not.

Chapter 4

At the Second Coming, the proud and wicked will be burned as stubble—Elijah will return before that great and dreadful day.

1 [aFor](#note1a), behold, the [bday](#note1b) cometh, that shall [cburn](#note1c) as an oven; and all the [dproud](#note1d), yea, and all that do [ewickedly](#note1e), shall be [fstubble](#note1f): and the day that cometh shall burn them up, saith the Lord of hosts, that it shall leave them neither root nor [gbranch](#note1g).

2 ¶ But unto you that fear my name shall the [aSun](#note2a) of righteousness [barise](#note2b) with healing in his wings; and ye shall go forth, and grow up as calves of the [cstall](#note2c).

3 And ye shall [atread](#note3a) down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet in the day that I shall do this, saith the Lord of hosts.

4 ¶ Remember ye the law of Moses my servant, which I [acommanded](#note4a) unto him in [bHoreb](#note4b) for all Israel, with the statutes and judgments.

5 ¶ Behold, I will [asend](#note5a) you [bElijah](#note5b) the prophet [cbefore](#note5c) the coming of the [dgreat](#note5d) and dreadful [eday](#note5e) of the Lord:

6 And he shall [aturn](#note6a) the [bheart](#note6b) of the [cfathers](#note6c) to the [dchildren](#note6d), and the heart of the [echildren](#note6e) to their fathers, lest I come and [fsmite](#note6f) the [gearth](#note6g) with a [hcurse](#note6h).
