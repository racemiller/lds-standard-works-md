Chapter 1

Christ chooses some as kings and priests unto God—Christ will come again—John sees the risen Lord.

1 [aThe](#note1a) [bRevelation](#note1b) of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his [cservants](#note1c) things which must [dshortly](#note1d) come to pass; and he sent and signified it by his [eangel](#note1e) unto his servant John:

2 Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw.

3 [aBlessed](#note3a) is he that [breadeth](#note3b), and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the [ctime](#note3c) is at hand.

4 John to the [aseven](#note4a) churches which are in Asia: Grace be unto you, and peace, from him which [bis](#note4b), and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven [cSpirits](#note4c) which are before his throne;

5 And from Jesus Christ, who is the faithful witness, and the [afirst](#note5a) begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and [bwashed](#note5b) us from our sins in his own [cblood](#note5c),

6 And hath made us [akings](#note6a) and [bpriests](#note6b) unto God and his Father; to him be [cglory](#note6c) and [ddominion](#note6d) for ever and ever. Amen.

7 Behold, he [acometh](#note7a) with clouds; and every eye shall [bsee](#note7b) him, and they also which pierced him: and all [ckindreds](#note7c) of the earth shall [dwail](#note7d) because of him. Even so, Amen.

8 I am [aAlpha and Omega](#note8a), the [bbeginning](#note8b) and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the [cAlmighty](#note8c).

9 I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and [apatience](#note9a) of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ.

10 I was in the Spirit on the [aLord’s](#note10a) [bday](#note10b), and heard behind me a great [cvoice](#note10c), as of a trumpet,

11 Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the [afirst](#note11a) and the last: and, What thou seest, [bwrite](#note11b) in a book, and send it unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto [cThyatira](#note11c), and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto [dLaodicea](#note11d).

12 And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden [acandlesticks](#note12a);

13 And in the midst of the seven candlesticks one like unto the [aSon of man](#note13a), [bclothed](#note13b) with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle.

14 His head and his hairs were white like wool, as white as snow; and his [aeyes](#note14a) were as a flame of [bfire](#note14b);

15 And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his [avoice](#note15a) as the sound of many waters.

16 And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged [asword](#note16a): and his [bcountenance](#note16b) was as the [csun](#note16c) shineth in his strength.

17 And when I [asaw](#note17a) him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last:

18 I am he that [aliveth](#note18a), and was [bdead](#note18b); and, behold, I am alive for [cevermore](#note18c), Amen; and have the [dkeys](#note18d) of [ehell](#note18e) and of death.

19 [aWrite](#note19a) the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter;

20 The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven [astars](#note20a) are the [bangels](#note20b) of the seven churches: and the seven [ccandlesticks](#note20c) which thou sawest are the seven churches.

Chapter 2

He who overcomes will gain eternal life, avoid the second death, inherit the celestial kingdom, and rule many kingdoms.

1 Unto the [aangel](#note1a) of the church of Ephesus [bwrite](#note1b); These things saith he that holdeth the seven stars in his right hand, who walketh in the midst of the seven golden candlesticks;

2 I [aknow](#note2a) thy works, and thy labour, and thy patience, and how thou canst not bear them which are evil: and thou hast tried them which say they are [bapostles](#note2b), and are not, and hast found them liars:

3 And hast borne, and hast patience, and for my name’s sake hast laboured, and hast not fainted.

4 Nevertheless I have somewhat against thee, because thou hast left thy first [alove](#note4a).

5 Remember therefore from whence thou art fallen, and repent, and do the first works; or else I will come unto thee quickly, and will remove thy [acandlestick](#note5a) out of his place, except thou [brepent](#note5b).

6 But this thou hast, that thou hatest the deeds of the [aNicolaitans](#note6a), which I also hate.

7 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the [aSpirit](#note7a) saith unto the churches; To him that [bovercometh](#note7b) will I give to eat of the [ctree of life](#note7c), which is in the midst of the [dparadise](#note7d) of God.

8 And unto the angel of the church in Smyrna write; These things saith the first and the last, which was dead, and is alive;

9 I know thy works, and tribulation, and poverty, (but thou art rich) and I know the [ablasphemy](#note9a) of them which say they are Jews, and are not, but are the synagogue of Satan.

10 Fear none of those things which thou shalt [asuffer](#note10a): behold, the devil shall cast some of you into prison, that ye may be [btried](#note10b); and ye shall have tribulation ten days: be thou faithful unto [cdeath](#note10c), and I will [dgive](#note10d) thee a [ecrown](#note10e) of life.

11 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; He that overcometh shall not be hurt of the second [adeath](#note11a).

12 And to the angel of the church in Pergamos write; These things saith he which hath the sharp [asword](#note12a) with two edges;

13 I know thy works, and where thou dwellest, even where Satan’s seat is: and thou holdest fast my name, and hast not denied my faith, even in those days wherein Antipas was my faithful [amartyr](#note13a), who was slain among you, where Satan dwelleth.

14 But I have a few things against thee, because thou hast there them that hold the doctrine of [aBalaam](#note14a), who taught Balac to cast a stumblingblock before the children of Israel, to eat things sacrificed unto idols, and to commit [bfornication](#note14b).

15 So hast thou also them that hold the doctrine of the Nicolaitans, which thing I hate.

16 Repent; or else I will come unto thee quickly, and will fight against them with the sword of my mouth.

17 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; To him that [aovercometh](#note17a) will I give to eat of the hidden [bmanna](#note17b), and will give him a [cwhite](#note17c) [dstone](#note17d), and in the stone a new [ename](#note17e) written, which no man knoweth saving he that receiveth it.

18 And unto the [aangel](#note18a) of the church in [bThyatira](#note18b) write; These things saith the Son of God, who hath his [ceyes](#note18c) like unto a flame of fire, and his feet are like fine brass;

19 I know thy works, and charity, and [aservice](#note19a), and faith, and thy patience, and thy works; and the last to be more than the first.

20 Notwithstanding I have a few things against thee, because thou sufferest that woman Jezebel, which calleth herself a prophetess, to teach and to seduce my servants to commit fornication, and to eat things sacrificed unto idols.

21 And I gave her space to repent of her fornication; and she repented not.

22 Behold, I will cast her [ainto](#note22a) a bed, and them that commit [badultery](#note22b) with her into great tribulation, except they repent of their deeds.

23 And I will kill her children with death; and all the churches shall know that I am he which [asearcheth](#note23a) the [breins and hearts](#note23b): and I will give unto every one of you according to your works.

24 But unto you I say, and unto the rest in Thyatira, as many as have not this doctrine, and which have not known the depths of Satan, as they speak; I will put upon you none other burden.

25 But that which ye have already [ahold](#note25a) fast till I come.

26 [aAnd](#note26a) he that [bovercometh](#note26b), and keepeth my works unto the end, to him will I give [cpower](#note26c) over the nations:

27 And he shall rule them with a [arod of iron](#note27a); as the [bvessels](#note27b) of a potter shall they be broken to shivers: even as I received of my Father.

28 And I will give him the morning [astar](#note28a).

29 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches.

Chapter 3

He who overcomes will retain his name in the book of life, reach godhood, and be with Jesus as He is with the Father.

1 [aAnd](#note1a) unto the [bangel](#note1b) of the church in Sardis write; These things saith he that hath the seven [cSpirits](#note1c) of God, and the seven [dstars](#note1d); I [eknow](#note1e) thy works, that thou hast a name that thou livest, and art [fdead](#note1f).

2 [aBe](#note2a) watchful, and strengthen the things which remain, that are ready to die: for I have not found thy works perfect before God.

3 Remember therefore how thou hast received and heard, and hold fast, and repent. If therefore thou shalt not [awatch](#note3a), I will come on thee as a [bthief](#note3b), and thou shalt not know what hour I will come upon thee.

4 Thou hast a few names even in Sardis which have not [adefiled](#note4a) their garments; and they shall [bwalk](#note4b) with me in [cwhite](#note4c): for they are [dworthy](#note4d).

5 He that [aovercometh](#note5a), the same shall be clothed in [bwhite](#note5b) [craiment](#note5c); and I will not [dblot out](#note5d) his name out of the [ebook of life](#note5e), but I will [fconfess](#note5f) his name before my Father, and before his angels.

6 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches.

7 And to the angel of the church in Philadelphia write; These things saith he that is holy, he that is true, he that hath the [akey](#note7a) of David, he that openeth, and no man shutteth; and shutteth, and no man openeth;

8 I know thy works: behold, I have set before thee an open door, and no man can shut it: for thou hast a little strength, and hast kept my word, and hast not denied my name.

9 Behold, I will make them of the synagogue of Satan, which say they are Jews, and are not, but do lie; behold, I will make them to come and [aworship](#note9a) before thy feet, and to know that I have loved thee.

10 Because thou hast kept the word of my patience, I also will [akeep](#note10a) thee from the hour of [btemptation](#note10b), which shall come upon all the world, to try them that dwell upon the earth.

11 Behold, I come [aquickly](#note11a): hold that fast which thou hast, that no man take thy crown.

12 Him that overcometh will I make a pillar in the temple of my God, and he shall go no more out: and I will write upon him the [aname](#note12a) of my God, and the name of the city of my God, which is [bnew Jerusalem](#note12b), which cometh down out of heaven from my God: and I will write upon him my new name.

13 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches.

14 And unto the angel of the church of the Laodiceans write; These things saith the Amen, the [afaithful](#note14a) and true witness, the [bbeginning](#note14b) of the [ccreation](#note14c) of God;

15 I know thy [aworks](#note15a), that thou art neither cold nor hot: I would thou wert cold or hot.

16 So then because thou art [alukewarm](#note16a), and neither cold nor hot, I will [bspue](#note16b) thee out of my mouth.

17 Because thou sayest, I am [arich](#note17a), and increased with goods, and have need of nothing; and knowest not that thou art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked:

18 I [acounsel](#note18a) thee to buy of me [bgold](#note18b) tried in the fire, that thou mayest be rich; and white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed, and that the shame of thy nakedness do not appear; and anoint thine eyes with eyesalve, that thou mayest see.

19 As many as I love, I rebuke and [achasten](#note19a): be zealous therefore, and repent.

20 Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and [aopen](#note20a) the door, I will [bcome](#note20b) in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me.

21 To him that [aovercometh](#note21a) will I grant to [bsit](#note21b) with me in my [cthrone](#note21c), even as I also overcame, and am set down with my Father in his throne.

22 He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches.

Chapter 5

1 And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book [awritten](#note1a) within and on the backside, [bsealed](#note1b) with seven [cseals](#note1c).

3 And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon.

4 And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to open and to read the book, neither to look thereon.

5 And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the [aLion](#note5a) of the tribe of [bJuda](#note5b), the [cRoot](#note5c) of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof.

7 And he came and took the book out of the right hand of him that sat upon the throne.

8 And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of [aodours](#note8a), which are the [bprayers](#note8b) of saints.

9 And they [asung](#note9a) a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast [bredeemed](#note9b) us to God by thy blood out of every [ckindred](#note9c), and tongue, and people, and nation;

10 And hast made us unto our God kings and [apriests](#note10a): and we shall reign on the earth.

11 And I beheld, and I heard the voice of many angels round about the throne and the beasts and the elders: and the number of them was ten thousand times ten thousand, and [athousands](#note11a) of thousands;

12 Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the [aLamb](#note12a) that was slain to receive [bpower](#note12b), and riches, and [cwisdom](#note12c), and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing.

13 And every [acreature](#note13a) which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and [bpower](#note13b), be unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever.

14 And the four beasts said, Amen. And the four and twenty elders fell down and worshipped him that liveth for ever and ever.

Chapter 6

1 [aAnd](#note1a) I saw when the Lamb opened one of the [bseals](#note1b), and I heard, as it were the noise of thunder, one of the four [cbeasts](#note1c) saying, Come and see.

2 And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer.

3 And when he had opened the [asecond](#note3a) seal, I heard the second beast say, Come and see.

4 And there went out another horse that was red: and power was given to him that sat thereon to take [apeace](#note4a) from the earth, and that they should kill one another: and there was given unto him a great sword.

5 And when he had opened the third seal, I heard the third beast say, Come and see. And I beheld, and lo a black horse; and he that sat on him had a pair of balances in his hand.

6 And I heard a voice in the midst of the four beasts say, A measure of wheat for a penny, and three measures of barley for a penny; and see thou hurt not the oil and the wine.

7 And when he had opened the fourth seal, I heard the voice of the fourth beast say, Come and see.

8 And I looked, and behold a pale horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell followed with him. And power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with [asword](#note8a), and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth.

9 And when he had opened the fifth seal, I saw under the [aaltar](#note9a) the souls of them that were [bslain](#note9b) for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held:

10 And they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and [aavenge](#note10a) our blood on them that dwell on the earth?

11 And [awhite](#note11a) [brobes](#note11b) were given unto every one of them; and it was said unto them, that they should [crest](#note11c) yet for a little season, until their fellowservants also and their brethren, that should be killed as they were, should be fulfilled.

12 And I beheld when he had opened the [asixth seal](#note12a), and, lo, there was a great [bearthquake](#note12b); and the [csun](#note12c) became [dblack](#note12d) as sackcloth of hair, and the moon became as [eblood](#note12e);

13 And the stars of heaven fell unto the earth, even as a fig tree casteth her [auntimely figs](#note13a), when she is shaken of a mighty wind.

14 [aAnd](#note14a) the heaven departed as a [bscroll](#note14b) when it is rolled together; and every [cmountain](#note14c) and island were moved out of their places.

15 And the [akings](#note15a) of the earth, and the great men, and the rich men, and the chief captains, and the mighty men, and every bondman, and every free man, hid themselves in the [bdens](#note15b) and in the rocks of the mountains;

16 And said to the [amountains](#note16a) and rocks, Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne, and from the wrath of the Lamb:

Chapter 8

John sees fire and desolation poured out during the seventh seal and preceding the Second Coming.

1 And when he had [aopened](#note1a) the [bseventh seal](#note1b), there was [csilence](#note1c) in heaven about the space of half an hour.

2 And I saw the seven angels which stood before God; and to them were given seven [atrumpets](#note2a).

3 And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much [aincense](#note3a), that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne.

4 And the smoke of the incense, which came with the [aprayers](#note4a) of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel’s hand.

5 And the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and [acast](#note5a) it into the earth: and there were [bvoices](#note5b), and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake.

6 And the seven angels which had the seven [atrumpets](#note6a) prepared themselves to sound.

7 The first angel sounded, and there followed hail and [afire](#note7a) mingled with blood, and they were cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up.

8 And the second angel sounded, and as it were a great mountain burning with fire was cast into the sea: and the third part of the sea became [ablood](#note8a);

9 And the third part of the creatures which were in the sea, and had life, died; and the third part of the ships were destroyed.

10 And the third angel sounded, and there [afell](#note10a) a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters;

11 And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter.

12 And the fourth angel sounded, and the third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it, and the night likewise.

13 And I beheld, and heard an [aangel](#note13a) flying through the midst of heaven, saying with a loud voice, [bWoe](#note13b), woe, woe, to the inhabiters of the earth by reason of the other voices of the trumpet of the three angels, which are yet to sound!

Chapter 9

John also sees the wars and plagues poured out during the seventh seal and before the Lord comes.

1 And the fifth angel sounded, and I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: [aand](#note1a) to him was given the key of the [bbottomless](#note1b) [cpit](#note1c).

2 And he opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke of the pit.

3 And there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth: and unto them was given power, as the scorpions of the earth have power.

4 And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those [amen](#note4a) which have not the [bseal](#note4b) of God in their foreheads.

5 And to them it was given that they should not kill them, but that they should be tormented five months: and their torment was as the torment of a scorpion, when he striketh a man.

6 And in those days shall men seek [adeath](#note6a), and shall not find it; and shall desire to die, and death shall flee from them.

7 And the shapes of the locusts were like unto horses prepared unto battle; and on their heads were as it were crowns like gold, and their faces were as the faces of men.

8 And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as the [ateeth](#note8a) of lions.

9 And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings was as the [asound](#note9a) of chariots of many horses running to battle.

10 And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails: and their power was to hurt men five months.

11 And they had a king over them, which is the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath his name Apollyon.

12 One woe is past; and, behold, there come two woes more hereafter.

13 And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God,

14 Saying to the sixth angel which had the trumpet, Loose the four angels which are bound [ain](#note14a) the great river Euphrates.

15 And the four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men.

16 And the [anumber](#note16a) of the army of the horsemen were two hundred thousand thousand: and I heard the number of them.

17 And thus I saw the horses in the vision, and them that sat on them, having breastplates of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone: and the heads of the horses were as the heads of lions; and out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone.

18 By these three was the third part of men killed, by the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone, which issued out of their mouths.

19 For their power is in their mouth, and in their tails: for their tails were like unto serpents, and had heads, and with them they do hurt.

20 And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk:

21 Neither repented they of their [amurders](#note21a), nor of their [bsorceries](#note21b), nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts.

2 For true and righteous are his [ajudgments](#note2a): for he hath [bjudged](#note2b) the great [cwhore](#note2c), which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and hath avenged the blood of his servants at her hand.

3 And again they said, Alleluia. And her [asmoke](#note3a) rose up for ever and ever.

4 And the four and twenty elders and the four [abeasts](#note4a) fell down and worshipped God that sat on the throne, saying, Amen; Alleluia.

5 And a voice came out of the throne, saying, Praise our God, all ye his servants, and ye that fear him, both small and great.

6 And I heard as it were the voice of a great multitude, and as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of mighty thunderings, saying, Alleluia: for the Lord [aGod](#note6a) [bomnipotent](#note6b) reigneth.

7 Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the [amarriage](#note7a) of the [bLamb](#note7b) is come, and his wife hath made herself ready.

8 And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine [alinen](#note8a) is the [brighteousness](#note8b) of saints.

9 And he saith unto me, Write, Blessed are they which are called unto the [amarriage](#note9a) [bsupper](#note9b) of the [cLamb](#note9c). And he saith unto me, These are the true sayings of God.

10 And I [afell](#note10a) at his feet to [bworship](#note10b) him. And he said unto me, See thou do it not: I am thy [cfellowservant](#note10c), and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus: worship God: for the [dtestimony](#note10d) of Jesus is the spirit of [eprophecy](#note10e).

11 And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth [ajudge](#note11a) and make [bwar](#note11b).

12 His [aeyes](#note12a) were as a flame of fire, and on his head were many crowns; and he had a [bname](#note12b) written, that no man knew, but he himself.

13 And he was [aclothed](#note13a) with a vesture dipped in blood: and his [bname](#note13b) is called The [cWord of God](#note13c).

14 And the armies which were in heaven followed him upon white horses, [aclothed](#note14a) in fine linen, white and clean.

15 [aAnd](#note15a) out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall [brule](#note15b) them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the [cwinepress](#note15c) of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God.

16 And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, [aKing of Kings](#note16a), and [bLord](#note16b) of Lords.

17 And I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the fowls that fly in the midst of heaven, Come and gather yourselves together unto the supper of the great God;

18 That ye may eat the flesh of kings, and the flesh of captains, and the flesh of mighty men, and the flesh of horses, and of them that sit on them, and the flesh of all [amen](#note18a), both free and bond, both small and great.

19 And I saw the beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together to make war against him that sat on the horse, and against his army.

20 And the beast was taken, and with him the [afalse prophet](#note20a) that wrought [bmiracles](#note20b) before him, with which he [cdeceived](#note20c) them that had received the mark of the beast, and them that worshipped his image. These both were cast alive into a [dlake](#note20d) of [efire](#note20e) burning with brimstone.

21 And the remnant were slain [awith](#note21a) the sword of him that sat upon the horse, which sword proceeded out of his mouth: and all the fowls were filled with their flesh.

Chapter 20

Satan is bound during the Millennium—The Saints will then live and reign with Christ—The dead stand before God and are judged out of the books according to their works.

1 And I saw an [aangel](#note1a) come down from heaven, having the key of the bottomless [bpit](#note1b) and a great chain in his hand.

2 And he laid hold on the dragon, that old [aserpent](#note2a), which is the [bDevil](#note2b), and Satan, and [cbound](#note2c) him a thousand years,

3 And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the [anations](#note3a) no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled: and after that he must be [bloosed](#note3b) a little season.

4 And I saw thrones, and they sat upon them, and [ajudgment](#note4a) was given unto them: and I saw the souls of them that were beheaded for the witness of Jesus, and for the word of God, and which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image, neither had received his mark upon their foreheads, or in their hands; and they lived and [breigned](#note4b) with Christ a [cthousand](#note4c) years.

5 But the rest of the [adead](#note5a) lived not again until the thousand years were finished. This is the first resurrection.

6 [aBlessed](#note6a) and holy is he that hath part in the [bfirst](#note6b) [cresurrection](#note6c): on such the [dsecond death](#note6d) hath no power, but they shall be [epriests](#note6e) of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years.

7 And when the [athousand](#note7a) years are expired, [bSatan](#note7b) shall be loosed out of his prison,

8 And shall go out to deceive the nations which are in the four quarters of the earth, [aGog](#note8a) and Magog, to gather them together to battle: the number of whom is as the sand of the sea.

9 And they went up on the breadth of the earth, and compassed the camp of the saints about, and the beloved city: and fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them.

10 And the [adevil](#note10a) that deceived them was [bcast](#note10b) into the lake of [cfire](#note10c) and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet are, and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever.

11 And I saw a great white throne, and him that sat on it, from whose face the [aearth](#note11a) and the heaven fled away; and there was found no [bplace](#note11b) for them.

12 And I saw the [adead](#note12a), small and great, [bstand](#note12b) before God; and the [cbooks](#note12c) were opened: and another book was opened, which is the [dbook of life](#note12d): and the dead were [ejudged](#note12e) out of those things which were written in the books, according to their [fworks](#note12f).

13 And the sea gave up the dead which were in it; and [adeath](#note13a) and [bhell](#note13b) delivered up the dead which were in them: and they were judged every man according to their works.

14 And [adeath](#note14a) and [bhell](#note14b) were cast into the lake of fire. This is the [csecond death](#note14c).

15 And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire.

Chapter 21

Those who overcome will be sons of God—The earth attains its celestial glory.

1 And I saw a [anew](#note1a) [bheaven](#note1b) and a new [cearth](#note1c): for the first heaven and the first [dearth](#note1d) were passed away; and there was no more sea.

2 And I John saw the holy city, [anew](#note2a) [bJerusalem](#note2b), coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a [cbride](#note2c) adorned for her husband.

3 And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will [adwell](#note3a) with them, and they shall be his people, and [bGod](#note3b) himself shall be with them, and be their God.

4 And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more [adeath](#note4a), neither [bsorrow](#note4b), nor crying, neither shall there be any more [cpain](#note4c): for the former things are passed away.

5 And he that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things [anew](#note5a). And he said unto me, [bWrite](#note5b): for these [cwords](#note5c) are true and faithful.

6 And he said unto me, It is done. [aI am](#note6a) [bAlpha and Omega](#note6b), the beginning and the end. I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the [cwater of life](#note6c) freely.

7 He that [aovercometh](#note7a) shall [binherit](#note7b) [call](#note7c) things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my [dson](#note7d).

8 But the fearful, and [aunbelieving](#note8a), and the abominable, and [bmurderers](#note8b), and [cwhoremongers](#note8c), and [dsorcerers](#note8d), and idolaters, and all [eliars](#note8e), shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the [fsecond](#note8f) [gdeath](#note8g).

9 And there came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last [aplagues](#note9a), and talked with me, saying, Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb’s wife.

10 And he carried me away in the spirit to a great and high [amountain](#note10a), and shewed me that great city, the holy [bJerusalem](#note10b), descending out of heaven from God,

11 Having the [aglory](#note11a) of God: and her light was like unto a stone most precious, even like a jasper stone, clear as crystal;

12 And had a wall great and high, and had twelve [agates](#note12a), and at the gates twelve angels, and names written thereon, which are the names of the twelve tribes of the children of Israel:

13 On the east three gates; on the north three gates; on the south three gates; and on the west three gates.

14 And the wall of the city had twelve foundations, and in them the names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

15 And he that talked with me had a golden reed to measure the city, and the gates thereof, and the wall thereof.

16 And the city lieth foursquare, and the [alength](#note16a) is as large as the breadth: and he measured the city with the reed, twelve thousand [bfurlongs](#note16b). The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal.

17 And he measured the wall thereof, an hundred and forty and four cubits, according to the measure of a man, that is, of the angel.

18 And the building of the wall of it was of jasper: and the city was pure gold, like unto clear glass.

19 And the foundations of the wall of the city were garnished with all manner of precious stones. The first foundation was jasper; the second, sapphire; the third, a chalcedony; the fourth, an emerald;

20 The fifth, sardonyx; the sixth, sardius; the seventh, chrysolite; the eighth, beryl; the ninth, a topaz; the tenth, a chrysoprasus; the eleventh, a jacinth; the twelfth, an amethyst.

21 And the twelve gates were twelve pearls; every several gate was of one pearl: and the street of the city was pure [agold](#note21a), as it were transparent [bglass](#note21b).

22 And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God [aAlmighty](#note22a) and the Lamb are the [btemple](#note22b) of it.

23 And the city had no need of the [asun](#note23a), neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the [bLamb](#note23b) is the [clight](#note23c) thereof.

24 And the nations of them which are saved shall [awalk](#note24a) in the light of it: and the [bkings](#note24b) of the [cearth](#note24c) do bring their glory and honour into it.

25 And the [agates](#note25a) of it shall not be shut at all by day: for there shall be no night there.

26 And they shall bring the glory and honour of the nations into it.

27 And there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that [adefileth](#note27a), neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb’s [bbook of life](#note27b).

Chapter 22

The Saints will reign in celestial splendor—Christ will come, and men will be judged—Blessed are they who keep His commandments.

1 And he shewed me a pure river of [awater of life](#note1a), clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb.

2 In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the [atree](#note2a) of [blife](#note2b), which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the [cleaves](#note2c) of the tree were for the healing of the nations.

3 And there shall be no more [acurse](#note3a): but the [bthrone](#note3b) of God and of the [cLamb](#note3c) shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him:

4 And they shall [asee](#note4a) his [bface](#note4b); and his [cname](#note4c) shall be in their foreheads.

5 And there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the [asun](#note5a); for the Lord God giveth them [blight](#note5b): and they shall [creign](#note5c) [dfor ever](#note5d) and ever.
