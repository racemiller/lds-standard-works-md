Chapter 1

The trial of our faith precedes salvation—Christ was foreordained to be the Redeemer.

1 Peter, an apostle of Jesus Christ, to the [astrangers](#note1a) scattered throughout Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia,

2 [aElect](#note2a) according to the [bforeknowledge](#note2b) of God the Father, through [csanctification](#note2c) of the Spirit, unto [dobedience](#note2d) and [esprinkling](#note2e) of the [fblood](#note2f) of Jesus Christ: Grace unto you, and peace, be multiplied.

3 Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, which according to his abundant [amercy](#note3a) hath [bbegotten](#note3b) us again unto a [clively](#note3c) [dhope](#note3d) by the resurrection of [eJesus Christ](#note3e) from the dead,

4 To an [ainheritance](#note4a) incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away, reserved in heaven for you,

5 Who are kept by the [apower](#note5a) of God through [bfaith](#note5b) unto salvation [cready](#note5c) to be revealed in the last time.

6 Wherein ye greatly rejoice, though now for a [aseason](#note6a), if need be, ye are in heaviness through manifold [btemptations](#note6b):

7 That the [atrial](#note7a) of your faith, being much more precious than of gold that perisheth, though it be tried with fire, might be found unto praise and honour and glory at the [bappearing](#note7b) of Jesus Christ:

8 Whom having not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see him not, yet [abelieving](#note8a), ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory:

9 Receiving the [aend](#note9a) of your [bfaith](#note9b), even the [csalvation](#note9c) of your souls.

10 Of which [asalvation](#note10a) the [bprophets](#note10b) have inquired and searched diligently, who prophesied of the grace that should come unto you:

11 Searching what, or what manner of time the Spirit of Christ which was in them did signify, when it [atestified](#note11a) beforehand the [bsufferings](#note11b) of Christ, and the [cglory](#note11c) that should follow.

12 Unto whom it was revealed, that not unto themselves, but unto us they did minister the things, which are now reported unto you by them that have [apreached](#note12a) the gospel unto you with the Holy Ghost sent down from heaven; which things the [bangels](#note12b) desire to look into.

13 Wherefore gird up the loins of your mind, be [asober](#note13a), and hope [bto the end](#note13b) for the [cgrace](#note13c) that is to be brought unto you at the revelation of Jesus Christ;

14 As obedient children, not fashioning yourselves according to the former [alusts](#note14a) in your [bignorance](#note14b):

15 But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye [aholy](#note15a) in all manner of [bconversation](#note15b);

16 Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am [aholy](#note16a).

17 And if ye call on the Father, who without respect of persons judgeth according to every man’s [awork](#note17a), pass the time of your sojourning here in [bfear](#note17b):

18 Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not [aredeemed](#note18a) with corruptible things, as silver and gold, from your [bvain conversation](#note18b) received by [ctradition](#note18c) from your fathers;

19 But with the precious [ablood](#note19a) of Christ, as of a [blamb](#note19b) without [cblemish](#note19c) and without spot:

20 [aWho](#note20a) verily was [bforeordained](#note20b) before the foundation of the world, but was manifest in these last times for you,

21 Who by him do believe in God, that raised him up from the dead, and gave him [aglory](#note21a); that your [bfaith](#note21b) and [chope](#note21c) might be in God.

22 Seeing ye have [apurified](#note22a) your [bsouls](#note22b) in obeying the truth through the Spirit unto unfeigned [clove](#note22c) of the brethren, see that ye [dlove](#note22d) one another with a pure heart fervently:

23 Being [aborn again](#note23a), not of corruptible seed, but of incorruptible, by the word of God, which liveth and [babideth](#note23b) for ever.

24 For all [aflesh](#note24a) is as grass, and all the glory of man as the flower of grass. The grass withereth, and the flower thereof falleth away:

25 But the [aword](#note25a) of the Lord endureth for ever. And this is the word which by the gospel is preached unto you.

Chapter 2

Converts are newborn babes in Christ—He is the chief cornerstone—Saints hold a royal priesthood and are a peculiar people—Saints are in subjection to the laws of man.

1 Wherefore laying aside all [amalice](#note1a), and all [bguile](#note1b), and hypocrisies, and envies, and all evil [cspeakings](#note1c),

2 As [anewborn](#note2a) babes, desire the [bsincere](#note2b) [cmilk](#note2c) of the word, that ye may grow thereby:

3 If so be ye have [atasted](#note3a) that the Lord is gracious.

4 To whom coming, as unto a living stone, disallowed indeed of men, but [achosen](#note4a) of God, and precious,

5 Ye also, as [alively](#note5a) stones, are built up a [bspiritual](#note5b) [chouse](#note5c), an holy priesthood, to offer up [dspiritual](#note5d) [esacrifices](#note5e), acceptable to God by Jesus Christ.

6 Wherefore also it is contained in the scripture, Behold, I lay in Sion a chief [acorner](#note6a) [bstone](#note6b), elect, precious: and he that [cbelieveth](#note6c) on him shall not be [dconfounded](#note6d).

7 Unto you therefore which believe he is precious: but unto them which be [adisobedient](#note7a), the stone which the builders [bdisallowed](#note7b), the same is made the head of the corner,

8 And a [astone](#note8a) of [bstumbling](#note8b), and a [crock](#note8c) of [doffence](#note8d), even to them which [estumble](#note8e) at the word, being disobedient: whereunto also they were [fappointed](#note8f).

9 But ye are a [achosen](#note9a) generation, a [broyal](#note9b) [cpriesthood](#note9c), an [dholy](#note9d) [enation](#note9e), a [fpeculiar](#note9f) people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of [gdarkness](#note9g) into his marvellous [hlight](#note9h):

10 Which in time past were not a people, but are now the people of God: which had not obtained mercy, but now have obtained mercy.

11 Dearly beloved, I beseech you as [astrangers](#note11a) and [bpilgrims](#note11b), [cabstain](#note11c) from fleshly [dlusts](#note11d), which [ewar](#note11e) against the soul;

12 Having your conversation [ahonest](#note12a) among the Gentiles: that, whereas they speak against you as evildoers, they may by your good [bworks](#note12b), which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation.

13 [aSubmit](#note13a) yourselves to every [bordinance](#note13b) of man for the Lord’s sake: whether it be to the king, as [csupreme](#note13c);

14 Or unto governors, as unto them that are sent by him for the punishment of evildoers, and for the praise of them that do well.

15 For so is the will of God, that with well doing ye may put to [asilence](#note15a) the [bignorance](#note15b) of foolish men:

16 As free, and not using your [aliberty](#note16a) for a cloak of [bmaliciousness](#note16b), but as the [cservants](#note16c) of God.

17 [aHonour](#note17a) all men. Love the [bbrotherhood](#note17b). [cFear](#note17c) God. Honour the [dking](#note17d).

18 [aServants](#note18a), be subject to your [bmasters](#note18b) with all fear; not only to the good and gentle, but also to the [cfroward](#note18c).

19 For this is [athankworthy](#note19a), if a man for conscience toward God endure [bgrief](#note19b), suffering wrongfully.

20 For what glory is it, if, when ye be buffeted for your faults, ye shall take it [apatiently](#note20a)? but if, when ye do well, and [bsuffer](#note20b) for it, ye take it [cpatiently](#note20c), this is [dacceptable](#note20d) with God.

21 For even hereunto were ye called: because Christ also [asuffered](#note21a) for us, leaving us an [bexample](#note21b), that ye should follow his steps:

22 Who did no [asin](#note22a), neither was [bguile](#note22b) found in his mouth:

23 Who, when he was [areviled](#note23a), reviled not again; when he suffered, he threatened not; but [bcommitted](#note23b) himself to him that judgeth righteously:

24 Who his own self [abare](#note24a) our [bsins](#note24b) in his own body on the tree, that we, being dead to sins, should live unto righteousness: by whose [cstripes](#note24c) ye were healed.

25 For ye were as [asheep](#note25a) going astray; but are now returned unto the [bShepherd](#note25b) and [cBishop](#note25c) of your souls.

Chapter 3

Husbands and wives should honor each other—Saints should live by gospel standards—Christ preached to the spirits in prison.

1 Likewise, ye [awives](#note1a), be in subjection to your own husbands; that, if any [bobey not](#note1b) the word, they also may without the word be won by the [cconversation](#note1c) of the wives;

2 While they behold your [achaste](#note2a) [bconversation](#note2b) coupled with fear.

3 Whose adorning let it not be that outward [aadorning](#note3a) of plaiting the hair, and of wearing of gold, or of putting on of apparel;

4 But let it be the hidden man of the heart, in that which is not corruptible, even the [aornament](#note4a) of a [bmeek](#note4b) and quiet spirit, which is in the sight of God of great price.

5 For after this manner in the old time the holy [awomen](#note5a) also, who [btrusted](#note5b) in God, adorned themselves, being [cin subjection](#note5c) unto their own husbands:

6 Even as Sara obeyed Abraham, calling him lord: whose [adaughters](#note6a) ye are, as long as ye do well, and are not afraid with any [bamazement](#note6b).

7 Likewise, ye [ahusbands](#note7a), dwell with them according to [bknowledge](#note7b), giving [chonour](#note7c) unto the [dwife](#note7d), as unto the weaker vessel, and as being [eheirs](#note7e) together of the grace of life; that your [fprayers](#note7f) be not hindered.

8 Finally, be ye all of [aone](#note8a) mind, having [bcompassion](#note8b) one of another, love as [cbrethren](#note8c), be [dpitiful](#note8d), be [ecourteous](#note8e):

9 Not [arendering](#note9a) [bevil](#note9b) for evil, or railing for railing: but contrariwise blessing; knowing that ye are thereunto called, that ye should inherit a [cblessing](#note9c).

10 For he that will love life, and see good days, let him [arefrain](#note10a) his [btongue](#note10b) from evil, and his lips that they speak no [cguile](#note10c):

Chapter 4

Peter explains why the gospel is preached to the dead—Saints should speak as the oracles of God—The righteous will be tried and tested in all things.

1 Forasmuch then as Christ hath suffered for us in the flesh, arm yourselves likewise with the same [amind](#note1a): [bfor](#note1b) he that hath suffered in the flesh hath ceased from [csin](#note1c);

2 That he no longer should live the rest of his time in the flesh to the [alusts](#note2a) of men, but to the will of God.

3 For the time past of our life may suffice us to have wrought the will of the Gentiles, when we walked in lasciviousness, lusts, [aexcess](#note3a) of wine, [brevellings](#note3b), banquetings, and [cabominable](#note3c) [didolatries](#note3d):

4 Wherein they think it strange that ye run not with them to the same excess of riot, speaking evil of you:

5 Who shall give account to him that is ready to [ajudge](#note5a) the [bquick](#note5b) and the dead.

6 [aFor](#note6a) for this cause was the [bgospel](#note6b) [cpreached](#note6c) also to them that are [ddead](#note6d), that they might be [ejudged](#note6e) according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit.

7 [aBut](#note7a) the end of all things is at hand: be ye therefore sober, and [bwatch](#note7b) unto prayer.

8 And above all things have fervent charity among yourselves: [afor](#note8a) [bcharity](#note8b) shall cover the multitude of sins.

9 Use [ahospitality](#note9a) one to another without [bgrudging](#note9b).

10 As every man hath received the gift, even so [aminister](#note10a) the same one to another, as good [bstewards](#note10b) of the manifold grace of God.

11 If any man [aspeak](#note11a), let him speak as the [boracles](#note11b) of God; if any man minister, let him do it as of the ability which God giveth: that God in all things may be glorified through Jesus Christ, to whom be praise and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

12 Beloved, think it not strange concerning the fiery [atrial](#note12a) which is to try you, as though some strange thing happened unto you:

13 But [arejoice](#note13a), inasmuch as ye are [bpartakers](#note13b) of Christ’s [csufferings](#note13c); that, when his glory shall be [drevealed](#note13d), ye may be glad also with exceeding joy.

14 If ye be [areproached](#note14a) for the name of Christ, [bhappy](#note14b) are ye; for the spirit of glory and of God resteth upon you: on their part he is evil spoken of, but on your part he is glorified.

15 But let none of you suffer as a murderer, or as a thief, or as an evildoer, or as a busybody in other men’s matters.

16 Yet if any man suffer as a [aChristian](#note16a), let him not be ashamed; but let him glorify God on this behalf.

17 For the time is come that [ajudgment](#note17a) must begin at the house of God: and if it first begin at us, what shall the end be of them that [bobey not](#note17b) the gospel of God?

18 And if the [arighteous](#note18a) scarcely be saved, where shall the [bungodly](#note18b) and the sinner appear?

19 Wherefore let them that suffer according to the [awill](#note19a) of God commit the [bkeeping](#note19b) of their souls to him in well doing, as unto a faithful Creator.

Chapter 5

The elders are to feed the flock of God—Humility and godly graces lead to perfection.

1 The [aelders](#note1a) which are among you I exhort, who am also an elder, and a witness of the sufferings of Christ, and also a [bpartaker](#note1b) of the glory that shall be revealed:

2 [aFeed](#note2a) the [bflock](#note2b) of God which is among you, taking the [coversight](#note2c) thereof, not by constraint, but [dwillingly](#note2d); not for [efilthy](#note2e) [flucre](#note2f), but of a ready mind;

3 Neither as being [alords](#note3a) over God’s heritage, but being [bensamples](#note3b) to the flock.

4 And when the chief [aShepherd](#note4a) shall appear, ye shall receive a [bcrown](#note4b) of [cglory](#note4c) that fadeth not away.

5 Likewise, ye younger, [asubmit](#note5a) yourselves unto the elder. Yea, all of you be subject one to another, and be clothed with [bhumility](#note5b): for God [cresisteth](#note5c) the [dproud](#note5d), and giveth grace to the [ehumble](#note5e).

6 [aHumble](#note6a) yourselves therefore under the mighty hand of God, that he may exalt you in due time:

7 Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you.

8 Be [asober](#note8a), be [bvigilant](#note8b); because your adversary the [cdevil](#note8c), as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour:

9 Whom resist [asteadfast](#note9a) in the faith, knowing that the same afflictions are [baccomplished in](#note9b) your brethren that are in the world.

10 But the God of all [agrace](#note10a), who hath called us unto his eternal glory by Christ Jesus, after that ye have suffered a while, make you [bperfect](#note10b), stablish, strengthen, settle you.

11 To him be glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

12 By [aSilvanus](#note12a), a faithful brother unto you, as I suppose, I have written briefly, exhorting, and testifying that this is the true grace of God wherein ye stand.

13 The church that is at [aBabylon](#note13a), elected together with you, saluteth you; and so doth Marcus my son.

14 Greet ye one another with a kiss of charity. Peace be with you all that are in Christ Jesus. Amen.
