Chapter 1

Peter urges the Saints to make their calling and election sure—Prophecy comes by the power of the Holy Ghost.

1 Simon Peter, a [aservant](#note1a) and an [bapostle](#note1b) of Jesus Christ, to them that have obtained [clike precious](#note1c) faith with us through the [drighteousness](#note1d) [eof](#note1e) God and our Saviour Jesus Christ:

2 Grace and peace be multiplied unto you through the [aknowledge](#note2a) of God, and of Jesus our Lord,

3 According as his divine power hath given unto us [aall](#note3a) things that pertain unto [blife](#note3b) and [cgodliness](#note3c), through the knowledge of him that hath called us [dto](#note3d) glory and [evirtue](#note3e):

4 Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious [apromises](#note4a): that by these ye might be [bpartakers](#note4b) of the [cdivine](#note4c) [dnature](#note4d), having [eescaped](#note4e) the [fcorruption](#note4f) that is in the world through [glust](#note4g).

5 And beside this, giving all [adiligence](#note5a), add to your faith [bvirtue](#note5b); and to virtue [cknowledge](#note5c);

6 And to knowledge [atemperance](#note6a); and to temperance [bpatience](#note6b); and to patience [cgodliness](#note6c);

7 And to godliness [abrotherly](#note7a) [bkindness](#note7b); and to brotherly kindness [ccharity](#note7c).

8 For if these things be in you, and [aabound](#note8a), they make you that ye shall neither be [bbarren](#note8b) nor [cunfruitful](#note8c) in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

9 But he that lacketh these things is blind, and cannot see afar off, and hath forgotten that he was purged from his old sins.

10 Wherefore the rather, brethren, give diligence to make your calling and [aelection](#note10a) sure: for if ye do these things, ye shall never [bfall](#note10b):

11 For so an entrance shall be ministered unto you abundantly into the [aeverlasting](#note11a) [bkingdom](#note11b) of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ.

12 Wherefore I will not be negligent to put you always in remembrance of these things, though ye know them, and be established in the present truth.

13 Yea, I think it [ameet](#note13a), as long as I am in this [btabernacle](#note13b), to stir you up by putting you in remembrance;

14 Knowing that shortly I must put off this my tabernacle, even as our Lord Jesus Christ hath [ashewed](#note14a) me.

15 Moreover I will endeavour that ye may be able after my decease to have these things always in remembrance.

16 For we have not followed cunningly devised [afables](#note16a), when we made known unto you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but were [beyewitnesses](#note16b) of his majesty.

17 For he received from God the Father honour and glory, when there came such a voice to him from the excellent glory, This is my [abeloved Son](#note17a), in whom I am well pleased.

18 And this [avoice](#note18a) which came from heaven we heard, when we were with him in the holy [bmount](#note18b).

19 [aWe](#note19a) have also a more [bsure](#note19b) word of [cprophecy](#note19c); whereunto ye do well that ye take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place, until the day dawn, and the day [dstar](#note19d) arise in your hearts:

20 Knowing this first, that [ano](#note20a) [bprophecy](#note20b) of the [cscripture](#note20c) is of any private [dinterpretation](#note20d).

21 For the [aprophecy](#note21a) came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God [bspake](#note21b) as they were [cmoved](#note21c) by the [dHoly Ghost](#note21d).

Chapter 2

False teachers among the Saints are damned—Lustful Saints will perish in their own corruption.

1 But there were [afalse prophets](#note1a) also among the people, even as there shall be [bfalse](#note1b) [cteachers](#note1c) among you, who privily shall bring in damnable heresies, even denying the Lord that [dbought](#note1d) them, and bring upon themselves swift destruction.

2 And many shall follow their pernicious ways; by reason of whom the way of [atruth](#note2a) shall be evil spoken of.

3 And through covetousness shall they with [afeigned](#note3a) words make merchandise of you: whose [bjudgment](#note3b) now of a long time lingereth not, and their [cdamnation](#note3c) slumbereth not.

4 For if God spared not the [aangels](#note4a) that sinned, but cast them down to [bhell](#note4b), and delivered them into chains of darkness, to be reserved unto [cjudgment](#note4c);

5 And spared not the old world, but saved [aNoah](#note5a) the eighth person, a preacher of [brighteousness](#note5b), bringing in the [cflood](#note5c) upon the world of the ungodly;

6 And turning the cities of [aSodom](#note6a) and Gomorrha into ashes condemned them with an overthrow, making them an [bensample](#note6b) unto those that after should live ungodly;

7 And delivered just [aLot](#note7a), [bvexed](#note7b) with the [cfilthy](#note7c) conversation of the wicked:

8 (For that righteous man dwelling among them, in seeing and hearing, [avexed](#note8a) his righteous soul from day to day with their unlawful deeds;)

9 The Lord knoweth how to [adeliver](#note9a) the [bgodly](#note9b) out of [ctemptations](#note9c), and to [dreserve](#note9d) the unjust unto the day of [ejudgment](#note9e) to be punished:

10 But chiefly them that [awalk](#note10a) after the flesh in the [blust](#note10b) of [cuncleanness](#note10c), and despise [dgovernment](#note10d). [ePresumptuous](#note10e) are they, selfwilled, they are not afraid to speak evil of dignities.

11 Whereas angels, which are greater in power and might, bring not railing accusation against them before the Lord.

12 But these, as [anatural](#note12a) brute beasts, made to be taken and destroyed, speak evil of the things that they understand not; and shall utterly [bperish](#note12b) in their own corruption;

13 And shall receive the [areward](#note13a) of unrighteousness, as they that count it [bpleasure](#note13b) to [criot](#note13c) in the day time. Spots they are and blemishes, sporting themselves with their own deceivings while they feast with you;

14 Having eyes full of adultery, and that cannot cease from sin; beguiling unstable souls: an heart they have exercised with covetous practices; cursed children:

15 Which have [aforsaken](#note15a) the right [bway](#note15b), and are gone [castray](#note15c), following the way of [dBalaam](#note15d) the son of Bosor, who loved the [ewages](#note15e) of unrighteousness;

16 But was rebuked for his iniquity: the dumb [aass](#note16a) speaking with man’s voice forbad the madness of the prophet.

17 These are [awells](#note17a) without water, clouds that are carried with a tempest; to whom the [bmist](#note17b) of [cdarkness](#note17c) is reserved for ever.

18 For when they [aspeak](#note18a) great swelling words of [bvanity](#note18b), they [callure](#note18c) through the [dlusts](#note18d) of the flesh, through much wantonness, those that were clean [eescaped](#note18e) from them who live in error.

19 While they promise them [aliberty](#note19a), they themselves are the [bservants](#note19b) of [ccorruption](#note19c): for of whom a man is overcome, of the same is he brought in [dbondage](#note19d).

20 For if after they have [aescaped](#note20a) the [bpollutions](#note20b) of the world through the knowledge of the Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ, they are [cagain](#note20c) [dentangled](#note20d) therein, and [eovercome](#note20e), the latter end is [fworse](#note20f) with them than the beginning.

21 For it had been better for them not to have [aknown](#note21a) the way of [brighteousness](#note21b), than, after they have known it, to [cturn](#note21c) from the holy commandment delivered unto them.

22 But it is happened unto them according to the true proverb, The dog is turned to his own [avomit](#note22a) again; and the sow that was [bwashed](#note22b) to her wallowing in the mire.

Chapter 3

Some in the latter days will doubt the Second Coming—The elements will melt at the coming of the Lord.

1 This second epistle, beloved, I now [awrite](#note1a) unto you; in both which I stir up your pure minds by way of [bremembrance](#note1b):

2 That ye may be mindful of the words which were spoken before by the holy [aprophets](#note2a), and of the commandment of us the apostles of the Lord and Saviour:

3 [aKnowing](#note3a) this first, that there shall come in the [blast days](#note3b) scoffers, walking after their own [clusts](#note3c),

4 And saying, [aWhere](#note4a) is the [bpromise](#note4b) of his [ccoming](#note4c)? for since the fathers fell asleep, all things continue as they were from the beginning of the creation.

5 For this they willingly are [aignorant](#note5a) of, that by the [bword](#note5b) of God the heavens were of old, and the [cearth](#note5c) standing out of the water and in the water:

6 Whereby the world that then was, being [aoverflowed](#note6a) with [bwater](#note6b), perished:

7 But the heavens and the earth, which are now, by the same word are kept in store, reserved unto [afire](#note7a) against the day of judgment and [bperdition](#note7b) of ungodly men.

8 But, beloved, be not ignorant of this one thing, that one day is with the Lord as a thousand [ayears](#note8a), and a thousand years as one day.

9 The Lord is not slack concerning his promise, as some men count slackness; but is [alongsuffering](#note9a) to us-ward, not willing that any should [bperish](#note9b), but that all should come to [crepentance](#note9c).

10 But the [aday](#note10a) of the Lord will [bcome](#note10b) as a thief in the night; in the which the heavens shall [cpass away](#note10c) with a great noise, and the [delements](#note10d) shall melt with fervent heat, the [eearth](#note10e) also and the works that are therein shall be burned up.

11 Seeing then that all these things shall be dissolved, what [amanner](#note11a) of persons ought ye to be in all holy [bconversation](#note11b) and godliness,

12 [aLooking](#note12a) for and [bhasting](#note12b) unto the [ccoming](#note12c) of the [dday](#note12d) of God, wherein the heavens being on fire shall be dissolved, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat?

13 Nevertheless we, according to his promise, look for new [aheavens](#note13a) and a [bnew](#note13b) [cearth](#note13c), wherein dwelleth [drighteousness](#note13d).

14 Wherefore, beloved, seeing that ye look for such things, be [adiligent](#note14a) that ye may be found of him in peace, without [bspot](#note14b), and blameless.

15 And [aaccount](#note15a) that the [blongsuffering](#note15b) of our Lord is salvation; even as our beloved brother Paul also according to the wisdom given unto him hath written unto you;

16 As also in all his epistles, speaking in them of these things; in which are some things hard to be understood, which they that are unlearned and unstable [awrest](#note16a), as they do also the other [bscriptures](#note16b), unto their own destruction.

17 Ye therefore, beloved, [aseeing](#note17a) ye know these things before, beware lest ye also, being [bled](#note17b) [caway](#note17c) with the error of the wicked, fall from your own [dsteadfastness](#note17d).

18 But grow in [agrace](#note18a), and in the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ. To him be glory both now and for ever. Amen.
