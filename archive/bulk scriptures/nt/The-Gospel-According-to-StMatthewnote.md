Chapter 1

Christ is born of Mary—She conceives by the power of the Holy Ghost—Our Lord is named Jesus.

1 The book of the generation of Jesus Christ, the son of [aDavid](#note1a), the son of Abraham.

2 [aAbraham](#note2a) begat Isaac; and Isaac begat Jacob; and Jacob begat [bJudas](#note2b) and his brethren;

3 And Judas begat [aPhares](#note3a) and [bZara](#note3b) of Thamar; and Phares begat [cEsrom](#note3c); and Esrom begat Aram;

4 And Aram begat Aminadab; and Aminadab begat Naasson; and [aNaasson](#note4a) begat Salmon;

5 And Salmon begat [aBooz](#note5a) of Rachab; and Booz begat Obed of Ruth; and Obed begat Jesse;

6 And Jesse begat David the king; and David the king begat [aSolomon](#note6a) of her that had been the wife of Urias;

7 And Solomon begat Roboam; and Roboam begat Abia; and Abia begat Asa;

8 And Asa begat Josaphat; and Josaphat begat Joram; and Joram begat Ozias;

9 And Ozias begat Joatham; and Joatham begat Achaz; and Achaz begat Ezekias;

10 And Ezekias begat Manasses; and Manasses begat Amon; and Amon begat Josias;

11 And Josias begat Jechonias and his brethren, about the time they were carried away to [aBabylon](#note11a):

12 And after they were brought to Babylon, Jechonias begat Salathiel; and Salathiel begat [aZorobabel](#note12a);

13 And Zorobabel begat Abiud; and Abiud begat Eliakim; and Eliakim begat Azor;

14 And Azor begat Sadoc; and Sadoc begat Achim; and Achim begat Eliud;

15 And Eliud begat Eleazar; and Eleazar begat Matthan; and Matthan begat Jacob;

16 And Jacob begat [aJoseph](#note16a) the husband of [bMary](#note16b), of whom was born [cJesus](#note16c), [dwho](#note16d) is called [eChrist](#note16e).

17 So all the generations from Abraham to David are fourteen generations; and from David until the carrying away into Babylon are fourteen generations; and from the carrying away into Babylon unto Christ are fourteen [agenerations](#note17a).

18 ¶ [aNow](#note18a) the [bbirth](#note18b) of Jesus Christ was [con this wise](#note18c): When as his mother Mary was espoused to Joseph, before they came together, she was found with child of the Holy Ghost.

19 Then Joseph her husband, being a just man, and not willing to make her a publick [aexample](#note19a), [bwas](#note19b) minded to [cput](#note19c) her away privily.

20 But while he thought on these things, behold, the angel of the Lord appeared unto him in a [adream](#note20a), saying, Joseph, thou son of [bDavid](#note20b), fear not to take unto thee Mary thy wife: for that which is conceived in her is of the [cHoly Ghost](#note20c).

21 And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his [aname](#note21a) [bJesus](#note21b): for he shall [csave](#note21c) his people from their sins.

22 Now all this was done, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet, saying,

23 [aBehold](#note23a), a [bvirgin](#note23b) shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call his [cname](#note23c) [dEmmanuel](#note23d), which being interpreted is, God with us.

24 Then Joseph being raised from sleep did as the angel of the Lord had bidden him, and took unto him his [awife](#note24a):

25 And knew her not till she had brought forth her [afirstborn](#note25a) son: and he called his name Jesus.

Chapter 2

The wise men are directed by a star to Jesus—Joseph takes the child to Egypt—Herod slays the children in Bethlehem—Jesus is taken to Nazareth to dwell.

1 Now when Jesus was [aborn](#note1a) in Bethlehem of Judæa in the days of Herod the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to Jerusalem,

2 Saying, [aWhere](#note2a) is he that is born [bKing of the Jews](#note2b)? for we have seen his [cstar](#note2c) in the east, and are come to [dworship](#note2d) him.

3 When Herod the king had heard these things, he was troubled, and all Jerusalem with him.

4 [aAnd](#note4a) when he had gathered all the chief priests and scribes of the people together, he [bdemanded](#note4b) of them where Christ should be born.

5 And they said unto him, In Bethlehem of Judæa: for thus it is written by the prophet,

6 And thou [aBethlehem](#note6a), in the land of [bJuda](#note6b), art not the least among the princes of Juda: for out of thee shall come a [cGovernor](#note6c), that shall [drule](#note6d) my people Israel.

7 Then Herod, when he had privily called the wise men, inquired of them diligently what time the star appeared.

8 And he sent them to Bethlehem, and said, Go and search diligently for the young child; and when ye have found him, bring me word again, that I may come and worship him also.

9 When they had heard the king, they departed; and, lo, the star, which they saw in the east, went before them, till it came and stood over where the young child was.

10 When they saw the star, they rejoiced with exceeding great joy.

11 ¶ And when they were come into the house, they saw the young child with Mary his mother, and fell down, and worshipped him: and when they had opened their treasures, they presented unto him gifts; gold, and [afrankincense](#note11a), and myrrh.

12 And being warned of God in a [adream](#note12a) that they should not return to Herod, they departed into their own country another way.

13 And when they were departed, behold, the angel of the Lord appeareth to Joseph in a dream, saying, Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and flee into Egypt, and be thou there until I bring thee word: for Herod will seek the young child to destroy him.

14 When he arose, he took the young child and his mother by night, and departed into Egypt:

15 And was there until the death of Herod: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet, saying, Out of [aEgypt](#note15a) have I called my son.

16 ¶ Then Herod, when he saw that he was [amocked of](#note16a) the wise men, [bwas exceeding wroth](#note16b), and sent forth, and [cslew](#note16c) all the children that were in Bethlehem, and in all the [dcoasts](#note16d) thereof, from two years old and under, according to the time which he had diligently inquired of the wise men.

17 Then was fulfilled that which was spoken by [aJeremy](#note17a) the prophet, saying,

18 In [aRama](#note18a) was there a voice heard, lamentation, and weeping, and great [bmourning](#note18b), Rachel weeping for her [cchildren](#note18c), and would not be comforted, because they are not.

19 ¶ But when Herod was dead, behold, an angel of the Lord appeareth in a [adream](#note19a) to Joseph in Egypt,

20 Saying, Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and go into the [aland of Israel](#note20a): for they are dead which sought the young child’s life.

21 And he arose, and took the young child and his mother, and came into the land of Israel.

22 But when he heard that Archelaus did reign in Judæa in the [aroom](#note22a) of his father Herod, he was afraid to go thither: notwithstanding, being warned of God in a [bdream](#note22b), he turned aside into the parts of Galilee:

23 And he came and dwelt in a city called [aNazareth](#note23a): that it might be fulfilled which was [bspoken](#note23b) by the prophets, He shall be called a [cNazarene](#note23c).

Chapter 3

John the Baptist preaches in Judæa—Jesus is baptized, and the Father acclaims Him as His Beloved Son.

1 In those days came [aJohn the Baptist](#note1a), preaching in the wilderness of Judæa,

2 And saying, [aRepent](#note2a) ye: for the [bkingdom](#note2b) of heaven [cis at hand](#note2c).

3 For this is he that was spoken of by the prophet [aEsaias](#note3a), saying, The [bvoice](#note3b) of one crying in the wilderness, [cPrepare](#note3c) ye the way of the Lord, make his [dpaths](#note3d) straight.

4 And the same John had his [araiment](#note4a) of [bcamel’s](#note4b) hair, and a leathern girdle about his loins; and his meat was [clocusts](#note4c) and wild honey.

5 Then went out to him Jerusalem, and all Judæa, and all the region round about Jordan,

6 And were baptized of him in Jordan, [aconfessing](#note6a) their sins.

7 ¶ But when he saw many of the Pharisees and Sadducees come to his [abaptism](#note7a), he said unto them, O [bgeneration of vipers](#note7b), who hath [cwarned](#note7c) you to flee from the wrath to come?

8 [aBring](#note8a) forth therefore fruits [bmeet for](#note8b) [crepentance](#note8c):

9 And think not to say within yourselves, We have Abraham [ato our father](#note9a): for I say unto you, that God is able of these stones to raise up children unto Abraham.

10 And now also the axe is laid unto the root of the trees: therefore every tree which [abringeth](#note10a) not forth good fruit is [bhewn](#note10b) down, and cast into the fire.

11 [aI](#note11a) indeed [bbaptize](#note11b) you with water unto [crepentance](#note11c): but [dhe](#note11d) that cometh after me is mightier than I, whose shoes I am not [eworthy](#note11e) to bear: he shall [fbaptize](#note11f) you with the Holy Ghost, and with [gfire](#note11g):

12 Whose [afan](#note12a) is in his hand, and he will throughly purge his floor, and gather his wheat into the [bgarner](#note12b); but he will burn up the chaff with unquenchable [cfire](#note12c).

13 ¶ Then cometh Jesus from Galilee to Jordan unto John, to be [abaptized](#note13a) of him.

14 But John forbad him, saying, I have need to be baptized of thee, and comest thou to me?

15 [aAnd](#note15a) Jesus answering said unto him, [bSuffer](#note15b) it to be so now: for thus it [cbecometh us](#note15c) to [dfulfil](#note15d) all [erighteousness](#note15e). Then he suffered him.

16 And Jesus, when he was [abaptized](#note16a), went up [bstraightway](#note16b) out of the water: and, lo, the heavens were opened unto him, and he saw the [cSpirit](#note16c) of God descending like a [ddove](#note16d), and lighting upon him:

17 And lo a [avoice](#note17a) from heaven, saying, This is my [bbeloved](#note17b) [cSon](#note17c), in whom I am well pleased.

Chapter 4

Jesus fasts forty days and is tempted—He begins His ministry, calls disciples, and heals the sick.

1 Then was Jesus [aled](#note1a) up of the Spirit into the wilderness [bto be](#note1b) [ctempted](#note1c) of the devil.

2 And when he had [afasted](#note2a) [bforty](#note2b) days and forty nights, [che](#note2c) was afterward an hungred.

3 And when the [atempter](#note3a) came to him, he said, If thou be the Son of God, command that these stones be made [bbread](#note3b).

4 But he answered and said, It is written, [aMan](#note4a) shall not [blive](#note4b) by [cbread](#note4c) alone, but by every [dword](#note4d) that proceedeth out of the [emouth](#note4e) of God.

5 [aThen](#note5a) the devil taketh him up into the [bholy city](#note5b), and setteth him on a pinnacle of the temple,

6 [aAnd saith](#note6a) unto him, [bIf](#note6b) thou be the Son of God, cast thyself down: for it is written, He shall give his angels charge concerning thee: and in their hands they shall bear thee up, lest at any time thou dash thy foot against a stone.

7 Jesus said unto him, It is written again, Thou shalt not [atempt](#note7a) the Lord thy God.

8 [aAgain](#note8a), the devil taketh him up into an exceeding high mountain, and sheweth him all the [bkingdoms](#note8b) of the world, and the glory of them;

9 [aAnd saith unto him](#note9a), All these things will I give thee, if thou wilt fall down and worship me.

10 Then saith Jesus unto him, Get thee hence, [aSatan](#note10a): for it is written, Thou shalt [bworship](#note10b) the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve.

11 Then the devil leaveth him, [aand](#note11a), behold, [bangels](#note11b) came and ministered unto him.

12 ¶ Now when Jesus had heard that John was cast into prison, he departed into [aGalilee](#note12a);

13 And leaving Nazareth, he came and dwelt in Capernaum, which is upon the sea coast, in the borders of Zabulon and Nephthalim:

14 That it might be [afulfilled](#note14a) which was spoken by Esaias the prophet, saying,

Chapter 5

Jesus preaches the Sermon on the Mount—Its teachings replace and transcend some aspects of the law of Moses—All are commanded to be perfect like their Father in Heaven.

1 [aAnd](#note1a) seeing the multitudes, he went up into a mountain: and when he was set, his disciples came unto him:

2 And he opened his mouth, and taught them, saying,

3 [aBlessed](#note3a) are the [bpoor in spirit](#note3b): for theirs is the [ckingdom](#note3c) of heaven.

4 Blessed are they that [amourn](#note4a): for they shall be [bcomforted](#note4b).

5 Blessed are the [ameek](#note5a): for they shall inherit the [bearth](#note5b).

6 Blessed are they which do [ahunger](#note6a) and thirst after [brighteousness](#note6b): for they shall be filled.

7 Blessed are the [amerciful](#note7a): for they shall obtain mercy.

8 Blessed are the [apure](#note8a) in [bheart](#note8b): for they shall [csee](#note8c) God.

9 Blessed are the [apeacemakers](#note9a): for they shall be called the [bchildren](#note9b) of God.

10 Blessed are they which are [apersecuted](#note10a) for [brighteousness’](#note10b) sake: for [ctheirs](#note10c) is the kingdom of heaven.

11 Blessed are ye, when men shall [arevile](#note11a) you, and persecute you, and shall say all manner of [bevil](#note11b) against you falsely, [cfor my sake](#note11c).

12 [aRejoice](#note12a), and be exceeding glad: for great is your [breward](#note12b) in heaven: for so [cpersecuted](#note12c) they the prophets which were before you.

13 ¶ Ye are the [asalt](#note13a) of the earth: but if the salt have lost his savour, wherewith shall it be salted? it is thenceforth good for nothing, but to be cast out, and to be trodden under foot of men.

14 Ye are the [alight](#note14a) of the world. A city that is set on an hill cannot be hid.

15 Neither do men light a [acandle](#note15a), and put it under a bushel, but on a candlestick; and it giveth light unto all that are in the house.

16 Let your [alight](#note16a) so shine before men, that they may see your good [bworks](#note16b), and [cglorify](#note16c) your Father which is in heaven.

17 ¶ Think not that I am come to [adestroy](#note17a) the [blaw](#note17b), or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil.

18 For verily I say unto you, Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the [alaw](#note18a), till all be [bfulfilled](#note18b).

19 [aWhosoever](#note19a) therefore shall [bbreak](#note19b) one of these least commandments, and shall [cteach](#note19c) men so, he shall be called the least in the kingdom of heaven: but whosoever shall do and [dteach](#note19d) them, the same shall be called great in the kingdom of heaven.

20 For I say unto you, That except your [arighteousness](#note20a) shall exceed the righteousness of the [bscribes](#note20b) and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of heaven.

21 ¶ Ye have heard that it was said by them of old time, Thou [ashalt](#note21a) not [bkill](#note21b); and whosoever shall kill shall be [cin](#note21c) danger of the judgment:

22 But I say unto you, That whosoever is [aangry](#note22a) with his brother [bwithout a cause](#note22b) shall be [cin](#note22c) danger of the judgment: and whosoever shall say to his brother, [dRaca](#note22d), shall be [ein](#note22e) danger of the council: but whosoever shall say, Thou fool, shall be in danger of hell fire.

23 Therefore if thou bring thy gift to the altar, and there rememberest that thy brother hath ought against thee;

24 Leave there thy gift before the altar, and go thy way; first be [areconciled](#note24a) to thy brother, and then come and offer thy gift.

25 [aAgree](#note25a) with thine adversary quickly, whiles thou art in the way with him; lest at any time the adversary deliver thee to the judge, and the judge deliver thee to the officer, and thou be cast into prison.

26 Verily I say unto thee, Thou shalt by no means come out thence, till thou hast paid [athe uttermost farthing](#note26a).

27 ¶ Ye have heard that it was said by them of old time, Thou shalt not commit [aadultery](#note27a):

28 But I say unto you, That whosoever [alooketh](#note28a) on a [bwoman](#note28b) to [clust after](#note28c) her hath committed [dadultery](#note28d) with her already in his heart.

29 And if thy right eye [aoffend thee](#note29a), pluck it out, and cast it from thee: for it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into [bhell](#note29b).

30 And if thy right [ahand](#note30a) offend thee, cut it off, and cast it from thee: for it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into [bhell](#note30b).

31 It hath been said, Whosoever shall put away his wife, let him give her a writing of [adivorcement](#note31a):

32 But I say unto you, That whosoever shall [aput away](#note32a) his [bwife](#note32b), saving for the cause of [cfornication](#note32c), causeth her to commit adultery: and whosoever shall marry her that is divorced committeth adultery.

33 ¶ Again, ye have heard that it hath been said by them of old time, Thou shalt not [aforswear thyself](#note33a), but shalt [bperform](#note33b) unto the Lord thine [coaths](#note33c):

34 But I say unto you, [aSwear](#note34a) not at all; neither by heaven; for it is God’s [bthrone](#note34b):

35 Nor by the earth; for it is his [afootstool](#note35a): neither by Jerusalem; for it is the [bcity](#note35b) of the great King.

36 Neither shalt thou swear by thy head, because thou canst not make one hair white or black.

37 But let your [acommunication](#note37a) be, Yea, yea; Nay, nay: for whatsoever is more than these cometh [bof evil](#note37b).

38 ¶ Ye have heard that it hath been said, An [aeye](#note38a) for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth:

39 But I say unto you, That ye resist not [aevil](#note39a): but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right [bcheek](#note39b), [cturn](#note39c) to him the other also.

40 And if any man will sue thee at the law, and take away thy coat, let him have thy cloak also.

41 And whosoever shall compel thee to go a mile, go with him twain.

42 [aGive](#note42a) to him that asketh thee, and from him that would [bborrow](#note42b) of thee turn not thou away.

43 ¶ Ye have heard that it hath been said, Thou shalt [alove](#note43a) thy [bneighbour](#note43b), and hate thine enemy.

44 But I say unto you, [aLove](#note44a) your [benemies](#note44b), [cbless](#note44c) them that [dcurse](#note44d) you, do [egood](#note44e) to them that [fhate](#note44f) you, and [gpray](#note44g) for them which despitefully use you, and [hpersecute](#note44h) you;

45 That ye [amay be](#note45a) the [bchildren](#note45b) of your Father which is in heaven: for he maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good, and sendeth [crain](#note45c) on the just and on the unjust.

46 For if ye [alove](#note46a) them which love you, what reward have ye? do not even the publicans the same?

47 And if ye salute your brethren only, what do ye more than others? do not even the publicans so?

48 [aBe](#note48a) ye therefore [bperfect](#note48b), even as your [cFather](#note48c) which is in heaven is [dperfect](#note48d).

Chapter 7

Jesus concludes the Sermon on the Mount—He commands, Judge not; ask of God; beware of false prophets—He promises salvation to those who do the will of the Father.

1 [aJudge](#note1a) not, that ye be not [bjudged](#note1b).

13 ¶ Enter ye in at the [astrait](#note13a) [bgate](#note13b): for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to [cdestruction](#note13c), and many there be which go in thereat:

15 ¶ Beware of [afalse prophets](#note15a), which come to you in [bsheep’s](#note15b) clothing, but [cinwardly](#note15c) they are ravening [dwolves](#note15d).

16 Ye shall [aknow](#note16a) them by their [bfruits](#note16b). Do men gather grapes of thorns, or figs of thistles?

17 Even so every good tree bringeth forth [agood](#note17a) [bfruit](#note17b); but a [ccorrupt](#note17c) tree bringeth forth [devil](#note17d) fruit.

19 Every tree that bringeth not forth good [afruit](#note19a) is [bhewn](#note19b) down, and cast into the fire.

20 Wherefore by their [afruits](#note20a) ye shall know them.

21 ¶ Not every one that [asaith](#note21a) unto me, [bLord](#note21b), Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that [cdoeth](#note21c) the [dwill](#note21d) of my Father which is in [eheaven](#note21e).

22 Many will say to me in that day, Lord, Lord, have we not [aprophesied](#note22a) in thy name? and in thy [bname](#note22b) have cast out devils? and in thy name done many wonderful works?

23 [aAnd](#note23a) then will I profess unto them, I never knew you: [bdepart](#note23b) from me, ye that work [ciniquity](#note23c).

24 ¶ Therefore whosoever [aheareth](#note24a) these sayings of mine, and [bdoeth](#note24b) them, [cI](#note24c) will liken him unto a [dwise](#note24d) man, which [ebuilt](#note24e) his house upon [fa rock](#note24f):

25 And the [arain](#note25a) descended, and the floods came, and the winds blew, and [bbeat](#note25b) upon that house; and it fell not: for it was founded upon a [crock](#note25c).

10 Nor [ascrip](#note10a) for your journey, neither two coats, neither shoes, nor yet staves: for the workman is [bworthy](#note10b) of his [cmeat](#note10c).

Chapter 11

1 And it came to pass, when Jesus had made an end of commanding his twelve disciples, he departed thence to teach and to [apreach](#note1a) in their cities.

2 Now when [aJohn](#note2a) had heard in the prison the works of Christ, he sent two of his disciples,

3 And said unto him, Art thou he that should come, or do we look for another?

4 Jesus answered and said unto them, Go and shew John again those [athings](#note4a) which ye do hear and see:

7 ¶ And as they departed, Jesus began to say unto the multitudes concerning John, What went ye out into the wilderness to see? A reed shaken with the wind?

16 ¶ But whereunto shall I liken this generation? It is like unto children sitting in the markets, and calling unto their fellows,

18 For John came neither eating nor drinking, and they say, He hath a devil.

22 But I say unto you, It shall be more [atolerable](#note22a) for Tyre and Sidon at the day of judgment, than for you.

23 And thou, Capernaum, which art [aexalted](#note23a) unto heaven, shalt be brought down to [bhell](#note23b): for if the mighty works, which have been done in thee, had been done in Sodom, it would have remained until this day.

24 But I say unto you, That it shall be more tolerable for the land of [aSodom](#note24a) in the day of judgment, than for thee.

25 ¶ At that time Jesus answered and said, I [athank](#note25a) thee, O Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because thou hast [bhid](#note25b) these things from the wise and [cprudent](#note25c), and hast [drevealed](#note25d) them unto [ebabes](#note25e).

26 Even so, Father: for so it seemed good in thy sight.

27 All [athings](#note27a) are delivered unto me of my Father: and no man knoweth the Son, but the Father; neither [bknoweth](#note27b) any man the Father, save the Son, [cand](#note27c) he to whomsoever the Son will [dreveal](#note27d) him.

28 ¶ [aCome](#note28a) unto me, all ye that [blabour](#note28b) and are heavy laden, and I will give you [crest](#note28c).

Chapter 12

Jesus proclaims Himself Lord of the Sabbath and heals on the Sabbath day—He is accused of casting out devils through the power of Beelzebub—He speaks of blasphemy against the Holy Ghost and says that an evil and adulterous generation seeks signs.

1 At that time Jesus went on the [asabbath](#note1a) day through the [bcorn](#note1b); and his disciples were an hungred, and began to pluck the [cears](#note1c) of corn, and to eat.

2 But when the Pharisees saw it, they said unto him, Behold, thy disciples do that which is not lawful to do upon the sabbath day.

3 But he said unto them, Have ye not [aread](#note3a) what [bDavid](#note3b) did, when he was an hungred, and they that were with him;

4 How he entered into the house of God, and did eat the [ashewbread](#note4a), which was not lawful for him to eat, neither for them which were with him, but only for the priests?

5 Or have ye not read in the law, how that on the [asabbath](#note5a) days the priests in the temple profane the sabbath, and are blameless?

6 But I say unto you, That in this place is one greater than the [atemple](#note6a).

7 But if ye had known what this meaneth, I will have [amercy](#note7a), and not sacrifice, ye would not have condemned the guiltless.

8 For the Son of man is Lord even of the [asabbath](#note8a) day.

9 And when he was departed thence, he went into their synagogue:

10 ¶ And, behold, there was a man which had his hand withered. And they asked him, saying, Is it lawful to heal on the sabbath days? that they might accuse him.

11 And he said unto them, What man shall there be among you, that shall have one sheep, and if it fall into a pit on the sabbath day, will he not lay hold on it, and lift it out?

12 How much then is a man better than a sheep? Wherefore it is lawful to do well on the sabbath days.

13 Then saith he to the man, Stretch forth thine hand. And he stretched it forth; and it was restored whole, like as the other.

14 ¶ Then the Pharisees went out, and held a council against him, how they might destroy him.

15 But [awhen](#note15a) Jesus knew it, he withdrew himself from thence: and great multitudes followed him, and he healed them all;

16 And charged them that they should not make him known:

17 That it might be fulfilled which was spoken by [aEsaias](#note17a) the prophet, saying,

18 Behold my [aservant](#note18a), whom I have [bchosen](#note18b); my [cbeloved](#note18c), in whom my soul is well pleased: I will put my spirit upon him, and he shall shew judgment to the Gentiles.

19 He shall not strive, nor [acry](#note19a); neither shall any man hear his voice in the streets.

20 A bruised reed shall he not break, and smoking flax shall he not quench, till he send forth [ajudgment](#note20a) unto victory.

21 And in his [aname](#note21a) shall the [bGentiles](#note21b) trust.

22 ¶ Then was brought unto him one possessed with a devil, blind, and dumb: and he healed him, insomuch that the blind and dumb both spake and saw.

23 And all the people were amazed, and said, Is not this the son of [aDavid](#note23a)?

24 But when the Pharisees heard it, they said, This fellow doth not cast out devils, but by [aBeelzebub](#note24a) the prince of the devils.

25 And Jesus knew their [athoughts](#note25a), and said unto them, Every [bkingdom](#note25b) divided against itself is brought to desolation; and every city or house divided against itself shall not stand:

26 And if [aSatan](#note26a) cast out Satan, he is divided against himself; how shall then his kingdom stand?

27 And if I by Beelzebub cast out devils, by whom do your children cast them out? therefore they shall be your judges.

28 But if I cast out [adevils](#note28a) by the [bSpirit](#note28b) of God, then the kingdom of God is come unto [cyou](#note28c).

29 Or else how can one enter into a strong man’s house, and [aspoil](#note29a) his goods, except he first bind the strong man? and then he will spoil his house.

30 He that is not with me is against me; and he that gathereth not [awith](#note30a) me scattereth abroad.

31 ¶ Wherefore I say unto you, All manner of sin and blasphemy shall be forgiven [aunto men: but](#note31a) the [bblasphemy](#note31b) against the Holy Ghost shall [cnot](#note31c) be forgiven unto men.

32 And whosoever speaketh a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him: but whosoever speaketh against the Holy Ghost, it shall not be [aforgiven](#note32a) him, neither in this world, neither in the world to come.

33 Either make the tree good, and his [afruit](#note33a) good; or else make the tree corrupt, and his fruit corrupt: for the tree is known by his [bfruit](#note33b).

34 O [ageneration](#note34a) of vipers, how can ye, being evil, [bspeak](#note34b) good things? for out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh.

35 A good man out of the good [atreasure](#note35a) of the heart bringeth forth good things: and an evil man out of the evil treasure bringeth forth [bevil](#note35b) things.

36 But I say unto you, That every [aidle](#note36a) [bword](#note36b) that men shall [cspeak](#note36c), they shall give [daccount](#note36d) thereof in the day of [ejudgment](#note36e).

37 For by thy [awords](#note37a) thou shalt be justified, and by thy words thou shalt be condemned.

38 ¶ Then certain of the scribes and of the Pharisees answered, saying, Master, we would see a [asign](#note38a) from thee.

39 But he answered and said unto them, An evil and adulterous generation seeketh after a [asign](#note39a); and there shall no sign be given to it, but the sign of the prophet Jonas:

41 The men of [aNineveh](#note41a) shall rise in judgment with this generation, and shall condemn it: because they [brepented](#note41b) at the preaching of Jonas; and, behold, a greater than Jonas is here.

42 The [aqueen](#note42a) of the south shall rise up in the judgment with this generation, and shall condemn it: for she came from the uttermost parts of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon; and, behold, a greater than Solomon is here.

Chapter 13

Jesus explains why He teaches with parables—He gives the parables of the sower, the wheat and the tares, the grain of mustard seed, the leaven, the treasure hidden in the field, the pearl of great price, and the net cast into the sea—A prophet is not honored by his own people.

1 The same day went Jesus out of the house, and sat by the [asea](#note1a) side.

2 And great multitudes were gathered together unto him, so that he went into a ship, and sat; and the whole multitude stood on the shore.

3 And he spake many things unto them in parables, saying, Behold, a sower went forth to sow;

4 And when he sowed, some seeds fell by the way side, and the fowls came and devoured them up:

5 Some fell upon [astony](#note5a) places, where they had not much earth: and forthwith they sprung up, because they had no deepness of earth:

6 And when the sun was up, they were scorched; and because they had no root, they withered away.

7 And some fell among [athorns](#note7a); and the thorns sprung up, and choked them:

8 But other fell into good ground, and [abrought forth](#note8a) fruit, some an hundredfold, some sixtyfold, some thirtyfold.

9 Who hath ears to hear, let him hear.

10 And the disciples came, and said unto him, Why speakest thou unto them in [aparables](#note10a)?

11 He answered and said unto them, Because it is given unto you to know the [amysteries](#note11a) of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it is not given.

12 [aFor](#note12a) whosoever hath, to him shall be [bgiven](#note12b), and he shall have more [cabundance](#note12c): but whosoever hath not, from him shall be taken away even that he hath.

13 Therefore speak I to them in parables: because they seeing see not; and hearing they hear not, neither do they understand.

14 And in them is fulfilled the prophecy of Esaias, which saith, By hearing ye shall [ahear](#note14a), and shall not understand; and seeing ye shall see, and shall not perceive:

15 For this people’s [aheart](#note15a) is waxed gross, and their ears are dull of hearing, and their [beyes](#note15b) they have [cclosed](#note15c); lest at any time they should see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and should understand with their heart, and should be converted, and I should [dheal](#note15d) them.

16 But blessed are your [aeyes](#note16a), for they see: and your ears, for they hear.

17 For verily I say unto you, That many [aprophets](#note17a) and righteous men have desired to see those things which ye see, and have not seen them; and to hear those things which ye hear, and have not heard them.

18 ¶ [aHear](#note18a) ye therefore the parable of the sower.

19 When any one heareth the word of the kingdom, and [aunderstandeth](#note19a) it not, then cometh the wicked one, and [bcatcheth](#note19b) away that which was sown in his heart. This is he which received seed by the way side.

20 But he that received the seed into [astony](#note20a) places, the same is he that heareth the word, and anon with joy receiveth it;

21 Yet hath he not root in himself, but dureth for a while: for when [atribulation](#note21a) or [bpersecution](#note21b) ariseth because of the word, by and by [che is offended](#note21c).

22 He also that received seed among the thorns is he that heareth the word; and the care of this [aworld](#note22a), and the [bdeceitfulness](#note22b) of [criches](#note22c), choke the word, and he becometh unfruitful.

23 But he that received seed into the good ground is he that [aheareth](#note23a) the word, and [bunderstandeth it; which](#note23b) also beareth [cfruit](#note23c), and bringeth forth, some an hundredfold, some sixty, some thirty.

24 ¶ Another [aparable](#note24a) put he forth unto them, saying, The [bkingdom](#note24b) of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good [cseed](#note24c) in his field:

25 But while men slept, his enemy came and sowed [atares](#note25a) among the wheat, and went his way.

26 But when the blade was sprung up, and brought forth fruit, then appeared the tares also.

27 So the servants of the householder came and said unto him, Sir, didst not thou sow good seed in thy field? from whence then hath it tares?

28 He said unto them, An enemy hath done this. The servants said unto him, Wilt thou then that we go and gather them up?

29 But he said, Nay; lest while ye gather up the tares, ye root up also the wheat with them.

30 Let both grow together until the [aharvest](#note30a): and in the time of harvest I will say to the reapers, Gather ye together [bfirst](#note30b) the tares, and bind them in bundles to [cburn](#note30c) them: but gather the wheat into my barn.

31 ¶ Another parable put he forth unto them, saying, The kingdom of heaven is like to a [agrain](#note31a) of mustard seed, which a man took, and sowed in his field:

32 Which indeed is the least of all seeds: but when it is grown, it is the greatest among herbs, and becometh a tree, so that the [abirds](#note32a) of the air come and lodge in the branches thereof.

33 ¶ Another parable spake he unto them; The kingdom of heaven is like unto [aleaven](#note33a), which a woman took, and hid in three measures of meal, till the whole was leavened.

34 All these things spake Jesus unto the multitude in parables; and without a parable spake he not unto them:

35 That it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet, saying, I will open my mouth in [aparables](#note35a); I will utter things which have been kept [bsecret](#note35b) from the foundation of the world.

36 Then Jesus sent the multitude away, and went into the house: and his disciples came unto him, saying, Declare unto us the parable of the [atares](#note36a) of the field.

37 He answered and said unto them, He that soweth the good [aseed](#note37a) is the Son of man;

38 The field is the world; the good seed are the children of the kingdom; but the [atares](#note38a) are the children of the wicked one;

39 The enemy that sowed them is the devil; [athe](#note39a) [bharvest](#note39b) is the end of the world; and the reapers are the angels.

40 As therefore the tares are gathered and burned in the fire; so shall it be in the [aend](#note40a) of this world.

41 The Son of man shall send forth his [aangels](#note41a), and they shall gather out of his kingdom all things that [boffend](#note41b), and them which do iniquity;

42 And shall cast them into a furnace of fire: there shall be [awailing](#note42a) and [bgnashing](#note42b) of teeth.

43 Then shall the [arighteous](#note43a) [bshine](#note43b) forth as the [csun](#note43c) in the kingdom of their Father. Who hath ears to hear, let him hear.

44 ¶ Again, the kingdom of heaven is like unto [atreasure](#note44a) hid in a field; the which when a man hath found, he hideth, and for joy thereof goeth and selleth all that he hath, and buyeth that field.

45 ¶ Again, the kingdom of heaven is like unto a merchant man, seeking goodly pearls:

46 Who, when he had found one [apearl](#note46a) of great price, went and sold [ball](#note46b) that he had, and bought it.

47 ¶ Again, the kingdom of heaven is like unto a net, that was cast into the sea, and [agathered](#note47a) of every kind:

48 Which, when it was full, they drew to shore, and sat down, and gathered the good into vessels, but cast the bad away.

49 So shall it be at the [aend](#note49a) of the [bworld: the angels](#note49b) shall come forth, and [csever](#note49c) the wicked from among the [djust](#note49d),

50 [aAnd](#note50a) shall cast them into the furnace of fire: there shall be wailing and gnashing of teeth.

51 Jesus saith unto them, Have ye understood all these things? They say unto him, Yea, Lord.

52 Then said he unto them, Therefore every [ascribe](#note52a) [bwhich is instructed unto](#note52b) the kingdom of heaven is like unto a man that is an householder, which bringeth forth out of his treasure things new and old.

53 ¶ And it came to pass, that when Jesus had finished these parables, he departed thence.

54 And when he was come into his own country, he taught them in their synagogue, insomuch that they were [aastonished](#note54a), and said, Whence hath this man this [bwisdom](#note54b), and these mighty works?

55 Is not this the carpenter’s [ason](#note55a)? is not his mother called Mary? and his [bbrethren](#note55b), James, and Joses, and Simon, and Judas?

56 And his sisters, are they not all with us? Whence then hath this man all these things?

57 And they were offended in him. But Jesus said unto them, A prophet is not without [ahonour](#note57a), save in his own country, and in his own house.

58 And he did not many mighty works there because of their [aunbelief](#note58a).

Chapter 14

John the Baptist is beheaded—Jesus feeds the five thousand and walks on the sea—Those who touch the hem of His garment are made whole.

1 At that time [aHerod](#note1a) the tetrarch heard of the fame of Jesus,

2 And said unto his servants, This is John the Baptist; he is risen from the dead; and therefore mighty works do shew forth themselves in him.

3 ¶ For Herod had laid hold on John, and bound him, and put him in prison for Herodias’ sake, his brother Philip’s wife.

4 For John said unto him, It is not [alawful](#note4a) for thee to have her.

5 And [awhen](#note5a) he would have put him to death, he feared the multitude, because they counted him as a [bprophet](#note5b).

6 But when Herod’s birthday was kept, the daughter of Herodias danced before them, and pleased Herod.

7 Whereupon he [apromised](#note7a) with an oath to give her whatsoever she would ask.

8 And she, being before instructed of her mother, said, Give me here John Baptist’s head in a [acharger](#note8a).

9 And the king was sorry: nevertheless for the oath’s sake, and them which sat with him at meat, he commanded it to be given her.

10 And he sent, and [abeheaded](#note10a) John in the prison.

11 And his head was brought in a charger, and given to the damsel: and she brought it to her mother.

12 And his disciples came, and took up the body, and buried it, and went and told Jesus.

13 ¶ When Jesus heard of it, he departed thence by ship into a desert place apart: and when the people had heard thereof, they followed him on foot out of the cities.

14 And Jesus went forth, and saw a great multitude, and was moved with [acompassion](#note14a) toward them, and he healed their [bsick](#note14b).

15 ¶ And when it was evening, his disciples came to him, saying, This is a desert place, and the time is now past; send the multitude away, that they may go into the villages, and buy themselves [avictuals](#note15a).

16 But Jesus said unto them, They need not depart; give ye them to eat.

17 And they say unto him, We have here but five loaves, and two fishes.

18 He said, Bring them hither to me.

19 And he commanded the multitude to sit down on the grass, and took the five loaves, and the two fishes, and looking up to heaven, he blessed, and [abrake](#note19a), and gave the loaves to his disciples, and the disciples to the multitude.

20 And they did all eat, and were filled: and they [atook](#note20a) up of the fragments that remained twelve baskets full.

Chapter 15

The scribes and Pharisees contend against Jesus—He heals the daughter of a gentile woman—He feeds the four thousand.

1 Then came to Jesus [ascribes](#note1a) and Pharisees, which were of Jerusalem, saying,

3 But he answered and said unto them, Why do ye also transgress the commandment of God by your [atradition](#note3a)?

21 ¶ Then Jesus went thence, and departed into the [acoasts](#note21a) of Tyre and Sidon.

Chapter 17

1 And after six days Jesus taketh [aPeter](#note1a), James, and John his brother, and bringeth them up into an high mountain apart,

2 And was [atransfigured](#note2a) before them: and his face did shine as the sun, and his raiment was white as the light.

3 And, behold, there appeared unto them [aMoses](#note3a) and [bElias](#note3b) talking with him.

4 Then answered Peter, and said unto Jesus, Lord, it is good for us to be here: if thou wilt, let us make here three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elias.

5 While he yet spake, behold, a bright [acloud](#note5a) overshadowed them: and behold a [bvoice](#note5b) out of the cloud, which said, This is my [cbeloved](#note5c) [dSon](#note5d), in whom I am well pleased; hear ye him.

6 And when the disciples heard it, they fell on their face, and were sore [aafraid](#note6a).

7 And Jesus came and touched them, and said, Arise, and be not afraid.

8 And when they had lifted up their eyes, they saw no man, save Jesus only.

9 And as they came down from the mountain, Jesus charged them, saying, Tell the [avision](#note9a) to no [bman](#note9b), until the Son of man be risen again from the [cdead](#note9c).

10 And his disciples asked him, saying, Why then say the scribes that Elias must first come?

11 [aAnd](#note11a) Jesus answered and said unto them, Elias truly shall first come, and [brestore](#note11b) all things.

12 But I say unto you, That Elias is come already, and they knew him not, but have done unto him whatsoever they [alisted](#note12a). Likewise shall also the Son of man suffer of them.

13 Then the disciples understood that he spake unto them of John the Baptist.

14 ¶ And when they were come to the multitude, there came to him a certain man, kneeling down to him, and saying,

15 Lord, have mercy on my son: for he is lunatic, and sore vexed: for ofttimes he [afalleth](#note15a) into the fire, and oft into the water.

16 And I brought him to thy disciples, and they could not cure him.

17 Then Jesus answered and said, O [afaithless](#note17a) and [bperverse](#note17b) generation, how long shall I be with you? how long shall I suffer you? bring him hither to me.

18 And Jesus rebuked the devil; and he departed out of him: and the child was cured from that very hour.

Chapter 18

Jesus explains how we are to treat our offending brethren—The Son of Man came to save that which was lost—All of the Twelve receive the keys of the kingdom—Jesus explains why we should forgive.

1 At the same time came the disciples unto Jesus, saying, Who is the [agreatest](#note1a) in the kingdom of heaven?

2 And Jesus called a little child unto him, and set him in the midst of them,

3 And said, Verily I say unto you, Except ye be converted, and become as little [achildren](#note3a), ye shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven.

4 Whosoever therefore shall [ahumble](#note4a) himself as this little [bchild](#note4b), the same is greatest in the kingdom of heaven.

6 But whoso shall [aoffend](#note6a) one of these little ones which believe in me, it were better for him that a millstone were hanged about his neck, and that he were drowned in the depth of the sea.

8 Wherefore if thy hand or thy foot offend thee, cut them off, and cast them from thee: it is better for thee to enter into life halt or maimed, rather than having two hands or two feet to be cast into everlasting fire.

9 And if thine eye offend thee, pluck it out, and cast it from thee: it is better for thee to enter into life with one eye, rather than having two eyes to be cast into [ahell fire](#note9a).

10 Take heed that ye despise not one of these [alittle ones](#note10a); for I say unto you, That in heaven their angels do always behold the face of my Father which is in heaven.

12 How think ye? if a man have an hundred [asheep](#note12a), and one of them be gone astray, doth he not leave the ninety and nine, and goeth into the mountains, and seeketh that which is gone astray?

30 And he would not: but went and cast him into prison, till he should pay the debt.

32 Then his lord, after that he had called him, said unto him, O thou wicked servant, I forgave thee all that [adebt](#note32a), because thou desiredst me:

34 And his lord was wroth, and delivered him to the tormentors, till he should pay all that was due unto him.

35 So likewise shall my heavenly Father do also unto you, if ye from your hearts [aforgive](#note35a) not every one his brother their trespasses.

Chapter 20

Jesus gives the parable of the laborers in the vineyard—He foretells His crucifixion and resurrection—He came to give His life as a ransom for many.

1 For the kingdom of heaven is like unto a man that is an householder, which went out early in the morning to hire [alabourers](#note1a) into his vineyard.

2 And when he had agreed with the labourers for a penny a day, he sent them into his [avineyard](#note2a).

3 And he went out about the third hour, and saw others standing [aidle](#note3a) in the marketplace,

4 And said unto them; Go ye also into the vineyard, and whatsoever is right I will give you. And they went their way.

5 Again he went out about the sixth and ninth hour, and did likewise.

6 And about the [aeleventh](#note6a) hour he went out, and found others standing idle, and saith unto them, Why stand ye here all the day idle?

7 They say unto him, Because no man hath hired us. He saith unto them, Go ye also into the vineyard; and whatsoever is right, that shall ye receive.

8 So when even was come, the lord of the vineyard saith unto his steward, Call the [alabourers](#note8a), and give them their [bhire](#note8b), beginning from the last unto the first.

9 And when they came that were hired about the eleventh hour, they received every man a penny.

10 But when the first came, they supposed that they should have received more; and they likewise received every man a penny.

11 And when they had received it, they murmured against the goodman of the house,

12 Saying, These last have wrought but one hour, and thou hast made them equal unto us, which have borne the burden and heat of the day.

13 But he answered one of them, and said, Friend, I do thee no wrong: didst not thou agree with me for a penny?

14 Take that thine is, and go thy way: I will give unto this last, even as unto thee.

15 Is it not lawful for me to do what I will with mine own? Is thine eye evil, because I am good?

16 So the [alast](#note16a) shall be first, and the first last: for many be [bcalled](#note16b), but few chosen.

17 ¶ And Jesus going up to Jerusalem took the twelve disciples apart in the way, and said unto them,

18 Behold, we go up to Jerusalem; and the Son of man shall be [abetrayed](#note18a) unto the chief priests and unto the scribes, and they shall condemn him to [bdeath](#note18b),

19 And shall deliver him to the Gentiles to [amock](#note19a), and to scourge, and to [bcrucify](#note19b) him: and the third day he shall [crise](#note19c) again.

20 ¶ Then came to him the mother of Zebedee’s children with her sons, worshipping him, and desiring a certain thing of him.

Chapter 22

Jesus gives the parable of the marriage of the king’s son—Pay tribute to Cæsar and to God—Worldly marriages endure in this life only—The first commandment is to love the Lord—Jesus asks, What think ye of Christ?

1 And Jesus answered and spake unto them again by parables, and said,

2 The kingdom of heaven is like unto a certain king, which [amade](#note2a) a [bmarriage](#note2b) for his son,

3 And sent forth his servants to call them that were bidden to the wedding: and [athey would not come](#note3a).

4 Again, he sent forth other servants, saying, Tell them which are bidden, Behold, I have prepared my dinner: my oxen and my fatlings are killed, and all things are ready: come unto the marriage.

5 But they made light of it, and went their ways, one to his farm, another to his merchandise:

6 And the remnant took his servants, and entreated them spitefully, and slew them.

8 Then saith he to his servants, The wedding is ready, but they which were bidden were not worthy.

9 Go ye therefore into the highways, and as many as ye shall find, bid to the marriage.

11 ¶ And when the king came in to see the guests, he saw there a man which had not on a wedding [agarment](#note11a):

Chapter 24

Jesus foretells the doom of Jerusalem and the destruction of the temple—Great calamities will precede His Second Coming—He gives the parable of the fig tree.

1 And Jesus went out, and departed from the temple: and his disciples came to him for to [ashew](#note1a) him the buildings of the [btemple](#note1b).

2 And Jesus said unto them, [aSee](#note2a) ye not all these things? verily I say unto you, There shall not be left here one [bstone](#note2b) upon another, that shall not be thrown down.

3 ¶ And as he sat upon the mount of Olives, the disciples came unto him privately, saying, Tell us, when shall these things be? and what shall be the [asign](#note3a) of thy coming, and of the end of the [bworld](#note3b)?

4 And Jesus answered and said unto them, Take heed that no man [adeceive](#note4a) you.

5 For many shall come in my [aname](#note5a), saying, I am [bChrist](#note5b); and shall [cdeceive](#note5c) many.

6 And ye shall hear of [awars](#note6a) and rumours of wars: see that ye be not [btroubled](#note6b): for all these things must come to pass, but the end is not yet.

7 For [anation](#note7a) shall rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom: and there shall be [bfamines](#note7b), and pestilences, and earthquakes, in divers places.

8 All these are the beginning of [asorrows](#note8a).

9 Then shall they deliver you up to be afflicted, and shall [akill](#note9a) you: and ye shall be [bhated](#note9b) of all nations [cfor my name’s sake](#note9c).

10 And then shall many be [aoffended](#note10a), and shall betray one another, and shall hate one another.

11 And many [afalse prophets](#note11a) shall rise, and shall deceive many.

12 And because [ainiquity](#note12a) shall abound, the love of many shall wax [bcold](#note12b).

13 But he that shall [aendure](#note13a) unto the end, the same shall be saved.

14 And this [agospel](#note14a) of the [bkingdom](#note14b) shall be preached in all the world for a witness unto all nations; and then shall the [cend](#note14c) come.

15 When ye therefore shall see the [aabomination of desolation](#note15a), spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the [bholy place](#note15b), (whoso readeth, let him [cunderstand](#note15c):)

16 Then let them which be in Judæa [aflee](#note16a) into the mountains:

17 Let him which is on the housetop not come down to take any thing out of his house:

18 Neither let him which is in the field return back to take his clothes.

19 And [awoe](#note19a) unto them that are with child, and to them that give suck in those days!

20 But pray ye that your flight be not in the winter, neither on the sabbath day:

21 For then shall be great [atribulation](#note21a), such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be.

22 And except those days should be shortened, there should no flesh be saved: but for the elect’s sake those [adays](#note22a) shall be shortened.

23 Then if any man shall say unto you, Lo, here is Christ, or there; [abelieve](#note23a) it not.

24 For there shall arise [afalse](#note24a) [bChrists](#note24b), and [cfalse prophets](#note24c), and shall shew great [dsigns](#note24d) and wonders; insomuch that, if it were possible, they shall [edeceive](#note24e) the very [felect](#note24f).

25 Behold, I have told you before.

26 Wherefore if they shall say unto you, Behold, he is in the desert; go not forth: behold, he is in the [asecret](#note26a) chambers; believe it not.

27 For as the [alightning](#note27a) cometh out of the [beast](#note27b), and shineth even unto the west; so shall also the coming of the Son of man be.

28 For wheresoever the carcase is, there will the eagles be [agathered](#note28a) together.

29 ¶ Immediately after the tribulation of those [adays](#note29a) shall the [bsun](#note29b) be [cdarkened](#note29c), and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven, and the powers of the heavens shall be shaken:

30 And then shall appear the [asign](#note30a) of the Son of man in [bheaven](#note30b): and then shall all the tribes of the earth [cmourn](#note30c), and they shall see the [dSon of man](#note30d) coming in the clouds of heaven with [epower](#note30e) and great [fglory](#note30f).

31 And he shall send his [aangels](#note31a) with a great sound of a [btrumpet](#note31b), and they shall gather together his [celect](#note31c) from the four winds, from one end of [dheaven](#note31d) to the other.

32 Now learn a parable of the [afig tree](#note32a); When his branch is yet tender, and putteth forth leaves, ye know that summer is nigh:

33 So likewise ye, when ye shall see all these things, know that [ait is near](#note33a), even at the [bdoors](#note33b).

34 Verily I say unto you, This [ageneration](#note34a) shall not pass, till all these [bthings](#note34b) be [cfulfilled](#note34c).

35 Heaven and earth shall [apass](#note35a) away, but my [bwords](#note35b) shall not [cpass away](#note35c).

36 ¶ But of that [aday](#note36a) and [bhour](#note36b) knoweth no man, no, not the [cangels](#note36c) of heaven, but my Father only.

37 But as the days of [aNoe](#note37a) were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be.

38 For as in the days that were before the [aflood](#note38a) they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark,

39 And knew not until the flood came, and took them all away; so shall also the coming of the Son of man be.

40 Then shall two be in the field; the one shall be taken, and the other left.

41 Two women shall be grinding at the mill; the one shall be taken, and the other left.

42 ¶ [aWatch](#note42a) therefore: for ye know not what hour your Lord doth come.

43 But know this, that if the goodman of the house had known in what watch the thief would come, he would have watched, and would not have suffered his house to be [abroken up](#note43a).

44 Therefore be ye also [aready](#note44a): for in such an [bhour](#note44b) [cas ye think not](#note44c) the Son of man cometh.

45 Who then is a [afaithful](#note45a) and [bwise](#note45b) [cservant](#note45c), whom his lord hath made ruler over his household, to give them meat in due season?

46 Blessed is that [aservant](#note46a), whom his lord when he cometh shall find so doing.

47 Verily I say unto you, That he shall make him ruler over all his goods.

48 But and if that evil servant shall say in his heart, My lord [adelayeth](#note48a) his coming;

49 And shall begin to smite his fellowservants, and to eat and drink with the drunken;

50 The lord of that servant shall come in a day when he looketh not for him, and in an hour that he is not aware of,

51 And shall cut him asunder, and appoint him his portion with the hypocrites: there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.

Chapter 25

Jesus gives the parables of the ten virgins, the talents, and the sheep and the goats.

1 [aThen](#note1a) shall the kingdom of heaven be likened unto ten [bvirgins](#note1b), which took their [clamps](#note1c), and went forth to meet the bridegroom.

2 And five of them were wise, and five were foolish.

3 They that were foolish took their lamps, and took no oil with them:

4 But the wise took oil in their vessels with their lamps.

5 While the bridegroom tarried, they all [aslumbered](#note5a) and [bslept](#note5b).

6 And at midnight there was a cry made, Behold, the [abridegroom](#note6a) cometh; go ye out to meet him.

7 Then all those virgins arose, and trimmed their [alamps](#note7a).

8 And the foolish said unto the wise, Give us of your oil; for our lamps [aare gone out](#note8a).

9 But the wise answered, saying, Not so; lest there be not enough for us and you: but go ye rather to them that sell, and buy for yourselves.

10 And while they went to buy, the bridegroom came; and they that were [aready](#note10a) went in with him to the marriage: and the door was [bshut](#note10b).

11 Afterward came also the other virgins, saying, Lord, Lord, open to us.

12 But he answered and said, [aVerily](#note12a) I say unto you, I [bknow](#note12b) you not.

13 [aWatch](#note13a) therefore, for ye know neither the day nor the hour wherein the Son of man cometh.

14 ¶ For the kingdom of heaven is as a man travelling into a far country, who called his own servants, and delivered unto them his goods.

15 And unto one he [agave](#note15a) five [btalents](#note15b), to another two, and to another one; to every man according to his several ability; and straightway took his journey.

16 Then he that had received the five talents went and traded with the same, and made them other five talents.

17 And likewise he that had received two, he also gained other two.

18 But he that had received one went and digged in the earth, and hid his lord’s money.

19 After a long time the lord of those servants cometh, and [areckoneth](#note19a) with them.

20 And so he that had received five talents came and brought other five talents, saying, Lord, thou [adeliveredst](#note20a) unto me five talents: behold, I have gained beside them five talents more.

21 His lord said unto him, Well done, thou good and faithful [aservant](#note21a): thou hast been [bfaithful](#note21b) over a few things, I will make thee [cruler](#note21c) over many things: enter thou into the [djoy](#note21d) of thy lord.

22 He also that had received two talents came and said, Lord, thou deliveredst unto me two talents: behold, I have gained two other talents beside them.

23 His lord said unto him, Well done, good and [afaithful](#note23a) servant; thou hast been faithful over a few things, I will make thee ruler over many things: enter thou into the joy of thy lord.

24 Then he which had received the one talent came and said, Lord, I knew thee that thou art an [ahard](#note24a) man, reaping where thou hast not sown, and gathering where thou hast not strawed:

25 And I was [aafraid](#note25a), and went and hid thy talent in the earth: lo, there thou hast that is thine.

26 His lord answered and said unto him, Thou wicked and [aslothful](#note26a) servant, thou knewest that I [breap](#note26b) where I sowed not, and gather where I have not strawed:

27 Thou oughtest therefore to have put my money to the exchangers, and then at my coming I should have received mine own with [ausury](#note27a).

28 Take therefore the [atalent](#note28a) from him, and give it unto him which hath ten talents.

29 For unto every one that hath shall be [agiven](#note29a), and he shall have [babundance](#note29b): but from him that hath not shall be [ctaken](#note29c) away even that which he hath.

30 And cast ye the unprofitable servant into outer darkness: there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.

31 ¶ When the [aSon of man](#note31a) shall come in his [bglory](#note31b), and all the holy angels with him, then shall he sit upon the throne of his glory:

32 And before him shall be gathered all [anations](#note32a): and he shall [bseparate](#note32b) them one from another, as a [cshepherd](#note32c) divideth his [dsheep](#note32d) from the goats:

33 And he shall set the sheep on his [aright hand](#note33a), but the goats on the left.

34 Then shall the King say unto them on his [aright hand](#note34a), Come, ye [bblessed](#note34b) of my Father, [cinherit](#note34c) the [dkingdom](#note34d) prepared for you from the foundation of the world:

35 For I was an [ahungred](#note35a), and ye [bgave](#note35b) me meat: I was thirsty, and ye gave me drink: I was a [cstranger](#note35c), and ye took me in:

36 Naked, and ye clothed me: I was sick, and ye [avisited](#note36a) me: I was in [bprison](#note36b), and ye came unto me.

37 Then shall the righteous answer him, saying, Lord, when saw we thee an hungred, and fed thee? or thirsty, and gave thee drink?

38 When saw we thee a stranger, and took thee in? or naked, and clothed thee?

39 Or when saw we thee [asick](#note39a), or in prison, and came unto thee?

40 And the King shall answer and say unto them, Verily I say unto you, Inasmuch as ye have [adone](#note40a) it unto one of the [bleast](#note40b) of these my [cbrethren](#note40c), ye have done it unto me.

41 Then shall he say also unto them on the [aleft](#note41a) hand, [bDepart](#note41b) from me, ye [ccursed](#note41c), into everlasting [dfire](#note41d), [eprepared](#note41e) for the devil and his angels:

42 For I was an [ahungred](#note42a), and ye gave me no meat: I was thirsty, and ye gave me no drink:

43 I was a stranger, and ye took me not in: naked, and ye clothed me not: sick, and in prison, and ye visited me not.

44 Then shall they also answer him, saying, Lord, when saw we thee an hungred, or athirst, or a stranger, or naked, or sick, or in prison, and did not minister unto thee?

45 Then shall he answer them, saying, Verily I say unto you, Inasmuch as ye did it not to one of the [aleast](#note45a) of these, ye did it not to me.

46 And these shall go away into [aeverlasting](#note46a) [bpunishment](#note46b): but the [crighteous](#note46c) into [dlife](#note46d) [eeternal](#note46e).

Chapter 26

Jesus is anointed—He keeps the Passover and institutes the sacrament—He suffers in Gethsemane, is betrayed by Judas, and is taken before Caiaphas—Peter denies that he knows Jesus.

1 And it came to pass, when Jesus had finished all these sayings, he said unto his disciples,

2 Ye know that after two days is the feast of the [apassover](#note2a), and the Son of man is [bbetrayed](#note2b) to be [ccrucified](#note2c).

3 Then assembled together the chief priests, and the scribes, and the elders of the people, unto the palace of the high priest, who was called [aCaiaphas](#note3a),

4 And [aconsulted](#note4a) that they might take Jesus by [bsubtilty](#note4b), and [ckill](#note4c) him.

5 But they said, Not on the feast day, lest there be an [auproar](#note5a) among the people.

6 ¶ Now when Jesus was in Bethany, in the house of Simon the leper,

7 There came unto him a woman having an alabaster box of very precious [aointment](#note7a), and poured it on his head, as he sat at meat.

8 But when his disciples saw it, they had indignation, saying, To what purpose is this [awaste](#note8a)?

9 For this ointment might have been sold for much, and given to the poor.

10 When Jesus understood it, he said unto them, Why trouble ye the woman? for she hath [awrought](#note10a) a good work upon me.

11 For ye have the poor always with you; but me ye have not always.

12 For in that she hath poured this ointment on my body, she did it [afor my burial](#note12a).

13 Verily I say unto you, Wheresoever this gospel shall be preached in the whole world, there shall also this, that this woman hath done, be told for a memorial of her.

14 ¶ Then one of the twelve, called Judas Iscariot, went unto the chief priests,

15 And said unto them, What will ye give me, and I will deliver him unto you? And they covenanted with him for [athirty](#note15a) pieces of silver.

16 And from that time he sought opportunity to [abetray](#note16a) him.

17 ¶ Now the first day of the feast of [aunleavened](#note17a) bread the disciples came to Jesus, saying unto him, Where wilt thou that we prepare for thee to eat the [bpassover](#note17b)?

18 And he said, Go into the city [ato such a man](#note18a), and say unto him, The Master saith, My [btime](#note18b) is at hand; I will keep the passover at thy house with my disciples.

19 And the disciples did as Jesus had appointed them; and they made ready the [apassover](#note19a).

20 Now when the even was come, he sat down with the twelve.

21 And as they did eat, he said, Verily I say unto you, that one of you shall betray me.

22 And they were exceeding sorrowful, and began every one of them to say unto him, Lord, is it I?

23 And he answered and said, He that dippeth his hand with me in the dish, the same shall betray me.

24 The Son of man goeth as it is [awritten](#note24a) of him: but woe unto that man by whom the [bSon of man](#note24b) is betrayed! [cit had been good](#note24c) for that man if he had not been [dborn](#note24d).

25 Then Judas, which betrayed him, answered and said, Master, is it I? He said unto him, Thou hast said.

26 ¶ [aAnd](#note26a) as they were eating, Jesus took [bbread](#note26b), [cand](#note26c) blessed it, and brake it, and gave it to the disciples, and said, Take, eat; this is my [dbody](#note26d).

27 And he took the [acup](#note27a), and gave thanks, and gave it to them, saying, [bDrink](#note27b) ye all of it;

28 For this is my [ablood](#note28a) of the new [btestament](#note28b), which is shed for many for the [cremission](#note28c) of sins.

29 But I say unto you, I will not [adrink](#note29a) henceforth of this fruit of the vine, until that day when I [bdrink it](#note29b) new with you in my Father’s kingdom.

30 And when they had sung an [ahymn](#note30a), they went out into the mount of Olives.

31 Then saith Jesus unto them, All ye shall be [aoffended](#note31a) because of me this night: for it is written, I will [bsmite](#note31b) the [cshepherd](#note31c), and the sheep of the flock shall be scattered abroad.

32 But after I am [arisen](#note32a) again, I will go before you into Galilee.

33 Peter answered and said unto him, Though all men shall be offended because of thee, yet will I never be [aoffended](#note33a).

34 Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, That this night, before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice.

35 Peter said unto him, Though I should die with thee, yet will I not deny thee. Likewise also said all the disciples.

36 ¶ Then cometh Jesus with them unto a place called [aGethsemane](#note36a), and saith unto the disciples, Sit ye here, while I go and [bpray](#note36b) yonder.

37 And he took with him Peter and the two sons of Zebedee, and [abegan](#note37a) to be sorrowful and very heavy.

38 Then saith he unto them, My soul is [aexceeding sorrowful](#note38a), even unto death: tarry ye here, and [bwatch](#note38b) with me.

39 And he went a little further, and fell on his face, and [aprayed](#note39a), saying, O my [bFather](#note39b), if it be possible, let this [ccup](#note39c) pass from me: nevertheless not as I [dwill](#note39d), but as [ethou](#note39e) [fwilt](#note39f).

40 And he cometh unto the disciples, and findeth them [aasleep](#note40a), and saith unto Peter, What, [bcould](#note40b) ye not watch with me one hour?

41 [aWatch](#note41a) and [bpray](#note41b), that ye enter not into [ctemptation](#note41c): the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.

42 He went away again the second time, and prayed, saying, O my Father, if this cup may not pass away from me, except I drink it, thy [awill be](#note42a) done.

43 And he came and found them asleep again: for their eyes were heavy.

44 And he left them, and went away again, and prayed the third time, saying the same words.

45 Then cometh he to his disciples, and saith unto them, Sleep on now, and take your rest: behold, the hour is at hand, and the Son of man is [abetrayed](#note45a) into the hands of sinners.

46 Rise, let us be going: behold, he is at hand that doth betray me.

47 ¶ And while he yet spake, lo, Judas, one of the twelve, came, and with him a great multitude with swords and staves, from the chief priests and elders of the people.

48 Now he that betrayed him gave them a sign, saying, Whomsoever I shall kiss, that same is he: hold him fast.

49 And [aforthwith](#note49a) he came to Jesus, and said, Hail, master; and kissed him.

50 And Jesus said unto him, Friend, wherefore art thou come? Then came they, and laid hands on Jesus, and took him.

51 And, behold, one of them which were with Jesus stretched out his hand, and drew his sword, and struck a [aservant](#note51a) of the high priest’s, and smote off his ear.

52 Then said Jesus unto him, Put up again thy sword into his place: for all they that take the sword shall [aperish](#note52a) with the sword.

53 Thinkest thou that I cannot now pray to my Father, and he shall presently give me more than twelve legions of angels?

54 But how then shall the [ascriptures](#note54a) be fulfilled, that thus it must be?

55 In that same hour said Jesus to the multitudes, Are ye come out as against a thief with swords and staves for to take me? I sat daily with you [ateaching](#note55a) in the temple, and ye laid no hold on me.

56 But all this was done, that the scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled. Then all the disciples forsook him, and fled.

57 ¶ And they that had laid hold on Jesus led him away to [aCaiaphas](#note57a) the high priest, where the scribes and the elders were assembled.

58 But Peter followed him afar off unto the high priest’s palace, and went in, and sat with the servants, to see the end.

59 Now the chief priests, and elders, and all the council, sought false [awitness](#note59a) against Jesus, [bto](#note59b) put him to death;

60 But found none: yea, though many false witnesses came, yet found they none. At the last came two false [awitnesses](#note60a),

61 And said, This fellow said, I am able to destroy the [atemple](#note61a) of God, and to build it in three days.

62 And the high priest arose, and said unto him, Answerest thou nothing? what is it which these witness against thee?

63 But Jesus [aheld](#note63a) his [bpeace](#note63b). And the high priest answered and said unto him, I [cadjure thee](#note63c) by the [dliving God](#note63d), that thou tell us whether thou be the Christ, the Son of God.

64 Jesus saith unto him, Thou hast said: nevertheless I say unto you, Hereafter shall ye see the [aSon of man](#note64a) sitting on the right hand of [bpower](#note64b), and [ccoming](#note64c) in the clouds of heaven.

65 Then the high priest rent his clothes, saying, He hath spoken [ablasphemy](#note65a); what further need have we of witnesses? behold, now ye have heard his blasphemy.

66 What think ye? They answered and said, He is [aguilty](#note66a) [bof](#note66b) [cdeath](#note66c).

67 Then did they [aspit](#note67a) in his face, and buffeted him; and others smote him with the palms of their hands,

68 Saying, Prophesy unto us, thou Christ, Who is he that [asmote](#note68a) thee?

69 ¶ Now Peter sat without in the palace: and a [adamsel](#note69a) came unto him, saying, Thou also wast with Jesus of Galilee.

70 But he denied before them all, saying, I know not what thou sayest.

71 And when he was gone out into the porch, another maid saw him, and said unto them that were there, This fellow was also with Jesus of Nazareth.

72 And again he denied with an oath, I do not know the man.

73 And after a while came unto him they that stood by, and said to Peter, Surely thou also art one of them; for thy speech [abewrayeth thee](#note73a).

74 Then began he to curse and to swear, saying, I know not the man. And immediately the [acock crew](#note74a).

75 And Peter remembered the word of Jesus, which said unto him, Before the cock crow, thou shalt [adeny](#note75a) me thrice. And he went out, and wept bitterly.

Chapter 27

Jesus is accused and condemned before Pilate—Barabbas is released—Jesus is mocked, crucified, and buried in the tomb of Joseph of Arimathæa.

1 When the morning was come, all the chief priests and elders of the people took counsel against Jesus to put him to death:

2 And when they had bound him, they led him away, and delivered him to [aPontius Pilate](#note2a) the governor.

3 ¶ Then Judas, which had betrayed him, when he saw that he was condemned, repented himself, and brought again the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders,

4 Saying, I have sinned in that I have [abetrayed](#note4a) the innocent [bblood](#note4b). And they said, What is that to us? [csee thou to that](#note4c).

5 And he cast down the pieces of silver in the temple, and departed, and went [aand](#note5a) [bhanged](#note5b) himself.

6 And the chief priests took the silver pieces, and said, It is not lawful for to put them into the treasury, because it is the price of blood.

7 And they took counsel, and bought with them the [apotter’s](#note7a) field, to bury [bstrangers](#note7b) in.

8 Wherefore that field was called, The field of blood, unto this day.

9 Then was fulfilled that which was spoken by Jeremy the prophet, saying, And they took the [athirty](#note9a) pieces of silver, the price of him that was valued, whom they of the children of Israel did value;

10 And gave them for the potter’s field, as the Lord appointed me.

11 And Jesus stood before the governor: and the governor asked him, saying, Art thou the King of the Jews? And Jesus said unto him, [aThou sayest](#note11a).

12 And when he was accused of the chief priests and elders, he answered [anothing](#note12a).

13 Then said Pilate unto him, Hearest thou not how many things they witness against thee?

14 And he answered him to never a word; insomuch that the governor marvelled greatly.

15 Now at that feast the governor was [awont](#note15a) to [brelease](#note15b) unto the people a prisoner, whom they [cwould](#note15c).

16 And they had then a notable prisoner, called Barabbas.

17 Therefore when they were gathered together, Pilate said unto them, Whom will ye that I release unto you? Barabbas, or Jesus which is called Christ?

18 For he knew that for [aenvy](#note18a) they had [bdelivered](#note18b) him.

19 ¶ When he was set down on the judgment seat, his wife sent unto him, saying, Have thou nothing to do with that just man: for I have suffered many things this day in a [adream](#note19a) because of him.

20 But the chief [apriests](#note20a) and elders [bpersuaded](#note20b) the multitude that they should [cask](#note20c) Barabbas, and destroy Jesus.

21 The governor answered and said unto them, Whether of the twain will ye that I release unto you? They said, Barabbas.

22 Pilate saith unto them, What shall I do then with Jesus which is called Christ? They all say unto him, Let him be [acrucified](#note22a).

23 And the governor said, Why, what evil hath he done? But they cried out [athe more](#note23a), saying, Let him be crucified.

24 ¶ When Pilate saw that he [acould prevail](#note24a) nothing, but that rather a [btumult](#note24b) was made, he took water, and washed his hands before the multitude, saying, I am innocent of the blood of this [cjust](#note24c) person: see ye to it.

25 Then answered all the people, and said, His [ablood](#note25a) be on us, and on our children.

26 ¶ Then released he Barabbas unto them: and when he had [ascourged](#note26a) Jesus, he delivered him to be crucified.

27 Then the soldiers of the governor took Jesus into the [acommon hall](#note27a), and gathered unto him the whole band of soldiers.

28 And they stripped him, and put on him a [ascarlet](#note28a) robe.

29 ¶ And when they had [aplaited](#note29a) a crown of thorns, they put it upon his head, and a [breed](#note29b) in his right hand: and they bowed the knee before him, and mocked him, saying, Hail, King of the Jews!

30 And they [aspit](#note30a) upon him, and took the reed, and smote him on the head.

31 And after that they had mocked him, they took the robe off from him, and put his own raiment on him, and led him away to crucify him.

32 And as they came out, they found a man of Cyrene, Simon by name: him they compelled to bear his cross.

33 And when they were come unto a place called Golgotha, that is to say, a place of [aa skull](#note33a),

34 ¶ They gave him [avinegar](#note34a) to drink mingled with gall: and when he had tasted thereof, he would not drink.

35 And they [acrucified](#note35a) him, and [bparted his garments](#note35b), casting lots: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet, They parted my [cgarments](#note35c) among them, and upon my vesture did they cast lots.

36 And sitting down they watched him there;

37 And set up over his head his accusation written, This Is Jesus the King of the Jews.

38 Then were there two thieves crucified with him, one on the right hand, and another on the left.

39 ¶ And they that passed by [areviled](#note39a) him, wagging their heads,

40 And saying, Thou that destroyest the [atemple](#note40a), and buildest it in three days, save thyself. If thou be the [bSon of God](#note40b), come down from the cross.

41 Likewise also the chief priests mocking him, with the scribes and elders, said,

42 He saved others; himself he cannot save. If he be the King of Israel, let him now come down from the cross, and we will believe him.

43 He [atrusted](#note43a) in God; let him [bdeliver](#note43b) him now, if he will have him: for he said, I am the [cSon of God](#note43c).

44 The thieves also, which were crucified with him, [acast](#note44a) the same in his teeth.

45 Now from the sixth hour there was [adarkness](#note45a) over all the land unto the ninth hour.

46 And about the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, saying, Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani? that is to say, [aMy](#note46a) God, my God, why hast thou [bforsaken](#note46b) me?

47 Some of them that stood there, when they heard that, said, This man calleth for [aElias](#note47a).

48 And straightway one of them ran, and took a sponge, and filled it with [avinegar](#note48a), and put it on a reed, and gave him to drink.

49 The rest said, Let be, let us see whether Elias will come to save him.

50 ¶ Jesus, when he had cried again with [aa](#note50a) loud voice, yielded up the ghost.

51 And, behold, the [aveil](#note51a) of the temple was [brent in twain](#note51b) from the top to the bottom; and the earth did [cquake](#note51c), and the rocks rent;

52 And the [agraves](#note52a) were opened; and many [bbodies](#note52b) of the [csaints](#note52c) [dwhich slept](#note52d) [earose](#note52e),

53 And came out of the graves after his resurrection, and went into the [aholy city](#note53a), and appeared unto many.

54 Now when the centurion, and they that were with him, watching Jesus, saw the earthquake, and those things that were done, they feared greatly, saying, Truly this was the Son of God.

55 And many [awomen](#note55a) were there beholding afar off, which followed Jesus from Galilee, ministering unto him:

56 Among which was Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of James and [aJoses](#note56a), and the mother of Zebedee’s children.

57 When the even was come, there came a rich man of Arimathæa, named [aJoseph](#note57a), who also himself was Jesus’ [bdisciple](#note57b):

58 He went to Pilate, and [abegged](#note58a) the body of Jesus. Then Pilate commanded the body to be delivered.

59 And when Joseph had taken the body, he wrapped it in a clean linen cloth,

60 And laid it in his own new tomb, which he had hewn out in the rock: and he rolled a great [astone](#note60a) to the door of the [bsepulchre](#note60b), and departed.

61 And there was Mary Magdalene, and the other Mary, sitting [aover against](#note61a) the sepulchre.

62 ¶ Now the next day, that followed the day of the [apreparation](#note62a), the chief priests and Pharisees came together unto Pilate,

63 Saying, Sir, we remember that that [adeceiver](#note63a) said, while he was yet alive, After three days I will rise again.

64 Command therefore that the sepulchre be made sure until the third day, lest his disciples come by night, and steal him away, and say unto the people, He is risen from the dead: so the last [aerror](#note64a) shall be worse than the first.

65 Pilate said unto them, Ye have a watch: go your way, make it as sure as ye can.

66 So they went, and made the sepulchre sure, sealing the stone, and setting a watch.
