Chapter 1

Eternal life was promised before the world began—The qualifications of bishops are given—Unto the pure, all things are pure.

1 Paul, a [aservant](#note1a) of God, and an [bapostle](#note1b) of Jesus Christ, according to the faith of God’s [celect](#note1c), and the acknowledging of the truth which is after godliness;

2 In [ahope](#note2a) of [beternal life](#note2b), which God, that cannot [clie](#note2c), [dpromised](#note2d) before the world began;

3 But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour;

4 To Titus, [amine own son after](#note4a) the common faith: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Saviour.

5 For this cause left I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in [aorder](#note5a) the things that are wanting, and [bordain](#note5b) [celders](#note5c) in every city, as I had [dappointed](#note5d) thee:

6 If any be blameless, the husband of one wife, having faithful children not accused of [ariot or unruly](#note6a).

7 For a [abishop](#note7a) must be blameless, as the [bsteward](#note7b) of God; not [cselfwilled](#note7c), not soon [dangry](#note7d), not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre;

8 But a lover of [ahospitality](#note8a), a lover of [bgood men](#note8b), sober, just, holy, [ctemperate](#note8c);

9 Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to [aconvince](#note9a) the [bgainsayers](#note9b).

10 For there are many unruly and [avain talkers](#note10a) and [bdeceivers](#note10b), specially they of the [ccircumcision](#note10c):

11 Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy [alucre’s](#note11a) sake.

12 One of themselves, even a prophet of their own, said, The Cretians are alway liars, evil beasts, [aslow bellies](#note12a).

13 This witness is true. Wherefore [arebuke](#note13a) them sharply, that they may be sound in the faith;

14 Not giving heed to Jewish [afables](#note14a), and [bcommandments](#note14b) of men, [cthat](#note14c) turn from the truth.

15 [aUnto](#note15a) the pure all things are [bpure](#note15b): but unto them that are [cdefiled](#note15c) and unbelieving is nothing pure; but even their [dmind](#note15d) and [econscience](#note15e) is [fdefiled](#note15f).

16 They [aprofess](#note16a) that they [bknow](#note16b) God; but in [cworks](#note16c) they [ddeny](#note16d) him, being abominable, and [edisobedient](#note16e), and unto every good work [freprobate](#note16f).

Chapter 2

Saints should live righteously, deny ungodliness, and seek the Lord.

1 But [aspeak](#note1a) thou the things which become sound [bdoctrine](#note1b):

2 That the [aaged](#note2a) men be [bsober](#note2b), grave, [ctemperate](#note2c), sound in faith, in charity, in patience.

3 The aged women likewise, that they be in behaviour as becometh holiness, not [afalse](#note3a) [baccusers](#note3b), not given to much wine, teachers of good things;

4 That they may teach the young [awomen](#note4a) to be sober, to [blove](#note4b) their husbands, to love their children,

5 To be discreet, [achaste](#note5a), [bkeepers](#note5b) at [chome](#note5c), good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed.

6 Young men likewise exhort to be sober minded.

7 In all things shewing thyself a pattern of good works: in doctrine shewing uncorruptness, [agravity](#note7a), [bsincerity](#note7b),

8 [aSound](#note8a) speech, that cannot be [bcondemned](#note8b); that he that is [cof the contrary part](#note8c) may be ashamed, having no evil thing to say of you.

9 Exhort [aservants](#note9a) to be obedient unto their own masters, and to please them well in all things; not [banswering again](#note9b);

10 Not [apurloining](#note10a), but shewing all good fidelity; that they may [badorn](#note10b) the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things.

11 For the [agrace](#note11a) of God [bthat](#note11b) bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men,

12 Teaching us that, denying [aungodliness](#note12a) and [bworldly](#note12b) [clusts](#note12c), we should live [dsoberly](#note12d), [erighteously](#note12e), and godly, in this present world;

13 Looking for that blessed hope, and the [aglorious](#note13a) appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ;

14 Who gave [ahimself](#note14a) for us, that he might [bredeem](#note14b) us from all iniquity, and [cpurify](#note14c) unto himself a [dpeculiar people](#note14d), [ezealous](#note14e) of good works.

15 These things speak, and exhort, and [arebuke](#note15a) with all [bauthority](#note15b). Let no man [cdespise](#note15c) thee.

Chapter 3

Saints must live righteously after baptism.

1 Put them in mind to be [asubject](#note1a) to [bprincipalities](#note1b) and powers, to obey magistrates, to be ready to every good work,

2 To [aspeak evil](#note2a) of no man, to be [bno brawlers](#note2b), but gentle, shewing all [cmeekness](#note2c) unto all men.

3 For we ourselves also were [asometimes](#note3a) foolish, [bdisobedient](#note3b), deceived, serving divers [clusts](#note3c) and pleasures, living in [dmalice](#note3d) and envy, hateful, and hating one another.

4 But after that the kindness and love of God our Saviour toward man appeared,

5 Not by works of [arighteousness](#note5a) which we have done, but according to his [bmercy](#note5b) he saved us, by the [cwashing](#note5c) of regeneration, and [drenewing](#note5d) of the Holy Ghost;

6 Which he shed on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Saviour;

7 That being [ajustified](#note7a) by his grace, we should be made [bheirs](#note7b) according to the hope of eternal life.

8 This is a faithful saying, and these things I will that thou affirm constantly, that they which have believed in God might be careful to maintain good [aworks](#note8a). These things are good and profitable unto men.

9 But avoid [afoolish](#note9a) questions, and genealogies, and [bcontentions](#note9b), and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain.

10 A man that is an [aheretick](#note10a) after the first and second [badmonition](#note10b) reject;

11 Knowing that he that is such is [asubverted](#note11a), and sinneth, being condemned of himself.

12 When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or Tychicus, be diligent to come unto me to Nicopolis: for I have determined there to winter.

13 Bring Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their journey diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them.

14 And let [aours](#note14a) also learn to maintain [bgood works](#note14b) for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful.

15 All that are with me salute thee. Greet them that love us in the faith. Grace be with you all. Amen.

¶ It was written to Titus, ordained the first bishop of the church of the Cretians, from Nicopolis of Macedonia.

Chapter 1

The gospel changes a servant into a brother.

1 Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer,

2 And to our beloved Apphia, and [aArchippus](#note2a) our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house:

3 Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

4 I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers,

5 Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints;

6 That the [acommunication](#note6a) of thy faith may become [beffectual](#note6b) by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.

7 For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the [abowels](#note7a) of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother.

8 Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient,

9 Yet for love’s sake I rather beseech thee, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ.

10 I beseech thee for my son [aOnesimus](#note10a), whom I have begotten in my bonds:

11 Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me:

12 Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels:

13 Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel:

14 But without thy [amind](#note14a) would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly.

15 For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever;

16 Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a [abrother](#note16a) beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?

17 If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself.

18 If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee [aought](#note18a), put that on mine account;

19 I Paul have written it with mine own hand, I will repay it: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides.

20 Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord.

21 Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.

22 But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your [aprayers](#note22a) I shall be given unto you.

23 There salute thee [aEpaphras](#note23a), my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus;

24 Marcus, Aristarchus, [aDemas](#note24a), Lucas, my fellowlabourers.

25 The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen.

¶ Written from Rome to Philemon, by Onesimus a servant.
