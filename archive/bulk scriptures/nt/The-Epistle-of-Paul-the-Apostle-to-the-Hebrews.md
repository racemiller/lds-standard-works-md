Chapter 1

The Son is in the express image of the person of the Father—Christ is the Only Begotten Son and thus above the angels.

1 God, who [aat](#note1a) sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the [bfathers](#note1b) by the [cprophets](#note1c),

2 Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his [aSon](#note2a), whom he hath [bappointed](#note2b) [cheir](#note2c) of all things, by whom also he [dmade](#note2d) the [eworlds](#note2e);

3 Who being the brightness of his [aglory](#note3a), and the express image of his [bperson](#note3b), and upholding all things by the word of his [cpower](#note3c), when he had by himself [dpurged](#note3d) our sins, sat down on the [eright hand](#note3e) of the [fMajesty](#note3f) on high;

4 Being made so much better than the angels, as he hath by inheritance obtained a more excellent [aname](#note4a) than they.

5 For unto which of the angels said he at any time, Thou art my [aSon](#note5a), this day have I begotten thee? And again, I will be to him a [bFather](#note5b), and he shall be to me a Son?

6 And again, when he bringeth in the [afirstbegotten](#note6a) into the world, he saith, [bAnd](#note6b) let all the angels of God [cworship](#note6c) him.

7 And of the angels he saith, Who maketh his angels spirits, and his ministers a flame of fire.

8 But unto the Son he saith, Thy [athrone](#note8a), O God, is for [bever](#note8b) and ever: a sceptre of righteousness is the sceptre of thy kingdom.

9 Thou hast loved [arighteousness](#note9a), and hated iniquity; therefore God, even thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.

10 And, Thou, Lord, in the beginning hast laid the [afoundation](#note10a) of the earth; and the heavens are the works of thine [bhands](#note10b):

11 They shall perish; but thou remainest; and they all shall [awax old](#note11a) as doth a garment;

12 And as a vesture shalt thou fold them up, and they shall be changed: but thou art the same, and thy years shall not fail.

13 But to which of the angels said he at any time, Sit on my [aright hand](#note13a), until I make thine enemies thy footstool?

14 Are they not all [aministering](#note14a) [bspirits](#note14b), sent forth [cto minister](#note14c) for them who shall be heirs of salvation?

Chapter 2

Jesus came to suffer death and save men—He came to make reconciliation for the sins of the people.

1 Therefore we ought to give the more [aearnest](#note1a) [bheed](#note1b) to the things which we have heard, lest at any time we should let them slip.

2 For if the word spoken by angels was steadfast, and every transgression and [adisobedience](#note2a) received a [bjust](#note2b) recompence of reward;

3 How shall we [aescape](#note3a), if we neglect so great salvation; which at the first began to be spoken by the Lord, and was confirmed unto us by them that heard him;

4 God also bearing them witness, both with [asigns](#note4a) and [bwonders](#note4b), and with divers miracles, and gifts of the Holy Ghost, according to his own [cwill](#note4c)?

5 For unto the angels hath he not put in subjection the world to come, whereof we speak.

6 But one in a certain place testified, saying, What is [aman](#note6a), that thou art mindful of him? or the son of man, that thou visitest him?

7 Thou madest him a little lower than the [aangels](#note7a); thou crownedst him with glory and honour, and didst set him over the works of thy hands:

8 Thou hast put all things in subjection under his [afeet](#note8a). For in that he put all in subjection under him, he left nothing that is not put under him. But now we see not yet all things put under him.

9 But we see Jesus, who was made a little [alower](#note9a) than the [bangels](#note9b) [cfor](#note9c) the suffering of [ddeath](#note9d), [ecrowned](#note9e) with glory and honour; that he by the [fgrace](#note9f) of God should taste [gdeath](#note9g) for every man.

10 For [ait became him](#note10a), for whom are all things, and by whom are all [bthings](#note10b), in bringing many sons unto glory, to make the [ccaptain](#note10c) of their salvation [dperfect](#note10d) through sufferings.

11 For both he that sanctifieth and they who are [asanctified](#note11a) are all of [bone](#note11b): for which cause he is not ashamed to call them [cbrethren](#note11c),

12 Saying, I will declare thy [aname](#note12a) unto my brethren, in the midst of the church will I sing praise unto thee.

13 And again, I will put my [atrust](#note13a) in him. And again, Behold I and the children which God hath given me.

14 [aForasmuch then as](#note14a) the children are partakers of [bflesh and blood](#note14b), he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might [cdestroy](#note14c) him that had the power of [ddeath](#note14d), that is, the [edevil](#note14e);

15 And deliver them who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to [abondage](#note15a).

16 For verily he took not on him the nature of [aangels](#note16a); but he took on him the [bseed of Abraham](#note16b).

17 Wherefore in all things it behoved him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make [areconciliation](#note17a) for the sins of the people.

18 For in that he himself hath suffered being [atempted](#note18a), he is able to [bsuccour](#note18b) them that are tempted.

Chapter 3

Christ is the Apostle and High Priest of our profession—Jesus, being the Son, is more than a servant—Now is the time and the day of our salvation.

1 Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, [aconsider](#note1a) the [bApostle](#note1b) and [cHigh Priest](#note1c) of our profession, Christ Jesus;

2 Who was faithful to him that appointed him, as also Moses was faithful in all his house.

3 For this man was counted worthy of more glory than Moses, inasmuch as he who hath builded the house hath more honour than the house.

4 For every house is builded by some man; but he that built all things is God.

5 And Moses verily was faithful in all his house, as a [aservant](#note5a), for a testimony of those things which were to be spoken after;

6 But Christ as a son over his own [ahouse](#note6a); whose [bhouse](#note6b) are we, if we hold fast the confidence and the rejoicing of the hope firm unto the end.

7 Wherefore (as the [aHoly Ghost](#note7a) saith, [bTo day](#note7b) if ye will hear his voice,

8 [aHarden](#note8a) not your hearts, as in the [bprovocation](#note8b), in the day of temptation in the wilderness:

9 When your fathers tempted me, [aproved](#note9a) me, and saw my works forty years.

10 Wherefore I was grieved with that [ageneration](#note10a), and said, They do alway err in their heart; and they have not known my ways.

11 So I sware in my wrath, They shall not [aenter](#note11a) into my [brest](#note11b).)

12 Take heed, brethren, lest there be in any of you an evil [aheart](#note12a) of [bunbelief](#note12b), in [cdeparting from](#note12c) the living God.

13 But [aexhort](#note13a) one another daily, while it is called [bTo day](#note13b); lest any of you be hardened through the deceitfulness of sin.

14 For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence [asteadfast](#note14a) unto the end;

15 While it is said, To day if ye will hear his voice, [aharden](#note15a) not your hearts, as in the [bprovocation](#note15b).

16 For some, when they had heard, did provoke: howbeit not all that came out of Egypt by Moses.

17 But with whom was he grieved forty years? was it not with them that had sinned, whose [acarcases](#note17a) fell in the wilderness?

18 And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his rest, but to them that believed not?

19 So we see that they could not enter in because of [aunbelief](#note19a).

Chapter 4

The gospel was offered to ancient Israel—Saints enter into the rest of the Lord—Though tempted in all points, Jesus was without sin.

1 Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left us of entering into his [arest](#note1a), any of you should seem to come short of it.

2 For unto us was the [agospel](#note2a) preached, as well as unto them: but the word preached did not profit them, not being mixed with [bfaith](#note2b) in them that heard it.

3 [aFor](#note3a) we which have believed do enter into rest, as he said, As I have sworn in my wrath, if they shall enter into my [brest](#note3b): although the works were finished from the foundation of the world.

4 For he spake in a certain place of the [aseventh day](#note4a) on this wise, And God did rest the seventh day from all his works.

5 [aAnd](#note5a) in this place again, If they shall enter into my rest.

6 Seeing therefore it remaineth that some must enter therein, and [athey](#note6a) to whom it was first preached entered not in because of [bunbelief](#note6b):

7 Again, he [alimiteth](#note7a) a certain day, saying in David, To day, after so long a time; as it is said, To day if ye will [bhear](#note7b) his voice, [charden](#note7c) not your hearts.

8 For if [aJesus](#note8a) had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day.

9 There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God.

10 For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his.

11 Let us [alabour](#note11a) therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of [bunbelief](#note11b).

12 For the [aword](#note12a) of God is [bquick](#note12b), and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged [csword](#note12c), [dpiercing](#note12d) even to the dividing asunder of [esoul](#note12e) and spirit, and of the joints and marrow, and is a [fdiscerner](#note12f) of the [gthoughts](#note12g) and [hintents](#note12h) of the heart.

13 Neither is there any creature that is not manifest in his sight: but all things are naked and opened unto the [aeyes](#note13a) of him with whom we have to do.

14 Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the [aheavens](#note14a), Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our profession.

15 For we have not an high priest [awhich](#note15a) cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points [btempted](#note15b) like as we are, yet without [csin](#note15c).

16 Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of [agrace](#note16a), that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.

Chapter 5

For a man to hold the priesthood, he must be called of God as was Aaron—Christ was a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek—Jesus Christ is the Author of eternal salvation.

1 For every high priest taken from among men is [aordained](#note1a) for men in things pertaining to God, that he may offer both gifts and [bsacrifices](#note1b) for sins:

2 Who can have [acompassion](#note2a) on the ignorant, and on them that are [bout of the way](#note2b); for that he himself also is compassed with infirmity.

3 And by reason hereof he ought, as for the people, so also for himself, to offer for [asins](#note3a).

4 And no man taketh this [ahonour](#note4a) unto himself, but he that is [bcalled](#note4b) of God, as was [cAaron](#note4c).

5 So also Christ glorified not himself to be made an high priest; but he that said unto him, Thou art my [aSon](#note5a), to day have I begotten thee.

6 As he saith also in another place, Thou art a [apriest](#note6a) [bfor ever](#note6b) after the order of [cMelchisedec](#note6c).

7 Who in the days of his flesh, when he had offered up [aprayers](#note7a) and supplications with [bstrong crying](#note7b) and tears unto him that was able to save him from death, and was heard [cin that he feared](#note7c);

8 Though he were a Son, yet [alearned](#note8a) he [bobedience](#note8b) by the things which he [csuffered](#note8c);

9 And being made [aperfect](#note9a), he became the [bauthor](#note9b) of eternal [csalvation](#note9c) unto all them that obey him;

10 [aCalled](#note10a) of God an [bhigh priest](#note10b) after the order of Melchisedec.

Chapter 6

Let us go on to perfection—The sons of perdition crucify Christ anew—God swears with an oath that the faithful will be saved.

1 Therefore [aleaving](#note1a) the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto [bperfection](#note1b); not laying again the foundation of [crepentance](#note1c) from dead works, and of faith toward God,

2 Of the [adoctrine](#note2a) of [bbaptisms](#note2b), and of [claying](#note2c) on of [dhands](#note2d), and of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment.

3 [aAnd](#note3a) this will we do, if God permit.

4 For it is impossible for those who were once [aenlightened](#note4a), and have tasted of the heavenly gift, and were made partakers of the Holy Ghost,

5 And have [atasted](#note5a) the good word of God, and the powers of the [bworld](#note5b) to come,

6 If they shall [afall away](#note6a), to renew them again unto repentance; seeing they [bcrucify](#note6b) to themselves the Son of God afresh, and put him to an open [cshame](#note6c).

7 For the earth which drinketh in the rain that cometh oft upon it, and bringeth forth herbs [ameet](#note7a) for them by whom it is [bdressed](#note7b), receiveth blessing from God:

8 But that which beareth thorns and briers is rejected, and is nigh unto [acursing](#note8a); whose end is to be burned.

9 But, beloved, we are persuaded better things of you, and things that accompany salvation, though we thus speak.

10 For God is not [aunrighteous](#note10a) to forget your work and [blabour](#note10b) of [clove](#note10c), which ye have shewed toward his [dname](#note10d), in that ye have ministered to the saints, and do minister.

11 And we desire that every one of you do shew the same [adiligence](#note11a) to the full assurance of hope unto the end:

12 That ye be not [aslothful](#note12a), but followers of them who through [bfaith](#note12b) and [cpatience](#note12c) inherit the [dpromises](#note12d).

13 For when God made promise to Abraham, because he could [aswear by](#note13a) no greater, he [bsware](#note13b) by himself,

14 Saying, Surely blessing I will [abless](#note14a) thee, and multiplying I will multiply thee.

15 And so, after he had patiently [aendured](#note15a), he obtained the promise.

16 For men verily swear by the greater: and an [aoath](#note16a) for confirmation is to them an end of all [bstrife](#note16b).

17 Wherein God, willing more abundantly to shew unto the heirs of promise the immutability of his [acounsel](#note17a), [bconfirmed](#note17b) it by an [coath](#note17c):

18 That by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to [alie](#note18a), we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for [brefuge](#note18b) to lay hold upon the [chope](#note18c) set before us:

20 Whither the forerunner is for us entered, even Jesus, made an [ahigh priest](#note20a) for ever after the order of Melchisedec.

Chapter 7

The Melchizedek Priesthood brings exaltation and administers the gospel—It is received with an oath and covenant—The superiority of the Melchizedek Priesthood over the Aaronic Priesthood is explained—Salvation comes through the intercession of Christ.

1 For this Melchisedec, king of Salem, [apriest](#note1a) of the most high God, who met Abraham returning from the slaughter of the kings, and blessed him;

2 To whom also Abraham gave a [atenth](#note2a) part of all; first being by interpretation King of [brighteousness](#note2b), and after that also King of [cSalem](#note2c), which is, King of peace;

3 [aWithout](#note3a) father, without mother, without descent, having neither [bbeginning](#note3b) of days, nor end of life; but made like unto the Son of God; abideth a [cpriest](#note3c) continually.

4 Now consider how great this man was, unto whom even the [apatriarch](#note4a) Abraham gave the tenth of the spoils.

5 And verily they that are of the sons of [aLevi](#note5a), who receive the [boffice](#note5b) of the priesthood, have a commandment to take tithes of the people according to the law, that is, of their brethren, though they come out of the loins of Abraham:

6 But he whose descent is not counted from them received tithes of Abraham, and blessed him that had the promises.

7 And without all contradiction the less is blessed of the better.

8 And here men that die receive tithes; but there he receiveth them, of whom it is witnessed that he liveth.

9 And as I may so say, Levi also, who receiveth tithes, payed tithes in Abraham.

10 For he was yet in the loins of his father, when Melchisedec met him.

11 If therefore [aperfection](#note11a) were by the [bLevitical priesthood](#note11b), (for under it the people received the law,) what further need was there that another priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec, and not be called after the order of Aaron?

12 For the [apriesthood](#note12a) being changed, there is made of necessity a change also of the law.

13 For he of whom these things are spoken pertaineth to another tribe, of which no man gave attendance at the altar.

14 For it is evident that our Lord sprang out of [aJuda](#note14a); of which tribe Moses spake nothing concerning priesthood.

15 And it is yet far more evident: for that after the similitude of Melchisedec there ariseth another priest,

16 Who is made, not after the law of a [acarnal](#note16a) commandment, but after the power of an endless life.

17 For he testifieth, Thou art a priest for ever after the order of [aMelchisedec](#note17a).

18 For there is verily a disannulling of the commandment going before for the [aweakness](#note18a) and unprofitableness thereof.

19 [aFor](#note19a) the law made nothing [bperfect](#note19b), but the bringing in of a [cbetter](#note19c) hope did; by the which we draw nigh unto God.

20 And inasmuch as not without an [aoath](#note20a) he was made priest:

21 (For those [apriests](#note21a) were made without an oath; but this with an [boath](#note21b) by him that said unto him, The Lord sware and will not repent, Thou art a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec:)

22 By so much was Jesus made a surety of a better [atestament](#note22a).

23 And they truly were many priests, because they were not suffered to continue by reason of death:

24 But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable [apriesthood](#note24a).

25 Wherefore he is able also to save them [ato the uttermost](#note25a) that [bcome](#note25b) unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make [cintercession](#note25c) for them.

26 [aFor](#note26a) such an [bhigh priest](#note26b) became us, who is holy, harmless, undefiled, [cseparate](#note26c) from [dsinners](#note26d), and made [ehigher](#note26e) than the [fheavens](#note26f);

27 Who needeth not [adaily](#note27a), as those high priests, to offer up [bsacrifice](#note27b), first for his own sins, and then for the people’s: for this he did [conce](#note27c), when he [doffered up](#note27d) himself.

28 For the law maketh men high priests which have infirmity; but the word of the [aoath](#note28a), which was since the law, maketh the [bSon](#note28b), who is [cconsecrated](#note28c) for evermore.

13 In that he saith, A [anew covenant](#note13a), he hath made the first old. Now that which decayeth and waxeth [bold](#note13b) is ready to [cvanish](#note13c) away.

Chapter 9

The Mosaic ordinances prefigured Christ’s ministry—Christ is the Mediator of the new covenant.

1 Then verily the first [acovenant](#note1a) had also [bordinances](#note1b) of divine [cservice](#note1c), and a worldly sanctuary.

2 For there was a [atabernacle](#note2a) made; the first, wherein was the [bcandlestick](#note2b), and the [ctable](#note2c), and the [dshewbread](#note2d); which is called the sanctuary.

3 And after the second [aveil](#note3a), the tabernacle which is called the [bHoliest](#note3b) of all;

4 Which had the golden censer, and the [aark](#note4a) of the covenant overlaid round about with gold, wherein was the golden [bpot](#note4b) that had manna, and [cAaron’s](#note4c) rod that budded, and the [dtables](#note4d) of the covenant;

5 And over it the [acherubims](#note5a) of glory shadowing the [bmercyseat](#note5b); of which we cannot now speak particularly.

6 Now when these things were thus [aordained](#note6a), the [bpriests](#note6b) went always into the first tabernacle, accomplishing the service of God.

7 But into the second went the [ahigh priest](#note7a) alone [bonce](#note7b) every year, not without blood, which he offered for himself, and for the errors of the people:

8 The Holy Ghost this signifying, that the way into the holiest of all was not yet made manifest, while as the first tabernacle was yet standing:

9 Which was a [afigure](#note9a) for the time then present, in which were offered both gifts and sacrifices, that could not make him that did the service [bperfect](#note9b), as pertaining to the conscience;

10 Which stood only in meats and drinks, and divers [awashings](#note10a), and [bcarnal](#note10b) [cordinances](#note10c), [dimposed](#note10d) on them until the time of reformation.

11 But Christ being come an [ahigh priest](#note11a) of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect [btabernacle](#note11b), not made with hands, that is to say, not of this building;

12 Neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own [ablood](#note12a) he entered in once into the [bholy place](#note12b), having obtained eternal [credemption](#note12c) for us.

13 For if the [ablood](#note13a) of bulls and of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the [bpurifying](#note13b) of the flesh:

14 How much more shall the [ablood](#note14a) of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself [bwithout](#note14b) [cspot](#note14c) to God, [dpurge](#note14d) your [econscience](#note14e) from dead works to serve the living God?

15 And for this cause he is the [amediator](#note15a) of the [bnew](#note15b) [ctestament](#note15c), that by means of [ddeath](#note15d), for the redemption of the [etransgressions](#note15e) that were under the first [ftestament](#note15f), they which are called might receive the [gpromise](#note15g) of eternal [hinheritance](#note15h).

16 For where a [atestament](#note16a) is, there must also of necessity be the [bdeath](#note16b) of the [ctestator](#note16c).

17 For a [atestament](#note17a) is of force after men are dead: [botherwise](#note17b) it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth.

18 Whereupon neither the first testament was dedicated without blood.

19 For when Moses had [aspoken](#note19a) every precept to all the people according to the law, he took the [bblood](#note19b) of calves and of goats, with water, and scarlet wool, and hyssop, and [csprinkled](#note19c) both the book, and all the people,

20 Saying, This is the blood of the testament which God hath enjoined unto you.

21 Moreover he sprinkled with blood both the tabernacle, and all the vessels of the ministry.

22 And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without [ashedding](#note22a) of blood is no [bremission](#note22b).

23 It was therefore necessary that the [apatterns](#note23a) of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these.

24 For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the [atrue](#note24a); but into [bheaven](#note24b) itself, now to appear in the [cpresence](#note24c) of God for us:

25 Nor yet that he should offer himself often, as the [ahigh priest](#note25a) entereth into the holy place every year with blood of others;

26 For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world: but now once [ain](#note26a) the [bend of the world](#note26b) hath he appeared to put away sin by the [csacrifice](#note26c) of himself.

27 And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment:

28 So Christ was once [aoffered](#note28a) to [bbear](#note28b) the [csins](#note28c) of many; and unto them that look for him shall he [dappear](#note28d) the second time without sin unto salvation.

Chapter 10

We are sanctified by the shedding of the blood of Christ—The superiority of His sacrifice is explained—Those who fall from grace through willful sin are damned—The just will live by faith.

1 For the [alaw](#note1a) having a [bshadow](#note1b) of good things to come, and not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices which they offered [cyear](#note1c) by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect.

2 For then would they not have ceased to be offered? because that the worshippers once [apurged](#note2a) should have had no more [bconscience](#note2b) of sins.

3 But in those [asacrifices](#note3a) there is a [bremembrance](#note3b) again made of sins every year.

4 For it is not possible that the blood of bulls and of goats should take away sins.

5 Wherefore when he cometh into the world, he saith, Sacrifice and offering thou wouldest not, but a body hast thou prepared me:

6 In burnt offerings and [asacrifices](#note6a) for sin thou hast had no pleasure.

7 Then said I, Lo, I come (in the volume of the book it is written of me,) to do thy will, O God.

8 Above when he said, Sacrifice and offering and burnt offerings and offering for sin thou wouldest not, neither hadst pleasure therein; which are offered by the law;

9 Then said he, Lo, I come to do thy will, O God. He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second.

10 By the which will we are [asanctified](#note10a) through the [boffering](#note10b) [cof](#note10c) the [dbody](#note10d) of Jesus Christ [eonce](#note10e) for all.

11 And every priest standeth [adaily](#note11a) ministering and offering oftentimes the same sacrifices, which can never take away [bsins](#note11b):

12 But this man, after he had offered one [asacrifice](#note12a) for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God;

13 [aFrom](#note13a) henceforth expecting till his enemies be made his footstool.

14 For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified.

15 Whereof the [aHoly Ghost](#note15a) also is a [bwitness](#note15b) to us: for after that he had said before,

16 This is the [acovenant](#note16a) that I will make with them after those days, saith the Lord, I will put my laws into their hearts, and in their [bminds](#note16b) will I write them;

17 And their sins and iniquities will I remember no more.

18 Now where [aremission](#note18a) of these is, there is no more offering for sin.

19 Having therefore, brethren, [aboldness](#note19a) to enter into the [bholiest](#note19b) by the blood of Jesus,

20 By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the [aveil](#note20a), that is to say, his flesh;

21 And having an [ahigh priest](#note21a) over the house of God;

22 Let us [adraw](#note22a) [bnear](#note22b) with a true heart in full assurance of [cfaith](#note22c), having our hearts [dsprinkled](#note22d) from an evil [econscience](#note22e), and our bodies washed with pure water.

23 Let us hold fast the [aprofession](#note23a) of our [bfaith](#note23b) without wavering; (for he is faithful that promised;)

24 And let us [aconsider](#note24a) one another to [bprovoke](#note24b) unto love and to good works:

25 Not forsaking the [aassembling](#note25a) of ourselves together, as the manner of some is; but exhorting one another: and so much the more, as ye see the day approaching.

26 For if we [asin](#note26a) [bwilfully](#note26b) after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins,

27 But a certain fearful looking for of judgment and fiery [aindignation](#note27a), which shall devour the adversaries.

28 He that [adespised](#note28a) Moses’ law died without mercy under two or three witnesses:

29 Of how much sorer [apunishment](#note29a), suppose ye, shall he be thought worthy, who hath trodden under foot the Son of God, and hath counted the blood of the covenant, wherewith he was sanctified, an unholy thing, and hath done [bdespite](#note29b) unto the Spirit of grace?

30 For we know him that hath said, [aVengeance](#note30a) belongeth unto me, I will [brecompense](#note30b), saith the Lord. And again, The Lord shall [cjudge](#note30c) his people.

31 It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God.

32 But call to remembrance the former days, in which, after ye were illuminated, ye endured a great [afight](#note32a) of afflictions;

33 Partly, whilst ye were made a gazingstock both by reproaches and afflictions; and partly, whilst ye became companions of them that were so used.

34 For ye had compassion of me in my bonds, and took [ajoyfully](#note34a) the spoiling of your goods, knowing in yourselves that ye have in heaven a better and an enduring [bsubstance](#note34b).

35 Cast not away therefore your confidence, which hath great recompence of reward.

36 For ye have need of [apatience](#note36a), that, after ye have done the [bwill](#note36b) of God, ye might receive the [cpromise](#note36c).

37 For yet a little while, and he that shall come will come, and will not tarry.

38 Now the just shall live by faith: but if any man [adraw back](#note38a), my soul shall have no pleasure in him.

39 But we are not of them who [adraw back](#note39a) unto [bperdition](#note39b); but of them that believe to the saving of the soul.

Chapter 11

By faith we understand the word and work of God—The faith of the ancients was centered in Christ—By faith, men subdued kingdoms, wrought righteousness, and worked miracles.

1 Now [afaith](#note1a) is the [bsubstance](#note1b) of things [choped](#note1c) for, the [devidence](#note1d) of things not seen.

2 For by it the elders [aobtained](#note2a) a good [breport](#note2b).

3 Through faith we understand that the worlds were [aframed](#note3a) by the [bword](#note3b) of God, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear.

4 By faith [aAbel](#note4a) offered unto God a more excellent [bsacrifice](#note4b) than Cain, by which he obtained witness that he was righteous, God testifying of his gifts: and by it he being dead yet speaketh.

5 By faith [aEnoch](#note5a) was [btranslated](#note5b) that he should not see death; and was not found, because God had translated him: for before his translation he had this [ctestimony](#note5c), that he pleased God.

6 But without [afaith](#note6a) it is impossible to please him: for he that [bcometh](#note6b) to God must believe that he is, and that he is a [crewarder](#note6c) of them that [ddiligently](#note6d) [eseek](#note6e) him.

7 By faith [aNoah](#note7a), being [bwarned](#note7b) of God of things not seen as yet, [cmoved with fear](#note7c), prepared an ark to the saving of his house; by the which he condemned the world, and became heir of the [drighteousness](#note7d) which is by faith.

8 By faith [aAbraham](#note8a), when he was called to go out into a place which he should after receive for an [binheritance](#note8b), [cobeyed](#note8c); and he went out, not [dknowing](#note8d) whither he went.

9 By faith he [asojourned](#note9a) in the [bland](#note9b) of [cpromise](#note9c), as in a strange country, dwelling in [dtabernacles](#note9d) with Isaac and Jacob, the heirs with him of the same promise:

10 For he looked for a [acity](#note10a) which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God.

11 Through faith also Sara herself received strength to conceive seed, and was delivered of a child when she was past age, because she judged him faithful who had promised.

12 Therefore sprang there even of one, and him as good as dead, so many as the stars of the sky in [amultitude](#note12a), and as the sand which is by the sea shore innumerable.

13 These all died in faith, not having received the [apromises](#note13a), but having seen them afar off, and were persuaded of them, and embraced them, and confessed that they were [bstrangers](#note13b) and pilgrims on the earth.

14 For they that say such things declare plainly that they seek a [acountry](#note14a).

15 And truly, if they had been mindful of that country from whence they came out, they might have had opportunity to have returned.

16 But now they desire a better [acountry](#note16a), that is, an heavenly: wherefore God is not ashamed to be called their God: for he hath prepared for them a city.

17 By [afaith](#note17a) Abraham, when he was [btried](#note17b), [coffered up](#note17c) Isaac: and he that had received the [dpromises](#note17d) offered up his only begotten son,

18 Of whom it was said, That in [aIsaac](#note18a) shall thy [bseed](#note18b) be called:

Chapter 12

Whom the Lord loves He chastens—God is the Father of spirits—To see God, follow peace and holiness—Exalted Saints belong to the Church of the Firstborn.

1 Wherefore seeing we also are compassed about with so great a cloud of witnesses, let us lay aside every weight, and the [asin](#note1a) which doth so easily [bbeset](#note1b) us, and let us run with [cpatience](#note1c) the race that is set before us,

2 Looking unto Jesus the [aauthor](#note2a) and [bfinisher of](#note2b) our faith; who for the joy that was set before him [cendured](#note2c) the cross, despising the [dshame](#note2d), and is set down at the right hand of the [ethrone](#note2e) of God.

3 For consider him that endured such [acontradiction](#note3a) of sinners against himself, lest ye be wearied and faint in your minds.

4 Ye have not yet resisted unto blood, [astriving](#note4a) against sin.

5 And ye have forgotten the exhortation which speaketh unto you as unto children, My son, despise not thou the chastening of the Lord, nor faint when thou art rebuked of him:

6 For whom the Lord loveth he [achasteneth](#note6a), and scourgeth every son whom he receiveth.

7 If ye [aendure](#note7a) [bchastening](#note7b), God dealeth with you as with [csons](#note7c); for what son is he whom the father chasteneth not?

8 But if ye be without chastisement, whereof all are partakers, then are ye bastards, and not sons.

9 Furthermore we have had [afathers](#note9a) of our flesh which [bcorrected](#note9b) us, and we gave them [creverence](#note9c): shall we not much rather be in [dsubjection](#note9d) unto the [eFather](#note9e) of [fspirits](#note9f), and live?

10 For they verily for a few days chastened us after their own pleasure; but he for our profit, that we might be partakers of his [aholiness](#note10a).

11 Now no chastening for the present seemeth to be joyous, but grievous: nevertheless afterward it yieldeth the [apeaceable](#note11a) fruit of righteousness unto them which are [bexercised](#note11b) thereby.

12 Wherefore lift up the hands which hang down, and the [afeeble](#note12a) knees;

13 And make straight paths for your feet, lest that which is lame be turned out of the way; but let it rather be [ahealed](#note13a).

14 Follow [apeace](#note14a) with all men, and [bholiness](#note14b), without which no man shall [csee](#note14c) the Lord:

15 Looking diligently lest any man [afail of](#note15a) the [bgrace](#note15b) of God; lest any root of bitterness springing up trouble you, and thereby many be defiled;

16 Lest there be any fornicator, or profane person, as Esau, who for one morsel of meat sold his [abirthright](#note16a).

17 For ye know how that afterward, when he would have inherited the blessing, he was rejected: for he found no place of repentance, though he sought it carefully with [atears](#note17a).

18 For ye are not come unto the [amount](#note18a) that might be touched, and that burned with fire, nor unto blackness, and darkness, and tempest,

19 And the sound of a trumpet, and the voice of words; which [avoice](#note19a) they that heard entreated that the word should not be spoken to them any more:

20 (For they could not endure that which was commanded, And if so much as a beast touch the mountain, it shall be stoned, or thrust through with a dart:

21 And so terrible was the sight, that Moses said, I exceedingly fear and quake:)

22 But ye are come unto mount [aSion](#note22a), and unto the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem, and to an innumerable company of [bangels](#note22b),

23 To the general assembly and church of the [afirstborn](#note23a), which are [bwritten](#note23b) in heaven, and to God the [cJudge](#note23c) of all, and to the [dspirits](#note23d) of [ejust](#note23e) men made perfect,

24 And to Jesus the [amediator](#note24a) of the [bnew](#note24b) [ccovenant](#note24c), and to the [dblood](#note24d) of sprinkling, that speaketh [ebetter](#note24e) things than that of Abel.

25 See that ye refuse not him that speaketh. For if they [aescaped](#note25a) not who refused him that spake on earth, much more shall not we escape, if we turn away from him that speaketh from heaven:

26 Whose voice then shook the earth: but now he hath promised, saying, Yet once more I [ashake](#note26a) not the earth only, but also heaven.

27 And this word, Yet once more, signifieth the removing of those things that are shaken, as of things that are made, that those things which cannot be shaken may remain.

28 Wherefore we receiving a kingdom which cannot be moved, let us have grace, whereby we may serve God acceptably with [areverence](#note28a) and godly [bfear](#note28b):

29 For our God is a consuming fire.

Chapter 13

Marriage is honorable—Christ is the same everlastingly—Paul explains how the Saints are to offer acceptable sacrifices.

1 Let [abrotherly](#note1a) [blove](#note1b) continue.

2 Be not forgetful to [aentertain](#note2a) [bstrangers](#note2b): for thereby some have entertained [cangels](#note2c) [dunawares](#note2d).

3 Remember them that are in [abonds](#note3a), as bound with them; and them which [bsuffer](#note3b) adversity, as being yourselves also in the body.

4 [aMarriage](#note4a) is honourable in all, and the bed undefiled: but [bwhoremongers](#note4b) and [cadulterers](#note4c) God will [djudge](#note4d).

5 Let your conversation be without [acovetousness](#note5a); and be [bcontent](#note5b) with such things as ye have: for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor [cforsake](#note5c) thee.

6 So that we may boldly say, The Lord is my helper, and I will not fear what man shall do unto me.

7 Remember them which have the rule over you, who have spoken unto you the word of God: whose faith follow, considering the end of their conversation.

8 Jesus Christ the [asame](#note8a) yesterday, and to day, and for ever.

9 Be not [acarried](#note9a) about with divers and strange [bdoctrines](#note9b). For it is a good thing that the heart be established with grace; not with meats, which have not profited them that have been occupied therein.

10 We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle.

11 For the bodies of those beasts, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned without the camp.

12 Wherefore Jesus also, that he might [asanctify](#note12a) the people with his own [bblood](#note12b), [csuffered](#note12c) [dwithout](#note12d) the gate.

13 Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his [areproach](#note13a).

14 For here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come.

15 By him therefore let us offer the [asacrifice](#note15a) of praise to God continually, that is, the [bfruit](#note15b) of our lips giving thanks to his name.

16 But to do good and to [acommunicate](#note16a) forget not: for with such [bsacrifices](#note16b) God is well pleased.

17 [aObey](#note17a) them that have the rule over you, and [bsubmit](#note17b) yourselves: for they watch for your souls, as they that must give account, that they may do it with joy, and not with grief: for that is unprofitable for you.

18 Pray for us: for we trust we have a good [aconscience](#note18a), in all things willing to live [bhonestly](#note18b).

19 But I beseech you the rather to do this, that I may be restored to you the sooner.

20 Now the God of peace, that brought again from the dead our Lord Jesus, that great [ashepherd](#note20a) of the sheep, through the blood of the [beverlasting](#note20b) [ccovenant](#note20c),

21 Make you perfect in every good [awork](#note21a) to do his [bwill](#note21b), working in you that which is wellpleasing in his sight, through Jesus Christ; to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen.

22 And I beseech you, brethren, suffer the word of exhortation: for I have written a letter unto you in few words.

23 Know ye that our brother Timothy is set at liberty; with whom, if he come shortly, I will see you.

24 Salute all them that have the rule over you, and all the saints. They of Italy salute you.

25 Grace be with you all. Amen.
