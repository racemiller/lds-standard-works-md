Chapter 1

If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God—Resist temptation—Be doers of the word—James explains how to recognize pure religion.

1 James, a [aservant](#note1a) of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes which are [bscattered](#note1b) abroad, greeting.

2 My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into [adivers](#note2a) [btemptations](#note2b);

3 Knowing this, that the [atrying](#note3a) of your faith worketh [bpatience](#note3b).

4 But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be [aperfect](#note4a) and entire, wanting nothing.

5 [aIf](#note5a) any of you lack [bwisdom](#note5b), let him ask of God, that [cgiveth](#note5c) to all men liberally, and [dupbraideth](#note5d) not; and it shall be given him.

6 But let him [aask](#note6a) in [bfaith](#note6b), nothing [cwavering](#note6c). For he that wavereth is like a wave of the sea driven with the wind and tossed.

7 For let not that man think that he shall receive any thing of the Lord.

8 A [adouble minded](#note8a) man is unstable in all his ways.

9 Let the brother of [alow](#note9a) degree rejoice in that he is exalted:

10 But the rich, in that he is made low: because as the flower of the [agrass](#note10a) he shall pass away.

11 For the sun is no sooner risen with a burning heat, but it withereth the grass, and the flower thereof falleth, and the [agrace](#note11a) of the fashion of it perisheth: so also shall the [brich](#note11b) man fade away in his ways.

12 [aBlessed](#note12a) is the man that [bendureth temptation](#note12b): for when he is [ctried](#note12c), he shall receive the [dcrown](#note12d) of life, which the Lord hath promised to them that love him.

13 Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be [atempted](#note13a) with [bevil](#note13b), neither tempteth he any man:

14 But every man is [atempted](#note14a), when he is drawn away of his own [blust](#note14b), and enticed.

15 Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth [asin](#note15a): and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death.

16 Do not err, my beloved brethren.

17 Every [agood](#note17a) [bgift](#note17b) and every perfect [cgift](#note17c) is from above, and [dcometh](#note17d) down from the [eFather](#note17e) of lights, with whom is no [fvariableness](#note17f), neither shadow of turning.

18 Of his own will begat he us with the word of truth, that we should be a kind of firstfruits of his creatures.

19 Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to [aspeak](#note19a), [bslow](#note19b) to [cwrath](#note19c):

20 For the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God.

21 Wherefore lay apart all [afilthiness](#note21a) and [bsuperfluity of naughtiness](#note21b), and receive with [cmeekness](#note21c) the [dengrafted](#note21d) word, which is able to save your souls.

22 But be ye [adoers](#note22a) of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves.

23 For if any be a [ahearer](#note23a) of the word, and not a doer, he is like unto a man beholding his natural face in a [bglass](#note23b):

24 For he beholdeth himself, and goeth his way, and straightway forgetteth what manner of man he was.

25 But whoso looketh into the perfect [alaw](#note25a) of [bliberty](#note25b), and continueth therein, he being not a forgetful hearer, but a doer of the work, this man shall be blessed in his deed.

26 If any man among you seem to be religious, and bridleth not his [atongue](#note26a), but deceiveth his own heart, this man’s religion is [bvain](#note26b).

27 Pure [areligion](#note27a) and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To [bvisit](#note27b) the [cfatherless](#note27c) and [dwidows](#note27d) in their [eaffliction](#note27e), and to keep himself [funspotted](#note27f) from [gthe](#note27g) [hworld](#note27h).

Chapter 2

God has chosen the poor of this world who are rich in faith—Salvation is gained by keeping the whole law—Faith without works is dead.

1 My brethren, [ahave](#note1a) not the faith of our [bLord Jesus Christ](#note1b), the Lord of glory, with respect of persons.

2 For if there come unto your assembly a man with a gold ring, in goodly apparel, and there come in also a [apoor](#note2a) man in [bvile](#note2b) raiment;

3 And ye have respect to him that weareth the [agay](#note3a) clothing, and say unto him, Sit thou here in a good place; and say to the poor, Stand thou there, or sit here under my footstool:

4 [aAre](#note4a) ye not then partial in yourselves, and are become judges of evil thoughts?

5 Hearken, my beloved brethren, Hath not God chosen the [apoor](#note5a) of this world rich in faith, and heirs of the kingdom which he hath promised to them that love him?

6 But ye have despised the poor. Do not rich men [aoppress](#note6a) you, and draw you before the judgment seats?

7 Do not they blaspheme that worthy [aname](#note7a) by the which ye are called?

8 If ye fulfil the royal law according to the scripture, Thou shalt [alove](#note8a) thy [bneighbour](#note8b) as thyself, ye do well:

9 But if ye have [arespect](#note9a) to persons, ye commit sin, and are convinced of the law as transgressors.

10 For whosoever shall keep the [awhole](#note10a) law, and yet [boffend](#note10b) in one point, he is [cguilty](#note10c) of all.

11 For he that said, Do not commit [aadultery](#note11a), said also, Do not kill. Now if thou commit no adultery, yet if thou kill, thou art become a transgressor of the law.

12 So speak ye, and so do, as they that shall be judged by the law of [aliberty](#note12a).

13 For he shall have [ajudgment](#note13a) without [bmercy](#note13b), that hath shewed no mercy; and mercy rejoiceth against judgment.

14 [aWhat](#note14a) doth it profit, my brethren, though a man say he hath [bfaith](#note14b), and have not works? can faith save him?

15 If a [abrother](#note15a) or sister be naked, and destitute of daily [bfood](#note15b),

16 And one of you say unto them, Depart in peace, be ye warmed and filled; notwithstanding ye [agive](#note16a) them not those things which are needful to the body; what doth it profit?

17 Even so [afaith](#note17a), if it hath not [bworks](#note17b), is dead, being alone.

18 Yea, a man may say, Thou hast faith, and I have works: shew me thy faith without thy works, and I will shew thee my [afaith](#note18a) by my [bworks](#note18b).

19 Thou believest that there is one God; thou doest well: the [adevils](#note19a) also [bbelieve](#note19b), and tremble.

20 But wilt thou know, O vain man, that [afaith](#note20a) without [bworks](#note20b) is dead?

21 Was not Abraham our father [ajustified](#note21a) by works, when he had [boffered](#note21b) Isaac his son upon the altar?

22 Seest thou how faith wrought with his works, and by [aworks](#note22a) was faith made [bperfect](#note22b)?

23 And the scripture was fulfilled which saith, Abraham [abelieved](#note23a) God, and it was imputed unto him for [brighteousness](#note23b): and he was called the [cFriend](#note23c) of God.

24 Ye see then how that by [aworks](#note24a) a man is [bjustified](#note24b), and not by faith only.

25 Likewise also was not [aRahab](#note25a) the harlot [bjustified](#note25b) by works, when she had received the messengers, and had sent them out another way?

26 For as the body without the [aspirit](#note26a) is [bdead](#note26b), so faith without [cworks](#note26c) is dead also.

Chapter 3

By governing the tongue, we gain perfection—Heavenly wisdom is pure, peaceable, and full of mercy.

1 My brethren, [abe](#note1a) not many [bmasters](#note1b), knowing that we shall [creceive](#note1c) the greater condemnation.

2 For in many things we [aoffend all](#note2a). If any man [boffend not](#note2b) in [cword](#note2c), the same is a [dperfect](#note2d) man, and able also to bridle the whole body.

3 Behold, we put bits in the horses’ mouths, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole body.

4 Behold also the ships, which though they be so great, and are driven of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the [agovernor](#note4a) listeth.

5 Even so the tongue is a little member, and [aboasteth](#note5a) great things. Behold, how great a [bmatter](#note5b) a little fire kindleth!

6 And the [atongue](#note6a) is a fire, a world of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is set on fire of hell.

7 For every kind of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea, is tamed, and hath been tamed of mankind:

8 But the [atongue](#note8a) can no man tame; it is an [bunruly](#note8b) evil, full of deadly poison.

9 Therewith bless we God, even the Father; and therewith [acurse](#note9a) we men, which are made after the [bsimilitude](#note9b) of God.

10 Out of the same [amouth](#note10a) proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be.

11 Doth a [afountain](#note11a) send forth at the same place sweet water and bitter?

12 Can the fig tree, my brethren, bear olive berries? either a vine, figs? so can no fountain both yield salt water and fresh.

13 Who is a wise man and endued with knowledge among you? let him shew out of a good [aconversation](#note13a) his works with [bmeekness](#note13b) of wisdom.

14 But if ye have bitter envying and [astrife](#note14a) in your hearts, [bglory not](#note14b), and lie not against the truth.

15 This wisdom descendeth not from above, but is earthly, sensual, devilish.

16 For where [aenvying](#note16a) and [bstrife](#note16b) is, there is confusion and every evil work.

17 But the [awisdom](#note17a) that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and [beasy to be entreated](#note17b), full of [cmercy](#note17c) and good fruits, without partiality, and without [dhypocrisy](#note17d).

18 And the fruit of [arighteousness](#note18a) is sown in [bpeace](#note18b) of them that make [cpeace](#note18c).

Chapter 4

Wars are born of lusts—The friends of the world are the enemies of God—Sin is failure to walk in the light we have received.

1 From whence come [awars](#note1a) and [bfightings](#note1b) among you? come they not hence, even of your [clusts](#note1c) that war in your members?

2 Ye lust, and have not: ye kill, and desire to have, and cannot obtain: ye fight and war, yet ye have not, because ye ask not.

3 Ye [aask](#note3a), and receive not, because ye [bask](#note3b) [camiss](#note3c), that ye may [dconsume](#note3d) it upon your [elusts](#note3e).

4 Ye [aadulterers](#note4a) and adulteresses, know ye not that the friendship of the [bworld](#note4b) is [cenmity](#note4c) with God? whosoever therefore will be a friend of the world is the [denemy](#note4d) of God.

5 Do ye think that the scripture saith in vain, The spirit that dwelleth in us lusteth to [aenvy](#note5a)?

6 But he giveth more [agrace](#note6a). Wherefore he saith, God resisteth the [bproud](#note6b), but giveth grace unto the [chumble](#note6c).

7 [aSubmit](#note7a) yourselves therefore to God. [bResist](#note7b) the [cdevil](#note7c), and he will flee from you.

12 There is one lawgiver, who is able to save and to destroy: who art thou that [ajudgest](#note12a) another?

13 Go to now, ye that say, To day or to [amorrow](#note13a) we will go into such a city, and continue there a year, and buy and sell, and get gain:

14 Whereas ye know not what shall be on the morrow. For what is your life? It is even a [avapour](#note14a), that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away.

16 But now ye rejoice in your [aboastings](#note16a): all such rejoicing is evil.

17 Therefore to him that [aknoweth](#note17a) to do good, and [bdoeth](#note17b) it not, to him it is [csin](#note17c).

Chapter 5

Misery awaits the wanton rich—Await the Lord’s coming with patience—The elders are to anoint and heal the sick.

1 Go to now, ye rich men, [aweep](#note1a) and howl for your miseries that shall come upon you.

2 Your [ariches](#note2a) are corrupted, and your garments are motheaten.

3 Your [agold](#note3a) and silver is [bcankered](#note3b); and the [crust](#note3c) of them shall be a witness against you, and shall eat your flesh as it were fire. Ye have heaped treasure together for the last days.

4 Behold, the [ahire](#note4a) of the [blabourers](#note4b) who have reaped down your fields, which is of you kept back by fraud, crieth: and the cries of them which have reaped are entered into the ears of the Lord of sabaoth.

5 Ye have lived [ain pleasure](#note5a) on the earth, and been wanton; ye have nourished your hearts, as in a day of slaughter.

6 Ye have condemned and killed the just; and he doth not resist you.

7 Be patient therefore, brethren, unto the coming of the Lord. Behold, the husbandman waiteth for the precious fruit of the earth, and hath long patience for it, until he receive the early and latter rain.

8 Be ye also [apatient](#note8a); [bstablish](#note8b) your hearts: for the [ccoming](#note8c) of the Lord draweth nigh.

9 [aGrudge](#note9a) not one against another, brethren, lest ye be condemned: behold, the judge standeth before the door.

10 Take, my brethren, the prophets, who have spoken in the name of the Lord, for an [aexample](#note10a) of [bsuffering](#note10b) affliction, and of patience.

11 Behold, we count them [ahappy](#note11a) which [bendure](#note11b). Ye have heard of the patience of [cJob](#note11c), and have seen the end of the Lord; that the Lord is very [dpitiful](#note11d), and of tender [emercy](#note11e).

12 But above all things, my brethren, [aswear](#note12a) not, neither by heaven, neither by the earth, neither by any other [boath](#note12b): but let your yea be yea; and your nay, nay; lest ye fall into condemnation.

13 Is any among you [aafflicted](#note13a)? let him pray. Is any merry? let him sing psalms.

14 Is any [asick](#note14a) among you? let him call for the [belders](#note14b) of the church; and let them [cpray](#note14c) over him, [danointing](#note14d) him with oil in the name of the Lord:

15 And the [aprayer](#note15a) of [bfaith](#note15b) shall save the sick, and the Lord shall raise him up; and if he have committed sins, they shall be [cforgiven](#note15c) him.

16 [aConfess](#note16a) your faults one to another, and [bpray](#note16b) one for another, that ye may be healed. [cThe](#note16c) effectual [dfervent](#note16d) [eprayer](#note16e) of a righteous man availeth much.

17 Elias was a man subject to like [apassions](#note17a) as we are, and he prayed earnestly that it might not rain: and it [brained](#note17b) not on the earth by the space of three years and six months.

18 And he prayed again, and the heaven gave rain, and the earth brought forth her fruit.

19 Brethren, if any of you do err from the truth, and one convert him;

20 Let him know, that he which [aconverteth](#note20a) the sinner from the error of his way shall [bsave](#note20b) a soul from death, and shall [chide](#note20c) a multitude of sins.
