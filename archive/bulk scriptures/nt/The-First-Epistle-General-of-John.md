Chapter 1

The Saints gain fellowship with God by obedience—We must confess our sins to gain forgiveness.

1 [aThat](#note1a) which was from the [bbeginning](#note1b), which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and our hands have handled, of the [cWord of life](#note1c);

2 (For the life was manifested, and we have seen it, and bear witness, and [ashew](#note2a) unto you that [beternal life](#note2b), which was with the Father, and was manifested unto us;)

3 That which we have [aseen](#note3a) and heard declare we unto you, that ye also may have fellowship with us: and truly our [bfellowship](#note3b) is with the [cFather](#note3c), and with his Son Jesus Christ.

4 And these things [awrite](#note4a) we unto you, that your joy may be full.

5 This then is the message which we have heard of him, and declare unto you, that God is [alight](#note5a), and in him is no [bdarkness](#note5b) at all.

6 If we say that we have fellowship with him, and [awalk](#note6a) in [bdarkness](#note6b), we lie, and do not the truth:

7 But if we [awalk](#note7a) in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship one with another, and the [bblood](#note7b) of Jesus Christ his Son [ccleanseth](#note7c) us from all sin.

8 If we say that we have [ano](#note8a) [bsin](#note8b), we [cdeceive](#note8c) ourselves, and the truth is not in us.

9 If we [aconfess](#note9a) our sins, he is faithful and [bjust](#note9b) to [cforgive](#note9c) us our sins, and to [dcleanse](#note9d) us from all unrighteousness.

10 If we say that we have [anot sinned](#note10a), we make him a liar, and his word is not in us.

Chapter 2

Christ is our Advocate with the Father—We know God by obedience—Love not the world—Anti-Christs will come in the last days.

1 My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not. [aAnd](#note1a) if any man sin, we have an [badvocate](#note1b) with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous:

2 And he is the [apropitiation](#note2a) for our sins: and not for ours only, but also for the sins of the whole [bworld](#note2b).

3 And hereby we do know that we know him, if we [akeep](#note3a) his commandments.

4 He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his [acommandments](#note4a), is a [bliar](#note4b), and the truth is not in him.

5 But whoso [akeepeth](#note5a) his word, in him verily is the love of God perfected: hereby know we that we are [bin him](#note5b).

6 He that saith he [aabideth](#note6a) in him ought himself also so to [bwalk](#note6b), even as he walked.

7 [aBrethren](#note7a), I write no new commandment unto you, but an old commandment which ye had from the beginning. The old commandment is the word which ye have heard from the beginning.

8 Again, a new commandment I write unto you, [awhich](#note8a) thing is true in him and in you: because the [bdarkness](#note8b) is [cpast](#note8c), and the true [dlight](#note8d) now shineth.

9 He that saith he is in the light, and hateth his brother, is in darkness even until now.

10 He that [aloveth](#note10a) his [bbrother](#note10b) abideth in the light, and there is none occasion of stumbling in him.

11 But he that hateth his brother is in darkness, and [awalketh](#note11a) in darkness, and knoweth not whither he goeth, because that darkness hath blinded his eyes.

12 I [awrite](#note12a) unto you, little [bchildren](#note12b), because your sins are forgiven you [cfor](#note12c) his [dname’s](#note12d) sake.

13 I write unto you, fathers, because ye have known him that is from the beginning. I write unto you, young men, because ye have overcome the wicked one. I write unto you, little children, because ye have known the Father.

14 I have written unto you, fathers, because ye have known him that is from the beginning. I have written unto you, young men, because ye are strong, and the word of God abideth in you, and ye have overcome the wicked one.

15 Love not the [aworld](#note15a), neither the things that are in the [bworld](#note15b). If any man love the world, the [clove](#note15c) of the Father is not in him.

16 For all that is in the world, the [alust](#note16a) of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the [bpride](#note16b) of life, is not of the Father, but is of the [cworld](#note16c).

17 And the [aworld](#note17a) [bpasseth](#note17b) away, and the lust thereof: but he that doeth the [cwill](#note17c) of God [dabideth](#note17d) for ever.

18 Little children, it is the last time: and as ye have heard that [aantichrist](#note18a) shall come, even now are there many [bantichrists](#note18b); whereby we know that it is the last time.

19 They went out from us, but they were not of us; for if they had been of us, they would no doubt have continued with us: but they went out, that they might be made manifest that they were not all of us.

20 But ye have an [aunction](#note20a) from the [bHoly One](#note20b), and ye know all things.

21 I have not written unto you because ye know not the truth, but because ye know it, and that no lie is of the truth.

22 Who is a [aliar](#note22a) but he that denieth that Jesus is the Christ? He is [bantichrist](#note22b), that denieth the Father and the Son.

23 Whosoever denieth the Son, the same hath not the Father: [but] he that [aacknowledgeth](#note23a) the Son hath the Father also.

24 Let that therefore abide in you, which ye have heard from the beginning. If that which ye have heard from the [abeginning](#note24a) shall remain in you, ye also shall continue in the Son, and in the Father.

25 And this is the [apromise](#note25a) that he hath promised us, even [beternal life](#note25b).

26 These things have I written unto you concerning them that [aseduce](#note26a) you.

27 But the anointing which ye have received of him abideth in you, and ye need not that any man [ateach](#note27a) you: but as the same [banointing](#note27b) [cteacheth](#note27c) you of all things, and is truth, and is no lie, and even as it hath taught you, ye shall abide in him.

28 And now, little children, abide in him; that, when he shall appear, we may have confidence, and not be ashamed before him at his coming.

29 If ye know that he is righteous, ye know that every one that doeth [arighteousness](#note29a) is [bborn](#note29b) of him.

Chapter 3

The sons of God will become like Christ—Love for others is required to gain eternal life—Obedience ensures us an answer to our prayers.

1 Behold, what manner of [alove](#note1a) the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be called the [bsons](#note1b) of God: therefore the [cworld](#note1c) knoweth us not, because it knew him not.

2 Beloved, now are we the [asons](#note2a) of God, and it doth not yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when he shall [bappear](#note2b), we shall be [clike](#note2c) him; for we shall [dsee](#note2d) him as he is.

3 And every man that hath this [ahope](#note3a) in him [bpurifieth](#note3b) himself, even as he is [cpure](#note3c).

4 Whosoever committeth sin [atransgresseth](#note4a) also the law: for [bsin](#note4b) is the transgression of the law.

5 And ye know that he was manifested to take away our [asins](#note5a); and in him is no sin.

6 Whosoever abideth in him [asinneth](#note6a) not: [bwhosoever](#note6b) sinneth hath not seen him, neither [cknown](#note6c) him.

7 Little children, let no man deceive you: he that doeth righteousness is righteous, even as he is righteous.

8 He that [acommitteth](#note8a) [bsin](#note8b) is of the devil; for the devil [csinneth](#note8c) from the beginning. For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the [ddevil](#note8d).

9 Whosoever is [aborn of God](#note9a) [bdoth](#note9b) not commit sin; for his seed remaineth in him: and he cannot sin, because he is born of God.

10 In this the children of God are [amanifest](#note10a), and the [bchildren](#note10b) of the devil: whosoever doeth not [crighteousness](#note10c) is not of God, neither he that loveth not his brother.

11 For this is the [amessage](#note11a) that ye heard from the beginning, that we should love one another.

12 Not as [aCain](#note12a), who was of that wicked one, and slew his brother. And wherefore slew he him? Because his own works were evil, and his brother’s righteous.

13 Marvel not, my brethren, if the world hate you.

14 We know that we have passed from death unto [alife](#note14a), because we love the [bbrethren](#note14b). He that [cloveth](#note14c) not his brother abideth in [ddeath](#note14d).

15 Whosoever [ahateth](#note15a) his brother is a murderer: and ye know that no [bmurderer](#note15b) hath eternal life abiding in him.

16 Hereby perceive we the [alove](#note16a) of [bGod](#note16b), because he laid down his life for us: and we ought to lay down our lives for the brethren.

17 But whoso hath this world’s good, and seeth his [abrother](#note17a) have [bneed](#note17b), and [cshutteth](#note17c) up his bowels of [dcompassion](#note17d) from him, how dwelleth the love of God in him?

18 My little children, let us not love in word, [aneither in tongue](#note18a); but in [bdeed](#note18b) and in truth.

19 And hereby we know that we are of the truth, and shall assure our hearts before him.

20 For if our heart condemn us, God is greater than our heart, and [aknoweth](#note20a) all things.

21 Beloved, if our heart condemn us not, then have we [aconfidence](#note21a) toward God.

22 And whatsoever we [aask](#note22a), we receive of him, because we [bkeep](#note22b) his [ccommandments](#note22c), and do those things that are pleasing in his sight.

23 And this is his [acommandment](#note23a), That we should [bbelieve](#note23b) on the [cname](#note23c) of his Son Jesus Christ, and [dlove](#note23d) one another, as he gave us commandment.

24 And he that keepeth his commandments [adwelleth](#note24a) in him, and he in him. And hereby we know that he [babideth](#note24b) in us, by the [cSpirit](#note24c) which he hath given us.

Chapter 4

Try the spirits—God is love and dwells in those who love Him.

10 Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that he [aloved](#note10a) us, and [bsent](#note10b) his Son to be the [cpropitiation](#note10c) for our sins.

11 Beloved, if God so loved us, we ought also to love one another.

12 [aNo](#note12a) man hath [bseen](#note12b) God at any time. If we love one another, [cGod](#note12c) [ddwelleth](#note12d) in us, and his love is perfected in us.

13 Hereby know we that we [adwell](#note13a) in him, and he in us, because he hath given us of his Spirit.

14 And we have seen and do [atestify](#note14a) that the Father [bsent](#note14b) the Son to be the [cSaviour](#note14c) of the [dworld](#note14d).

16 And we have known and believed the love that God hath to us. God is love; and he that [adwelleth](#note16a) in love dwelleth in God, and God in him.

17 Herein is our love made perfect, that we may have boldness in the day of judgment: because as he [ais](#note17a), so are we in this world.

18 There is no [afear](#note18a) in [blove](#note18b); but perfect [clove](#note18c) casteth out fear: because fear hath [dtorment](#note18d). He that feareth is not made perfect in love.

Saints are born of God through belief in Christ—Water, blood, and the Spirit testify of Christ—Belief in Christ is required in order to gain eternal life.

2 By this we know that we [alove](#note2a) the [bchildren](#note2b) of God, when we love God, and keep his commandments.

3 For this is the [alove](#note3a) of God, that we [bkeep](#note3b) his commandments: and his commandments are not [cgrievous](#note3c).

7 For there are three that bear record in heaven, the [aFather](#note7a), the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are [bone](#note7b).

8 And there are three that bear witness in earth, the [aSpirit](#note8a), and the water, and the [bblood](#note8b): and these three agree in one.

9 If we receive the [awitness](#note9a) of men, the [bwitness](#note9b) of God is greater: for this is the [cwitness](#note9c) of God which he hath testified of his Son.

10 He that [abelieveth](#note10a) on the Son of God hath the [bwitness](#note10b) in himself: he that believeth not God hath made him a liar; because he believeth not the [crecord](#note10c) that God gave of his Son.

11 And this is the record, that God hath given to us [aeternal life](#note11a), and this [blife](#note11b) is in his Son.

12 He that hath the Son hath life; and he that hath not the Son of God hath not life.

13 These things have I [awritten](#note13a) unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal [blife](#note13b), and that ye may believe on the name of the Son of God.

14 And this is the confidence that we have in him, that, if we ask any thing according to his [awill](#note14a), he [bheareth](#note14b) us:

Chapter 1

Contend for the faith—Some angels kept not their first estate—Michael disputed about the body of Moses—Enoch prophesied of the Second Coming—Mockers will come in the last days.

1 [aJude](#note1a), the [bservant](#note1b) of Jesus Christ, and brother of James, to them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ, and called:

2 Mercy unto you, and peace, and love, be multiplied.

3 Beloved, when I gave all diligence to [awrite](#note3a) unto you of the common [bsalvation](#note3b), it was needful for me to write unto you, and exhort you that ye should [cearnestly](#note3c) [dcontend](#note3d) for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.

4 For there are certain [amen](#note4a) crept in unawares, who were before of old ordained to this condemnation, [bungodly](#note4b) men, turning the grace of our God into [clasciviousness](#note4c), and [ddenying](#note4d) the only Lord God, and our Lord Jesus Christ.

5 I will therefore put you in remembrance, though ye once knew this, how that the Lord, having [asaved](#note5a) the people out of the land of Egypt, afterward [bdestroyed](#note5b) them that [cbelieved not](#note5c).

6 And the [aangels](#note6a) which kept not their [bfirst](#note6b) [cestate](#note6c), but left their own habitation, he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness unto the [djudgment](#note6d) of the great day.

7 Even as [aSodom](#note7a) and Gomorrha, and the cities about them in like manner, giving themselves over to [bfornication](#note7b), and going after [cstrange flesh](#note7c), are set forth for an [dexample](#note7d), suffering the [evengeance](#note7e) of eternal fire.

8 Likewise also these [afilthy](#note8a) dreamers [bdefile](#note8b) the flesh, despise dominion, and speak evil of dignities.

9 Yet [aMichael](#note9a) the [barchangel](#note9b), when contending with the devil he disputed about the body of [cMoses](#note9c), durst not bring against him a railing accusation, but said, The Lord rebuke thee.

10 But these speak evil of those things which they know not: but what they know naturally, as brute beasts, in those things they corrupt themselves.

11 Woe unto them! for they have gone in the way of [aCain](#note11a), and ran greedily after the error of [bBalaam](#note11b) for reward, and [cperished](#note11c) in the gainsaying of Core.

12 These are spots in your feasts of charity, when they feast with you, feeding themselves without fear: [aclouds](#note12a) they are without water, carried about of winds; trees whose fruit withereth, without fruit, twice dead, plucked up by the roots;

13 Raging waves of the sea, foaming out their own shame; wandering [astars](#note13a), to whom is reserved the blackness of darkness for ever.

14 And [aEnoch](#note14a) also, the seventh from Adam, prophesied of these, saying, Behold, the Lord [bcometh](#note14b) with ten thousands of his [csaints](#note14c),

15 To [aexecute](#note15a) [bjudgment](#note15b) upon all, and to convince all that are ungodly among them of all their ungodly deeds which they have ungodly committed, and of all their hard [cspeeches](#note15c) which ungodly sinners have spoken against him.

18 How that they told you there should be [amockers](#note18a) in the [blast time](#note18b), who should [cwalk](#note18c) after their own ungodly lusts.

19 These be they who [aseparate](#note19a) themselves, sensual, having not the [bSpirit](#note19b).

21 Keep yourselves in the love of God, looking for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ unto eternal life.

22 And of some have [acompassion](#note22a), making a difference:

23 And others save with fear, pulling them out of the [afire](#note23a); hating even the [bgarment](#note23b) spotted by the flesh.

24 Now unto him that is able to keep you from falling, and to present you [afaultless](#note24a) before the presence of his glory with exceeding joy,

25 To the only wise God our Saviour, be glory and majesty, dominion and power, both now and ever. Amen.
