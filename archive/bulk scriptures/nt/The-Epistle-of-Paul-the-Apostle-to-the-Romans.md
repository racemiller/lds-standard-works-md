Chapter 1

The gospel is the power of God unto salvation through Jesus Christ—The wrath of God rests on those guilty of murder, homosexual practices, fornication, and other sins if the guilty do not repent.

1 Paul, a [aservant](#note1a) of Jesus Christ, called to be an [bapostle](#note1b), [cseparated](#note1c) unto the gospel of God,

2 (Which he had promised afore by his prophets in the holy scriptures,)

3 Concerning his Son [aJesus Christ our Lord](#note3a), which was made of the [bseed](#note3b) of [cDavid](#note3c) according to the flesh;

4 And [adeclared](#note4a) to be the [bSon of God](#note4b) with [cpower](#note4c), according to the spirit of holiness, by the resurrection [dfrom the dead](#note4d):

5 By whom we have received [agrace](#note5a) and apostleship, [bfor](#note5b) [cobedience](#note5c) to the faith among all nations, for his name:

6 Among whom are ye also the called of Jesus Christ:

7 To all that be in Rome, beloved of God, called to be [asaints](#note7a): Grace to you and peace from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ.

8 First, I thank my God through Jesus Christ for you all, that your [afaith](#note8a) is [bspoken of](#note8b) throughout the whole world.

9 For God is my witness, whom I serve with my spirit in the gospel of his Son, that without ceasing I make mention of you always in my prayers;

10 Making request, if by any means now at length I might have a prosperous journey by the will of God to come unto you.

11 For I long to see you, that I may impart unto you some spiritual [agift](#note11a), to the end ye may be established;

12 That is, that I may be [acomforted](#note12a) together with you by the mutual faith both of you and me.

13 Now I would not have you ignorant, brethren, that oftentimes I purposed to come unto you, (but was [alet hitherto](#note13a),) that I might have some [bfruit](#note13b) among you also, even as among other Gentiles.

14 I am debtor both to the Greeks, and to the [aBarbarians](#note14a); both to the wise, and to the unwise.

15 So, as much as in me is, I am [aready](#note15a) to preach the gospel to you that are at Rome also.

16 For I am not [aashamed](#note16a) of the [bgospel](#note16b) of Christ: for it is the [cpower](#note16c) of God unto [dsalvation](#note16d) to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.

17 For therein is the [arighteousness](#note17a) of God revealed [bfrom](#note17b) faith to faith: as it is written, The just shall live by [cfaith](#note17c).

18 For the [awrath](#note18a) of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, [bwho](#note18b) [chold](#note18c) the truth in [dunrighteousness](#note18d);

19 Because that which may be known of God is manifest [ain](#note19a) them; for God hath shewed it unto them.

20 For the [ainvisible](#note20a) things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even his [beternal](#note20b) [cpower](#note20c) and Godhead; so that they are without excuse:

21 Because that, when they knew God, they glorified him not as God, neither were [athankful](#note21a); but became [bvain in their imaginations](#note21b), and their [cfoolish](#note21c) heart was [ddarkened](#note21d).

22 Professing themselves to be [awise](#note22a), they became fools,

23 And changed the glory of the [auncorruptible](#note23a) God into an [bimage](#note23b) made like to [ccorruptible](#note23c) man, and to birds, and fourfooted beasts, and creeping things.

24 Wherefore God also [agave](#note24a) them up to [buncleanness](#note24b) through the lusts of their own hearts, to dishonour their own bodies between themselves:

25 Who changed the truth of God into a lie, and worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator, who is blessed for ever. Amen.

26 For this cause God [agave](#note26a) them up unto [bvile](#note26b) [caffections](#note26c): for even their [dwomen](#note26d) did change the natural use into that which is against nature:

27 And likewise also the men, leaving the natural use of the woman, burned in their [alust](#note27a) one toward another; men with men working that which is unseemly, and receiving in themselves that recompence of their error which was meet.

28 And even as they did not like to [aretain](#note28a) God in their knowledge, God [bgave](#note28b) them over to a [creprobate](#note28c) mind, to do those things which are not convenient;

29 Being filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, [awickedness](#note29a), covetousness, [bmaliciousness](#note29b); full of envy, murder, [cdebate](#note29c), [ddeceit](#note29d), malignity; whisperers,

30 [aBackbiters](#note30a), haters of God, [bdespiteful](#note30b), [cproud](#note30c), [dboasters](#note30d), inventors of evil things, [edisobedient](#note30e) to parents,

31 Without [aunderstanding](#note31a), covenantbreakers, without natural affection, implacable, unmerciful:

32 Who knowing the judgment of God, that they which commit such things are worthy of [adeath](#note32a), not only do the same, but [bhave](#note32b) [cpleasure](#note32c) in them that do them.

Chapter 2

God will render to every person according to his or her deeds—Both Jews and Gentiles will be judged by gospel laws.

1 Therefore thou art inexcusable, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest: for wherein thou [ajudgest](#note1a) another, thou condemnest thyself; for thou that judgest doest the same things.

2 But we [aare sure](#note2a) that the [bjudgment](#note2b) of God is according to truth against them which commit such things.

3 And thinkest thou this, O man, that [ajudgest](#note3a) them which do such things, and doest the same, that thou shalt escape the [bjudgment](#note3b) of God?

4 Or despisest thou the [ariches](#note4a) of his [bgoodness](#note4b) and [cforbearance](#note4c) and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?

5 But [aafter](#note5a) thy [bhardness](#note5b) and impenitent heart treasurest up unto thyself wrath against the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God;

6 Who will [arender](#note6a) to every man [baccording](#note6b) to his [cdeeds](#note6c):

7 To them who by [apatient](#note7a) [bcontinuance](#note7b) in well doing seek for glory and honour and [cimmortality](#note7c), eternal life:

8 But unto them that are [acontentious](#note8a), and do not [bobey](#note8b) the truth, but obey unrighteousness, [cindignation](#note8c) and wrath,

9 [aTribulation](#note9a) and [banguish](#note9b), upon every soul of man that doeth evil, of the Jew first, and also of the [cGentile](#note9c);

10 But glory, honour, and [apeace](#note10a), to every man that [bworketh](#note10b) good, to the Jew first, and also to the [cGentile](#note10c):

11 For there is no [arespect](#note11a) of [bpersons](#note11b) with God.

12 For as many as have sinned without [alaw](#note12a) shall also perish without law: and as many as have sinned in the law shall be judged by the law;

13 (For not the hearers of the law are just before God, but the [adoers](#note13a) of the law shall be [bjustified](#note13b).

14 For when the Gentiles, which have not the law, do by nature the things contained in the law, these, having not the law, are a law unto themselves:

15 [aWhich](#note15a) shew the work of the [blaw](#note15b) written in their hearts, their [cconscience](#note15c) also bearing [dwitness](#note15d), and their thoughts the mean while accusing or else excusing one another;)

16 In the day when God shall [ajudge](#note16a) the secrets of men by Jesus Christ according to my [bgospel](#note16b).

17 Behold, thou art called a Jew, and restest in the law, and makest thy boast of God,

18 And knowest his will, and approvest the things that are more excellent, being instructed out of the law;

19 And art confident that thou thyself art a guide of the blind, a light of them which are in darkness,

20 An instructor of the foolish, a teacher of babes, which hast the [aform](#note20a) of knowledge and of the truth in the law.

21 Thou therefore which teachest another, [ateachest](#note21a) thou not thyself? thou that [bpreachest](#note21b) a man should not [csteal](#note21c), dost thou steal?

22 Thou that sayest a man should not commit [aadultery](#note22a), dost thou commit adultery? thou that abhorrest idols, dost thou [bcommit](#note22b) [csacrilege](#note22c)?

23 Thou that makest thy boast of the law, through breaking the law dishonourest thou God?

24 For the name of God is [ablasphemed](#note24a) among the Gentiles through you, as it is written.

25 For [acircumcision](#note25a) verily profiteth, if thou keep the law: but if thou be a breaker of the law, thy circumcision is made uncircumcision.

26 Therefore if the uncircumcision keep the righteousness of the law, shall not his uncircumcision be counted for circumcision?

27 And shall not uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfil the law, judge thee, who by the letter and circumcision dost transgress the law?

28 For he is not a Jew, which is one outwardly; neither is that circumcision, which is outward in the flesh:

29 But he is a Jew, which is one inwardly; and [acircumcision](#note29a) is that of the heart, in the [bspirit](#note29b), and not in the letter; whose praise is not of men, but of God.

Chapter 3

Man is not justified by the law of Moses—He is justified through righteousness, which comes through faith in Christ, made possible through Christ’s atoning sacrifice.

1 [aWhat](#note1a) [badvantage](#note1b) then hath the Jew? or what profit is there of [ccircumcision](#note1c)?

2 Much every way: chiefly, because that unto them were committed the [aoracles](#note2a) of God.

3 For what if some did not believe? shall their [aunbelief](#note3a) make the faith of God without effect?

4 [aGod forbid](#note4a): yea, let God be true, but every man a [bliar](#note4b); as it is written, That thou mightest be [cjustified](#note4c) in thy sayings, and mightest overcome when thou art judged.

5 [aBut](#note5a) if our unrighteousness [bcommend](#note5b) the righteousness of God, what shall we say? Is God [cunrighteous](#note5c) who taketh vengeance? (I speak as a man)

6 [aGod forbid](#note6a): for then how shall God [bjudge](#note6b) the world?

7 For if the truth of God hath more abounded [athrough](#note7a) my lie unto his glory; why yet am I also judged as a sinner?

8 And not rather, (as we be slanderously reported, and as some affirm that we say,) Let us do evil, that good may come? whose [adamnation](#note8a) is just.

9 What then? are we better than they? No, in no wise: for we have before proved both Jews and Gentiles, that they are all under sin;

10 As it is written, There is [anone](#note10a) [brighteous](#note10b), no, not one:

11 There is none that understandeth, there is none that seeketh after God.

12 They are all gone out of the [away](#note12a), they are together become [bunprofitable](#note12b); there is none that doeth [cgood](#note12c), no, not one.

13 Their [athroat](#note13a) is an open sepulchre; with their tongues they have used deceit; the [bpoison](#note13b) of asps is under their lips:

14 Whose mouth is full of cursing and [abitterness](#note14a):

15 Their feet are swift to [ashed](#note15a) blood:

16 Destruction and [amisery](#note16a) are in their ways:

17 And the way of [apeace](#note17a) have they not known:

18 There is no [afear](#note18a) of God before their eyes.

19 Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every [amouth](#note19a) may be stopped, and all the world may become [bguilty](#note19b) before God.

20 Therefore by the [adeeds](#note20a) of the [blaw](#note20b) there shall no flesh be [cjustified](#note20c) in his sight: for [dby](#note20d) the law is the knowledge of sin.

21 But now the [arighteousness](#note21a) of God [bwithout](#note21b) the law is manifested, being witnessed by the law and the prophets;

22 Even the righteousness of God which is by faith of Jesus Christ unto all and upon all them that believe: for there is no difference:

23 For all have [asinned](#note23a), and come short of the glory of God;

24 [aBeing](#note24a) [bjustified](#note24b) freely by his [cgrace](#note24c) through the [dredemption](#note24d) that is in Christ Jesus:

25 Whom God hath [aset forth](#note25a) to be a [bpropitiation](#note25b) through faith in his [cblood](#note25c), to declare his righteousness for the [dremission](#note25d) of sins that are past, through the [eforbearance](#note25e) of God;

26 To declare, I say, at this time his righteousness: that he might be [ajust](#note26a), and the justifier of him which believeth in Jesus.

Chapter 4

Abraham’s faith was accounted to him for righteousness—Man is justified by faith, righteous works, and grace.

1 What shall we say then that Abraham our father, as pertaining to the flesh, hath found?

2 [aFor](#note2a) if Abraham were justified by works, he hath [bwhereof to glory](#note2b); but not before God.

3 For what saith the scripture? Abraham [abelieved](#note3a) God, and it was counted unto him for [brighteousness](#note3b).

4 Now to him that worketh is the [areward](#note4a) not [breckoned](#note4b) of grace, but of debt.

5 But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness.

6 Even as David also describeth the blessedness of the man, unto whom God imputeth righteousness without works,

7 Saying, [aBlessed](#note7a) are they whose iniquities are forgiven, and whose sins are covered.

8 Blessed is the man to whom the Lord will not impute sin.

9 Cometh this blessedness then upon the circumcision only, or upon the uncircumcision also? for we say that faith was reckoned to Abraham for righteousness.

10 How was it then reckoned? when he was in circumcision, or in uncircumcision? Not in circumcision, but in uncircumcision.

11 And he received the [asign](#note11a) of [bcircumcision](#note11b), a seal of the righteousness of the faith which he had yet being uncircumcised: that he might be the [cfather](#note11c) of all them that believe, though they be not circumcised; that righteousness might be imputed unto them also:

12 And the father of circumcision to them who are not of the circumcision only, but who also [awalk](#note12a) in the steps of that faith of our father Abraham, which he had being yet uncircumcised.

13 For the promise, that he should be the [aheir](#note13a) of the world, was not to Abraham, or to his [bseed](#note13b), through the law, but through the righteousness of faith.

14 For if they which are of the law be heirs, faith is made void, and the promise made of none effect:

15 Because the law worketh wrath: for where no [alaw](#note15a) is, there is no [btransgression](#note15b).

16 [aTherefore](#note16a) it is of faith, that it might be by [bgrace](#note16b); to the end the promise might be sure to all the seed; not to that only which is of the law, but to that also which is of the [cfaith](#note16c) of Abraham; who is the [dfather](#note16d) of us all,

17 (As it is written, I have made thee a father of many nations,) before him whom he believed, even God, who [aquickeneth](#note17a) the dead, and calleth those things which be not as though they were.

18 Who against hope believed in [ahope](#note18a), that he might become the father of many [bnations](#note18b), according to that which was spoken, So shall thy [cseed](#note18c) be.

19 And being not weak in faith, he considered not his own body now dead, when he was about an hundred years [aold](#note19a), neither yet the deadness of Sara’s womb:

20 He [astaggered](#note20a) not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God;

21 And being fully persuaded that, what he had [apromised](#note21a), he was able also to perform.

22 And therefore it was imputed to him for righteousness.

23 Now it was not written for his sake alone, that it was imputed to him;

24 But for [aus](#note24a) also, to whom it shall be imputed, if we believe on him that raised up Jesus our Lord from the dead;

25 Who was delivered for our [aoffences](#note25a), and was raised again for our [bjustification](#note25b).

Chapter 5

Man is justified through the blood of Christ—Adam fell, and Christ atoned that man might be saved.

1 Therefore being [ajustified](#note1a) by [bfaith](#note1b), we have [cpeace](#note1c) with God through our Lord Jesus Christ:

2 By whom also we have access by [afaith](#note2a) into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God.

3 And not only so, but we glory in [atribulations](#note3a) also: knowing that [btribulation](#note3b) worketh [cpatience](#note3c);

4 And patience, experience; and experience, hope:

5 And [ahope](#note5a) maketh not ashamed; because the [blove](#note5b) of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

6 For when we were yet without [astrength](#note6a), in due time Christ died for the ungodly.

7 For scarcely for a righteous man will one die: yet peradventure for a good man some would even dare to die.

8 But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet [asinners](#note8a), [bChrist](#note8b) died for us.

9 Much more then, being now [ajustified](#note9a) by his [bblood](#note9b), we shall be saved from wrath through him.

10 For if, when we were enemies, we were [areconciled](#note10a) to God by the [bdeath](#note10b) of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life.

11 And not only so, but we also joy in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom we have now received the [aatonement](#note11a).

12 Wherefore, as by one man [asin](#note12a) entered into the world, and [bdeath](#note12b) by sin; and so [cdeath](#note12c) passed upon all men, for that all have [dsinned](#note12d):

13 (For until the law sin was in the world: but [asin](#note13a) is not imputed when there is no [blaw](#note13b).

14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over them that had not sinned after the similitude of Adam’s [atransgression](#note14a), who is the [bfigure](#note14b) of him that was to come.

15 But not as the offence, so also is the free gift. For if through the offence of one many be dead, much more the grace of God, and the gift by grace, which is by [aone](#note15a) man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many.

16 And not as it was by one that sinned, so is the gift: for the judgment was by one to condemnation, but the free gift is of many offences unto justification.

17 For if by one man’s offence death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance of [agrace](#note17a) and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ.)

18 Therefore as by the offence of one judgment came upon all men to condemnation; even so by the [arighteousness](#note18a) of one the free gift came upon all men unto [bjustification](#note18b) of life.

19 For as by one man’s [adisobedience](#note19a) many were made sinners, so by the [bobedience](#note19b) of one shall many be made righteous.

20 Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound:

21 That as sin hath reigned unto death, even so might grace reign through [arighteousness](#note21a) unto [beternal life](#note21b) by Jesus Christ our Lord.

Chapter 6

Baptism is in similitude of the death, burial, and resurrection of Christ—The wages of sin is death—Christ brings eternal life.

1 What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin, that [agrace](#note1a) may abound?

2 [aGod forbid](#note2a). How shall we, that are dead to [bsin](#note2b), live any longer therein?

3 Know ye not, that so many of us as were [abaptized](#note3a) into Jesus Christ were baptized into his death?

4 Therefore we are [aburied](#note4a) with him by [bbaptism](#note4b) into death: that like as Christ was raised up from the [cdead](#note4c) by the glory of the Father, even so we also should [dwalk](#note4d) in [enewness](#note4e) of life.

5 For if we have been planted together in the [alikeness](#note5a) of his [bdeath](#note5b), we shall be also in the likeness of his [cresurrection](#note5c):

6 Knowing this, that our [aold](#note6a) man is crucified with him, that the [bbody](#note6b) of sin might be [cdestroyed](#note6c), that henceforth we should not serve [dsin](#note6d).

7 For he that is [adead](#note7a) is [bfreed](#note7b) from sin.

8 Now if we be [adead](#note8a) with Christ, we believe that we shall also live with him:

9 Knowing that Christ being [araised](#note9a) from the dead dieth no more; death hath no more dominion over him.

10 For in that he died, he died unto [asin](#note10a) [bonce](#note10b): but in that he liveth, he liveth unto God.

11 Likewise reckon ye also yourselves to be dead indeed unto sin, but [aalive](#note11a) unto God through Jesus Christ our Lord.

12 Let not sin therefore reign in your [amortal](#note12a) body, that ye should obey it in the [blusts](#note12b) thereof.

13 Neither [ayield](#note13a) ye your [bmembers](#note13b) as [cinstruments](#note13c) of unrighteousness unto sin: but [dyield](#note13d) yourselves unto God, as those that are alive from the dead, and your members as instruments of [erighteousness](#note13e) unto God.

14 [aFor sin](#note14a) shall not have [bdominion](#note14b) over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace.

15 What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under [agrace](#note15a)? [bGod forbid](#note15b).

16 Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves [aservants](#note16a) to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye [bobey](#note16b); whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?

17 But God be thanked, that ye were the servants of sin, but ye have [aobeyed](#note17a) from the heart that form of doctrine which was delivered you.

18 Being then made [afree](#note18a) from [bsin](#note18b), ye became the servants of righteousness.

19 I speak after the manner of men because of the infirmity of your flesh: for as ye have yielded your members servants to uncleanness and to iniquity unto iniquity; even so now yield your members servants to righteousness unto holiness.

20 For when ye were the servants of sin, ye were [afree from](#note20a) righteousness.

21 What [afruit](#note21a) had ye then in those things whereof ye are now ashamed? for the end of those things is death.

22 But now being made [afree](#note22a) from sin, and become servants to God, ye have your [bfruit](#note22b) unto [choliness](#note22c), and the end everlasting life.

23 For the [awages](#note23a) of [bsin](#note23b) is [cdeath](#note23c); but the [dgift](#note23d) of God is [eeternal life](#note23e) through Jesus Christ our Lord.

Chapter 7

The law of Moses is fulfilled in Christ—Paul delights in the law of God after the inward man.

1 Know ye not, brethren, (for I speak to them that know the law,) how that the [alaw](#note1a) hath dominion over a man as long as he liveth?

2 For the [awoman](#note2a) which hath an [bhusband](#note2b) is bound by the law to her husband so long as he liveth; but if the husband be dead, she is loosed from the law of her husband.

3 So then if, while her husband liveth, she be married to another man, she shall be called an adulteress: but if her husband be dead, she is free from that law; so that she is no adulteress, though she be married to another man.

4 Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become [adead](#note4a) to the [blaw](#note4b) by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God.

5 [aFor](#note5a) when we were in the flesh, the [bmotions of sins](#note5b), which were by the law, did work in our members to bring forth fruit unto [cdeath](#note5c).

6 But now we are [adelivered from](#note6a) the law, that being [bdead](#note6b) wherein we were held; that we should serve in newness of [cspirit](#note6c), and not in the oldness of the letter.

7 What shall we say then? Is the law sin? [aGod forbid](#note7a). Nay, I had not known sin, but by the [blaw](#note7b): for I had not known [clust](#note7c), except the law had said, Thou shalt not [dcovet](#note7d).

8 But sin, taking occasion by the commandment, wrought in me all manner of concupiscence. For without the [alaw](#note8a) sin was dead.

9 For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died.

10 And the commandment, which was ordained to life, I found to be unto [adeath](#note10a).

11 For sin, [ataking occasion](#note11a) by the commandment, deceived me, and by it slew me.

12 Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good.

13 Was then that which is good made death unto me? [aGod forbid](#note13a). But sin, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful.

14 For we know that the [alaw](#note14a) is spiritual: but I am [bcarnal](#note14b), [csold under](#note14c) sin.

15 [aFor](#note15a) [bthat which I do](#note15b) I [callow](#note15c) not: for what I [dwould](#note15d), that do I not; but what I hate, that do I.

16 If then I do that which I [awould](#note16a) not, I consent unto the law that it is good.

17 Now then it is no more I that [ado](#note17a) it, but sin that dwelleth in me.

18 For I know that in me (that is, in my [aflesh](#note18a),) [bdwelleth](#note18b) no good thing: for to will is present with me; but how to [cperform](#note18c) that which is good I find not.

19 For the [agood](#note19a) that I [bwould](#note19b) I do not: but the evil which I would not, that I do.

20 Now if I do [athat I would not](#note20a), it is no more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me.

21 I find then a law, that, when I would do good, evil is [apresent](#note21a) with me.

22 For I [adelight](#note22a) in the law of God after the inward man:

23 But I see another law in my members, [awarring](#note23a) against the law of my mind, and bringing me into [bcaptivity](#note23b) to the law of sin which is in my members.

24 O [awretched](#note24a) man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death?

25 I thank God through Jesus Christ our Lord. So then with the mind I myself serve the law of God; but with the flesh the law of sin.

Chapter 8

The law of Christ brings life and peace—Those adopted as children of God become joint heirs with Christ—God’s elect are foreordained to eternal life—Christ makes intercession for man.

1 There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who [awalk](#note1a) not after the [bflesh](#note1b), but after the [cSpirit](#note1c).

2 For the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus hath made me [afree](#note2a) from the law of sin and death.

3 For what the law could not do, in that it was [aweak](#note3a) through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful [bflesh](#note3b), and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh:

4 That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the [aSpirit](#note4a).

5 For they that are after the flesh do [amind](#note5a) the things of the flesh; but they that are after the Spirit the things of the Spirit.

6 For to be [acarnally](#note6a) minded is [bdeath](#note6b); but to be [cspiritually](#note6c) minded is life and [dpeace](#note6d).

7 Because the [acarnal](#note7a) mind is [benmity](#note7b) against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be.

8 So then they that are [ain the flesh](#note8a) cannot please God.

9 [aBut](#note9a) ye are not in the flesh, but in the Spirit, if so be that the [bSpirit](#note9b) of God [cdwell](#note9c) in you. Now if any man have not the [dSpirit of Christ](#note9d), he is none of his.

10 And if [aChrist](#note10a) be in you, [bthe](#note10b) body is dead because of sin; but the Spirit is life because of [crighteousness](#note10c).

11 But if the Spirit of him that raised up Jesus from the dead dwell in you, he that raised up Christ from the dead shall also [aquicken](#note11a) your [bmortal](#note11b) bodies by his Spirit that dwelleth in you.

12 Therefore, brethren, we are debtors, not to the flesh, to live after the flesh.

13 For if ye live after the flesh, ye [ashall die](#note13a): but if ye through the Spirit do [bmortify](#note13b) the deeds of the body, ye shall live.

14 For as many as are [aled](#note14a) by the [bSpirit](#note14b) of God, they are the [csons](#note14c) of God.

15 For ye have not received the spirit of [abondage](#note15a) again to fear; but ye have received the Spirit of [badoption](#note15b), whereby we cry, Abba, Father.

16 The Spirit itself beareth [awitness](#note16a) with our [bspirit](#note16b), that we are the [cchildren](#note16c) of God:

17 And if children, then heirs; [aheirs](#note17a) of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we [bsuffer](#note17b) with him, that we may be also glorified together.

18 For I reckon that the [asufferings](#note18a) of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the [bglory](#note18b) which shall be revealed [cin us](#note18c).

19 For the [aearnest expectation](#note19a) of the [bcreature](#note19b) waiteth for the [cmanifestation](#note19c) of the sons of God.

20 For the creature was made subject to [avanity](#note20a), not willingly, but by reason of him who hath subjected the same in hope,

21 Because the [acreature](#note21a) itself also shall be [bdelivered](#note21b) from the bondage of [ccorruption](#note21c) into the glorious [dliberty](#note21d) of the children of God.

22 For we know that the whole creation [agroaneth](#note22a) and travaileth in pain together until now.

23 And not only they, but ourselves also, [awhich](#note23a) have the [bfirstfruits](#note23b) of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting for the [cadoption](#note23c), to wit, the redemption of our body.

24 For we are saved by [ahope](#note24a): but hope that is [bseen](#note24b) is not hope: for what a man seeth, why doth he yet hope for?

25 But if we hope for that we see not, then do we with patience wait for it.

26 Likewise the Spirit also [ahelpeth](#note26a) our infirmities: for we know not what we should [bpray](#note26b) for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh [cintercession](#note26c) for us with [dgroanings](#note26d) which cannot be uttered.

27 And he that [asearcheth](#note27a) the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh [bintercession](#note27b) for the saints according to the will of God.

28 And we know that all things work together for [agood](#note28a) to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose.

29 [aFor](#note29a) whom he did [bforeknow](#note29b), he also [cdid predestinate](#note29c) [dto be conformed to](#note29d) the [eimage](#note29e) of his Son, that he might be the [ffirstborn](#note29f) among many brethren.

30 Moreover whom he [adid predestinate](#note30a), them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.

31 What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us, who can [abe against us](#note31a)?

32 He that spared not his own [aSon](#note32a), but [bdelivered](#note32b) him up for us all, how shall he not with him also freely give us [call](#note32c) things?

33 Who shall [alay](#note33a) any thing to the charge of God’s elect? It is God that justifieth.

34 Who is he that condemneth? It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh [aintercession](#note34a) for us.

35 Who shall separate us from the [alove](#note35a) of Christ? shall [btribulation](#note35b), or distress, or [cpersecution](#note35c), or famine, or nakedness, or peril, or sword?

36 As it is written, For thy sake we are [akilled](#note36a) all the day long; we are accounted as [bsheep](#note36b) for the slaughter.

37 Nay, in all these things we are [amore](#note37a) than [bconquerors](#note37b) through him that loved us.

38 For I am persuaded, that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come,

39 Nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature, shall be able to [aseparate](#note39a) us from the [blove](#note39b) of God, which is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Chapter 9

Paul explains how the law of election (foreordination) operates—The people of Israel are chosen (foreordained) to receive the adoption, covenants, promises, and blessings of the gospel; yet they are not all Israel who are of Israel—They must seek their blessings by faith—The Gentiles also attain to righteousness and salvation by faith.

1 I say the truth in Christ, I [alie](#note1a) not, my [bconscience](#note1b) also bearing me witness in the Holy Ghost,

2 That I have great heaviness and continual sorrow in my heart.

3 [aFor](#note3a) I could wish that myself were [baccursed](#note3b) from Christ for my brethren, my kinsmen according to the flesh:

4 Who are Israelites; to whom pertaineth the [aadoption](#note4a), and the glory, and the [bcovenants](#note4b), and the giving of the law, and the service of God, and the promises;

5 Whose are the fathers, and of whom as concerning the [aflesh](#note5a) Christ came, who is over all, God blessed for ever. Amen.

6 Not as though the word of God [ahath taken none effect](#note6a). For they are not all Israel, which are of [bIsrael](#note6b):

7 [aNeither](#note7a), because they are the seed of Abraham, are they all children: but, In [bIsaac](#note7b) shall thy seed be called.

8 That is, They which are the children of the flesh, these are not the [achildren](#note8a) of God: but the children of the promise are counted for the seed.

9 For this is the word of promise, At this time will I come, and Sara shall have a [ason](#note9a).

10 And not only this; but when [aRebecca](#note10a) also had conceived by one, even by our father Isaac;

11 (For the children being not yet born, neither having done any good or evil, that the purpose of God according to [aelection](#note11a) might stand, not of works, but of him that calleth;)

12 It was said unto her, The [aelder](#note12a) shall serve the younger.

13 As it is written, Jacob have I [aloved](#note13a), but Esau have I hated.

14 What shall we say then? Is there [aunrighteousness](#note14a) with God? [bGod forbid](#note14b).

15 For he saith to Moses, I will have mercy on whom I will have mercy, and I will have [acompassion](#note15a) on whom I will have compassion.

16 So then it is not of him that willeth, nor of him that runneth, but of God that sheweth [amercy](#note16a).

17 For the scripture saith unto Pharaoh, Even for this same [apurpose](#note17a) have I raised thee up, that I might shew my power in thee, and that my name might be declared throughout all the earth.

18 Therefore hath he [amercy](#note18a) on whom he will have mercy, and whom he will he [bhardeneth](#note18b).

19 Thou wilt say then unto me, Why doth he yet find fault? For who hath resisted his will?

20 Nay but, O man, who art thou that [arepliest against](#note20a) God? Shall the thing formed say to him that formed it, Why hast thou [bmade](#note20b) me thus?

21 Hath not the [apotter](#note21a) power over the clay, of the same lump to make one vessel unto honour, and another unto dishonour?

22 What if God, willing to shew his wrath, and to make his power known, endured with much longsuffering the vessels of wrath fitted to [adestruction](#note22a):

23 And that he might make known the [ariches](#note23a) of his [bglory](#note23b) on the vessels of mercy, which he had afore prepared unto glory,

24 Even us, whom he hath called, not of the Jews only, but also of the Gentiles?

25 As he saith also in [aOsee](#note25a), I will call them my people, which were not my [bpeople](#note25b); and her beloved, which was not beloved.

26 And it shall come to pass, that in the place where it was said unto them, Ye are not my people; there shall they be called the [achildren](#note26a) of the living God.

27 [aEsaias](#note27a) also crieth concerning Israel, Though the [bnumber](#note27b) of the children of [cIsrael](#note27c) be as the sand of the sea, a [dremnant](#note27d) shall be saved:

28 For he will finish the work, and cut it short in [arighteousness](#note28a): because a short work will the Lord make upon the earth.

29 And as Esaias said before, Except the Lord of [aSabaoth](#note29a) had left us a [bseed](#note29b), we had been as Sodoma, and been made like unto Gomorrha.

30 What shall we say then? That the Gentiles, which followed not after righteousness, have attained to righteousness, even the [arighteousness](#note30a) which is of faith.

31 But [aIsrael](#note31a), which followed after the law of righteousness, hath not attained to the law of righteousness.

32 [aWherefore](#note32a)? Because they sought it not by faith, but as it were by the works of the law. For they [bstumbled](#note32b) at that stumblingstone;

33 As it is written, Behold, I lay in Sion a [astumblingstone](#note33a) and [brock](#note33b) of [coffence](#note33c): and whosoever believeth on him shall not be ashamed.

Chapter 10

Salvation comes through righteousness to those who believe in Christ—Faith comes by hearing the gospel taught by legal administrators sent of God.

1 Brethren, my heart’s desire and prayer to God for Israel is, that they might be [asaved](#note1a).

2 For I bear them record that they have a [azeal](#note2a) of God, but not according to knowledge.

3 For they being ignorant of God’s [arighteousness](#note3a), and [bgoing about](#note3b) to establish their own righteousness, have not [csubmitted](#note3c) themselves unto the righteousness of God.

4 For Christ is the [aend](#note4a) of the law for righteousness to every one that believeth.

5 For Moses describeth the righteousness which is of the law, That the man which doeth those things shall live by them.

6 But the [arighteousness](#note6a) which is of faith speaketh on this wise, Say not in thine heart, Who shall ascend into heaven? (that is, to bring Christ down from above:)

7 Or, Who shall descend into the deep? (that is, to bring up Christ again from the dead.)

8 But what saith it? The word is nigh thee, even in thy mouth, and in thy heart: that is, the word of faith, which we preach;

9 That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved.

10 For with the [aheart](#note10a) man [bbelieveth](#note10b) unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto [csalvation](#note10c).

11 For the scripture saith, Whosoever believeth on him shall not be ashamed.

12 For there is no [adifference](#note12a) between the Jew and the Greek: for the same Lord over all is [brich](#note12b) unto all that [ccall](#note12c) upon him.

13 For whosoever shall [acall](#note13a) upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.

14 How then shall they call on him in whom they have not [abelieved](#note14a)? and how shall they believe in him of whom they have not heard? and how shall they hear without a [bpreacher](#note14b)?

15 And how shall they [apreach](#note15a), except they be [bsent](#note15b)? as it is written, How beautiful are the [cfeet](#note15c) of them that preach the [dgospel](#note15d) of [epeace](#note15e), and bring glad tidings of good things!

16 But they have not all [aobeyed](#note16a) the gospel. For Esaias saith, Lord, who hath [bbelieved](#note16b) our report?

17 So then [afaith](#note17a) cometh by [bhearing](#note17b), and hearing by the word of God.

18 But I say, Have they not heard? Yes verily, their sound went into all the earth, and their [awords](#note18a) unto the ends of the [bworld](#note18b).

19 But I say, Did not Israel know? First Moses saith, I will provoke you to [ajealousy](#note19a) by them that are no people, and by a foolish nation I will anger you.

20 But [aEsaias](#note20a) is very bold, and saith, I was found of them that [bsought](#note20b) me not; I was made manifest unto them that asked not after me.

21 But to Israel he saith, All day long I have stretched forth my hands unto a [adisobedient](#note21a) and [bgainsaying](#note21b) people.

Chapter 11

Israel was chosen (foreordained) according to the election of grace—But some harden their hearts against it—The Gentiles are adopted into the house of Israel—The gospel goes preferentially to the Gentiles until the fulness of the Gentiles.

1 I say then, Hath God cast away his people? [aGod forbid](#note1a). For I also am an [bIsraelite](#note1b), of the seed of Abraham, of the tribe of Benjamin.

2 God hath not cast away his [apeople](#note2a) which he [bforeknew](#note2b). [cWot](#note2c) ye not what the scripture saith of [dElias](#note2d)? how he maketh intercession to God against Israel, saying,

3 Lord, they have [akilled](#note3a) thy prophets, and digged down thine altars; and I am [bleft](#note3b) alone, and they seek my life.

4 But what saith the answer of God unto him? I have [areserved](#note4a) to myself seven thousand men, who have not bowed the knee to the image of Baal.

5 Even so then at this present time also there is a remnant according to the [aelection](#note5a) of grace.

6 And if by [agrace](#note6a), then is it no more of [bworks](#note6b): otherwise grace is no more grace. But if it be of works, then is it no more grace: otherwise work is no more work.

7 What then? Israel hath not obtained that which he seeketh for; but the election hath obtained it, and the rest [awere blinded](#note7a)

8 (According as it is written, God hath given them the spirit of [aslumber](#note8a), eyes that they should not see, and ears that they should not [bhear](#note8b);) unto this day.

9 And David saith, Let their table be made a [asnare](#note9a), and a trap, and a stumblingblock, and a [brecompence](#note9b) unto them:

10 Let their eyes be darkened, that they may not see, and bow down their back alway.

11 I say then, Have they stumbled that they should fall? [aGod forbid](#note11a): but rather through their fall salvation is come unto the Gentiles, for to provoke them to [bjealousy](#note11b).

12 Now if the fall of them be the riches of the world, and the [adiminishing](#note12a) of them the riches of the Gentiles; how much more their fulness?

13 For I speak to you [aGentiles](#note13a), inasmuch as I am the apostle of the Gentiles, I [bmagnify](#note13b) mine office:

14 If by any means I may provoke to emulation them which are my flesh, and might save some of them.

15 For if the casting away of them be the [areconciling](#note15a) of the world, what shall the receiving of them be, but life from the dead?

16 For if the firstfruit be holy, the lump is also holy: and if the [aroot](#note16a) be holy, so are the branches.

17 And if some of the branches be broken off, and thou, being a wild olive [atree](#note17a), wert grafted in among them, and with them partakest of the root and fatness of the olive tree;

18 [aBoast](#note18a) not against the branches. But if thou boast, thou bearest not the root, but the root thee.

19 Thou wilt say then, The branches were broken off, that I might be grafted in.

20 Well; because of [aunbelief](#note20a) they were [bbroken](#note20b) off, and thou standest by [cfaith](#note20c). Be not highminded, but [dfear](#note20d):

21 For if God spared not the [anatural](#note21a) branches, take heed lest he also [bspare](#note21b) not thee.

22 Behold therefore the goodness and severity of God: on them which fell, severity; but toward thee, goodness, if thou continue in his goodness: otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.

23 And they also, if they abide not still in unbelief, shall be grafted in: for God is able to graft them in again.

24 For if thou wert cut out of the olive tree which is wild by nature, and wert grafted contrary to nature into a good olive tree: how much more shall these, which be the natural branches, be grafted into their own olive tree?

25 For I would not, brethren, that ye should be ignorant of this mystery, lest ye should be wise in your own [aconceits](#note25a); that [bblindness](#note25b) in part is happened to Israel, until the [cfulness](#note25c) of the [dGentiles](#note25d) be come in.

26 And so all [aIsrael](#note26a) shall be saved: as it is written, There shall come out of [bSion](#note26b) the [cDeliverer](#note26c), and shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob:

27 For this is my [acovenant](#note27a) unto them, when I shall take away their sins.

28 As concerning the gospel, they are enemies for your sakes: but as touching the election, they are beloved for the fathers’ sakes.

29 For the gifts and calling of God are without repentance.

30 For as ye in times past [ahave not believed](#note30a) God, yet have now obtained mercy through their [bunbelief](#note30b):

31 Even so have these also now not [abelieved](#note31a), that through your mercy they also may obtain mercy.

32 For God hath [aconcluded](#note32a) them all in unbelief, that he might have mercy upon all.

33 O the depth of the [ariches](#note33a) both of the [bwisdom](#note33b) and [cknowledge](#note33c) of God! how [dunsearchable](#note33d) are his judgments, and his ways past [efinding](#note33e) out!

34 For who hath known the [amind](#note34a) of the Lord? or who hath been his [bcounsellor](#note34b)?

35 Or who hath first given to him, and it shall be recompensed unto him again?

36 For of him, and [athrough](#note36a) him, and to him, are all things: to whom be glory for ever. Amen.

Avoid quarreling about opinions and making unrighteous judgment of each other—Every knee will bow to Christ—The kingdom of God embraces righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost.

3 Let not him that eateth despise him that eateth not; and let not him which eateth not [ajudge](#note3a) him that eateth: for God hath received him.

4 Who art thou that [ajudgest](#note4a) another man’s servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall be holden up: for God is able to make him stand.

5 One man esteemeth one [aday](#note5a) above another: another esteemeth every day alike. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind.

6 He that regardeth the day, regardeth it unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard it. He that eateth, eateth to the Lord, for he giveth God thanks; and he that eateth not, to the Lord he eateth not, and giveth God thanks.

7 For none of us [aliveth](#note7a) to himself, and no man dieth to himself.

8 For whether we live, we live unto the Lord; and whether we die, we [adie](#note8a) unto the Lord: whether we live therefore, or die, we are the Lord’s.

9 For to this end Christ both died, and rose, and revived, that he might be [aLord](#note9a) both of the [bdead](#note9b) and living.

10 But why dost thou [ajudge](#note10a) thy brother? or why dost thou set at nought thy brother? for we shall all stand before the [bjudgment](#note10b) seat of Christ.

11 For it is written, As I live, saith the Lord, every [aknee](#note11a) shall bow to me, and every tongue shall [bconfess](#note11b) to God.

12 So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God.

13 Let us not therefore judge one another any more: but judge this rather, that no man put a [astumblingblock](#note13a) or an occasion to fall in his brother’s way.

14 I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing [aunclean](#note14a) of itself: but to him that [besteemeth](#note14b) any thing to be unclean, to him it is unclean.

15 But if thy brother be grieved with thy meat, [anow](#note15a) walkest thou not [bcharitably](#note15b). Destroy not him [cwith thy meat](#note15c), for whom Christ died.

16 Let not then your good be evil spoken of:

17 For the kingdom of God is not [ameat](#note17a) and drink; but [brighteousness](#note17b), and [cpeace](#note17c), and [djoy](#note17d) in the Holy Ghost.

18 For he that in these things serveth Christ is acceptable to God, and approved of men.

20 [aFor meat](#note20a) destroy not the [bwork](#note20b) of God. All things indeed are pure; but it is evil for that man who eateth with offence.

21 It is good neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor any thing whereby thy brother stumbleth, or is [aoffended](#note21a), or is made weak.

22 Hast thou faith? have it to thyself before God. Happy is he that condemneth not himself [ain](#note22a) that thing which he alloweth.

23 And he that [adoubteth](#note23a) is damned if he eat, because he eateth not of faith: for whatsoever is not of [bfaith](#note23b) is [csin](#note23c).

17 I have therefore whereof I may [aglory](#note17a) through Jesus Christ in those things which pertain to God.

Chapter 16

Paul sends greetings to various Saints—He counsels the Saints to avoid those who cause divisions—The Saints should be wise concerning good and innocent concerning evil.

1 I commend unto you Phebe our sister, which is a servant of the church which is at Cenchrea:

2 That ye receive her in the Lord, as becometh saints, and that ye assist her in whatsoever business she hath need of you: for she hath been a succourer of many, and of myself also.

3 Greet Priscilla and Aquila my helpers in Christ Jesus:

4 Who have for my life laid down their own necks: unto whom not only I give thanks, but also all the churches of the Gentiles.

5 Likewise greet the church that is in their house. Salute my wellbeloved Epænetus, who is the firstfruits of Achaia unto Christ.

6 Greet Mary, who bestowed much labour on us.

7 Salute Andronicus and Junia, my kinsmen, and my fellowprisoners, who are of note among the apostles, who also were in Christ before me.

8 Greet Amplias my beloved in the Lord.

9 Salute Urbane, our helper in Christ, and Stachys my beloved.

10 Salute Apelles approved in Christ. Salute them which are of Aristobulus’ [ahousehold](#note10a).

11 Salute Herodion my kinsman. Greet them that be of the [ahousehold](#note11a) of Narcissus, which are in the Lord.

12 Salute Tryphena and Tryphosa, who labour in the Lord. Salute the beloved Persis, which laboured much in the Lord.

13 Salute Rufus chosen in the Lord, and his mother and mine.

14 Salute Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermas, Patrobas, Hermes, and the brethren which are with them.

15 Salute Philologus, and Julia, Nereus, and his sister, and Olympas, and all the saints which are with them.

16 Salute one another with an holy [akiss](#note16a). The [bchurches](#note16b) of Christ salute you.

17 Now I beseech you, brethren, [amark](#note17a) them which cause [bdivisions](#note17b) and [coffences](#note17c) contrary to the [ddoctrine](#note17d) which ye have learned; and [eavoid](#note17e) them.

18 For they that are such serve not our Lord Jesus Christ, but their own belly; and by good words and [afair](#note18a) speeches deceive the hearts of the [bsimple](#note18b).

19 For your [aobedience](#note19a) is come abroad unto all men. I am glad therefore on your behalf: but yet I would have you [bwise](#note19b) unto that which is good, and [csimple](#note19c) concerning evil.

20 And the God of [apeace](#note20a) shall [bbruise](#note20b) [cSatan](#note20c) under your feet shortly. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you. Amen.

21 Timotheus my workfellow, and Lucius, and Jason, and Sosipater, my kinsmen, salute you.

22 I Tertius, who wrote this epistle, salute you in the Lord.

23 Gaius mine host, and of the whole church, saluteth you. Erastus the chamberlain of the city saluteth you, and Quartus a brother.

24 The [agrace](#note24a) of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.

25 Now to him that is of power to [astablish](#note25a) you according to my gospel, and the preaching of Jesus Christ, according to the revelation of the [bmystery](#note25b), which was kept secret since the world began,

26 But now is made manifest, and by the scriptures of the prophets, according to the commandment of the [aeverlasting](#note26a) God, made known to all nations for the [bobedience](#note26b) of faith:

27 To God only wise, be glory through Jesus Christ for ever. Amen.

¶ Written to the Romans from Corinthus, and sent by Phebe servant of the church at Cenchrea.
