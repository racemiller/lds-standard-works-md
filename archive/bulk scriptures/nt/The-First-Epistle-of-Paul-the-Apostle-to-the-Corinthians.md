Chapter 1

True Saints are perfectly united in the same mind and in the same judgment—Preach the gospel and save souls—The gospel is preached by the weak and the simple.

1 Paul, called to be an apostle of Jesus Christ through the will of God, and Sosthenes our brother,

2 Unto the church of God which is at Corinth, to them that are [asanctified](#note2a) in Christ Jesus, called to be [bsaints](#note2b), with all that in every place call upon the name of Jesus Christ our Lord, both theirs and ours:

3 Grace be unto you, and [apeace](#note3a), from God our Father, and from the Lord Jesus Christ.

4 I thank my God always on your behalf, for the grace of God which is given you by Jesus Christ;

5 That in every thing ye are enriched by him, in all utterance, and in all knowledge;

6 Even as the [atestimony](#note6a) of Christ was [bconfirmed](#note6b) in you:

7 So that ye [acome behind in](#note7a) no gift; waiting for the [bcoming](#note7b) of our Lord Jesus Christ:

8 Who shall also [aconfirm](#note8a) you unto the end, that ye may be [bblameless](#note8b) in the day of our Lord Jesus Christ.

9 God is faithful, by whom ye were called unto the [afellowship](#note9a) of his Son Jesus Christ our Lord.

10 Now I beseech you, brethren, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that ye all speak the same thing, and that there be no [adivisions](#note10a) among you; but that ye be perfectly joined together in the same [bmind](#note10b) and in the same judgment.

11 For it hath been declared unto me [aof](#note11a) you, my brethren, by them which are of the house of Chloe, that there are [bcontentions](#note11b) among you.

12 Now this I say, that every one of you saith, I am of Paul; and I of Apollos; and I of Cephas; and I of [aChrist](#note12a).

13 Is Christ [adivided](#note13a)? was Paul crucified for you? or were ye baptized in the name of Paul?

14 I thank God that I baptized none of you, [abut](#note14a) [bCrispus](#note14b) and Gaius;

15 Lest any should say that I had baptized in mine own name.

16 And I baptized also the household of [aStephanas](#note16a): besides, I know not whether I baptized any other.

17 For Christ sent me not to baptize, but to preach the gospel: not with [awisdom](#note17a) of words, lest the cross of Christ should be made of none effect.

18 For the [apreaching](#note18a) of the cross is to them that perish foolishness; but unto us which are saved it is the [bpower](#note18b) of God.

19 For it is written, I will destroy the wisdom of the wise, and will bring to nothing the [aunderstanding](#note19a) of the [bprudent](#note19b).

20 Where is the wise? where is the [ascribe](#note20a)? where is the [bdisputer](#note20b) of this [cworld](#note20c)? hath not God made foolish the [dwisdom](#note20d) of this [eworld](#note20e)?

21 For after that in the wisdom of God the world [aby](#note21a) wisdom knew not God, it pleased God by the foolishness of [bpreaching](#note21b) to save them that believe.

22 For the Jews require a [asign](#note22a), and the Greeks seek after wisdom:

23 But we [apreach](#note23a) Christ [bcrucified](#note23b), unto the Jews a [cstumblingblock](#note23c), and unto the Greeks foolishness;

24 But unto them [awhich are called](#note24a), both Jews and Greeks, Christ the [bpower](#note24b) of God, and the [cwisdom](#note24c) of God.

25 Because the [afoolishness](#note25a) of God is wiser than men; and the weakness of God is stronger than men.

26 For ye see your calling, brethren, how that not many wise men after the [aflesh](#note26a), not many mighty, not many noble, are [bcalled](#note26b):

27 But God hath chosen the [afoolish](#note27a) things of the world to [bconfound](#note27b) the wise; and God hath chosen the [cweak](#note27c) things of the world to confound the things which are mighty;

28 And base things of the world, and things which are despised, hath God chosen, yea, and things which are not, to bring to nought things that are:

29 That no flesh should glory in his presence.

30 But of him are ye in Christ Jesus, who of God is made unto us wisdom, and righteousness, and [asanctification](#note30a), and redemption:

31 That, according as it is written, He that glorieth, let him [aglory](#note31a) in the Lord.

Chapter 2

The gospel is preached by the power of the Spirit—The Spirit reveals all things to the Saints—The unrepentant natural man cannot receive the things of the Spirit of God.

1 And I, brethren, when I came to you, came not with excellency of speech or of wisdom, declaring unto you the [atestimony](#note1a) of God.

2 For I determined not to know any thing among you, [asave](#note2a) Jesus Christ, and him [bcrucified](#note2b).

3 And I was with you in [aweakness](#note3a), and in fear, and in much trembling.

4 And my [aspeech](#note4a) and my preaching was not with [benticing](#note4b) words of man’s wisdom, but in demonstration of the [cSpirit](#note4c) and of [dpower](#note4d):

5 That your faith should not stand in the wisdom of men, but in the [apower](#note5a) of God.

6 Howbeit we speak wisdom among them that are [aperfect](#note6a): yet not the wisdom of this world, nor of the princes of this world, that come to nought:

7 But we speak the [awisdom](#note7a) of God in a [bmystery](#note7b), even the hidden [cwisdom](#note7c), which God [dordained](#note7d) before the world unto our glory:

8 Which none of the princes of this world knew: for had they known it, they would not have [acrucified](#note8a) the Lord of glory.

9 But as it is written, [aEye](#note9a) hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which God hath [bprepared](#note9b) for them that love him.

10 But God hath [arevealed](#note10a) them unto us by his [bSpirit](#note10b): for the [cSpirit](#note10c) [dsearcheth](#note10d) all things, yea, the deep things of God.

11 For what man [aknoweth](#note11a) the things of a man, save the spirit of man which is in him? even so the things of God [bknoweth](#note11b) no man, [cbut](#note11c) the [dSpirit](#note11d) of God.

12 Now we have received, not the [aspirit](#note12a) of the world, but the spirit which is of God; that we might know the things that are freely given to us of God.

13 Which things also we speak, not in the words which man’s [awisdom](#note13a) teacheth, but which the Holy Ghost [bteacheth](#note13b); comparing spiritual things with spiritual.

14 But the [anatural man](#note14a) [breceiveth](#note14b) not the things of the [cSpirit](#note14c) of God: for they are [dfoolishness](#note14d) unto him: neither can he [eknow](#note14e) them, because they are [fspiritually](#note14f) [gdiscerned](#note14g).

15 But he that is spiritual [ajudgeth](#note15a) all things, yet he himself is judged of no man.

16 For who hath known the mind of the Lord, that he may [ainstruct](#note16a) him? But we have the [bmind](#note16b) of Christ.

Chapter 3

Milk comes before meat in the Church—Men’s works will be tried by fire—The Saints are the temple of God, and if they are faithful, they will inherit all things.

1 And I, brethren, could not speak unto you as unto spiritual, but as unto carnal, even as unto babes in Christ.

2 I have fed you with [amilk](#note2a), and not with meat: for hitherto ye were not able to bear it, neither yet now are ye able.

3 For ye are yet [acarnal](#note3a): for whereas there is among you [benvying](#note3b), and [cstrife](#note3c), and [ddivisions](#note3d), are ye not carnal, and walk as men?

4 For while one saith, I am of Paul; and another, I am of Apollos; are ye not carnal?

5 Who then is Paul, and who is Apollos, but [aministers](#note5a) by whom ye believed, even as the Lord gave to every man?

6 I have [aplanted](#note6a), Apollos watered; but God [bgave](#note6b) the [cincrease](#note6c).

7 So then neither is he that planteth any thing, neither he that watereth; but God that giveth the increase.

8 Now he that planteth and he that watereth are one: and every man shall receive his own [areward](#note8a) according to his own labour.

9 For we are [alabourers](#note9a) together with God: ye are God’s [bhusbandry](#note9b), ye are God’s building.

10 According to the [agrace](#note10a) of God which is given unto me, as a wise masterbuilder, I have laid the foundation, and another buildeth thereon. But let every man take heed how he buildeth thereupon.

11 For other [afoundation](#note11a) can no man lay than that is laid, which is Jesus Christ.

12 Now if any man build upon this foundation gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, stubble;

13 Every man’s work shall be made manifest: for the day shall declare it, because it shall be revealed by fire; and the [afire](#note13a) shall [btry](#note13b) every man’s work of what sort it is.

14 If any man’s work abide which he hath built thereupon, he shall receive a reward.

15 If any man’s work shall be burned, he shall suffer loss: but he himself [ashall](#note15a) be saved; yet so as by fire.

16 Know ye not that ye are the [atemple](#note16a) of God, and that the [bSpirit](#note16b) of God dwelleth [cin](#note16c) you?

17 If any man [adefile](#note17a) the temple of God, him shall God destroy; for the temple of God is [bholy](#note17b), which temple ye are.

18 Let no man deceive himself. If any man among you [aseemeth](#note18a) to be wise in this world, let him become a [bfool](#note18b), that he may be [cwise](#note18c).

19 For the [awisdom](#note19a) of this [bworld](#note19b) is [cfoolishness](#note19c) with God. For it is written, He taketh the wise in their own [dcraftiness](#note19d).

20 And again, The Lord [aknoweth](#note20a) the thoughts of the wise, that they are [bvain](#note20b).

21 Therefore let no man [aglory](#note21a) in men. For all things are yours;

22 Whether [aPaul](#note22a), or Apollos, or Cephas, or the world, or life, or death, or things present, or things to come; [ball](#note22b) are yours;

23 And ye are [aChrist’s](#note23a); and Christ is God’s.

Chapter 4

Christ’s ministers must be faithful—The Apostles suffer, minister, and keep the faith—The kingdom of God is not in word but in power.

1 Let a man so account of us, as of the ministers of Christ, and stewards of the [amysteries](#note1a) of God.

2 Moreover it is required in [astewards](#note2a), that a man be found [bfaithful](#note2b).

3 But with me it is a very small thing that I should be judged of you, or of man’s judgment: yea, I judge not mine own self.

4 For I know nothing [aby myself](#note4a); yet am I not hereby justified: but he that [bjudgeth](#note4b) me is the Lord.

5 Therefore judge nothing before the time, until the Lord come, who both will bring to light the [ahidden things](#note5a) of darkness, and will make [bmanifest](#note5b) the counsels of the hearts: and then shall every man have praise of God.

Chapter 6

Church members should not fight one another in the courts—The unrighteous will not be saved—True Saints are the temple of the Holy Ghost.

1 Dare any of you, having a [amatter](#note1a) against another, go to [blaw](#note1b) before the unjust, and not before the saints?

2 Do ye not know that the saints shall [ajudge](#note2a) the [bworld](#note2b)? and if the world shall be judged by you, are ye unworthy to judge the smallest matters?

3 Know ye not that we shall judge angels? how much more things that pertain to this life?

4 If then ye have judgments of things pertaining to this life, set them to judge who are least esteemed in the church.

5 I speak to your [ashame](#note5a). Is it so, that there is not a wise man among you? no, not one that shall be able to judge between his brethren?

6 But brother goeth to law with brother, and that before the unbelievers.

7 Now therefore there is utterly a fault among you, because ye go to law one with another. Why do ye not rather take [awrong](#note7a)? why do ye not rather [bsuffer](#note7b) yourselves to be [cdefrauded](#note7c)?

8 Nay, ye do wrong, and [adefraud](#note8a), and that your brethren.

9 Know ye not that the [aunrighteous](#note9a) shall not [binherit](#note9b) the kingdom of God? Be not deceived: neither [cfornicators](#note9c), nor idolaters, nor [dadulterers](#note9d), nor [eeffeminate](#note9e), nor [fabusers](#note9f) of themselves with mankind,

10 Nor [athieves](#note10a), nor covetous, nor [bdrunkards](#note10b), nor [crevilers](#note10c), nor [dextortioners](#note10d), shall inherit the kingdom of God.

11 And such were some of you: but ye are [awashed](#note11a), but ye are [bsanctified](#note11b), but ye are justified in the [cname](#note11c) of the Lord Jesus, and by the [dSpirit](#note11d) of our God.

12 [aAll](#note12a) things are lawful unto me, but all things are not [bexpedient](#note12b): all things are lawful for me, but I will not be brought under the power of any.

13 Meats for the belly, and the belly for meats: but God shall destroy both it and them. Now the body is not for [afornication](#note13a), but for the Lord; and the Lord for the body.

14 And God hath both raised up the Lord, and will also [araise up](#note14a) us by his own power.

15 Know ye not that your bodies are the [amembers](#note15a) of Christ? shall I then take the members of Christ, and make them the members of an harlot? God forbid.

16 What? know ye not that he which is joined to an harlot is one body? for two, saith he, shall be [aone](#note16a) flesh.

17 But he that is joined unto the Lord is [aone](#note17a) spirit.

18 Flee fornication. Every sin that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body.

19 What? know ye not that your [abody](#note19a) is the [btemple](#note19b) of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your [cown](#note19c)?

20 For ye are [abought](#note20a) with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God’s.

Chapter 7

Paul answers special questions about marriage among those called on missions—Paul praises self-discipline.

1 Now concerning the things whereof ye wrote unto me: [aIt is good](#note1a) for a man not to touch a woman.

2 Nevertheless, [ato avoid](#note2a) [bfornication](#note2b), let every man have his own [cwife](#note2c), and let every woman have her own husband.

3 Let the [ahusband](#note3a) render unto the [bwife](#note3b) due [cbenevolence](#note3c): and likewise also the wife unto the husband.

4 The wife hath not power of her own body, but the husband: and likewise also the husband hath not power of his own body, but the wife.

5 [aDefraud](#note5a) ye not one the other, except it be with consent for a time, that ye may give yourselves to fasting and prayer; and come together again, that [bSatan](#note5b) [ctempt](#note5c) you not for your incontinency.

6 But I speak this by permission, and not of commandment.

7 For I would that all men were even as I myself. But every man hath his [aproper](#note7a) [bgift](#note7b) of God, one after this manner, and another after that.

8 I say therefore to the unmarried and [awidows](#note8a), It is good for them if they abide even as I.

9 [aBut](#note9a) if they cannot [bcontain](#note9b), let them marry: for it is better to marry than to [cburn](#note9c).

10 And unto the married I command, yet not I, but the Lord, Let not the [awife](#note10a) [bdepart](#note10b) from her husband:

11 But and if she depart, let her remain unmarried, or be [areconciled](#note11a) to her husband: and let not the husband [bput away](#note11b) his wife.

12 But to the rest speak I, not the Lord: If any brother hath a wife that believeth not, and she be pleased to dwell with him, let him not put her away.

13 And the woman which hath an [ahusband](#note13a) that believeth not, and if he be pleased to dwell with her, let her not leave him.

14 [aFor](#note14a) the unbelieving [bhusband](#note14b) is [csanctified](#note14c) by the wife, and the unbelieving wife is [dsanctified](#note14d) by the husband: else were your children unclean; but now are they holy.

15 But if the unbelieving depart, let him depart. A brother or a sister is not under bondage in such cases: but God hath called us [ato](#note15a) peace.

16 For what knowest thou, O wife, whether thou shalt [asave](#note16a) thy husband? or how knowest thou, O man, whether thou shalt save thy wife?

17 But as [aGod](#note17a) hath distributed to every man, as the Lord hath called every one, so let him walk. And so [bordain](#note17b) I in all churches.

18 Is any man called being circumcised? let him not [abecome uncircumcised](#note18a). Is any called in uncircumcision? let him not be circumcised.

19 [aCircumcision](#note19a) is nothing, and uncircumcision is nothing, but the keeping of the commandments of God.

20 Let every man abide in the same calling wherein he was called.

21 Art thou called being a servant? care not for it: but if thou mayest be made free, use it rather.

22 For he that is called in the Lord, being a servant, is the Lord’s [afreeman](#note22a): likewise also he that is called, being free, is Christ’s [bservant](#note22b).

23 Ye are [abought](#note23a) with a price; be not ye the [bservants](#note23b) of men.

24 Brethren, let every man, wherein he is called, therein abide with God.

25 Now concerning virgins I have no commandment of the Lord: yet I give my judgment, as one that hath obtained [amercy](#note25a) of the Lord to be faithful.

26 I suppose therefore that this is good for the present [adistress](#note26a), I say, that it is good for a man so to be.

27 Art thou bound unto a wife? seek not to be loosed. Art thou loosed from a wife? seek not a wife.

28 But and if thou marry, thou hast not sinned; and if a virgin marry, she hath not sinned. Nevertheless such shall have trouble in the flesh: but I spare you.

29 [aBut](#note29a) this I say, brethren, the time is short: it remaineth, that both they that have wives be as though they had none;

30 And they that weep, as though they wept not; and they that rejoice, as though they rejoiced not; and they that buy, as though they possessed not;

31 And they that use this world, as not abusing it: for the [afashion](#note31a) of this [bworld](#note31b) passeth away.

32 But I would have you without [acarefulness](#note32a). He that is unmarried careth for the things that belong to the Lord, how he may please the Lord:

33 But he that is married careth for the things that are of the world, how he may please his wife.

34 There is difference also between a wife and a virgin. The unmarried woman careth for the things of the Lord, that she may be holy both in body and in spirit: but she that is married careth for the things of the world, how she may please her husband.

35 And this I speak for your own profit; not that I may cast a snare upon you, but for that which is comely, and that ye may attend upon the Lord without distraction.

36 But if any man think that he behaveth himself uncomely toward his virgin, if she pass the flower of her age, and need so require, let him do what he will, he sinneth not: let them marry.

37 Nevertheless he that standeth steadfast in his heart, having no necessity, but hath power over his own will, and hath so decreed in his heart that he will keep his virgin, doeth well.

38 [aSo](#note38a) then he that giveth her in marriage doeth well; but he that giveth her not in marriage doeth better.

39 The [awife](#note39a) is bound by the [blaw](#note39b) as long as her husband liveth; but if her husband be dead, she is at liberty to be married to whom she will; only in the Lord.

40 But she is happier if she so abide, after my judgment: and I think also that I have the Spirit of God.

Chapter 8

There are many gods and many lords—To us there is one God (the Father) and one Lord, who is Christ.

1 Now [aas touching](#note1a) things offered unto [bidols](#note1b), we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge puffeth up, but [ccharity](#note1c) [dedifieth](#note1d).

2 And if any man think that he knoweth any thing, he knoweth nothing yet as he ought to [aknow](#note2a).

3 But if any man love God, the same is [aknown](#note3a) of him.

4 As concerning therefore the eating of those things [athat](#note4a) are offered in sacrifice unto [bidols](#note4b), we know that an [cidol](#note4c) is nothing in the world, and that there is none other God but one.

5 For though there be that are called gods, whether in heaven or in earth, (as there be gods many, and lords many,)

6 But to us there is but one [aGod](#note6a), the [bFather](#note6b), of whom are all things, and we in him; and one [cLord Jesus Christ](#note6c), by whom are [dall](#note6d) things, and we by him.

7 Howbeit there is not in every man that knowledge: for some with conscience of the idol unto this hour eat it as a thing offered unto an idol; and their [aconscience](#note7a) being weak is defiled.

8 But [ameat](#note8a) commendeth us not to God: for neither, if we eat, are we the better; neither, if we eat not, are we the worse.

9 But take heed lest by any means this [aliberty](#note9a) of yours become a [bstumblingblock](#note9b) to them that are [cweak](#note9c).

10 For if any man see thee which hast knowledge sit at meat in the idol’s temple, shall not the conscience of him which is weak be emboldened to eat those things which are offered to idols;

11 And through thy knowledge shall the weak brother perish, for whom Christ died?

12 But when ye sin so against the [abrethren](#note12a), and wound their weak conscience, ye sin against Christ.

13 Wherefore, if meat [amake](#note13a) my brother to [boffend](#note13b), I will [ceat no](#note13c) flesh while the world standeth, lest I make my brother to offend.

Chapter 9

Paul rejoices in his Christian liberty—He preaches the gospel to all without charge—He is all things to all men to gain converts.

1 Am I not an [aapostle](#note1a)? am I not free? have I not [bseen](#note1b) Jesus Christ our Lord? are not ye my [cwork](#note1c) in the Lord?

2 If I be not an apostle unto others, yet doubtless I am to you: for the [aseal](#note2a) of mine apostleship are ye in the Lord.

3 Mine [aanswer](#note3a) to them that do [bexamine](#note3b) me is this,

4 Have we not power to eat and to drink?

5 Have we not power to lead about a sister, a [awife](#note5a), as well as other apostles, and as the [bbrethren](#note5b) of the Lord, and Cephas?

6 Or I only and Barnabas, have not we power to forbear working?

7 Who goeth a [awarfare](#note7a) any time at his own charges? who planteth a [bvineyard](#note7b), and [ceateth](#note7c) not of the fruit thereof? or who feedeth a flock, and eateth not of the milk of the flock?

8 Say I these things as a man? or saith not the law the same also?

9 For it is written in the law of Moses, Thou shalt not [amuzzle](#note9a) the mouth of the ox that treadeth out the corn. Doth God take care for oxen?

10 Or saith he it altogether for our sakes? For our sakes, no doubt, this is [awritten](#note10a): that he that ploweth should plow in hope; and that he that thresheth in hope should be [bpartaker](#note10b) of his hope.

11 If we have sown unto you [aspiritual](#note11a) things, is it a great thing if we shall reap your carnal things?

12 If others be partakers of this power over you, are not we rather? Nevertheless we have not used this [apower](#note12a); but suffer all things, lest we should hinder the gospel of Christ.

13 Do ye not know that they which [aminister](#note13a) about holy things [blive](#note13b) of the things of the temple? and they which wait at the altar are partakers with the altar?

14 Even so hath the Lord ordained that they which [apreach](#note14a) the [bgospel](#note14b) should [clive](#note14c) of the gospel.

15 But I have used none of these things: neither have I written these things, that it should be so done unto me: for it were better for me to die, than that any man should make my glorying void.

16 For though I preach the [agospel](#note16a), I have nothing to glory of: for [bnecessity](#note16b) is laid upon me; yea, woe is unto me, if I [cpreach](#note16c) not the gospel!

17 For if I do this thing [awillingly](#note17a), I have a [breward](#note17b): but if against my will, a [cdispensation](#note17c) of the gospel is committed unto me.

18 What is my reward then? Verily that, when I preach the gospel, I may make the gospel of Christ without [acharge](#note18a), that I abuse not my power in the gospel.

19 For though I be free from all men, yet have I made myself [aservant](#note19a) unto all, that I might gain the more.

20 And unto the Jews I became as a Jew, that I might gain the Jews; to them that are under the law, as under the law, that I might gain them that are under the law;

21 To them that are without law, as without law, (being not without law to God, but under the law to Christ,) that I might gain them that are without law.

22 To the weak became I as [aweak](#note22a), that I might gain the weak: I am made [ball](#note22b) things to all men, that I might by all means save some.

23 And this I do for the gospel’s sake, that I might be partaker thereof with you.

24 Know ye not that they which run in a race run all, but one receiveth the prize? So run, that ye may obtain.

25 And every man that striveth for the [amastery](#note25a) is [btemperate](#note25b) in all things. Now they do it to obtain a corruptible [ccrown](#note25c); but we an [dincorruptible](#note25d).

26 I therefore so run, not as uncertainly; so fight I, not as one that beateth the air:

27 But I [akeep under](#note27a) my [bbody](#note27b), and bring it into subjection: lest that by any means, when I have preached to others, I myself should be a castaway.

Chapter 10

Christ is the God of Israel and the spiritual Rock that guided them—Ancient Israel rebelled against Christ—Paul contrasts true and false sacraments.

1 Moreover, brethren, I would not that ye should be ignorant, how that all our fathers were under the [acloud](#note1a), and all passed through the [bsea](#note1b);

2 And were all [abaptized](#note2a) unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea;

3 And did all eat the same spiritual [ameat](#note3a);

4 And did all drink the same spiritual drink: for they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that [aRock](#note4a) was Christ.

5 But with many of them [aGod](#note5a) was not well [bpleased](#note5b): for they were [coverthrown](#note5c) in the [dwilderness](#note5d).

6 Now these things were our [aexamples](#note6a), to the intent we should not [blust after](#note6b) evil things, as they also lusted.

7 Neither be ye idolaters, as were some of them; as it is written, The [apeople](#note7a) sat down to eat and drink, and rose up to play.

8 Neither let us commit [afornication](#note8a), as some of them committed, and fell in one day three and twenty thousand.

9 Neither let us [atempt](#note9a) Christ, as some of them also tempted, and were destroyed of serpents.

10 Neither [amurmur](#note10a) ye, as some of them also murmured, and were [bdestroyed](#note10b) of the destroyer.

11 Now all these things happened unto them for [aensamples](#note11a): [band](#note11b) they are [cwritten](#note11c) for our [dadmonition](#note11d), upon whom the ends of the world are come.

12 Wherefore let him that thinketh he [astandeth](#note12a) take heed lest he [bfall](#note12b).

13 There hath no temptation [ataken](#note13a) you but such as is common to man: but God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be [btempted](#note13b) above that ye are able; but will with the [ctemptation](#note13c) also make a way to [descape](#note13d), that ye may be able to [ebear](#note13e) it.

14 Wherefore, my dearly beloved, flee from [aidolatry](#note14a).

15 I speak as to wise men; judge ye what I say.

16 The [acup](#note16a) of [bblessing](#note16b) which we bless, is it not the [ccommunion](#note16c) of the blood of Christ? The bread which we break, is it not the communion of the body of Christ?

17 For we being many are one bread, and one body: for we are all partakers of that one bread.

18 Behold Israel after the flesh: are not they which eat of the sacrifices [apartakers](#note18a) of the altar?

19 What say I then? that the idol is any thing, or that which is offered in sacrifice to [aidols](#note19a) is any thing?

20 But I say, that the things which the Gentiles sacrifice, they [asacrifice](#note20a) to devils, and not to God: and I would not that ye should have fellowship with devils.

21 Ye cannot drink the [acup](#note21a) of the Lord, and the cup of devils: ye cannot be partakers of the Lord’s table, and of the table of devils.

22 Do we provoke the Lord to [ajealousy](#note22a)? are we stronger than he?

23 [aAll](#note23a) things are lawful for me, but all things are not [bexpedient](#note23b): all things are lawful for me, but all things [cedify](#note23c) not.

24 Let no man seek his [aown](#note24a), but every man another’s [bwealth](#note24b).

25 Whatsoever is sold in the [ashambles](#note25a), that eat, asking no question for conscience sake:

26 For the [aearth](#note26a) is the Lord’s, and the fulness thereof.

27 If any of them that believe not bid you to a [afeast](#note27a), and ye be disposed to go; whatsoever is set before you, eat, asking no question for conscience sake.

28 But if any man say unto you, This is offered in sacrifice unto idols, eat not for his sake that shewed it, and for conscience sake: for the earth is the Lord’s, and the fulness thereof:

29 Conscience, I say, not thine own, but of the other: for why is my liberty judged of another man’s [aconscience](#note29a)?

30 For if I [aby grace](#note30a) be a partaker, why am I evil spoken of for that for which I give thanks?

31 Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, [ado](#note31a) all to the glory of God.

32 Give none [aoffence](#note32a), neither to the Jews, nor to the Gentiles, nor to the church of God:

33 Even as I please all men in all things, not [aseeking](#note33a) mine own profit, but the [bprofit](#note33b) of many, that they may be [csaved](#note33c).

Chapter 11

Paul speaks of certain customs of hair and grooming—Heresies will arise that test and prove the faithful—The sacramental emblems are partaken in remembrance of the flesh and blood of Christ—Beware of partaking unworthily.

1 Be ye [afollowers](#note1a) of me, even as I also am of Christ.

2 Now I praise you, brethren, [athat](#note2a) ye remember me in all things, and keep the [bordinances](#note2b), as I delivered them to you.

3 But I would have you know, that the head of every man is Christ; and the [ahead](#note3a) of the [bwoman](#note3b) is the man; and the [chead](#note3c) of Christ is God.

4 Every man praying or prophesying, having his head covered, dishonoureth his head.

5 But every woman that prayeth or prophesieth with her head uncovered dishonoureth her head: for that is [aeven all one as](#note5a) if she were shaven.

6 For if the woman be not covered, let her also be shorn: but if it be a [ashame](#note6a) for a woman to be shorn or shaven, let her be covered.

7 For a man indeed ought not to cover his head, forasmuch as he is the image and glory of God: but the woman is the glory of the man.

8 For the man is not of the woman; but the woman of the man.

9 Neither was the man created for the woman; but the woman for the man.

10 For this cause ought the woman to have power on her head because of the angels.

11 Nevertheless neither is the [aman](#note11a) without the woman, neither the [bwoman](#note11b) without the man, in the Lord.

12 For as the woman is of the man, even so is the man also by the woman; but all things of God.

13 Judge in yourselves: is it [acomely](#note13a) that a woman pray unto God uncovered?

14 Doth not even nature itself teach you, that, if a man have long [ahair](#note14a), it is a shame unto him?

15 But if a woman have long hair, it is a glory to her: for her hair is given her for a covering.

16 But if any man seem to be [acontentious](#note16a), we have no such custom, neither the churches of God.

17 Now in this that I declare unto you I praise you not, that ye come together not for the better, but for the worse.

18 For first of all, when ye come together in the [achurch](#note18a), I hear that there be [bdivisions](#note18b) among you; and I partly believe it.

19 For there must be also [aheresies](#note19a) among you, that they which are approved may be made manifest among you.

20 When ye come together therefore into one place, [athis](#note20a) is not to eat the Lord’s supper.

21 For in eating every one taketh before other his own supper: and one is hungry, and another is drunken.

22 What? have ye not houses to eat and to drink in? or despise ye the [achurch](#note22a) of God, and [bshame](#note22b) them that have not? What shall I say to you? shall I praise you in this? I praise you not.

23 For I have received of the Lord that which also I delivered unto you, That the Lord Jesus the same night in which he was betrayed took [abread](#note23a):

24 And when he had given thanks, he brake it, and said, Take, eat: this is my body, which is broken for you: this do in [aremembrance](#note24a) of me.

25 After the same manner also he took the cup, when he had supped, saying, This cup is the new testament in my blood: this do ye, as oft as ye drink it, in remembrance of me.

26 For as often as ye eat this [abread](#note26a), and drink this cup, ye do [bshew](#note26b) the Lord’s [cdeath](#note26c) till he come.

27 Wherefore whosoever shall eat this [abread](#note27a), and drink this cup of the Lord, [bunworthily](#note27b), shall [cbe](#note27c) [dguilty](#note27d) of the body and blood of the Lord.

28 But let a man [aexamine](#note28a) himself, and so let him eat of that bread, and drink of that cup.

29 For he that eateth and drinketh [aunworthily](#note29a), eateth and drinketh [bdamnation](#note29b) to himself, not discerning the Lord’s body.

30 For this cause many are weak and [asickly](#note30a) among you, and many sleep.

31 For if we would [ajudge](#note31a) ourselves, we should not be judged.

32 But when we are judged, we are [achastened](#note32a) of the Lord, that we should not be condemned with the world.

33 Wherefore, my brethren, when ye come together to eat, tarry one for another.

34 And if any man hunger, let him eat at home; that ye come not together unto condemnation. And the rest will I set in order when I come.

Chapter 12

The Holy Ghost reveals that Jesus is the Christ—Spiritual gifts are present among the Saints—Apostles, prophets, and miracles are found in the true Church.

1 Now concerning spiritual gifts, brethren, I would not have you ignorant.

2 Ye know that ye were Gentiles, [acarried away](#note2a) unto these dumb [bidols](#note2b), even as ye were led.

3 Wherefore I give you to understand, that no man speaking by the Spirit of God calleth Jesus accursed: and that no man can [asay](#note3a) that Jesus is the [bLord](#note3b), but by the [cHoly Ghost](#note3c).

4 Now there are diversities of [agifts](#note4a), but the same Spirit.

5 And there are differences of administrations, but the same Lord.

6 And there are diversities of operations, but it is the same God which worketh all in all.

7 But the manifestation of the [aSpirit](#note7a) is given to every man to profit withal.

8 For to one is [agiven](#note8a) by the [bSpirit](#note8b) the [cword](#note8c) of [dwisdom](#note8d); to another the word of [eknowledge](#note8e) by the same Spirit;

9 To another [afaith](#note9a) by the same Spirit; to another the gifts of [bhealing](#note9b) by the same Spirit;

10 To another the working of [amiracles](#note10a); to another [bprophecy](#note10b); to another [cdiscerning](#note10c) of spirits; to another divers kinds of tongues; to another the interpretation of [dtongues](#note10d):

11 But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will.

12 For as the body is one, and hath many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body: so also is Christ.

13 For by one Spirit are we all baptized into one [abody](#note13a), whether we be Jews or [bGentiles](#note13b), whether we be [cbond](#note13c) or free; and have been all made to drink into one Spirit.

14 For the body is not one member, but many.

15 If the foot shall say, Because I am not the hand, I am not of the body; is it therefore not of the body?

16 And if the ear shall say, Because I am not the eye, I am not of the body; is it therefore not of the body?

17 If the whole body were an eye, where were the hearing? If the whole were hearing, where were the smelling?

18 But now hath God set the members every one of them in the body, as it hath pleased him.

19 And if they were all one member, where were the body?

20 But now are they many members, yet but one body.

21 And the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee: nor again the [ahead](#note21a) to the feet, I have no need of you.

22 Nay, much more those members of the body, which seem to be [amore feeble](#note22a), are necessary:

23 And those members of the body, which we think to be less honourable, upon these we bestow more abundant honour; and our uncomely parts have more abundant comeliness.

24 For our comely parts have no need: but God hath tempered the body together, having given more abundant honour to that part which lacked:

25 That there should be no schism in the body; but that the members should have the same [acare](#note25a) one for another.

26 And whether one member [asuffer](#note26a), all the members [bsuffer](#note26b) with it; or one member be honoured, all the members rejoice with it.

27 Now ye are the body of [aChrist](#note27a), and [bmembers](#note27b) in particular.

28 And God hath set some in the church, first [aapostles](#note28a), secondarily [bprophets](#note28b), thirdly [cteachers](#note28c), after that miracles, then gifts of healings, helps, [dgovernments](#note28d), diversities of tongues.

29 Are all apostles? are all prophets? are all teachers? are all workers of [amiracles](#note29a)?

30 Have all the gifts of healing? do all speak with tongues? do all interpret?

31 But [acovet earnestly](#note31a) the best [bgifts](#note31b): and yet shew I unto you a more [cexcellent](#note31c) way.

Chapter 13

Paul discusses the high status of charity—Charity, a pure love, excels and exceeds almost all else.

1 Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not [acharity](#note1a), I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal.

2 And though I have the gift of [aprophecy](#note2a), and understand all [bmysteries](#note2b), and all knowledge; and though I have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing.

3 And though I bestow all my goods to feed the [apoor](#note3a), and though I give my body to be burned, and have not charity, it profiteth me nothing.

4 [aCharity](#note4a) [bsuffereth](#note4b) long, and is [ckind](#note4c); charity [denvieth](#note4d) not; charity vaunteth not itself, is not puffed up,

5 Doth not behave itself [aunseemly](#note5a), [bseeketh not](#note5b) her own, is not easily [cprovoked](#note5c), thinketh no evil;

6 Rejoiceth not in [ainiquity](#note6a), but rejoiceth in the [btruth](#note6b);

7 Beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things.

8 Charity never [afaileth](#note8a): but whether there be prophecies, they shall fail; whether there be tongues, they shall cease; whether there be knowledge, it shall vanish away.

9 For we know in part, and we prophesy in part.

10 But when that which is perfect is come, then that which is in part shall be done away.

11 When I was a child, I spake as a child, I understood as a child, I thought as a child: but when I became a man, I put away childish things.

12 For now we see through a [aglass](#note12a), [bdarkly](#note12b); but then face to face: now I know in part; but then shall I know even as also I am known.

13 And now abideth [afaith](#note13a), [bhope](#note13b), [ccharity](#note13c), these three; but the greatest of these is charity.

Chapter 14

People should desire spiritual gifts—Tongues and prophecy are compared—Prophecy is the greater gift—Paul says, You may all prophesy; covet to prophesy.

1 [aFollow](#note1a) after charity, and desire spiritual [bgifts](#note1b), but rather that ye may prophesy.

2 For he that speaketh in [aan unknown tongue](#note2a) speaketh not unto men, but unto God: for no man understandeth him; howbeit in the spirit he speaketh mysteries.

3 But he that [aprophesieth](#note3a) speaketh unto men to [bedification](#note3b), and exhortation, and comfort.

4 He that speaketh in an unknown tongue edifieth himself; but he that prophesieth edifieth the church.

5 I [awould](#note5a) that ye all spake with tongues, but rather that ye [bprophesied](#note5b): for greater is he that prophesieth than he that speaketh with tongues, except he interpret, that the church may receive edifying.

6 Now, brethren, if I come unto you speaking with tongues, what shall I profit you, [aexcept](#note6a) I shall speak to you either by [brevelation](#note6b), or by knowledge, or by prophesying, or by doctrine?

7 And even things without life giving sound, whether pipe or harp, except they give a distinction in the sounds, how shall it be known what is piped or harped?

8 For if the trumpet give an uncertain sound, who shall prepare himself to the battle?

9 So likewise ye, except ye utter by the tongue words easy to be understood, how shall it be known what is spoken? for ye shall speak into the air.

10 There are, it may be, so many kinds of [avoices](#note10a) in the world, and none of them is without signification.

11 Therefore if I know not the meaning of the voice, I shall be unto him that speaketh a barbarian, and he that speaketh shall be a barbarian unto me.

12 Even so ye, [aforasmuch as](#note12a) ye are zealous of spiritual [bgifts](#note12b), seek that ye may excel to the [cedifying](#note12c) of the church.

13 Wherefore let him that speaketh in an unknown tongue pray that he may interpret.

14 For if I pray in an unknown tongue, my spirit prayeth, but my [aunderstanding](#note14a) is unfruitful.

15 What is it then? I will pray with the spirit, and I will pray with the understanding also: I will sing with the spirit, and I will sing with the understanding also.

16 Else when thou shalt bless with the spirit, how shall he that occupieth the room of the unlearned say Amen at thy giving of thanks, seeing he understandeth not what thou sayest?

17 For thou verily givest thanks well, but the other is not edified.

18 I thank my God, I speak with tongues more than ye all:

19 Yet in the church I had rather [aspeak](#note19a) five words with my understanding, that by my voice I might teach others also, than ten thousand words in an unknown tongue.

20 Brethren, be not [achildren](#note20a) in [bunderstanding](#note20b): howbeit in [cmalice](#note20c) be ye children, but in [dunderstanding](#note20d) be men.

21 In the law it is written, With men of other tongues and other lips will I speak unto this people; and yet for all that will they not [ahear](#note21a) me, saith the Lord.

22 Wherefore [atongues](#note22a) are for a [bsign](#note22b), not to them that believe, but to them that believe not: but [cprophesying](#note22c) serveth not for them that believe not, but for them which believe.

23 If therefore the whole church be come together into one place, and all speak with tongues, and there come in those that are unlearned, or [aunbelievers](#note23a), will they not say that ye are mad?

24 But if all prophesy, and there come in one that believeth not, or one unlearned, he is convinced of all, he is judged of all:

25 And thus are the [asecrets](#note25a) of his heart made manifest; and so falling down on his face he will worship God, and report that God is in you of a truth.

26 How is it then, brethren? when ye come together, every one of you hath a psalm, hath a doctrine, hath a tongue, hath a revelation, hath an interpretation. Let all things be done unto [aedifying](#note26a).

27 If any man speak in an unknown tongue, let it be by two, or at the most by three, and that by course; and let one interpret.

28 But if there be no interpreter, let him keep [asilence](#note28a) in the church; and let him speak to himself, and to God.

29 Let the prophets speak two or three, and let the other judge.

30 If any thing be revealed to another that sitteth by, let the first hold his peace.

31 For ye may all prophesy one by one, that all may learn, and all may be comforted.

32 And the spirits of the prophets are subject to the prophets.

33 For God is not the [aauthor](#note33a) of [bconfusion](#note33b), but of [cpeace](#note33c), as in all [dchurches](#note33d) of the [esaints](#note33e).

34 Let your [awomen](#note34a) keep silence in the churches: for it is not permitted unto them to [bspeak](#note34b); but they are commanded [cto](#note34c) be under [dobedience](#note34d), as also saith the law.

35 And if they will learn any thing, let them ask their husbands at home: for it is a shame for women to [aspeak](#note35a) in the church.

36 What? came the word of God out from you? or came it unto you only?

37 If any man think himself to be a prophet, or spiritual, let him acknowledge that the things that I write unto you are the commandments of the Lord.

38 But if any man be ignorant, let him be ignorant.

39 Wherefore, brethren, [acovet](#note39a) to prophesy, and forbid not to speak with tongues.

40 Let all things be done decently and in [aorder](#note40a).

Chapter 15

Christ died for our sins—He rose from the dead and was seen by many—All men will be resurrected—Paul speaks of baptism for the dead—The three degrees of glory are described—Victory over death comes through Christ.

1 Moreover, brethren, I [adeclare](#note1a) unto you the [bgospel](#note1b) which I preached unto you, which also ye have received, and wherein ye stand;

2 By which also ye are [asaved](#note2a), if ye [bkeep in memory](#note2b) what I preached unto you, unless ye have believed in vain.

3 For I delivered unto you first of all that which I also received, how that Christ [adied](#note3a) for our [bsins](#note3b) according to the scriptures;

4 And that he was buried, and that he [arose](#note4a) again the third day according to the scriptures:

5 And that he was [aseen](#note5a) of [bCephas](#note5b), then of the twelve:

6 After that, he was [aseen](#note6a) of [babove](#note6b) five hundred brethren at once; of whom the greater part remain unto this present, but some are fallen asleep.

7 After that, he was seen of James; then of all the apostles.

8 And last of all he was [aseen](#note8a) of me also, as of one born out of due time.

9 For I am the least of the apostles, that am not [ameet](#note9a) to be called an apostle, because I [bpersecuted](#note9b) the church of God.

10 But by the [agrace](#note10a) of God I am what I am: and his grace which was bestowed upon me was not in vain; but I [blaboured](#note10b) more abundantly than they all: yet not I, but the grace of God which was with me.

11 Therefore whether it were I or they, so we preach, and so ye believed.

12 Now if Christ be preached that he rose from the dead, how say some among you that there is no resurrection of the dead?

13 But if there be no resurrection of the dead, then is Christ not risen:

14 And if Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain, and your faith is also vain.

15 Yea, and we are found false witnesses of God; because we have [atestified](#note15a) of God that he raised up Christ: whom he raised not up, if so be that the dead rise not.

16 For if the dead rise not, then is not Christ raised:

17 And if Christ be not raised, your faith is [avain](#note17a); ye are yet in your sins.

18 Then they also which are fallen asleep in Christ are [aperished](#note18a).

19 If in this [alife](#note19a) only we have [bhope](#note19b) in Christ, we are of all men most miserable.

20 But now is [aChrist](#note20a) [brisen](#note20b) from the dead, and become the [cfirstfruits](#note20c) of them that slept.

21 For since by man came [adeath](#note21a), by man came also the [bresurrection](#note21b) of the dead.

22 For as in [aAdam](#note22a) all [bdie](#note22b), even so in [cChrist](#note22c) shall all be made [dalive](#note22d).

23 But every man in his own [aorder](#note23a): Christ the [bfirstfruits](#note23b); afterward they that are Christ’s at his [ccoming](#note23c).

24 Then cometh the end, when he shall have delivered up the kingdom to God, even the Father; when he shall have [aput down](#note24a) all [brule](#note24b) and all [cauthority](#note24c) and [dpower](#note24d).

25 For he must [areign](#note25a), till he hath put all [benemies](#note25b) under his [cfeet](#note25c).

26 The last [aenemy](#note26a) that shall be [bdestroyed](#note26b) is [cdeath](#note26c).

27 For he hath put all things under his feet. But when he saith all things are put under him, it is [amanifest](#note27a) that he is excepted, which did put all things under him.

28 And when all things shall be [asubdued](#note28a) unto him, then shall the Son also himself be [bsubject](#note28b) unto him that put all things under him, that God may be all in all.

29 Else what shall they do which are [abaptized](#note29a) [bfor](#note29b) the dead, if the dead [crise](#note29c) not at all? why are they then baptized for the [ddead](#note29d)?

30 And why stand we in [ajeopardy](#note30a) every hour?

31 [aI](#note31a) protest by your rejoicing which I have in Christ Jesus our Lord, I die daily.

32 If after the manner of men I have fought with beasts at Ephesus, what advantageth it me, if the dead rise not? let us [aeat](#note32a) and drink; for [bto morrow](#note32b) we die.

33 Be not deceived: evil [acommunications](#note33a) corrupt good manners.

34 Awake to righteousness, and sin not; for some have not the knowledge of God: I speak this to your [ashame](#note34a).

35 But some man will say, How are the dead raised up? and with what body do they come?

36 Thou fool, that which thou sowest is not quickened, except it [adie](#note36a):

37 And that which thou sowest, thou sowest not that body that shall be, but bare grain, [ait may chance](#note37a) of wheat, or of some other grain:

38 But God giveth it a body as it hath pleased him, and to every seed his own body.

39 All flesh is not the same flesh: but there is one kind of flesh of men, another flesh of beasts, another of fishes, and another of birds.

40 [aThere](#note40a) are also celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial: but the glory of the [bcelestial](#note40b) is one, and the glory of the [cterrestrial](#note40c) is another.

41 There is one glory of the [asun](#note41a), and another glory of the moon, and another glory of the [bstars](#note41b): for one star [cdiffereth](#note41c) from another star in [dglory](#note41d).

42 So also is the resurrection of the dead. It is sown in [acorruption](#note42a); it is raised in incorruption:

43 It is sown in dishonour; it is raised in [aglory](#note43a): it is sown in weakness; it is raised in power:

44 It is sown a [anatural](#note44a) body; it is raised a [bspiritual body](#note44b). There is a natural body, and there is a spiritual body.

45 And so it is written, The [afirst](#note45a) man [bAdam](#note45b) was made a living soul; the last [cAdam](#note45c) was made a [dquickening](#note45d) spirit.

46 Howbeit that was not [afirst](#note46a) which is spiritual, but that which is natural; and afterward that which is spiritual.

47 The first man is of the earth, earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven.

48 As is the earthy, such are they also that are earthy: and as is the heavenly, such are they also that are heavenly.

49 And as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly.

50 Now this I say, brethren, that [aflesh](#note50a) and [bblood](#note50b) cannot [cinherit](#note50c) the kingdom of God; neither doth [dcorruption](#note50d) inherit incorruption.

51 Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all [asleep](#note51a), but we shall all be [bchanged](#note51b),

52 In a moment, in the [atwinkling](#note52a) of an eye, at the last trump: for the [btrumpet](#note52b) shall sound, and the [cdead](#note52c) shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be [dchanged](#note52d).

53 For this [acorruptible](#note53a) must put on incorruption, and this [bmortal](#note53b) must put on immortality.

54 So when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption, and this mortal shall have put on [aimmortality](#note54a), then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, [bDeath](#note54b) is swallowed up in victory.

55 O [adeath](#note55a), where is thy sting? O [bgrave](#note55b), where is thy victory?

56 The sting of [adeath](#note56a) is sin; and the [bstrength](#note56b) of sin is the law.

57 But [athanks](#note57a) be to God, which giveth us the [bvictory](#note57b) through our Lord Jesus Christ.

58 Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye [asteadfast](#note58a), unmoveable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord.

Chapter 16

Paul counsels, Stand fast in the faith; let all things be done with charity.

1 Now concerning the [acollection](#note1a) for the saints, as I have [bgiven order to](#note1b) the churches of Galatia, even so do ye.

2 Upon the [afirst](#note2a) day of the week let every one of you lay by him in store, as God hath [bprospered](#note2b) him, that there be no gatherings when I come.

3 And when I come, whomsoever ye shall approve by your letters, them will I send to bring your [aliberality](#note3a) unto Jerusalem.

4 And if it be [ameet](#note4a) that I go also, they shall go with me.

5 Now I will come unto you, when I shall pass through Macedonia: for I do pass through Macedonia.

6 And it may be that I will abide, yea, and winter with you, that ye may bring me on my journey whithersoever I go.

7 For I will not see you now [aby the way](#note7a); but I [btrust](#note7b) to [ctarry](#note7c) a while with you, if the Lord permit.

8 But I will tarry at Ephesus until Pentecost.

9 For a great [adoor](#note9a) and [beffectual](#note9b) is opened unto me, and there are many adversaries.

10 Now if [aTimotheus](#note10a) come, see that he may be with you without fear: for he worketh the work of the Lord, as I also do.

11 Let no man therefore [adespise](#note11a) him: but conduct him forth in peace, that he may come unto me: for I look for him with the brethren.

12 As touching our brother [aApollos](#note12a), I greatly desired him to come unto you with the brethren: but his will was not at all to come at this time; but he will come when he shall have convenient time.

13 [aWatch](#note13a) ye, [bstand fast](#note13b) in the [cfaith](#note13c), [dquit](#note13d) you like [emen](#note13e), be strong.

14 Let all your things be done with [acharity](#note14a).

15 I beseech you, brethren, (ye know the house of [aStephanas](#note15a), that it is the firstfruits of Achaia, and that they have [baddicted](#note15b) themselves to the ministry of the saints,)

16 That ye submit yourselves unto such, and to every one that helpeth with us, and laboureth.

17 I am glad of the coming of Stephanas and Fortunatus and Achaicus: for that which was [alacking](#note17a) on your part they have supplied.

18 For they have refreshed my spirit and yours: therefore acknowledge ye them that are such.

19 The churches of Asia salute you. [aAquila](#note19a) and Priscilla salute you much in the Lord, with the church that is in their house.

20 All the brethren greet you. Greet ye one another with an holy [akiss](#note20a).

21 The salutation of me Paul with mine own hand.

22 If any man love not the Lord Jesus Christ, let him be Anathema [aMaran-atha](#note22a).

23 The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.

24 My love be with you all in Christ Jesus. Amen.
