Chapter 1

Counsel is given to teach true doctrine only—Christ came to save repentant sinners.

1 Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the commandment of God our Saviour, and Lord Jesus Christ, which is our hope;

2 Unto [aTimothy](#note2a), [bmy](#note2b) [cown](#note2c) son in the faith: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.

3 As I besought thee to abide still at Ephesus, when I went into Macedonia, that thou mightest charge some that they [ateach](#note3a) no [bother](#note3b) doctrine,

4 Neither give heed to [afables](#note4a) and endless genealogies, which [bminister](#note4b) [cquestions](#note4c), rather than godly [dedifying](#note4d) which is in faith: so do.

5 Now the end of the [acommandment](#note5a) is [bcharity](#note5b) out of a [cpure](#note5c) heart, and of a good conscience, and of faith unfeigned:

6 From which some having [aswerved](#note6a) have [bturned](#note6b) aside unto [cvain jangling](#note6c);

7 Desiring to be teachers of the law; understanding neither what they say, nor whereof they [aaffirm](#note7a).

8 But we know that the law is good, if a man use it lawfully;

9 Knowing this, that the [alaw](#note9a) is not made for a [brighteous](#note9b) man, but for the [clawless](#note9c) and [ddisobedient](#note9d), for the ungodly and for sinners, for unholy and profane, for murderers of fathers and murderers of mothers, for manslayers,

10 For [awhoremongers](#note10a), for [bthem](#note10b) that [cdefile](#note10c) themselves with mankind, for [dmenstealers](#note10d), for liars, for perjured persons, and if there be any other thing that is contrary to sound [edoctrine](#note10e);

11 According to the glorious gospel of the blessed God, which was committed to my [atrust](#note11a).

12 And I thank Christ Jesus our Lord, who hath [aenabled](#note12a) me, for that he counted me faithful, putting me into the ministry;

13 Who was before a [ablasphemer](#note13a), and a [bpersecutor](#note13b), and [cinjurious](#note13c): but I obtained [dmercy](#note13d), because I did it [eignorantly](#note13e) in unbelief.

14 And the [agrace](#note14a) of our Lord was exceeding abundant with faith and love which is in Christ Jesus.

15 This is a faithful [asaying](#note15a), and worthy of all acceptation, that Christ Jesus came into the world to [bsave](#note15b) [csinners](#note15c); of whom I am chief.

16 Howbeit for this cause I obtained [amercy](#note16a), that in me first Jesus Christ might shew forth all [blongsuffering](#note16b), for a [cpattern](#note16c) to them which should hereafter believe on him to life everlasting.

17 Now unto the [aKing](#note17a) eternal, [bimmortal](#note17b), invisible, the only wise God, be honour and glory for ever and ever. Amen.

18 This charge I commit unto thee, son Timothy, according to the [aprophecies](#note18a) which went before on thee, that thou by them mightest war a good warfare;

19 [aHolding](#note19a) faith, and a good conscience; which some having put away concerning faith have made [bshipwreck](#note19b):

20 Of whom is [aHymenæus](#note20a) and [bAlexander](#note20b); whom I have [cdelivered](#note20c) unto Satan, that they may learn not to blaspheme.

Chapter 2

We should pray for all people—Christ is our Mediator—Women should dress modestly—Women are blessed in childbearing and are admonished to continue in faith, charity, and holiness.

1 I exhort therefore, that, first of all, supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks, be made for all men;

2 For [akings](#note2a), and for all that are in authority; that we may lead a quiet and [bpeaceable](#note2b) [clife](#note2c) in all godliness and [dhonesty](#note2d).

3 For this is good and acceptable in the sight of God our Saviour;

4 [aWho](#note4a) [bwill have](#note4b) [call](#note4c) men to be [dsaved](#note4d), and to come unto the knowledge of the truth.

5 For there is [aone God](#note5a), and one [bmediator](#note5b) between God and men, the man Christ Jesus;

6 Who [agave](#note6a) himself a [bransom](#note6b) for all, to be [ctestified](#note6c) in due time.

7 Whereunto I am [aordained](#note7a) a [bpreacher](#note7b), and an [capostle](#note7c), (I speak the truth in Christ, and [dlie](#note7d) not;) a teacher of the [eGentiles](#note7e) in faith and [fverity](#note7f).

8 I [awill](#note8a) therefore that men [bpray](#note8b) every where, lifting up holy [chands](#note8c), without wrath and [ddoubting](#note8d).

9 In like manner also, that [awomen](#note9a) adorn themselves in [bmodest](#note9b) [capparel](#note9c), with [dshamefacedness](#note9d) and sobriety; not with [ebroided](#note9e) hair, or gold, or pearls, or costly array;

10 But (which becometh women professing [agodliness](#note10a)) with good works.

11 Let the woman learn in [asilence](#note11a) with all subjection.

12 But I suffer not a woman to [ateach](#note12a), nor to [busurp authority](#note12b) over the man, but to be in [csilence](#note12c).

13 For Adam was first [aformed](#note13a), then Eve.

14 And Adam was not deceived, but the woman being [adeceived](#note14a) was in the [btransgression](#note14b).

15 Notwithstanding [ashe](#note15a) shall be saved in [bchildbearing](#note15b), if they continue in faith and charity and [choliness](#note15c) with [dsobriety](#note15d).

Chapter 3

Qualifications are given for bishops and deacons—Great is the mystery of godliness.

1 This is a true [asaying](#note1a), If a man desire the office of a [bbishop](#note1b), he desireth a good work.

2 A bishop then must be [ablameless](#note2a), the husband of one wife, vigilant, [bsober](#note2b), of good behaviour, given to [chospitality](#note2c), apt to [dteach](#note2d);

3 Not given to [awine](#note3a), no [bstriker](#note3b), not greedy of [cfilthy](#note3c) lucre; but patient, not a brawler, not covetous;

4 One that [aruleth](#note4a) well his own [bhouse](#note4b), having his [cchildren](#note4c) in subjection with all gravity;

5 (For if a man know not how to [arule](#note5a) his own house, how shall he take care of the church of God?)

6 Not a [anovice](#note6a), lest being lifted up with [bpride](#note6b) he fall into the [ccondemnation](#note6c) of the devil.

7 Moreover he must have a good [areport](#note7a) of them which are [bwithout](#note7b); lest he fall into reproach and the [csnare](#note7c) of the devil.

8 Likewise must the [adeacons](#note8a) be [bgrave](#note8b), not [cdoubletongued](#note8c), not given to much wine, not greedy of filthy lucre;

9 Holding the mystery of the faith in a pure [aconscience](#note9a).

10 And let these also first be [aproved](#note10a); then let them use the office of a deacon, being found blameless.

11 [aEven](#note11a) so must their [bwives](#note11b) be [cgrave](#note11c), not [dslanderers](#note11d), [esober](#note11e), [ffaithful](#note11f) in all things.

12 Let the deacons be the husbands of one wife, ruling their children and their own houses well.

13 For they that have used the office of a deacon well [apurchase](#note13a) to themselves a good degree, and great boldness in the faith which is in Christ Jesus.

14 These things write I unto thee, hoping to come unto thee shortly:

15 [aBut](#note15a) if I tarry long, that thou mayest know how thou oughtest to behave thyself in the house of God, which is the [bchurch](#note15b) of the living God, the pillar and [cground](#note15c) of the [dtruth](#note15d).

16 And without controversy great is the [amystery](#note16a) of godliness: [bGod](#note16b) was [cmanifest](#note16c) in the [dflesh](#note16d), [ejustified in the](#note16e) Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, [freceived](#note16f) up into glory.

Chapter 4

Paul describes the latter-day apostasy—Christ is the Savior of all men, especially of those who believe.

1 Now the Spirit speaketh expressly, that in the [alatter times](#note1a) some shall [bdepart](#note1b) from the faith, giving heed to [cseducing](#note1c) spirits, and [ddoctrines of devils](#note1d);

2 Speaking [alies](#note2a) in [bhypocrisy](#note2b); having their [cconscience](#note2c) seared with a hot iron;

3 [aForbidding](#note3a) to [bmarry](#note3b), and commanding to [cabstain](#note3c) from [dmeats](#note3d), which God hath [ecreated](#note3e) to be received with [fthanksgiving](#note3f) of them which believe and know the truth.

4 For every [acreature](#note4a) of God is [bgood](#note4b), and nothing to be refused, if it be received with [cthanksgiving](#note4c):

5 For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer.

6 If thou put the brethren in remembrance of these things, thou shalt be a good minister of Jesus Christ, nourished up in the words of faith and of good doctrine, [awhereunto thou hast attained](#note6a).

7 But refuse profane and old wives’ fables, and exercise thyself rather unto godliness.

8 For bodily exercise profiteth [alittle](#note8a): but [bgodliness](#note8b) is profitable unto all things, having promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come.

9 This is a faithful saying and worthy of all acceptation.

10 For therefore we both [alabour](#note10a) and suffer [breproach](#note10b), because we [ctrust](#note10c) in the living God, who is the [dSaviour](#note10d) of all men, specially of those that [ebelieve](#note10e).

11 These things command and teach.

12 Let no man [adespise](#note12a) thy youth; but be thou an [bexample](#note12b) of the believers, in word, in [cconversation](#note12c), in charity, in spirit, in faith, in [dpurity](#note12d).

13 Till I come, give attendance to [areading](#note13a), to exhortation, to doctrine.

14 [aNeglect](#note14a) not the [bgift](#note14b) that is in thee, which was given thee by [cprophecy](#note14c), with the [dlaying](#note14d) on of the [ehands](#note14e) of the [fpresbytery](#note14f).

15 [aMeditate](#note15a) upon these things; give thyself wholly to them; that thy [bprofiting](#note15b) may [cappear to all](#note15c).

16 Take heed unto thyself, and unto the doctrine; continue in them: for in doing this thou shalt both [asave](#note16a) thyself, and them that hear thee.

Chapter 5

Saints are to care for their worthy poor—Policies concerning elders are given.

1 [aRebuke](#note1a) not an elder, but [bentreat](#note1b) him as a father; and the younger men as brethren;

2 The elder women as mothers; the younger as [asisters](#note2a), with all purity.

3 Honour [awidows](#note3a) that are widows indeed.

4 But if any widow have [achildren](#note4a) or [bnephews](#note4b), let them learn first to shew [cpiety](#note4c) at home, and to requite their parents: for that is good and acceptable before God.

5 Now she that is a widow indeed, and [adesolate](#note5a), trusteth in God, and continueth in supplications and prayers night and day.

6 But she that liveth [ain](#note6a) [bpleasure](#note6b) is dead while she liveth.

7 And these things [agive in charge](#note7a), that they may be blameless.

8 But if any [aprovide](#note8a) not for his own, and specially for those of his own house, he hath denied the faith, and is worse than an infidel.

9 Let not a widow be taken into the number under threescore years [aold](#note9a), having been the wife of one man,

10 Well reported of for good works; if she have brought up children, if she have [alodged](#note10a) strangers, if she have [bwashed](#note10b) the saints’ [cfeet](#note10c), if she have relieved the afflicted, if she have diligently followed every good work.

11 But the younger widows refuse: for when they have begun to wax wanton against Christ, they will marry;

12 Having [adamnation](#note12a), because they have cast off their first faith.

13 And withal they learn to be [aidle](#note13a), wandering about from house to house; and not only idle, but tattlers also and [bbusybodies](#note13b), speaking things which they ought not.

14 I [awill](#note14a) therefore that the younger [bwomen](#note14b) marry, bear [cchildren](#note14c), guide the [dhouse](#note14d), give none occasion to the adversary to speak reproachfully.

15 For some are already turned aside after Satan.

16 If any man or woman that believeth have widows, let them [arelieve](#note16a) them, and let not the church be [bcharged](#note16b); that it may relieve them that are widows indeed.

17 Let the [aelders](#note17a) that rule well be counted worthy of double honour, especially they who labour in the word and doctrine.

18 For the scripture saith, Thou shalt not [amuzzle](#note18a) the ox that treadeth out the corn. And, The [blabourer](#note18b) is worthy of his [creward](#note18c).

19 Against an elder receive not an accusation, but before two or three [awitnesses](#note19a).

20 Them that sin [arebuke](#note20a) before all, that others also may fear.

21 I charge thee before God, and the Lord Jesus Christ, and the elect [aangels](#note21a), that thou observe these things [bwithout](#note21b) preferring one before another, doing nothing by partiality.

22 [aLay](#note22a) [bhands](#note22b) suddenly on no [cman](#note22c), neither be [dpartaker](#note22d) of other men’s sins: keep thyself [epure](#note22e).

23 Drink no longer water, but use a little wine for thy stomach’s sake and thine often infirmities.

24 Some men’s sins are open beforehand, going before to judgment; and some men they [afollow after](#note24a).

25 Likewise also the good works of some are manifest beforehand; and they that are otherwise cannot be hid.

Chapter 6

The love of money is the root of all evil—Fight the good fight of faith—Do not trust in worldly riches.

1 Let as many [aservants](#note1a) as are under the [byoke](#note1b) count their own masters worthy of all honour, that the name of God and his doctrine be not blasphemed.

2 And they that have believing masters, let them not despise them, because they are [abrethren](#note2a); but rather do them [bservice](#note2b), because they are faithful and beloved, partakers of the benefit. These things teach and exhort.

3 If any man [ateach](#note3a) otherwise, and consent not to [bwholesome](#note3b) words, even the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, and to the doctrine which is according to godliness;

4 He is [aproud](#note4a), knowing nothing, but [bdoting](#note4b) about [cquestions](#note4c) and [dstrifes](#note4d) of words, whereof cometh [eenvy](#note4e), strife, railings, [fevil surmisings](#note4f),

5 Perverse [adisputings](#note5a) of men of corrupt minds, and destitute of the truth, supposing that [bgain](#note5b) is godliness: from such withdraw thyself.

6 But [agodliness](#note6a) with [bcontentment](#note6b) is great gain.

7 For we brought [anothing](#note7a) into this world, and it is certain we can carry nothing out.

8 And having [afood](#note8a) and raiment let us be therewith [bcontent](#note8b).

9 But [athey](#note9a) that will be [brich](#note9b) fall into [ctemptation](#note9c) and a snare, and into many foolish and hurtful [dlusts](#note9d), which drown men in destruction and [eperdition](#note9e).

10 For the [alove](#note10a) of [bmoney](#note10b) is the root of all evil: which while some [ccoveted](#note10c) after, they have [derred](#note10d) from the faith, and pierced themselves through with many [esorrows](#note10e).

11 But thou, O man of God, flee these things; and follow after [arighteousness](#note11a), godliness, faith, love, patience, meekness.

12 Fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on [aeternal life](#note12a), whereunto thou art also called, and hast [bprofessed](#note12b) a good profession before many witnesses.

13 I give thee charge in the sight of God, who [aquickeneth](#note13a) all things, and before Christ Jesus, who before [bPontius Pilate](#note13b) [cwitnessed a good confession](#note13c);

14 That thou keep this commandment without spot, unrebukeable, until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ:

15 [aWhich](#note15a) in his times he shall shew, who is the blessed and only [bPotentate](#note15b), the [cKing of kings](#note15c), and Lord of lords;

16 Who only hath [aimmortality](#note16a), dwelling in the [blight](#note16b) which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath [cseen](#note16c), nor can see: to whom be honour and power everlasting. Amen.

17 Charge them that are [arich](#note17a) in this world, that they be not highminded, nor trust in uncertain [briches](#note17b), but in the living God, who giveth us richly all things to enjoy;

18 That they do good, that they be rich in good works, ready to [adistribute](#note18a), willing to [bcommunicate](#note18b);

19 [aLaying](#note19a) up in [bstore](#note19b) for themselves a good foundation [cagainst](#note19c) the time to come, that they may lay hold on [deternal life](#note19d).

20 O Timothy, keep that which is committed to thy [atrust](#note20a), avoiding profane and vain babblings, and [boppositions](#note20b) of science falsely so called:

21 Which some professing have erred concerning the faith. Grace be with thee. Amen.
