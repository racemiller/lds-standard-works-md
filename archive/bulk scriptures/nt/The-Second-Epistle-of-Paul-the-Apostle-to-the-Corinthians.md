Chapter 1

God comforts and cares for His Saints—The Saints are sealed and given assurance by the Spirit in their hearts.

1 Paul, an [aapostle](#note1a) of Jesus Christ by the will of God, and Timothy our brother, unto the church of God which is at Corinth, with all the saints which are in all Achaia:

2 Grace be to you and peace from God our Father, and from the Lord Jesus Christ.

3 Blessed be God, even the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of [amercies](#note3a), and the God of all [bcomfort](#note3b);

4 Who comforteth us in all our [atribulation](#note4a), that we may be able to comfort them which are in any trouble, by the [bcomfort](#note4b) wherewith we ourselves are comforted of God.

5 For as the [asufferings](#note5a) of Christ abound in us, so our consolation also aboundeth by Christ.

6 And whether we be afflicted, it is for your consolation and salvation, which is [aeffectual](#note6a) in the [benduring](#note6b) of the same sufferings which we also suffer: or whether we be comforted, it is for your consolation and salvation.

7 And our hope of you is steadfast, knowing, that as ye are partakers of the [asufferings](#note7a), so shall ye be also of the consolation.

8 For we would not, brethren, have you ignorant of our trouble which came to us in Asia, that we were pressed out of measure, above strength, insomuch that we [adespaired](#note8a) even of life:

9 But we had the sentence of death in ourselves, that we should not trust in ourselves, but in God which [araiseth](#note9a) the dead:

10 Who [adelivered](#note10a) us from so great a death, and doth deliver: in whom we trust that he will yet deliver us;

11 Ye also helping together by [aprayer](#note11a) for us, that for the gift bestowed upon us by the means of many persons thanks may be given by many on our behalf.

12 For our rejoicing is this, the [atestimony](#note12a) of our conscience, that in [bsimplicity](#note12b) and godly sincerity, not with fleshly [cwisdom](#note12c), but by the grace of God, we have had our conversation in the world, and more abundantly to you-ward.

13 For we [awrite](#note13a) none other things unto you, than what ye read or acknowledge; and I trust ye shall acknowledge even to the end;

14 As also ye have acknowledged us in part, that we are your rejoicing, even as ye also are ours in the [aday](#note14a) of the Lord Jesus.

15 And in this confidence I was minded to [acome](#note15a) unto you before, that ye might have a second benefit;

16 And to pass by you into Macedonia, and to come again out of Macedonia unto you, and of you to be brought on my way toward Judæa.

17 When I therefore was thus minded, did I use [alightness](#note17a)? or the things that I purpose, do I purpose according to the flesh, that with me there should be yea yea, and nay nay?

18 But as God is true, our word toward you was not yea and nay.

19 For the [aSon of God](#note19a), Jesus Christ, who was preached among you by us, even by me and [bSilvanus](#note19b) and [cTimotheus](#note19c), was not yea and nay, but in him was yea.

20 For all the [apromises](#note20a) of God in him are yea, and in him Amen, unto the glory of God by us.

21 Now he which stablisheth us with you in Christ, and hath [aanointed](#note21a) us, is God;

22 Who hath also [asealed](#note22a) us, and given the earnest of the Spirit in our hearts.

23 Moreover I call God for a record upon my soul, that to spare you I came not as yet unto Corinth.

24 Not for that we have dominion over your faith, but are helpers of your joy: for by [afaith](#note24a) ye stand.

Chapter 2

Saints should love and forgive one another—They always triumph in Christ.

1 But I determined this with myself, that I would not come again to you in heaviness.

2 For if I make you sorry, who is he then that maketh me glad, but the same which is made sorry by me?

3 And I wrote this same unto you, lest, when I came, I should have sorrow from them of whom I ought to rejoice; having [aconfidence](#note3a) in you all, that my joy is the joy of you all.

4 For out of much affliction and anguish of heart I wrote unto you with many tears; not that ye should be grieved, but that ye might know the love which I have more abundantly unto you.

5 But if any have caused grief, he hath not grieved me, but in part: that I may not overcharge you all.

6 Sufficient to such a man is this punishment, which was inflicted of many.

7 So that contrariwise ye ought rather to [aforgive](#note7a) him, and comfort him, lest perhaps such a one should be swallowed up with overmuch sorrow.

8 Wherefore I beseech you that ye would confirm your love toward him.

9 For to this end also did I [awrite](#note9a), that I might know the proof of you, whether ye be [bobedient](#note9b) in all things.

10 To whom ye forgive any thing, I forgive also: for if I forgave any thing, to whom I forgave it, for your sakes forgave I it in the [aperson](#note10a) of Christ;

11 Lest [aSatan](#note11a) should get an advantage of us: for we are not ignorant of his devices.

12 Furthermore, when I came to Troas to preach Christ’s gospel, and a [adoor](#note12a) was opened unto me of the Lord,

13 I had no rest in my spirit, because I found not Titus my brother: but taking my leave of them, I went from thence into Macedonia.

14 Now thanks be unto God, which always causeth us to [atriumph](#note14a) in Christ, and maketh manifest the savour of his knowledge by us in every place.

15 For we are unto God a sweet savour of Christ, in them that are saved, and in them that perish:

16 To the one we are the savour of death unto death; and to the other the savour of life unto life. And who is [asufficient](#note16a) for these things?

17 For we are not as many, which [acorrupt](#note17a) the [bword](#note17b) of God: but as of [csincerity](#note17c), but as of God, in the sight of God speak we in Christ.

Chapter 3

The gospel surpasses the law of Moses—Where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is liberty.

1 Do we begin again to commend ourselves? or need we, as some others, epistles of commendation to you, or letters of commendation from you?

2 Ye are our [aepistle](#note2a) written in our hearts, known and read of all men:

3 Forasmuch as ye are manifestly declared to be the epistle of Christ ministered by us, [awritten](#note3a) not with ink, but with the Spirit of the living God; not in [btables](#note3b) of stone, but in [cfleshy](#note3c) tables of the [dheart](#note3d).

4 And such trust have we through Christ to God-ward:

5 Not that we are sufficient of ourselves to think any thing as of ourselves; but our [asufficiency](#note5a) is of God;

6 Who also hath made us able [aministers](#note6a) of the new testament; not of the letter, but of the spirit: for the letter killeth, but the [bspirit](#note6b) giveth life.

7 But if the ministration of [adeath](#note7a), written and engraven in stones, was glorious, so that the children of Israel could not steadfastly behold the face of Moses for the [bglory](#note7b) of his countenance; which glory was to be done away:

8 How shall not the ministration of the spirit be rather glorious?

9 For if the ministration of condemnation be glory, much more doth the ministration of righteousness exceed in glory.

10 For even that which was made glorious had no glory in this respect, by reason of the glory that excelleth.

11 For if that which is done away was glorious, much more that which remaineth is glorious.

12 Seeing then that we have such hope, we use great [aplainness](#note12a) of speech:

13 And not as Moses, which put a veil over his face, that the children of Israel could not steadfastly look to the end of that which is abolished:

14 But their [aminds](#note14a) were [bblinded](#note14b): for until this day remaineth the same veil untaken away in the [creading](#note14c) of the old testament; which [dveil](#note14d) is done away in Christ.

15 But even unto this day, when Moses is read, the veil is upon their [aheart](#note15a).

16 Nevertheless when it shall turn to the Lord, the veil shall be taken away.

17 Now the Lord is [athat Spirit](#note17a): and where the [bSpirit](#note17b) of the Lord is, there is [cliberty](#note17c).

18 But we all, with open face beholding as in a [aglass](#note18a) the [bglory](#note18b) of the Lord, are changed into the same [cimage](#note18c) from [dglory](#note18d) to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord.

Chapter 4

Gospel light shines on the Saints—Mortal trials are nothing as contrasted with eternal glory.

1 Therefore seeing we have this ministry, as we have received [amercy](#note1a), we faint not;

2 But have [arenounced](#note2a) the hidden things of [bdishonesty](#note2b), not walking in craftiness, nor handling the word of God [cdeceitfully](#note2c); but by manifestation of the [dtruth](#note2d) commending ourselves to every man’s [econscience](#note2e) in the sight of God.

3 But if our gospel be hid, it is hid to them that are lost:

4 In whom the [agod](#note4a) of this [bworld](#note4b) hath [cblinded](#note4c) the [dminds](#note4d) of them which [ebelieve not](#note4e), lest the [flight](#note4f) of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the [gimage](#note4g) of [hGod](#note4h), should shine unto them.

5 For we [apreach](#note5a) not ourselves, but Christ Jesus the Lord; and ourselves your [bservants](#note5b) for Jesus’ sake.

6 For God, who [acommanded](#note6a) the light to shine out of darkness, hath [bshined](#note6b) in our hearts, to give the [clight](#note6c) of the knowledge of the [dglory](#note6d) of God in the face of Jesus Christ.

7 But we have this treasure in earthen vessels, that the excellency of the [apower](#note7a) may be of God, and not of us.

8 We are [atroubled](#note8a) on every side, yet not distressed; we are perplexed, but not in [bdespair](#note8b);

9 [aPersecuted](#note9a), but not forsaken; cast down, but not [bdestroyed](#note9b);

10 Always bearing about in the body the dying of the Lord Jesus, that the life also of Jesus might be made manifest in our body.

11 For we which live are alway delivered unto [adeath](#note11a) for Jesus’ sake, that the life also of Jesus might be made manifest in our [bmortal](#note11b) flesh.

12 So then death worketh in us, but life in you.

13 We having the same spirit of faith, according as it is written, I [abelieved](#note13a), and therefore have I spoken; we also believe, and therefore [bspeak](#note13b);

14 Knowing that he which raised up the Lord Jesus shall raise up us also by Jesus, and shall present us with you.

15 For all things are for your sakes, that the abundant grace might through the [athanksgiving](#note15a) of many redound to the glory of God.

16 For which cause we faint not; but though our outward man perish, yet the [ainward](#note16a) man is renewed day by day.

17 For our light [aaffliction](#note17a), which is but for a moment, worketh for us a far more exceeding and [beternal](#note17b) [cweight](#note17c) of glory;

18 While we look not at the things which are [aseen](#note18a), but at the things which are not seen: for the things which are seen are temporal; but the things which are not [bseen](#note18b) are [ceternal](#note18c).

Chapter 5

Saints walk by faith and seek tabernacles of immortal glory—The gospel reconciles man to God—God’s ministers carry the word of reconciliation to the world.

1 For we know that if our earthly house of this tabernacle were dissolved, we have a building of God, an house not made with hands, eternal in the heavens.

2 For in this we groan, earnestly desiring to be [aclothed](#note2a) upon with our house which is from heaven:

3 If so be that being clothed we shall not be found naked.

4 For we that are in this tabernacle do [agroan](#note4a), being burdened: not for that we would be unclothed, but clothed upon, that [bmortality](#note4b) might be swallowed up of life.

5 Now he that hath wrought us for the selfsame thing is God, who also hath given unto us the earnest of the Spirit.

6 Therefore we are always [aconfident](#note6a), knowing that, whilst we are at home in the body, we are absent from the Lord:

7 (For we walk by [afaith](#note7a), not by [bsight](#note7b):)

8 We are confident, I say, and willing rather to be absent from the body, and to be present with the Lord.

9 Wherefore we [alabour](#note9a), that, whether present or absent, we may be accepted of him.

10 For we must all appear before the [ajudgment](#note10a) seat of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, [baccording](#note10b) to that he hath [cdone](#note10c), whether it be good or bad.

11 Knowing therefore the terror of the Lord, we persuade men; but we are made manifest unto God; and I trust also are made manifest in your [aconsciences](#note11a).

12 For we commend not ourselves again unto you, but give you occasion to glory on our behalf, that ye may have somewhat to answer them which glory in appearance, and not in heart.

13 [aFor](#note13a) whether we be beside ourselves, it is to God: or whether we be sober, it is for your cause.

14 For the love of Christ constraineth us; because we thus judge, that if [aone](#note14a) [bdied](#note14b) for all, then were all dead:

15 And that he died for all, that they which live should not henceforth [alive](#note15a) unto themselves, but unto him which died for them, and rose again.

16 [aWherefore](#note16a) henceforth know we no man after the flesh: yea, though we have known Christ after the flesh, yet now henceforth know we him no more.

17 Therefore if any man be [ain Christ](#note17a), he is a [bnew](#note17b) creature: [cold](#note17c) things are [dpassed](#note17d) away; behold, all things are become [enew](#note17e).

18 And all things are of God, who hath [areconciled](#note18a) us to himself by Jesus Christ, and hath given to us the ministry of reconciliation;

19 To wit, that God was in Christ, [areconciling](#note19a) the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them; and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation.

20 Now then we are [aambassadors](#note20a) for Christ, as though God did beseech you by us: we pray you in Christ’s stead, be ye reconciled to God.

21 For he hath made him to be [asin](#note21a) for us, who knew no [bsin](#note21b); that we might be made the [crighteousness](#note21c) of God in him.

Chapter 7

Godly sorrow for sin leads to repentance—The sorrow of the world leads to death.

1 Having therefore these promises, dearly beloved, let us [acleanse](#note1a) ourselves from all [bfilthiness](#note1b) of the flesh and spirit, perfecting [choliness](#note1c) in the fear of God.

2 Receive us; we have [awronged](#note2a) no man, we have corrupted no man, we have [bdefrauded](#note2b) no man.

3 I speak not this to condemn you: for I have said before, that ye are in our hearts to die and live with you.

4 Great is my boldness of speech toward you, great is my glorying of you: I am filled with [acomfort](#note4a), I am exceeding [bjoyful](#note4b) in all our [ctribulation](#note4c).

5 For, when we were come into Macedonia, our flesh had no rest, but we were [atroubled](#note5a) on every side; without were fightings, within were [bfears](#note5b).

6 Nevertheless God, that comforteth those that are cast down, comforted us by the coming of Titus;

7 And not by his coming only, but by the consolation wherewith he was [acomforted](#note7a) in you, when he told us your earnest desire, your mourning, your fervent mind toward me; so that I rejoiced the more.

8 For though I made you sorry with a letter, I do not repent, though I did repent: for I perceive that the same epistle hath made you sorry, though it were but for a season.

9 Now I rejoice, not that ye were made sorry, but that ye sorrowed to repentance: for ye were made [asorry](#note9a) after a godly manner, that ye might receive damage by us in nothing.

10 For [agodly](#note10a) [bsorrow](#note10b) worketh [crepentance](#note10c) to [dsalvation](#note10d) not to be repented of: but the [esorrow](#note10e) of the world worketh death.

11 For behold this selfsame thing, that ye sorrowed after a godly sort, what carefulness it wrought in you, yea, what clearing of yourselves, yea, what indignation, yea, what fear, yea, what vehement desire, yea, what zeal, yea, what revenge! In all things ye have approved yourselves to be clear in this matter.

12 Wherefore, though I wrote unto you, I did it not for his cause that had done the wrong, nor for his cause that suffered wrong, but that our care for you in the sight of God might appear unto you.

13 Therefore we were comforted in your comfort: yea, and exceedingly the more joyed we for the joy of Titus, because his spirit was [arefreshed](#note13a) by you all.

14 For if I have [aboasted](#note14a) any thing to him of you, I am not ashamed; but as we spake all things to you in truth, even so our [bboasting](#note14b), which I made before Titus, is found a truth.

15 And his [ainward affection](#note15a) is more abundant toward you, whilst he remembereth the [bobedience](#note15b) of you all, how with fear and trembling ye received him.

16 I rejoice therefore that I have confidence in you in all things.

Chapter 10

Bring every thought into obedience—Paul glories in the Lord.

1 Now I Paul myself beseech you by the [ameekness](#note1a) and gentleness of Christ, who in presence am base among you, but being absent am bold toward you:

2 But I beseech you, that I may not be bold when I am present with that confidence, wherewith I think to be bold against some, which think of us as if we walked according to the flesh.

3 For though we walk in the flesh, we do not [awar](#note3a) after the flesh:

4 (For the [aweapons](#note4a) of our [bwarfare](#note4b) are not carnal, but mighty through God to the pulling down of strong holds;)

5 Casting down imaginations, and every high thing that exalteth itself against the knowledge of God, and bringing into captivity every [athought](#note5a) to the [bobedience](#note5b) of Christ;

6 And having in a readiness to revenge all [adisobedience](#note6a), when your [bobedience](#note6b) is fulfilled.

7 Do ye look on things after the outward [aappearance](#note7a)? If any man trust to himself that he is Christ’s, let him of himself think this again, that, as he is Christ’s, even so are we [bChrist’s](#note7b).

8 For though I should boast somewhat more of our [aauthority](#note8a), which the Lord hath given us for [bedification](#note8b), and not for your destruction, I should not be ashamed:

9 That I may not seem as if I would terrify you by letters.

10 For his letters, say they, are weighty and powerful; but his [abodily](#note10a) presence is weak, and his speech [bcontemptible](#note10b).

11 Let such an one think this, that, such as we are in word by letters when we are absent, such will we be also in deed when we are present.

12 For we dare not make ourselves of the number, or compare ourselves with some that commend themselves: but they measuring themselves by themselves, and comparing themselves among themselves, are not wise.

13 But we will not [aboast](#note13a) of things without our measure, but according to the measure of the rule which God hath distributed to us, a measure to reach even unto you.

14 For we stretch not ourselves beyond our measure, as though we reached not unto you: for we are come as far as to you also in preaching the gospel of Christ:

15 Not boasting of things without our measure, that is, of other men’s [alabours](#note15a); but having hope, when your faith is increased, that we shall be enlarged by you according to our rule abundantly,

16 To preach the gospel in the regions beyond you, and not to boast in another man’s line of things made ready to our hand.

Chapter 11

Maintain the simplicity that is in Christ—Satan sends forth false apostles—Paul glories in his sufferings for Christ.

1 Would to God ye could bear with me a little in my folly: and indeed bear with me.

2 For I am [ajealous](#note2a) over you with godly jealousy: for I have [bespoused](#note2b) you to one husband, that I may present you as a chaste virgin to Christ.

3 But I fear, lest by any means, as the [aserpent](#note3a) [bbeguiled](#note3b) Eve through his [csubtilty](#note3c), so your minds should be [dcorrupted](#note3d) from the [esimplicity](#note3e) that is in Christ.

4 For if he that cometh preacheth another [aJesus](#note4a), whom we have not preached, or if ye receive another spirit, which ye have not received, or another [bgospel](#note4b), which ye have not accepted, ye might well bear with him.

5 For I suppose I was not a whit behind the very chiefest apostles.

6 But though I be [arude](#note6a) in speech, yet not in knowledge; but we have been throughly made manifest among you in all things.

7 Have I committed an offence in abasing myself that ye might be exalted, because I have preached to you the gospel of God [afreely](#note7a)?

8 I [arobbed other churches](#note8a), taking [bwages](#note8b) of them, to do you service.

9 And when I was present with you, and wanted, I was [achargeable](#note9a) to no man: for that which was [blacking](#note9b) to me the brethren which came from Macedonia [csupplied](#note9c): and in all things I have kept myself from being burdensome unto you, and so will I keep myself.

10 As the truth of Christ is in me, no man shall stop me of this boasting in the regions of Achaia.

11 Wherefore? because I love you not? God knoweth.

12 But what I do, that I will do, that I may cut off occasion from them which desire occasion; that wherein they [aglory](#note12a), they may be found even as we.

13 For such are [afalse apostles](#note13a), deceitful workers, transforming themselves into the apostles of Christ.

14 And no marvel; for [aSatan](#note14a) himself is transformed into an [bangel](#note14b) of light.

15 Therefore it is no great thing if his ministers also be transformed as the ministers of righteousness; whose [aend](#note15a) shall be according to their works.

16 I say again, Let no man think me a fool; if otherwise, yet as a fool [areceive](#note16a) me, that I may [bboast](#note16b) myself a little.

17 That which I speak, I speak it not after the Lord, but as it were foolishly, in this confidence of boasting.

18 Seeing that many glory after the flesh, I will glory also.

19 For ye suffer fools gladly, seeing ye yourselves are wise.

20 For ye suffer, if a man bring you into [abondage](#note20a), if a man devour you, if a man take of you, if a man exalt himself, if a man smite you on the face.

21 I speak as concerning [areproach](#note21a), as though we had been weak. Howbeit whereinsoever any is bold, (I speak foolishly,) I am bold also.

22 Are they [aHebrews](#note22a)? so am I. Are they [bIsraelites](#note22b)? so am I. Are they the seed of Abraham? so am I.

23 Are they [aministers](#note23a) of Christ? (I speak as a fool) [bI](#note23b) am more; in [clabours](#note23c) more abundant, in [dstripes](#note23d) above measure, in [eprisons](#note23e) more frequent, in deaths oft.

24 Of the Jews five times received I [aforty](#note24a) stripes save one.

25 Thrice was I [abeaten](#note25a) with rods, once was I stoned, thrice I suffered shipwreck, a night and a day I have been in the deep;

26 In journeyings often, in perils of waters, in perils of robbers, in perils by mine own [acountrymen](#note26a), in perils by the [bheathen](#note26b), in [cperils](#note26c) in the city, in perils in the wilderness, in perils in the sea, in perils among false brethren;

27 In [aweariness](#note27a) and [bpainfulness](#note27b), in watchings often, in hunger and thirst, in fastings often, in cold and nakedness.

28 Beside those things that are without, that which cometh upon me daily, the care of all the churches.

29 Who is weak, and I am not [aweak](#note29a)? who is offended, and I [bburn not](#note29b)?

30 If I must needs glory, I will glory of the things which concern mine infirmities.

31 The God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, which is blessed for evermore, knoweth that I lie not.

32 In Damascus the governor under Aretas the king kept the city of the Damascenes with a garrison, desirous to [aapprehend](#note32a) me:

33 And through a window in a basket was I let down by the wall, and escaped his hands.

Chapter 12

Paul is caught up to the third heaven—The Lord gives men weaknesses that they may triumph over them—Paul manifests the signs of an Apostle.

1 It is not expedient for me doubtless to [aglory](#note1a). I will come to [bvisions](#note1b) and [crevelations](#note1c) of the Lord.

2 [aI](#note2a) knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the [bbody](#note2b), I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) such an one caught up to the [cthird](#note2c) [dheaven](#note2d).

3 And I knew such a man, (whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;)

4 How that he was caught up into [aparadise](#note4a), and heard [bunspeakable](#note4b) words, which it is [cnot](#note4c) [dlawful](#note4d) for a man to utter.

5 Of such an one will I glory: yet of myself I will not [aglory](#note5a), but in mine infirmities.

6 For though I would desire to glory, I shall not be a fool; for I will say the truth: but now I forbear, lest any man should think of me above that which he seeth me to be, or that he heareth of me.

7 And lest I should be [aexalted](#note7a) above measure through the abundance of the revelations, there was given to me a [bthorn](#note7b) in the flesh, the messenger of Satan to buffet me, lest I should be exalted above measure.

8 For this thing I besought the Lord thrice, that it might depart from me.

9 And he said unto me, My [agrace](#note9a) is sufficient for thee: for my [bstrength](#note9b) is made perfect in [cweakness](#note9c). Most gladly therefore will I rather glory in my infirmities, that the power of Christ may [drest](#note9d) upon me.

10 Therefore I take pleasure in [ainfirmities](#note10a), in reproaches, in necessities, in [bpersecutions](#note10b), in distresses for Christ’s sake: for when I am [cweak](#note10c), then am I [dstrong](#note10d).

11 I am become a fool in glorying; ye have compelled me: for I ought to have been commended of you: for in nothing am I behind the very [achiefest](#note11a) apostles, though I be nothing.

12 Truly the [asigns](#note12a) of an apostle were wrought among you in all patience, in signs, and wonders, and mighty deeds.

13 For what is it wherein ye were inferior to other churches, except it be that I myself was not [aburdensome](#note13a) to you? forgive me this wrong.

14 Behold, the third time I am ready to come to you; and I will not be burdensome to you: for I [aseek](#note14a) not yours, but you: for the children ought not to lay up for the parents, but the parents for the children.

15 And I will very gladly spend and be spent for you; though the more abundantly I love you, the less I be loved.

16 But be it so, I did not burden you: nevertheless, being crafty, I caught you with guile.

17 Did I make a gain of you by any of them whom I sent unto you?

18 I desired Titus, and with him I sent a brother. Did Titus make a gain of you? walked we not in the same spirit? walked we not in the same steps?

19 Again, think ye that we excuse ourselves unto you? we speak before God in Christ: but we do all things, dearly beloved, for your [aedifying](#note19a).

20 For I fear, lest, when I come, I shall not find you such as I would, and that I shall be found unto you such as ye would not: lest there be debates, envyings, wraths, [astrifes](#note20a), [bbackbitings](#note20b), whisperings, swellings, [ctumults](#note20c):

21 And lest, when I come again, my God will humble me among you, and that I shall bewail many which have sinned already, and have not repented of the uncleanness and fornication and lasciviousness which they have committed.

Chapter 13

Saints should test themselves as to righteousness—Be perfect and of one mind; live in peace.

1 This is the third time I am coming to you. In the mouth of two or three [awitnesses](#note1a) shall every word be established.

2 I told you before, and foretell you, as if I were present, the second time; and being absent now I write to them which heretofore have sinned, and to all other, that, if I come again, I will not spare:

3 Since ye seek a proof of Christ speaking in me, which to you-ward is not weak, but is mighty in you.

4 For though he was [acrucified](#note4a) through weakness, yet he liveth by the power of God. For we also are weak in him, but we shall live with him by the power of God toward you.

5 [aExamine](#note5a) yourselves, whether ye be in the faith; prove your own selves. Know ye not your own selves, how that [bJesus](#note5b) [cChrist](#note5c) is in you, except ye be reprobates?

6 But I trust that ye shall know that we are not reprobates.

7 Now I pray to God that ye do no evil; not that we should appear approved, but that ye should do that which is [ahonest](#note7a), though we be as reprobates.

8 For we can do nothing against the truth, but for the truth.

9 For we are glad, when we are [aweak](#note9a), and ye are strong: and this also we wish, even your perfection.

10 Therefore I write these things being absent, lest being present I should use sharpness, according to the [apower](#note10a) which the Lord hath given me to edification, and not to destruction.

11 Finally, brethren, farewell. Be [aperfect](#note11a), be of good comfort, be of [bone](#note11b) [cmind](#note11c), live in [dpeace](#note11d); and the God of [elove](#note11e) and peace shall be with you.

12 Greet one another with an holy [akiss](#note12a).

13 All the saints salute you.

14 The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, be with you all. Amen.
