Chapter 1

(June 1830)

God reveals Himself to Moses—Moses is transfigured—He is confronted by Satan—Moses sees many inhabited worlds—Worlds without number were created by the Son—God’s work and glory is to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man.

1 The words of God, which he [aspake](#note1a) unto Moses at a time when Moses was caught up into an exceedingly high [bmountain](#note1b),

2 And he [asaw](#note2a) God [bface to face](#note2b), and he [ctalked](#note2c) with him, and the [dglory](#note2d) of God was upon Moses; therefore Moses could endure his presence.

3 And God spake unto Moses, saying: Behold, I am the Lord God [aAlmighty](#note3a), and [bEndless](#note3b) is my [cname](#note3c); for I am without beginning of days or end of years; and is not this endless?

4 And, behold, thou art my son; wherefore [alook](#note4a), and I will show thee the [bworkmanship](#note4b) of mine [chands](#note4c); but not all, for my [dworks](#note4d) are without [eend](#note4e), and also my [fwords](#note4f), for they never cease.

5 Wherefore, no man can behold all my [aworks](#note5a), except he behold all my [bglory](#note5b); and no man can [cbehold](#note5c) all my [dglory](#note5d), and afterwards remain in the flesh on the earth.

6 And I have a work for thee, Moses, my son; and thou art in the [asimilitude](#note6a) of mine [bOnly](#note6b) [cBegotten](#note6c); and mine Only Begotten is and shall be the [dSavior](#note6d), for he is full of [egrace](#note6e) and [ftruth](#note6f); but there is [gno](#note6g) God beside me, and all things are present with me, for I [hknow](#note6h) them all.

7 And now, behold, this one thing I show unto thee, Moses, my son, for thou art in the world, and now I show it unto thee.

8 And it came to pass that Moses looked, and beheld the [aworld](#note8a) upon which he was created; and Moses [bbeheld](#note8b) the world and the ends thereof, and all the children of men which are, and which were created; of the same he greatly [cmarveled](#note8c) and wondered.

9 And the [apresence](#note9a) of God withdrew from Moses, that his [bglory](#note9b) was not upon Moses; and Moses was left unto himself. And as he was left unto himself, he [cfell](#note9c) unto the earth.

10 And it came to pass that it was for the space of many hours before Moses did again receive his natural [astrength](#note10a) like unto man; and he said unto himself: Now, for this cause I know that [bman](#note10b) is [cnothing](#note10c), which thing I never had supposed.

11 But now mine own eyes have [abeheld](#note11a) God; but not my [bnatural](#note11b), but my [cspiritual](#note11c) eyes, for my [dnatural](#note11d) eyes could not have [ebeheld](#note11e); for I should have [fwithered](#note11f) and [gdied](#note11g) in his presence; but his [hglory](#note11h) was upon me; and I beheld his [iface](#note11i), for I was [jtransfigured](#note11j) before him.

12 And it came to pass that when Moses had said these words, behold, [aSatan](#note12a) came [btempting](#note12b) him, saying: Moses, son of man, worship me.

13 And it came to pass that Moses looked upon Satan and said: Who art thou? For behold, I am a [ason](#note13a) of God, in the similitude of his Only Begotten; and where is thy [bglory](#note13b), that I should worship thee?

14 For behold, I could not look upon God, except his [aglory](#note14a) should come upon me, and I were transfigured before him. But I [bcan](#note14b) look upon thee in the natural man. Is it not so, surely?

15 Blessed be the name of my God, for his [aSpirit](#note15a) hath not altogether withdrawn from me, or else where is thy glory, for it is darkness unto me? And I can judge between thee and God; for God said unto me: [bWorship](#note15b) God, for him only shalt thou [cserve](#note15c).

16 Get thee hence, Satan; deceive me not; for God said unto me: Thou art after the [asimilitude](#note16a) of mine Only Begotten.

17 And he also gave me commandments when he [acalled](#note17a) unto me out of the burning [bbush](#note17b), saying: [cCall](#note17c) upon God in the name of mine Only Begotten, and worship me.

18 And again Moses said: I will not cease to call upon God, I have other things to inquire of him: for his [aglory](#note18a) has been upon me, wherefore I can judge between him and thee. [bDepart](#note18b) hence, Satan.

19 And now, when Moses had said these words, [aSatan](#note19a) cried with a loud voice, and ranted upon the earth, and commanded, saying: I am the [bOnly Begotten](#note19b), worship me.

20 And it came to pass that Moses began to [afear](#note20a) exceedingly; and as he began to fear, he saw the bitterness of [bhell](#note20b). Nevertheless, [ccalling](#note20c) upon God, he received [dstrength](#note20d), and he commanded, saying: Depart from me, Satan, for this one God only will I worship, which is the God of [eglory](#note20e).

21 And now Satan began to tremble, and the earth shook; and Moses received strength, and called upon God, saying: In the name of the Only Begotten, [adepart](#note21a) hence, [bSatan](#note21b).

22 And it came to pass that Satan cried with a loud voice, with weeping, and wailing, and [agnashing](#note22a) of teeth; and he departed hence, even from the presence of Moses, that he beheld him not.

23 And now of this thing Moses bore record; but because of [awickedness](#note23a) it is [bnot](#note23b) had among the children of men.

24 And it came to pass that when Satan had departed from the presence of Moses, that Moses lifted up his eyes unto heaven, being filled with the [aHoly Ghost](#note24a), which beareth record of the Father and the Son;

25 And calling upon the name of God, he beheld his [aglory](#note25a) again, for it was upon him; and he heard a [bvoice](#note25b), saying: Blessed art thou, Moses, for I, the Almighty, have [cchosen](#note25c) thee, and thou shalt be made stronger than many [dwaters](#note25d); for they shall obey thy [ecommand](#note25e) as if thou wert [fGod](#note25f).

26 And lo, I am [awith thee](#note26a), even unto the end of thy days; for thou shalt [bdeliver](#note26b) my people from [cbondage](#note26c), even [dIsrael](#note26d) my [echosen](#note26e).

27 And it came to pass, as the voice was still speaking, Moses cast his eyes and [abeheld](#note27a) the earth, yea, even all of it; and there was not a particle of it which he did not behold, [bdiscerning](#note27b) it by the [cSpirit](#note27c) of God.

28 And he beheld also the inhabitants thereof, and there was not a [asoul](#note28a) which he beheld not; and he discerned them by the Spirit of God; and their numbers were great, even numberless as the sand upon the sea shore.

29 And he beheld many lands; and each land was called [aearth](#note29a), and there were [binhabitants](#note29b) on the face thereof.

30 And it came to pass that Moses called upon God, saying: [aTell](#note30a) me, I pray thee, why these things are so, and by what thou madest them?

31 And behold, the glory of the Lord was upon Moses, so that Moses stood in the presence of God, and talked with him [aface to face](#note31a). And the Lord God said unto Moses: For mine own [bpurpose](#note31b) have I made these things. Here is [cwisdom](#note31c) and it remaineth in me.

32 And by the [aword](#note32a) of my power, have I created them, which is mine Only Begotten Son, who is full of [bgrace](#note32b) and truth.

33 And [aworlds](#note33a) without number have I [bcreated](#note33b); and I also created them for mine own purpose; and by the [cSon](#note33c) I [dcreated](#note33d) them, which is mine [eOnly Begotten](#note33e).

34 And the [afirst](#note34a) man of all men have I called [bAdam](#note34b), which is [cmany](#note34c).

35 But only an account of this earth, and the inhabitants thereof, give I unto you. For behold, there are many worlds that have passed away by the word of my power. And there are many that now stand, and innumerable are they unto man; but all things are numbered unto me, for they are mine and I [aknow](#note35a) them.

36 And it came to pass that Moses spake unto the Lord, saying: Be merciful unto thy servant, O God, and [atell](#note36a) me concerning this earth, and the inhabitants thereof, and also the heavens, and then thy servant will be content.

37 And the Lord God spake unto Moses, saying: The [aheavens](#note37a), they are many, and they cannot be numbered unto man; but they are numbered unto me, for they are mine.

38 And as one earth shall pass away, and the heavens thereof even so shall another come; and there is no [aend](#note38a) to my works, neither to my words.

39 For behold, this is my [awork](#note39a) and my [bglory](#note39b)—to bring to pass the [cimmortality](#note39c) and [deternal](#note39d) [elife](#note39e) of man.

40 And now, Moses, my son, I will speak unto thee concerning this earth upon which thou standest; and thou shalt [awrite](#note40a) the things which I shall speak.

41 And in a day when the children of men shall esteem my words as [anaught](#note41a) and [btake](#note41b) many of them from the [cbook](#note41c) which thou shalt write, behold, I will raise up another [dlike](#note41d) unto thee; and they shall be [ehad](#note41e) again among the children of men—among as many as shall believe.

42 (These words were [aspoken](#note42a) unto Moses in the mount, the name of which shall not be known among the children of men. And now they are spoken unto you. Show them not unto any except them that believe. Even so. Amen.)

Chapter 2

(June–October 1830)

God creates the heavens and the earth—All forms of life are created—God makes man and gives him dominion over all else.

1 And it came to pass that the Lord spake unto Moses, saying: Behold, I [areveal](#note1a) unto you concerning this [bheaven](#note1b), and this [cearth](#note1c); [dwrite](#note1d) the words which I speak. I am the Beginning and the End, the [eAlmighty God](#note1e); by mine [fOnly Begotten](#note1f) I [gcreated](#note1g) these things; yea, in the beginning I [hcreated](#note1h) the [iheaven](#note1i), and the earth upon which thou standest.

2 And the earth was without [aform](#note2a), and void; and I caused [bdarkness](#note2b) to come up upon the face of the deep; and my [cSpirit](#note2c) [dmoved](#note2d) upon the face of the water; for I am God.

3 And I, God, said: Let there be [alight](#note3a); and there was light.

4 And I, God, saw the light; and that light was [agood](#note4a). And I, God, divided the [blight](#note4b) from the darkness.

5 And I, God, called the light Day; and the darkness, I called Night; and this I did by the [aword](#note5a) of my power, and it was done as I [bspake](#note5b); and the evening and the morning were the first [cday](#note5c).

6 And again, I, God, said: Let there be a [afirmament](#note6a) in the midst of the water, and it was so, even as I spake; and I said: Let it divide the waters from the waters; and it was done;

7 And I, God, made the firmament and divided the [awaters](#note7a), yea, the great waters under the firmament from the waters which were above the firmament, and it was so even as I spake.

8 And I, God, called the firmament [aHeaven](#note8a); and the evening and the morning were the second day.

9 And I, God, said: Let the [awaters](#note9a) under the heaven be gathered together unto [bone](#note9b) place, and it was so; and I, God, said: Let there be dry land; and it was so.

10 And I, God, called the dry land [aEarth](#note10a); and the gathering together of the waters, called I the Sea; and I, God, saw that all things which I had made were good.

11 And I, God, said: Let the earth bring forth [agrass](#note11a), the herb yielding seed, the fruit tree yielding fruit, after his kind, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed should be in itself upon the earth, and it was so even as I spake.

12 And the earth brought forth grass, every herb yielding seed after his kind, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed should be in itself, after his [akind](#note12a); and I, God, saw that all things which I had made were good;

13 And the evening and the morning were the third day.

14 And I, God, said: Let there be [alights](#note14a) in the firmament of the heaven, to divide the day from the night, and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days, and for years;

15 And let them be for lights in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth; and it was so.

16 And I, God, made two great lights; the greater [alight](#note16a) to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night, and the [bgreater](#note16b) light was the sun, and the lesser light was the moon; and the stars also were made even according to my word.

17 And I, God, set them in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth,

18 And the [asun](#note18a) to rule over the day, and the moon to rule over the night, and to divide the light from the [bdarkness](#note18b); and I, God, saw that all things which I had made were good;

19 And the evening and the morning were the fourth day.

20 And I, God, said: Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl which may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven.

21 And I, God, created great [awhales](#note21a), and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind; and I, God, saw that all things which I had created were good.

22 And I, God, blessed them, saying: Be fruitful, and [amultiply](#note22a), and fill the waters in the sea; and let fowl multiply in the earth;

23 And the evening and the morning were the fifth day.

24 And I, God, said: Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind, cattle, and creeping things, and beasts of the earth after their kind, and it was so;

25 And I, God, made the beasts of the earth after their kind, and cattle after their kind, and everything which creepeth upon the earth after his kind; and I, God, saw that all these things were good.

26 And I, God, said unto mine [aOnly Begotten](#note26a), which was with me from the [bbeginning](#note26b): Let [cus](#note26c) [dmake](#note26d) man in our [eimage](#note26e), after our likeness; and it was so. And I, God, said: Let them have [fdominion](#note26f) over the fishes of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth.

27 And I, God, created man in mine own [aimage](#note27a), in the image of mine Only Begotten created I him; male and female created I them.

28 And I, God, blessed them, and said unto them: Be [afruitful](#note28a), and [bmultiply](#note28b), and replenish the earth, and subdue it, and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.

29 And I, God, said unto man: Behold, I have given you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree in the which shall be the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for [ameat](#note29a).

30 And to every beast of the earth, and to every fowl of the air, and to everything that creepeth upon the earth, wherein I grant life, there shall be given every clean herb for meat; and it was so, even as I spake.

31 And I, God, saw everything that I had made, and, behold, all things which I had made were very [agood](#note31a); and the evening and the morning were the [bsixth](#note31b) day.

Chapter 3

(June–October 1830)

God created all things spiritually before they were naturally upon the earth—He created man, the first flesh, upon the earth—Woman is a help meet for man.

1 Thus the [aheaven](#note1a) and the earth were finished, and all the [bhost](#note1b) of them.

2 And on the seventh day I, God, ended my work, and all things which I had made; and I [arested](#note2a) on the [bseventh day](#note2b) from all my work, and all things which I had made were finished, and I, God, saw that they were good;

3 And I, God, [ablessed](#note3a) the seventh day, and [bsanctified](#note3b) it; because that in it I had rested from all my [cwork](#note3c) which I, God, had created and made.

4 And now, behold, I say unto you, that these are the generations of the heaven and of the earth, when they were [acreated](#note4a), in the day that I, the Lord God, made the [bheaven](#note4b) and the earth,

5 And every plant of the field before it was in the earth, and every herb of the field before it grew. For I, the Lord God, [acreated](#note5a) all things, of which I have spoken, [bspiritually](#note5b), before they were [cnaturally](#note5c) upon the face of the earth. For I, the Lord God, had not caused it to rain upon the face of the earth. And I, the Lord God, had [dcreated](#note5d) all the children of men; and not yet a man to till the [eground](#note5e); for in [fheaven](#note5f) [gcreated](#note5g) I them; and there was not yet flesh upon the earth, neither in the water, neither in the air;

6 But I, the Lord God, spake, and there went up a [amist](#note6a) from the earth, and watered the whole face of the ground.

7 And I, the Lord God, formed man from the [adust](#note7a) of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the [bbreath of life](#note7b); and [cman](#note7c) became a living [dsoul](#note7d), the [efirst](#note7e) flesh upon the earth, the first man also; nevertheless, all things were before created; but spiritually were they created and made according to my word.

8 And I, the Lord God, planted a garden eastward in [aEden](#note8a), and there I put the man whom I had formed.

9 And out of the ground made I, the Lord God, to grow every tree, [anaturally](#note9a), that is pleasant to the sight of man; and man could behold it. And it became also a [bliving](#note9b) soul. For it was spiritual in the day that I created it; for it remaineth in the sphere in which I, God, created it, yea, even all things which I prepared for the use of man; and man saw that it was good for food. And I, the Lord God, planted the [ctree of life](#note9c) also in the midst of the garden, and also the tree of knowledge of good and evil.

10 And I, the Lord God, caused a river to go out of [aEden](#note10a) to water the garden; and from thence it was parted, and became into four [bheads](#note10b).

11 And I, the Lord God, called the name of the first Pison, and it compasseth the whole land of [aHavilah](#note11a), where I, the Lord God, created much gold;

12 And the gold of that land was good, and there was bdellium and the [aonyx](#note12a) stone.

13 And the name of the second river was called Gihon; the same that compasseth the whole land of [aEthiopia](#note13a).

14 And the name of the third river was Hiddekel; that which goeth toward the east of Assyria. And the fourth river was the Euphrates.

15 And I, the Lord God, took the man, and put him into the Garden of [aEden](#note15a), to dress it, and to keep it.

16 And I, the Lord God, commanded the man, saying: Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat,

17 But of the tree of the [aknowledge](#note17a) of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it, nevertheless, thou mayest [bchoose](#note17b) for thyself, for it is given unto thee; but, remember that I [cforbid](#note17c) it, for in the [dday](#note17d) thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely [edie](#note17e).

18 And I, the Lord God, said unto mine [aOnly Begotten](#note18a), that it was not good that the man should be [balone](#note18b); wherefore, I will make an [chelp](#note18c) meet for him.

19 And out of the ground I, the Lord God, formed every [abeast](#note19a) of the field, and every fowl of the air; and commanded that they should come unto Adam, to see what he would call them; and they were also living souls; for I, God, breathed into them the [bbreath of life](#note19b), and commanded that whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that should be the name thereof.

20 And Adam gave [anames](#note20a) to all cattle, and to the fowl of the air, and to every beast of the field; but as for Adam, there was not found an help meet for him.

21 And I, the Lord God, caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam; and he slept, and I took one of his ribs and closed up the flesh in the stead thereof;

22 And the rib which I, the Lord God, had taken from man, made I a [awoman](#note22a), and brought her unto the man.

23 And [aAdam](#note23a) said: This I know now is bone of my bones, and [bflesh](#note23b) of my flesh; she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of man.

24 Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall [acleave](#note24a) unto his [bwife](#note24b); and [cthey](#note24c) shall be [done](#note24d) flesh.

25 And they were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed.

Chapter 4

(June–October 1830)

How Satan became the devil—He tempts Eve—Adam and Eve fall, and death enters the world.

1 And I, the [aLord God](#note1a), spake unto Moses, saying: That [bSatan](#note1b), whom thou hast commanded in the name of mine Only Begotten, is the same which was from the [cbeginning](#note1c), and he came before me, saying—Behold, here am I, send me, I will be thy son, and I will [dredeem](#note1d) all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely [eI](#note1e) will do it; wherefore [fgive](#note1f) me thine honor.

2 But, behold, my Beloved [aSon](#note2a), which was my Beloved and [bChosen](#note2b) from the beginning, said unto me—[cFather](#note2c), thy [dwill be](#note2d) done, and the [eglory](#note2e) be thine forever.

3 Wherefore, because that [aSatan](#note3a) [brebelled](#note3b) against me, and sought to destroy the [cagency](#note3c) of man, which I, the Lord God, had given him, and also, that I should give unto him mine own power; by the power of mine Only Begotten, I caused that he should be [dcast down](#note3d);

4 And he became [aSatan](#note4a), yea, even the [bdevil](#note4b), the father of all [clies](#note4c), to [ddeceive](#note4d) and to blind men, and to lead them [ecaptive](#note4e) at his will, even as many as would not [fhearken](#note4f) unto my voice.

5 And now the serpent was more [asubtle](#note5a) than any beast of the field which I, the Lord God, had made.

6 And [aSatan](#note6a) put it into the heart of the serpent, (for he had drawn away [bmany](#note6b) after him,) and he sought also to [cbeguile](#note6c) Eve, for he [dknew not](#note6d) the [emind](#note6e) of God, wherefore he sought to destroy the world.

7 And he said unto the woman: Yea, hath God said—Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden? (And he spake by the mouth of the serpent.)

8 And the woman said unto the serpent: We may eat of the fruit of the trees of the garden;

9 But of the fruit of the tree which thou beholdest in the midst of the garden, God hath said—Ye shall not eat of it, neither shall ye touch it, lest ye die.

10 And the serpent said unto the [awoman](#note10a): Ye shall not surely die;

11 For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your [aeyes](#note11a) shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, [bknowing](#note11b) good and evil.

12 And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it became pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be [adesired](#note12a) to make her wise, she took of the [bfruit](#note12b) thereof, and did [ceat](#note12c), and also gave unto her husband with her, and he did eat.

13 And the eyes of them both were opened, and they knew that they had been [anaked](#note13a). And they sewed fig leaves together and made themselves [baprons](#note13b).

14 And they heard the voice of the Lord God, as they were [awalking](#note14a) in the garden, in the cool of the day; and Adam and his wife went to hide themselves from the [bpresence](#note14b) of the Lord God amongst the trees of the garden.

15 And I, the Lord God, called unto Adam, and said unto him: Where [agoest](#note15a) thou?

16 And he said: I heard thy voice in the garden, and I was afraid, because I beheld that I was naked, and I hid myself.

17 And I, the Lord God, said unto Adam: Who told thee thou wast naked? Hast thou eaten of the tree whereof I commanded thee that thou shouldst not eat, if so thou shouldst surely [adie](#note17a)?

18 And the man said: The woman thou gavest me, and commandest that she should remain with me, she gave me of the fruit of the tree and I did eat.

19 And I, the Lord God, said unto the woman: What is this thing which thou hast done? And the woman said: The serpent [abeguiled](#note19a) me, and I did eat.

20 And I, the Lord God, said unto the serpent: Because thou hast done this thou shalt be [acursed](#note20a) above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life;

21 And I will put [aenmity](#note21a) between thee and the woman, between thy seed and her seed; and he shall [bbruise](#note21b) thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel.

22 Unto the woman, I, the Lord God, said: I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception. In [asorrow](#note22a) thou shalt bring forth children, and thy desire shall be to thy [bhusband](#note22b), and he shall rule over thee.

23 And unto Adam, I, the Lord God, said: Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the fruit of the tree of which I commanded thee, saying—Thou shalt not eat of it, [acursed](#note23a) shall be the ground for thy sake; in [bsorrow](#note23b) shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life.

24 Thorns also, and thistles shall it bring forth to thee, and thou shalt eat the herb of the field.

25 By the [asweat](#note25a) of thy [bface](#note25b) shalt thou eat bread, until thou shalt return unto the ground—for thou shalt surely die—for out of it wast thou taken: for [cdust](#note25c) thou wast, and unto dust shalt thou return.

26 And Adam called his wife’s name Eve, because she was the mother of all living; for thus have I, the Lord God, called the first of all women, which are [amany](#note26a).

27 Unto Adam, and also unto his wife, did I, the Lord God, make coats of [askins](#note27a), and [bclothed](#note27b) them.

28 And I, the Lord God, [asaid](#note28a) unto mine Only Begotten: Behold, the [bman](#note28b) is become as one of us to [cknow](#note28c) good and evil; and now lest he put forth his hand and [dpartake](#note28d) also of the [etree of life](#note28e), and eat and live forever,

29 Therefore I, the Lord God, will send him forth from the Garden of [aEden](#note29a), to till the ground from whence he was taken;

30 For as I, the Lord God, liveth, even so my [awords](#note30a) cannot return void, for as they go forth out of my mouth they must be fulfilled.

31 So I drove out the man, and I placed at the east of the Garden of [aEden](#note31a), [bcherubim](#note31b) and a flaming sword, which turned every way to keep the way of the tree of life.

32 (And these are the words which I spake unto my servant Moses, and they are true even as I will; and I have spoken them unto you. See thou show them unto no man, until I command you, except to them that believe. Amen.)

Chapter 5

(June–October 1830)

Adam and Eve bring forth children—Adam offers sacrifice and serves God—Cain and Abel are born—Cain rebels, loves Satan more than God, and becomes Perdition—Murder and wickedness spread—The gospel is preached from the beginning.

1 And it came to pass that after I, the Lord God, had driven them out, that Adam began to till the earth, and to have [adominion](#note1a) over all the beasts of the field, and to eat his bread by the sweat of his [bbrow](#note1b), as I the Lord had commanded him. And Eve, also, his wife, did [clabor](#note1c) with him.

2 And [aAdam](#note2a) knew his wife, and she bare unto him [bsons](#note2b) and [cdaughters](#note2c), and they began to [dmultiply](#note2d) and to replenish the earth.

3 And from that time forth, the sons and [adaughters](#note3a) of Adam began to divide two and two in the land, and to till the land, and to tend flocks, and they also begat sons and daughters.

4 And Adam and Eve, his wife, [acalled upon](#note4a) the name of the Lord, and they heard the voice of the Lord from the way toward the Garden of [bEden](#note4b), speaking unto them, and they saw him not; for they were shut out from his [cpresence](#note4c).

5 And he gave unto them commandments, that they should [aworship](#note5a) the Lord their God, and should offer the [bfirstlings](#note5b) of their [cflocks](#note5c), for an offering unto the Lord. And Adam was [dobedient](#note5d) unto the commandments of the Lord.

6 And after many days an [aangel](#note6a) of the Lord appeared unto Adam, saying: Why dost thou offer [bsacrifices](#note6b) unto the Lord? And Adam said unto him: I know not, save the Lord commanded me.

7 And then the angel spake, saying: This thing is a [asimilitude](#note7a) of the [bsacrifice](#note7b) of the Only Begotten of the Father, which is full of [cgrace](#note7c) and [dtruth](#note7d).

8 Wherefore, thou shalt do all that thou doest in the [aname](#note8a) of the Son, and thou shalt [brepent](#note8b) and [ccall](#note8c) upon God in the name of the Son forevermore.

9 And in that day the [aHoly Ghost](#note9a) fell upon Adam, which beareth record of the Father and the Son, saying: I am the [bOnly Begotten](#note9b) of the Father from the beginning, henceforth and forever, that as thou hast [cfallen](#note9c) thou mayest be [dredeemed](#note9d), and all mankind, even as many as will.

10 And in that day Adam blessed God and was [afilled](#note10a), and began to [bprophesy](#note10b) concerning all the families of the earth, saying: Blessed be the name of God, for because of my [ctransgression](#note10c) my [deyes](#note10d) are opened, and in this life I shall have [ejoy](#note10e), and again in the [fflesh](#note10f) I shall see God.

11 And Eve, his wife, heard all these things and was glad, saying: Were it not for our transgression we never should have had [aseed](#note11a), and never should have [bknown](#note11b) good and evil, and the joy of our redemption, and the eternal life which God giveth unto all the obedient.

12 And Adam and [aEve](#note12a) blessed the name of God, and they made all things [bknown](#note12b) unto their sons and their daughters.

13 And [aSatan](#note13a) came among them, saying: I am also a son of God; and he commanded them, saying: [bBelieve](#note13b) it not; and they believed it not, and they [cloved](#note13c) Satan more than God. And men began from that time forth to be [dcarnal](#note13d), sensual, and devilish.

14 And the Lord God called upon men by the [aHoly Ghost](#note14a) everywhere and commanded them that they should repent;

15 And as many as [abelieved](#note15a) in the Son, and repented of their sins, should be [bsaved](#note15b); and as many as believed not and repented not, should be [cdamned](#note15c); and the words went forth out of the mouth of God in a firm decree; wherefore they must be fulfilled.

16 And Adam and Eve, his wife, ceased not to call upon God. And Adam knew Eve his wife, and she conceived and bare [aCain](#note16a), and said: I have gotten a man from the Lord; wherefore he may not reject his words. But behold, Cain [bhearkened](#note16b) not, saying: Who is the Lord that I should [cknow](#note16c) him?

17 And she again conceived and bare his brother Abel. And Abel [ahearkened](#note17a) unto the voice of the Lord. And [bAbel](#note17b) was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was a tiller of the ground.

18 And Cain [aloved](#note18a) Satan more than God. And Satan commanded him, saying: [bMake](#note18b) an offering unto the Lord.

19 And in process of time it came to pass that Cain brought of the [afruit](#note19a) of the ground an offering unto the Lord.

20 And Abel, he also brought of the [afirstlings](#note20a) of his flock, and of the fat thereof. And the Lord had [brespect](#note20b) unto Abel, and to his [coffering](#note20c);

21 But unto Cain, and to his [aoffering](#note21a), he had not respect. Now Satan knew this, and it [bpleased](#note21b) him. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.

22 And the Lord said unto Cain: Why art thou wroth? Why is thy countenance fallen?

23 If thou doest well, thou shalt be [aaccepted](#note23a). And if thou doest not well, sin lieth at the door, and Satan [bdesireth](#note23b) to have thee; and except thou shalt hearken unto my commandments, I will [cdeliver](#note23c) thee up, and it shall be unto thee according to his desire. And thou shalt [drule](#note23d) over him;

24 For from this time forth thou shalt be the father of his [alies](#note24a); thou shalt be called [bPerdition](#note24b); for thou wast also [cbefore](#note24c) the world.

25 And it shall be said in time to come—That these abominations were had from [aCain](#note25a); for he rejected the greater counsel which was had from God; and this is a [bcursing](#note25b) which I will put upon thee, except thou repent.

26 And Cain was wroth, and listened not any more to the voice of the Lord, neither to Abel, his brother, who walked in holiness before the Lord.

27 And Adam and his wife [amourned](#note27a) before the Lord, because of Cain and his brethren.

28 And it came to pass that Cain took one of his brothers’ daughters to [awife](#note28a), and they [bloved](#note28b) Satan more than God.

29 And Satan said unto Cain: [aSwear](#note29a) unto me by thy throat, and if thou tell it thou shalt die; and swear thy brethren by their heads, and by the living God, that they tell it not; for if they tell it, they shall surely die; and this that thy father may not know it; and this day I will deliver thy brother Abel into thine hands.

30 And Satan sware unto Cain that he would do according to his [acommands](#note30a). And all these things were done in secret.

31 And Cain said: Truly I am Mahan, the master of this great [asecret](#note31a), that I may [bmurder](#note31b) and get [cgain](#note31c). Wherefore Cain was called Master [dMahan](#note31d), and he gloried in his wickedness.

32 And Cain went into the field, and Cain talked with Abel, his brother. And it came to pass that while they were in the field, Cain rose up against Abel, his brother, and slew him.

33 And Cain [agloried](#note33a) in that which he had done, saying: I am free; surely the [bflocks](#note33b) of my brother falleth into my hands.

34 And the Lord said unto Cain: Where is Abel, thy brother? And he said: I know not. Am I my brother’s [akeeper](#note34a)?

35 And the Lord said: What hast thou done? The voice of thy brother’s [ablood](#note35a) cries unto me from the ground.

36 And now thou shalt be [acursed](#note36a) from the earth which hath opened her mouth to receive thy brother’s blood from thy hand.

37 When thou tillest the ground it shall not henceforth yield unto thee her [astrength](#note37a). A [bfugitive](#note37b) and a vagabond shalt thou be in the earth.

38 And Cain said unto the Lord: Satan [atempted](#note38a) me because of my brother’s flocks. And I was wroth also; for his offering thou didst accept and not mine; my [bpunishment](#note38b) is greater than I can bear.

39 Behold thou hast driven me out this day from the face of the Lord, and from thy face shall I be hid; and I shall be a fugitive and a vagabond in the earth; and it shall come to pass, that he that findeth me will slay me, because of mine iniquities, for these things are not hid from the Lord.

40 And I the Lord said unto him: Whosoever slayeth thee, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold. And I the Lord set a [amark](#note40a) upon Cain, lest any finding him should kill him.

41 And Cain was [ashut out](#note41a) from the [bpresence](#note41b) of the Lord, and with his wife and many of his brethren dwelt in the land of Nod, on the east of Eden.

42 And Cain knew his wife, and she conceived and bare Enoch, and he also begat many sons and daughters. And he builded a city, and he called the name of the [acity](#note42a) after the name of his son, Enoch.

43 And unto Enoch was born Irad, and other sons and daughters. And Irad begat Mahujael, and other sons and daughters. And Mahujael begat Methusael, and other sons and daughters. And Methusael begat Lamech.

44 And Lamech took unto himself two wives; the name of one being Adah, and the name of the other, Zillah.

45 And Adah bare Jabal; he was the father of such as dwell in [atents](#note45a), and they were keepers of cattle; and his brother’s name was Jubal, who was the father of all such as handle the harp and organ.

46 And Zillah, she also bare Tubal Cain, an instructor of every artificer in brass and iron. And the sister of Tubal Cain was called Naamah.

47 And Lamech said unto his wives, Adah and Zillah: Hear my voice, ye wives of Lamech, hearken unto my speech; for I have slain a man to my wounding, and a young man to my hurt.

48 If Cain shall be avenged sevenfold, truly Lamech shall be [aseventy and seven fold](#note48a);

49 For [aLamech](#note49a) having entered into a covenant with Satan, after the manner of Cain, wherein he became Master Mahan, master of that great secret which was administered unto Cain by Satan; and Irad, the son of Enoch, having known their secret, began to reveal it unto the sons of Adam;

50 Wherefore Lamech, being angry, slew him, not like unto Cain, his brother Abel, for the sake of getting gain, but he slew him for the [aoath’s](#note50a) sake.

51 For, from the days of Cain, there was a secret [acombination](#note51a), and their works were in the dark, and they knew every man his brother.

52 Wherefore the Lord [acursed](#note52a) Lamech, and his house, and all them that had covenanted with Satan; for they kept not the commandments of God, and it displeased God, and he ministered not unto them, and their works were abominations, and began to spread among all the [bsons](#note52b) of men. And it was among the sons of men.

53 And among the daughters of men these things were not spoken, because that Lamech had spoken the secret unto his wives, and they rebelled against him, and declared these things abroad, and had not compassion;

54 Wherefore Lamech was despised, and cast out, and came not among the sons of men, lest he should die.

55 And thus the works of [adarkness](#note55a) began to prevail among all the sons of men.

56 And God [acursed](#note56a) the earth with a sore curse, and was angry with the wicked, with all the sons of men whom he had made;

57 For they would not [ahearken](#note57a) unto his voice, nor believe on his Only Begotten Son, even him whom he declared should [bcome](#note57b) in the meridian of time, who was [cprepared](#note57c) from before the foundation of the world.

58 And thus the [aGospel](#note58a) began to be [bpreached](#note58b), from the beginning, being declared by [choly](#note58c) [dangels](#note58d) sent forth from the presence of God, and by his own voice, and by the gift of the Holy Ghost.

59 And thus all things were confirmed unto [aAdam](#note59a), by an holy ordinance, and the Gospel preached, and a decree sent forth, that it should be in the world, until the end thereof; and thus it was. Amen.

Chapter 6

(November–December 1830)

Adam’s seed keep a book of remembrance—His righteous posterity preach repentance—God reveals Himself to Enoch—Enoch preaches the gospel—The plan of salvation was revealed to Adam—He received baptism and the priesthood.

1 And Adam hearkened unto the voice of God, and called upon his sons to repent.

2 And Adam knew his wife again, and she bare a son, and he called his name [aSeth](#note2a). And Adam glorified the name of God; for he said: God hath appointed me another seed, instead of Abel, whom Cain slew.

3 And God revealed himself unto [aSeth](#note3a), and he rebelled not, but offered an acceptable [bsacrifice](#note3b), like unto his brother Abel. And to him also was born a son, and he called his name Enos.

4 And then began these men to [acall](#note4a) upon the name of the Lord, and the Lord blessed them;

5 And a [abook](#note5a) of [bremembrance](#note5b) was kept, in the which was recorded, in the [clanguage](#note5c) of Adam, for it was given unto as many as called upon God to write by the spirit of [dinspiration](#note5d);

6 And by them their [achildren](#note6a) were taught to read and write, having a [blanguage](#note6b) which was [cpure](#note6c) and undefiled.

7 Now this same [aPriesthood](#note7a), which was in the beginning, shall be in the end of the world also.

8 Now this prophecy Adam spake, as he was moved upon by the [aHoly Ghost](#note8a), and a [bgenealogy](#note8b) was kept of the [cchildren](#note8c) of God. And this was the [dbook](#note8d) of the generations of Adam, saying: In the day that God created man, in the likeness of God made he him;

9 In the [aimage](#note9a) of his own [bbody](#note9b), male and female, [ccreated](#note9c) he them, and blessed them, and called their [dname](#note9d) Adam, in the day when they were created and became living [esouls](#note9e) in the land upon the [ffootstool](#note9f) of God.

10 And [aAdam](#note10a) lived one hundred and thirty years, and begat a son in his own likeness, after his own [bimage](#note10b), and called his name Seth.

11 And the days of Adam, after he had begotten Seth, were eight hundred years, and he begat many sons and daughters;

12 And all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years, and he died.

13 Seth lived one hundred and five years, and begat Enos, and [aprophesied](#note13a) in all his days, and taught his son Enos in the ways of God; wherefore Enos prophesied also.

14 And Seth lived, after he begat Enos, eight hundred and seven years, and begat many sons and daughters.

15 And the children of [amen](#note15a) were numerous upon all the face of the land. And in those days [bSatan](#note15b) had great [cdominion](#note15c) among men, and raged in their hearts; and from thenceforth came [dwars](#note15d) and bloodshed; and a man’s hand was against his own brother, in administering death, because of [esecret](#note15e) [fworks](#note15f), seeking for [gpower](#note15g).

16 All the days of Seth were nine hundred and twelve years, and he died.

17 And Enos lived ninety years, and begat [aCainan](#note17a). And Enos and the residue of the people of God came out from the land, which was called Shulon, and dwelt in a land of promise, which he called after his own son, whom he had named [bCainan](#note17b).

18 And Enos lived, after he begat Cainan, eight hundred and fifteen years, and begat many sons and daughters. And all the days of Enos were nine hundred and five years, and he died.

19 And Cainan lived seventy years, and begat Mahalaleel; and Cainan lived after he begat Mahalaleel eight hundred and forty years, and begat sons and daughters. And all the days of [aCainan](#note19a) were nine hundred and ten years, and he died.

20 And Mahalaleel lived sixty-five years, and begat Jared; and Mahalaleel lived, after he begat Jared, eight hundred and thirty years, and begat sons and daughters. And all the days of Mahalaleel were eight hundred and ninety-five years, and he died.

21 And Jared lived one hundred and sixty-two years, and begat [aEnoch](#note21a); and Jared lived, after he begat Enoch, eight hundred years, and begat sons and daughters. And Jared [btaught](#note21b) Enoch in all the ways of God.

22 And this is the genealogy of the sons of Adam, who was the [ason](#note22a) of God, with whom God, himself, conversed.

23 And they were [apreachers](#note23a) of [brighteousness](#note23b), and spake and [cprophesied](#note23c), and called upon all men, everywhere, to repent; and [dfaith](#note23d) was [etaught](#note23e) unto the children of men.

24 And it came to pass that all the days of Jared were nine hundred and sixty-two years, and he died.

25 And Enoch lived sixty-five years, and begat Methuselah.

26 And it came to pass that Enoch journeyed in the land, among the people; and as he journeyed, the [aSpirit](#note26a) of God descended out of heaven, and abode upon him.

27 And he heard a [avoice](#note27a) from heaven, saying: [bEnoch](#note27b), my son, [cprophesy](#note27c) unto this people, and say unto them—Repent, for thus saith the Lord: I am [dangry](#note27d) with this people, and my fierce anger is kindled against them; for their hearts have waxed [ehard](#note27e), and their [fears](#note27f) are dull of hearing, and their eyes [gcannot](#note27g) see afar off;

28 And for these many generations, ever since the day that I created them, have they gone astray, and have [adenied](#note28a) me, and have sought their own counsels in the dark; and in their own abominations have they devised murder, and have not kept the commandments, which I gave unto their father, Adam.

29 Wherefore, they have foresworn themselves, and, by their oaths, they have brought upon themselves death; and a [ahell](#note29a) I have prepared for them, if they repent not;

30 And this is a decree, which I have sent forth in the beginning of the world, from my own mouth, from the foundation thereof, and by the mouths of my servants, thy fathers, have I decreed it, even as it shall be sent forth in the world, unto the ends thereof.

31 And when Enoch had heard these words, he [abowed](#note31a) himself to the earth, before the Lord, and spake before the Lord, saying: [bWhy](#note31b) is it that I have found favor in thy sight, and am but a lad, and all the people [chate](#note31c) me; for I am [dslow](#note31d) of speech; wherefore am I thy servant?

32 And the Lord said unto Enoch: Go forth and do as I have commanded thee, and no man shall pierce thee. Open thy [amouth](#note32a), and it shall be filled, and I will give thee utterance, for all flesh is in my hands, and I will do as seemeth me good.

33 Say unto this people: [aChoose](#note33a) ye [bthis day](#note33b), to serve the Lord God who made you.

34 Behold my [aSpirit](#note34a) is upon you, wherefore all thy words will I justify; and the [bmountains](#note34b) shall flee before you, and the [crivers](#note34c) shall turn from their course; and thou shalt abide in me, and I in you; therefore [dwalk](#note34d) with me.

35 And the Lord spake unto Enoch, and said unto him: Anoint thine eyes with [aclay](#note35a), and wash them, and thou shalt see. And he did so.

36 And he beheld the [aspirits](#note36a) that God had created; and he beheld also things which were not visible to the [bnatural](#note36b) eye; and from thenceforth came the saying abroad in the land: A [cseer](#note36c) hath the Lord raised up unto his people.

37 And it came to pass that Enoch went forth in the land, among the people, standing upon the hills and the high places, and cried with a loud voice, testifying against their works; and all men were [aoffended](#note37a) because of him.

38 And they came forth to hear him, upon the high places, saying unto the [atent-keepers](#note38a): Tarry ye here and keep the tents, while we go yonder to behold the seer, for he prophesieth, and there is a strange thing in the land; a [bwild](#note38b) man hath come among us.

39 And it came to pass when they heard him, no man laid hands on him; for [afear](#note39a) came on all them that heard him; for he [bwalked](#note39b) with God.

40 And there came a man unto him, whose name was Mahijah, and said unto him: Tell us plainly who thou art, and from whence thou comest?

41 And he said unto them: I came out from the land of [aCainan](#note41a), the land of my fathers, a land of [brighteousness](#note41b) unto this day. And my father [ctaught](#note41c) me in all the ways of God.

42 And it came to pass, as I journeyed from the land of Cainan, by the sea east, I beheld a vision; and lo, the heavens I saw, and the Lord spake with me, and gave me commandment; wherefore, for this cause, to keep the commandment, I speak forth these words.

43 And Enoch continued his speech, saying: The Lord which spake with me, the same is the God of heaven, and he is my God, and your God, and ye are my brethren, and why [acounsel](#note43a) ye yourselves, and deny the God of heaven?

44 The heavens he made; the [aearth](#note44a) is his [bfootstool](#note44b); and the foundation thereof is his. Behold, he laid it, an host of men hath he brought in upon the face thereof.

45 And death hath come upon our fathers; nevertheless we know them, and cannot deny, and even the first of all we know, even [aAdam](#note45a).

46 For a book of [aremembrance](#note46a) we have [bwritten](#note46b) among us, according to the pattern given by the finger of God; and it is given in our own [clanguage](#note46c).

47 And as Enoch spake forth the words of God, the people trembled, and could not [astand](#note47a) in his presence.

48 And he said unto them: Because that Adam [afell](#note48a), we are; and by his fall came [bdeath](#note48b); and we are made partakers of misery and woe.

49 Behold Satan hath come among the children of men, and [atempteth](#note49a) them to [bworship](#note49b) him; and men have become [ccarnal](#note49c), [dsensual](#note49d), and devilish, and are shut out from the [epresence](#note49e) of God.

50 But God hath made known unto our fathers that all men must repent.

51 And he called upon our father Adam by his own voice, saying: I am God; I [amade](#note51a) the world, and [bmen](#note51b) [cbefore](#note51c) they were in the flesh.

52 And he also said unto him: If thou wilt turn unto me, and hearken unto my voice, and believe, and repent of all thy transgressions, and be [abaptized](#note52a), even in water, in the name of mine Only Begotten Son, who is full of [bgrace](#note52b) and truth, which is [cJesus Christ](#note52c), the only [dname](#note52d) which shall be given under heaven, whereby [esalvation](#note52e) shall come unto the children of men, ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost, asking all things in his name, and whatsoever ye shall ask, it shall be given you.

53 And our father Adam spake unto the Lord, and said: Why is it that men must repent and be baptized in water? And the Lord said unto Adam: Behold I have [aforgiven](#note53a) thee thy transgression in the Garden of Eden.

54 Hence came the saying abroad among the people, that the [aSon of God](#note54a) hath [batoned](#note54b) for original guilt, wherein the sins of the parents cannot be answered upon the heads of the [cchildren](#note54c), for they are [dwhole](#note54d) from the foundation of the world.

55 And the Lord spake unto Adam, saying: Inasmuch as thy children are [aconceived](#note55a) in sin, even so when they begin to grow up, [bsin](#note55b) conceiveth in their hearts, and they taste the [cbitter](#note55c), that they may know to prize the good.

56 And it is given unto them to know good from evil; wherefore they are [aagents](#note56a) unto themselves, and I have given unto you another law and commandment.

57 Wherefore teach it unto your children, that all men, everywhere, must [arepent](#note57a), or they can in nowise inherit the kingdom of God, for no [bunclean thing](#note57b) can dwell there, or [cdwell](#note57c) in his [dpresence](#note57d); for, in the language of Adam, [eMan](#note57e) of Holiness is his name, and the name of his Only Begotten is the [fSon of Man](#note57f), even [gJesus Christ](#note57g), a righteous [hJudge](#note57h), who shall come in the meridian of time.

58 Therefore I give unto you a [acommandment](#note58a), to [bteach](#note58b) these things freely unto your [cchildren](#note58c), saying:

Chapter 7

(December 1830)

Enoch teaches, leads the people, and moves mountains—The city of Zion is established—Enoch foresees the coming of the Son of Man, His atoning sacrifice, and the resurrection of the Saints—He foresees the Restoration, the Gathering, the Second Coming, and the return of Zion.

1 And it came to pass that Enoch continued his speech, saying: Behold, our father Adam taught these things, and many have believed and become the [asons](#note1a) of God, and many have believed not, and have perished in their sins, and are looking forth with [bfear](#note1b), in torment, for the fiery indignation of the wrath of God to be poured out upon them.

2 And from that time forth Enoch began to prophesy, saying unto the people, that: As I was journeying, and stood upon the place Mahujah, and cried unto the Lord, there came a voice out of heaven, saying—Turn ye, and get ye upon the mount [aSimeon](#note2a).

3 And it came to pass that I turned and went up on the mount; and as I stood upon the mount, I beheld the heavens open, and I was clothed upon with [aglory](#note3a);

4 And I [asaw](#note4a) the Lord; and he stood before my face, and he talked with me, even as a man talketh one with another, [bface to face](#note4b); and he said unto me: [cLook](#note4c), and I will [dshow](#note4d) unto thee the world for the space of many generations.

5 And it came to pass that I beheld in the valley of Shum, and lo, a great people which dwelt in [atents](#note5a), which were the people of Shum.

6 And again the Lord said unto me: Look; and I looked towards the north, and I beheld the people of [aCanaan](#note6a), which dwelt in tents.

7 And the Lord said unto me: Prophesy; and I prophesied, saying: Behold the people of Canaan, which are numerous, shall go forth in battle array against the people of Shum, and shall slay them that they shall utterly be destroyed; and the people of Canaan shall divide themselves in the land, and the land shall be barren and unfruitful, and none other people shall dwell there but the people of Canaan;

8 For behold, the Lord shall [acurse](#note8a) the land with much heat, and the [bbarrenness](#note8b) thereof shall go forth forever; and there was a [cblackness](#note8c) came upon all the children of Canaan, that they were despised among all people.

9 And it came to pass that the Lord said unto me: Look; and I looked, and I beheld the land of Sharon, and the land of Enoch, and the land of Omner, and the land of Heni, and the land of Shem, and the land of Haner, and the land of Hanannihah, and all the inhabitants thereof;

10 And the Lord said unto me: Go to this people, and say unto them—[aRepent](#note10a), lest I come out and smite them with a curse, and they die.

11 And he gave unto me a commandment that I should [abaptize](#note11a) in the name of the Father, and of the Son, which is full of [bgrace](#note11b) and truth, and of the Holy Ghost, which beareth record of the Father and the Son.

12 And it came to pass that Enoch continued to call upon all the people, save it were the people of Canaan, to repent;

38 But behold, these which thine eyes are upon shall perish in the floods; and behold, I will shut them up; a [aprison](#note38a) have I prepared for them.

Forasmuch as thou art God, and I know thee, and thou hast sworn unto me, and commanded me that I should ask in the name of thine Only Begotten; thou hast made me, and given unto me a right to thy throne, and not of myself, but through thine own grace; wherefore, I ask thee if thou wilt not come again on the earth.

Chapter 8

(February 1831)

Methuselah prophesies—Noah and his sons preach the gospel—Great wickedness prevails—The call to repentance is unheeded—God decrees the destruction of all flesh by the Flood.

1 And all the days of [aEnoch](#note1a) were four hundred and thirty years.

2 And it came to pass that Methuselah, the son of Enoch, was [anot](#note2a) taken, that the covenants of the Lord might be fulfilled, which he made to Enoch; for he truly covenanted with Enoch that Noah should be of the fruit of his loins.

3 And it came to pass that Methuselah [aprophesied](#note3a) that from his loins should spring all the kingdoms of the earth (through Noah), and he took glory unto himself.

4 And there came forth a great [afamine](#note4a) into the land, and the Lord [bcursed](#note4b) the earth with a sore curse, and many of the inhabitants thereof died.

5 And it came to pass that Methuselah lived one hundred and eighty-seven years, and begat Lamech;

6 And Methuselah lived, after he begat Lamech, seven hundred and eighty-two years, and begat sons and daughters;

7 And all the days of Methuselah were nine hundred and sixty-nine years, and he died.

8 And Lamech lived one hundred and eighty-two years, and begat a son,

9 And he called his name Noah, saying: This son shall comfort us concerning our work and toil of our hands, because of the ground which the Lord hath [acursed](#note9a).

10 And Lamech lived, after he begat Noah, five hundred and ninety-five years, and begat sons and daughters;

11 And all the days of Lamech were seven hundred and seventy-seven years, and he died.

12 And Noah was four hundred and fifty years old, and [abegat](#note12a) Japheth; and forty-two years afterward he begat [bShem](#note12b) of her who was the mother of Japheth, and when he was five hundred years old he begat [cHam](#note12c).

13 And [aNoah](#note13a) and his sons hearkened unto the Lord, and gave heed, and they were called the [bsons](#note13b) of God.

14 And when these men began to multiply on the face of the earth, and daughters were born unto them, the [asons](#note14a) of men saw that those daughters were fair, and they took them [bwives](#note14b), even as they chose.

15 And the Lord said unto Noah: The daughters of thy sons have sold themselves; for behold mine anger is kindled against the sons of men, for they will not [ahearken](#note15a) to my voice.

16 And it came to pass that Noah [aprophesied](#note16a), and taught the things of God, even as it was in the beginning.

17 And the Lord said unto Noah: My Spirit shall not always [astrive](#note17a) with man, for he shall know that all [bflesh](#note17b) shall die; yet his days shall be an [chundred](#note17c) and twenty years; and if men do not repent, I will send in the [dfloods](#note17d) upon them.

18 And in those days there were [agiants](#note18a) on the earth, and they sought Noah to take away his [blife](#note18b); but the Lord was with Noah, and the [cpower](#note18c) of the Lord was upon him.

19 And the Lord [aordained](#note19a) [bNoah](#note19b) after his own [corder](#note19c), and commanded him that he should go forth and [ddeclare](#note19d) his Gospel unto the children of men, even as it was given unto Enoch.

20 And it came to pass that Noah called upon the children of men that they should [arepent](#note20a); but they hearkened not unto his words;

21 And also, after that they had heard him, they came up before him, saying: Behold, we are the sons of God; have we not taken unto ourselves the daughters of men? And are we not [aeating](#note21a) and drinking, and marrying and giving in marriage? And our wives bear unto us children, and the same are mighty men, which are like unto men of old, men of great renown. And they hearkened not unto the words of Noah.

22 And God saw that the [awickedness](#note22a) of men had become great in the earth; and every man was lifted up in the [bimagination](#note22b) of the thoughts of his heart, being only evil continually.

23 And it came to pass that Noah continued his [apreaching](#note23a) unto the people, saying: Hearken, and give heed unto my words;

24 [aBelieve](#note24a) and repent of your sins and be [bbaptized](#note24b) in the name of Jesus Christ, the Son of God, even as our fathers, and ye shall receive the Holy Ghost, that ye may have all things made [cmanifest](#note24c); and if ye do not this, the floods will come in upon you; nevertheless they hearkened not.

25 And it [arepented](#note25a) Noah, and his heart was pained that the Lord had made man on the earth, and it grieved him at the heart.

26 And the Lord said: I will [adestroy](#note26a) man whom I have created, from the face of the earth, both man and beast, and the creeping things, and the fowls of the air; for it repenteth Noah that I have created them, and that I have made them; and he hath called upon me; for they have sought his [blife](#note26b).

27 And thus Noah found [agrace](#note27a) in the eyes of the Lord; for Noah was a just man, and [bperfect](#note27b) in his generation; and he [cwalked](#note27c) with God, as did also his three sons, Shem, Ham, and Japheth.

28 The [aearth](#note28a) was [bcorrupt](#note28b) before God, and it was filled with violence.

29 And God looked upon the earth, and, behold, it was corrupt, for all flesh had corrupted its [away](#note29a) upon the earth.

30 And God said unto Noah: The end of all flesh is come before me, for the earth is filled with violence, and behold I will [adestroy](#note30a) all flesh from off the earth.
