# The First Book of Nephi

His Reign and Ministry

An account of Lehi and his wife Sariah, and his four sons, being called, (beginning at the eldest) Laman, Lemuel, Sam, and Nephi. The Lord warns Lehi to depart out of the land of Jerusalem, because he prophesieth unto the people concerning their iniquity and they seek to destroy his life. He taketh three days’ journey into the wilderness with his family. Nephi taketh his brethren and returneth to the land of Jerusalem after the record of the Jews. The account of their sufferings. They take the daughters of Ishmael to wife. They take their families and depart into the wilderness. Their sufferings and afflictions in the wilderness. The course of their travels. They come to the large waters. Nephi’s brethren rebel against him. He confoundeth them, and buildeth a ship. They call the name of the place Bountiful. They cross the large waters into the promised land, and so forth. This is according to the account of Nephi; or in other words, I, Nephi, wrote this record.

Chapter 1

Nephi begins the record of his people—Lehi sees in vision a pillar of fire and reads from a book of prophecy—He praises God, foretells the coming of the Messiah, and prophesies the destruction of Jerusalem—He is persecuted by the Jews. About 600 B.C.

1 I, Nephi, having been [aborn](#note1a) of [bgoodly](#note1b) [cparents](#note1c), therefore I was [dtaught](#note1d) somewhat in all the learning of my father; and having seen many [eafflictions](#note1e) in the course of my days, nevertheless, having been highly favored of the Lord in all my days; yea, having had a great knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God, therefore I make a [frecord](#note1f) of my proceedings in my days.

2 Yea, I make a record in the [alanguage](#note2a) of my father, which consists of the learning of the Jews and the language of the Egyptians.

3 And I know that the record which I make is [atrue](#note3a); and I make it with mine own hand; and I make it according to my knowledge.

4 For it came to pass in the commencement of the [afirst](#note4a) year of the reign of [bZedekiah](#note4b), king of Judah, (my father, Lehi, having dwelt at [cJerusalem](#note4c) in all his days); and in that same year there came many [dprophets](#note4d), prophesying unto the people that they must [erepent](#note4e), or the great city [fJerusalem](#note4f) must be destroyed.

5 Wherefore it came to pass that my father, Lehi, as he went forth prayed unto the Lord, yea, even with all his [aheart](#note5a), in behalf of his people.

6 And it came to pass as he prayed unto the Lord, there came a [apillar](#note6a) of fire and dwelt upon a rock before him; and he saw and heard much; and because of the things which he saw and heard he did [bquake](#note6b) and tremble exceedingly.

7 And it came to pass that he returned to his own house at Jerusalem; and he cast himself upon his bed, being [aovercome](#note7a) with the Spirit and the things which he had seen.

8 And being thus overcome with the Spirit, he was carried away in a [avision](#note8a), even that he saw the [bheavens](#note8b) open, and he thought he [csaw](#note8c) God sitting upon his throne, surrounded with numberless concourses of angels in the attitude of singing and praising their God.

9 And it came to pass that he saw One descending out of the midst of heaven, and he beheld that his [aluster](#note9a) was above that of the sun at noon-day.

10 And he also saw [atwelve](#note10a) others following him, and their brightness did exceed that of the stars in the firmament.

11 And they came down and went forth upon the face of the earth; and the first came and [astood](#note11a) before my father, and gave unto him a [bbook](#note11b), and bade him that he should read.

12 And it came to pass that as he read, he was filled with the [aSpirit](#note12a) of the Lord.

13 And he read, saying: Wo, wo, unto Jerusalem, for I have seen thine [aabominations](#note13a)! Yea, and many things did my father read concerning [bJerusalem](#note13b)—that it should be destroyed, and the inhabitants thereof; many should perish by the sword, and many should be [ccarried away](#note13c) captive into Babylon.

14 And it came to pass that when my father had read and seen many great and marvelous things, he did exclaim many things unto the Lord; such as: Great and marvelous are thy works, O Lord God Almighty! Thy throne is high in the heavens, and thy [apower](#note14a), and goodness, and mercy are over all the inhabitants of the earth; and, because thou art merciful, thou wilt not suffer those who [bcome](#note14b) unto thee that they shall perish!

15 And after this manner was the language of my father in the praising of his God; for his soul did rejoice, and his whole heart was filled, because of the things which he had seen, yea, which the Lord had shown unto him.

16 And now I, Nephi, do not make a full account of the things which my father hath written, for he hath written many things which he saw in [avisions](#note16a) and in [bdreams](#note16b); and he also hath written many things which he [cprophesied](#note16c) and spake unto his children, of which I shall not make a full account.

17 But I shall make an account of my proceedings in my days. Behold, I make an [aabridgment](#note17a) of the record of my [bfather](#note17b), upon [cplates](#note17c) which I have made with mine own hands; wherefore, after I have abridged the record of my [dfather](#note17d) then will I make an account of mine own life.

18 Therefore, I would that ye should know, that after the Lord had shown so many marvelous things unto my father, Lehi, yea, concerning the [adestruction](#note18a) of Jerusalem, behold he went forth among the people, and began to [bprophesy](#note18b) and to declare unto them concerning the things which he had both seen and heard.

19 And it came to pass that the [aJews](#note19a) did [bmock](#note19b) him because of the things which he testified of them; for he truly testified of their [cwickedness](#note19c) and their abominations; and he testified that the things which he saw and heard, and also the things which he read in the book, manifested plainly of the coming of a [dMessiah](#note19d), and also the redemption of the world.

20 And when the Jews heard these things they were angry with him; yea, even as with the prophets of old, whom they had [acast out](#note20a), and stoned, and slain; and they also [bsought](#note20b) his life, that they might take it away. But behold, I, Nephi, will show unto you that the tender [cmercies](#note20c) of the Lord are over all those whom he hath chosen, because of their faith, to make them mighty even unto the power of [ddeliverance](#note20d).

Chapter 2

Lehi takes his family into the wilderness by the Red Sea—They leave their property—Lehi offers a sacrifice to the Lord and teaches his sons to keep the commandments—Laman and Lemuel murmur against their father—Nephi is obedient and prays in faith; the Lord speaks to him, and he is chosen to rule over his brethren. About 600 B.C.

1 For behold, it came to pass that the Lord spake unto my father, yea, even in a dream, and said unto him: Blessed art thou Lehi, because of the things which thou hast done; and because thou hast been faithful and declared unto this people the things which I commanded thee, behold, they seek to [atake away](#note1a) thy [blife](#note1b).

2 And it came to pass that the Lord [acommanded](#note2a) my father, even in a [bdream](#note2b), that he should [ctake](#note2c) his family and depart into the wilderness.

3 And it came to pass that he was [aobedient](#note3a) unto the word of the Lord, wherefore he did as the Lord commanded him.

4 And it came to pass that he departed into the wilderness. And he left his house, and the land of his inheritance, and his gold, and his silver, and his precious things, and took nothing with him, save it were his family, and provisions, and tents, and [adeparted](#note4a) into the wilderness.

5 And he came down by the borders near the shore of the [aRed Sea](#note5a); and he traveled in the wilderness in the borders which are nearer the Red Sea; and he did travel in the wilderness with his family, which consisted of my mother, Sariah, and my elder brothers, who were Laman, Lemuel, and Sam.

6 And it came to pass that when he had traveled three days in the wilderness, he pitched his tent in a [avalley](#note6a) by the side of a [briver](#note6b) of water.

7 And it came to pass that he built an [aaltar](#note7a) of [bstones](#note7b), and made an [coffering](#note7c) unto the Lord, and gave [dthanks](#note7d) unto the Lord our God.

8 And it came to pass that he called the name of the river, Laman, and it emptied into the Red Sea; and the valley was in the borders near the mouth thereof.

9 And when my father saw that the waters of the river emptied into the [afountain](#note9a) of the Red Sea, he spake unto Laman, saying: O that thou mightest be like unto this river, continually running into the fountain of all righteousness!

10 And he also spake unto Lemuel: O that thou mightest be like unto this valley, [afirm](#note10a) and [bsteadfast](#note10b), and immovable in keeping the commandments of the Lord!

11 Now this he spake because of the [astiffneckedness](#note11a) of Laman and Lemuel; for behold they did [bmurmur](#note11b) in many things against their [cfather](#note11c), because he was a [dvisionary](#note11d) man, and had led them out of the land of Jerusalem, to leave the land of their inheritance, and their gold, and their silver, and their precious things, to perish in the wilderness. And this they said he had done because of the foolish imaginations of his heart.

12 And thus Laman and Lemuel, being the eldest, did murmur against their [afather](#note12a). And they did [bmurmur](#note12b) because they [cknew not](#note12c) the dealings of that God who had [dcreated](#note12d) them.

13 Neither did they [abelieve](#note13a) that Jerusalem, that great city, could be [bdestroyed](#note13b) according to the words of the prophets. And they were like unto the Jews who were at Jerusalem, who sought to take away the life of my father.

14 And it came to pass that my father did speak unto them in the [avalley of Lemuel](#note14a), with [bpower](#note14b), being filled with the Spirit, until their frames did [cshake](#note14c) before him. And he did confound them, that they durst not utter against him; wherefore, they did as he commanded them.

15 And my father dwelt in a [atent](#note15a).

16 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, being exceedingly young, nevertheless being large in stature, and also having great desires to know of the [amysteries](#note16a) of God, wherefore, I did cry unto the Lord; and behold he did [bvisit](#note16b) me, and did [csoften](#note16c) my heart that I did [dbelieve](#note16d) all the words which had been spoken by my [efather](#note16e); wherefore, I did not [frebel](#note16f) against him like unto my brothers.

17 And I spake unto Sam, making known unto him the things which the Lord had manifested unto me by his Holy Spirit. And it came to pass that he believed in my words.

18 But, behold, Laman and Lemuel would not hearken unto my words; and being [agrieved](#note18a) because of the hardness of their hearts I cried unto the Lord for them.

19 And it came to pass that the Lord spake unto me, saying: Blessed art thou, Nephi, because of thy [afaith](#note19a), for thou hast sought me diligently, with lowliness of heart.

20 And inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall [aprosper](#note20a), and shall be led to a [bland of promise](#note20b); yea, even a land which I have prepared for you; yea, a land which is choice above all other lands.

21 And inasmuch as thy brethren shall rebel against thee, they shall be [acut off](#note21a) from the presence of the Lord.

22 And inasmuch as thou shalt keep my commandments, thou shalt be made a [aruler](#note22a) and a teacher over thy brethren.

23 For behold, in that day that they shall [arebel](#note23a) against me, I will [bcurse](#note23b) them even with a sore curse, and they shall have no power over thy seed except they shall [crebel](#note23c) against me also.

24 And if it so be that they rebel against me, they shall be a [ascourge](#note24a) unto thy seed, to [bstir](#note24b) them up in the ways of remembrance.

Chapter 3

Lehi’s sons return to Jerusalem to obtain the plates of brass—Laban refuses to give the plates up—Nephi exhorts and encourages his brethren—Laban steals their property and attempts to slay them—Laman and Lemuel smite Nephi and Sam and are reproved by an angel. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, returned from [aspeaking](#note1a) with the Lord, to the tent of my father.

2 And it came to pass that he spake unto me, saying: Behold I have dreamed a [adream](#note2a), in the which the Lord hath commanded me that thou and thy brethren shall [breturn](#note2b) to Jerusalem.

3 For behold, Laban hath the record of the Jews and also a [agenealogy](#note3a) of my forefathers, and they are [bengraven](#note3b) upon plates of brass.

4 Wherefore, the Lord hath commanded me that thou and thy brothers should go unto the house of Laban, and seek the records, and bring them down hither into the wilderness.

5 And now, behold thy brothers murmur, saying it is a hard thing which I have required of them; but behold I have not required it of them, but it is a commandment of the Lord.

6 Therefore go, my son, and thou shalt be favored of the Lord, because thou hast [anot](#note6a) [bmurmured](#note6b).

7 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, said unto my father: I [awill](#note7a) go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded, for I know that the Lord giveth no [bcommandments](#note7b) unto the children of men, save he shall [cprepare](#note7c) a way for them that they may accomplish the thing which he commandeth them.

8 And it came to pass that when my father had heard these words he was exceedingly glad, for he knew that I had been blessed of the Lord.

9 And I, Nephi, and my brethren took our journey in the wilderness, with our tents, to go up to the land of Jerusalem.

10 And it came to pass that when we had gone up to the land of Jerusalem, I and my brethren did consult one with another.

11 And we [acast lots](#note11a)—who of us should go in unto the house of Laban. And it came to pass that the lot fell upon Laman; and Laman went in unto the house of Laban, and he talked with him as he sat in his house.

12 And he desired of Laban the records which were engraven upon the plates of brass, which contained the [agenealogy](#note12a) of my father.

13 And behold, it came to pass that Laban was angry, and thrust him out from his presence; and he would not that he should have the records. Wherefore, he said unto him: Behold thou art a robber, and I will slay thee.

14 But Laman fled out of his presence, and told the things which Laban had done, unto us. And we began to be exceedingly sorrowful, and my brethren were about to return unto my father in the wilderness.

15 But behold I said unto them that: [aAs](#note15a) the Lord liveth, and as we live, we will not go down unto our father in the wilderness until we have [baccomplished](#note15b) the thing which the Lord hath commanded us.

17 For he knew that Jerusalem must be [adestroyed](#note17a), because of the wickedness of the people.

18 For behold, they have [arejected](#note18a) the words of the prophets. Wherefore, if my father should dwell in the land after he hath been [bcommanded](#note18b) to flee out of the land, behold, he would also perish. Wherefore, it must needs be that he flee out of the land.

19 And behold, it is wisdom in God that we should obtain these [arecords](#note19a), that we may preserve unto our children the language of our fathers;

20 And also that we may [apreserve](#note20a) unto them the words which have been spoken by the mouth of all the holy [bprophets](#note20b), which have been delivered unto them by the Spirit and power of God, since the world began, even down unto this present time.

21 And it came to pass that after this manner of language did I [apersuade](#note21a) my brethren, that they might be faithful in keeping the commandments of God.

22 And it came to pass that we went down to the land of our inheritance, and we did gather together our [agold](#note22a), and our silver, and our precious things.

23 And after we had gathered these things together, we went up again unto the house of Laban.

24 And it came to pass that we went in unto Laban, and desired him that he would give unto us the records which were engraven upon the [aplates of brass](#note24a), for which we would give unto him our gold, and our silver, and all our precious things.

25 And it came to pass that when Laban saw our property, and that it was exceedingly great, he did [alust](#note25a) after it, insomuch that he thrust us out, and sent his servants to slay us, that he might obtain our property.

26 And [ait](#note26a) came to pass that we did flee before the servants of Laban, and we were obliged to leave behind our property, and it fell into the hands of Laban.

27 And it came to pass that we fled into the wilderness, and the servants of Laban did not overtake us, and we [ahid](#note27a) ourselves in the cavity of a rock.

28 And it came to pass that Laman was angry with me, and also with my father; and also was Lemuel, for he hearkened unto the words of Laman. Wherefore Laman and Lemuel did speak many [ahard words](#note28a) unto us, their younger brothers, and they did smite us even with a rod.

29 And it came to pass as they smote us with a rod, behold, an [aangel](#note29a) of the Lord came and stood before them, and he spake unto them, saying: Why do ye smite your younger brother with a rod? Know ye not that the Lord hath chosen him to be a [bruler](#note29b) over you, and this because of your iniquities? Behold ye shall go up to Jerusalem again, and the Lord will [cdeliver](#note29c) Laban into your hands.

30 And after the [aangel](#note30a) had spoken unto us, he departed.

31 And after the angel had departed, Laman and Lemuel again began to [amurmur](#note31a), saying: How is it possible that the Lord will deliver Laban into our hands? Behold, he is a mighty man, and he can command fifty, yea, even he can slay fifty; then why not us?

Chapter 4

Nephi slays Laban at the Lord’s command and then secures the plates of brass by stratagem—Zoram chooses to join Lehi’s family in the wilderness. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that I spake unto my brethren, saying: Let us go up again unto Jerusalem, and let us be [afaithful](#note1a) in keeping the commandments of the Lord; for behold he is mightier than all the earth, then why not [bmightier](#note1b) than Laban and his fifty, yea, or even than his tens of thousands?

2 Therefore let us go up; let us be [astrong](#note2a) like unto Moses; for he truly spake unto the waters of the [bRed Sea](#note2b) and they divided hither and thither, and our fathers came through, out of captivity, on dry ground, and the armies of Pharaoh did follow and were drowned in the waters of the Red Sea.

3 Now behold ye know that this is true; and ye also know that an [aangel](#note3a) hath spoken unto you; wherefore can ye [bdoubt](#note3b)? Let us go up; the Lord is able to [cdeliver](#note3c) us, even as our fathers, and to destroy Laban, even as the Egyptians.

4 Now when I had spoken these words, they were yet wroth, and did still continue to murmur; nevertheless they did follow me up until we came without the walls of Jerusalem.

5 And it was by night; and I caused that they should hide themselves without the walls. And after they had hid themselves, I, Nephi, crept into the city and went forth towards the house of Laban.

6 And I was [aled](#note6a) by the Spirit, not [bknowing](#note6b) beforehand the things which I should do.

7 Nevertheless I went forth, and as I came near unto the house of Laban I beheld a man, and he had fallen to the earth before me, for he was [adrunken](#note7a) with wine.

8 And when I came to him I found that it was Laban.

9 And I beheld his [asword](#note9a), and I drew it forth from the sheath thereof; and the hilt thereof was of pure gold, and the workmanship thereof was exceedingly fine, and I saw that the blade thereof was of the most precious steel.

10 And it came to pass that I was [aconstrained](#note10a) by the Spirit that I should kill Laban; but I said in my heart: Never at any time have I shed the blood of man. And I shrunk and would that I might not slay him.

11 And the Spirit said unto me again: Behold the [aLord](#note11a) hath [bdelivered](#note11b) him into thy hands. Yea, and I also knew that he had sought to take away mine own life; yea, and he would not hearken unto the commandments of the Lord; and he also had [ctaken](#note11c) away our property.

12 And it came to pass that the Spirit said unto me again: Slay him, for the Lord hath delivered him into thy hands;

13 Behold the Lord [aslayeth](#note13a) the [bwicked](#note13b) to bring forth his righteous purposes. It is [cbetter](#note13c) that one man should perish than that a nation should dwindle and perish in [dunbelief](#note13d).

14 And now, when I, Nephi, had heard these words, I remembered the words of the Lord which he spake unto me in the wilderness, saying that: [aInasmuch](#note14a) as thy seed shall keep my [bcommandments](#note14b), they shall [cprosper](#note14c) in the [dland of promise](#note14d).

16 And I also knew that the [alaw](#note16a) was engraven upon the plates of brass.

17 And again, I knew that the Lord had delivered Laban into my hands for this cause—that I might obtain the records according to his commandments.

18 Therefore I did obey the voice of the Spirit, and took Laban by the hair of the head, and I smote off his head with his own [asword](#note18a).

19 And after I had smitten off his head with his own sword, I took the garments of Laban and put them upon mine own body; yea, even every whit; and I did gird on his armor about my loins.

20 And after I had done this, I went forth unto the treasury of Laban. And as I went forth towards the treasury of Laban, behold, I saw the [aservant](#note20a) of Laban who had the keys of the treasury. And I commanded him in the voice of Laban, that he should go with me into the treasury.

21 And he supposed me to be his master, Laban, for he beheld the garments and also the sword girded about my loins.

22 And he spake unto me concerning the [aelders](#note22a) of the Jews, he knowing that his master, Laban, had been out by night among them.

23 And I spake unto him as if it had been Laban.

24 And I also spake unto him that I should carry the engravings, which were upon the [aplates of brass](#note24a), to my elder brethren, who were without the walls.

25 And I also bade him that he should follow me.

26 And he, supposing that I spake of the [abrethren](#note26a) of the [bchurch](#note26b), and that I was truly that Laban whom I had slain, wherefore he did follow me.

27 And he spake unto me many times concerning the elders of the Jews, as I went forth unto my brethren, who were without the walls.

28 And it came to pass that when Laman saw me he was exceedingly frightened, and also Lemuel and Sam. And they fled from before my presence; for they supposed it was Laban, and that he had slain me and had sought to take away their lives also.

29 And it came to pass that I called after them, and they did hear me; wherefore they did cease to flee from my presence.

30 And it came to pass that when the servant of Laban beheld my brethren he began to tremble, and was about to flee from before me and return to the city of Jerusalem.

31 And now I, Nephi, being a man large in stature, and also having received much [astrength](#note31a) of the Lord, therefore I did seize upon the servant of Laban, and held him, that he should not flee.

32 And it came to pass that I spake with him, that if he would hearken unto my words, as the Lord liveth, and as I live, even so that if he would hearken unto our words, we would spare his life.

33 And I spake unto him, even with an [aoath](#note33a), that he need not fear; that he should be a [bfree](#note33b) man like unto us if he would go down in the wilderness with us.

34 And I also spake unto him, saying: Surely the Lord hath [acommanded](#note34a) us to do this thing; and shall we not be diligent in keeping the commandments of the Lord? Therefore, if thou wilt go down into the wilderness to my father thou shalt have place with us.

35 And it came to pass that [aZoram](#note35a) did take courage at the words which I spake. Now Zoram was the name of the servant; and he promised that he would go down into the wilderness unto our father. Yea, and he also made an oath unto us that he would tarry with us from that time forth.

36 Now we were desirous that he should tarry with us for this cause, that the Jews might not know concerning our flight into the wilderness, lest they should pursue us and destroy us.

37 And it came to pass that when Zoram had made an [aoath](#note37a) unto us, our [bfears](#note37b) did cease concerning him.

38 And it came to pass that we took the plates of brass and the servant of Laban, and departed into the wilderness, and journeyed unto the [atent](#note38a) of our father.

Chapter 5

About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that after we had come down into the wilderness unto our father, behold, he was filled with joy, and also my mother, Sariah, was exceedingly glad, for she truly had mourned because of us.

2 For she had supposed that we had perished in the wilderness; and she also had [acomplained](#note2a) against my father, telling him that he was a [bvisionary](#note2b) man; saying: Behold thou hast led us forth from the land of our inheritance, and my sons are no more, and we perish in the wilderness.

3 And after this manner of language had my mother complained against my father.

4 And it had come to pass that my father spake unto her, saying: I know that I am a [avisionary](#note4a) man; for if I had not seen the things of God in a [bvision](#note4b) I should not have known the goodness of God, but had tarried at Jerusalem, and had perished with my brethren.

5 But behold, I have [aobtained](#note5a) a [bland of promise](#note5b), in the which things I do rejoice; yea, and I [cknow](#note5c) that the Lord will deliver my sons out of the hands of Laban, and bring them down again unto us in the wilderness.

Chapter 8

Lehi sees a vision of the tree of life—He partakes of its fruit and desires his family to do likewise—He sees a rod of iron, a strait and narrow path, and the mists of darkness that enshroud men—Sariah, Nephi, and Sam partake of the fruit, but Laman and Lemuel refuse. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that we had gathered together all manner of [aseeds](#note1a) of every kind, both of grain of every kind, and also of the seeds of fruit of every kind.

2 And it came to pass that while my father tarried in the wilderness he spake unto us, saying: Behold, I have [adreamed](#note2a) a dream; or, in other words, I have [bseen](#note2b) a [cvision](#note2c).

3 And behold, because of the thing which I have seen, I have reason to rejoice in the Lord because of [aNephi](#note3a) and also of Sam; for I have reason to suppose that they, and also many of their seed, will be saved.

4 But behold, [aLaman](#note4a) and Lemuel, I fear exceedingly because of you; for behold, methought I saw in my dream, a dark and dreary wilderness.

5 And it came to pass that I saw a [aman](#note5a), and he was dressed in a white [brobe](#note5b); and he came and stood before me.

6 And it came to pass that he spake unto me, and bade me follow him.

7 And it came to pass that as I followed him I beheld myself that I was in a dark and dreary waste.

8 And after I had traveled for the space of many hours in darkness, I began to pray unto the Lord that he would have [amercy](#note8a) on me, according to the multitude of his tender mercies.

9 And it came to pass after I had prayed unto the Lord I beheld a large and spacious [afield](#note9a).

10 And it came to pass that I beheld a [atree](#note10a), whose [bfruit](#note10b) was desirable to make one [chappy](#note10c).

11 And it came to pass that I did go forth and partake of the [afruit](#note11a) thereof; and I beheld that it was most sweet, above all that I ever before tasted. Yea, and I beheld that the fruit thereof was white, to exceed all the [bwhiteness](#note11b) that I had ever seen.

12 And as I partook of the fruit thereof it filled my soul with exceedingly great [ajoy](#note12a); wherefore, I began to be [bdesirous](#note12b) that my family should partake of it also; for I knew that it was [cdesirable](#note12c) above all other fruit.

13 And as I cast my eyes round about, that perhaps I might discover my family also, I beheld a [ariver](#note13a) of water; and it ran along, and it was near the tree of which I was partaking the fruit.

14 And I looked to behold from whence it came; and I saw the head thereof a little way off; and at the head thereof I beheld your mother Sariah, and Sam, and [aNephi](#note14a); and they stood as if they knew not whither they should go.

15 And it came to pass that I beckoned unto them; and I also did say unto them with a loud voice that they should come unto me, and partake of the fruit, which was desirable above all other fruit.

16 And it came to pass that they did come unto me and partake of the fruit also.

17 And it came to pass that I was desirous that Laman and Lemuel should come and partake of the fruit also; wherefore, I cast mine eyes towards the head of the river, that perhaps I might see them.

18 And it came to pass that I saw them, but they would [anot](#note18a) come unto me and partake of the fruit.

19 And I beheld a [arod of iron](#note19a), and it extended along the bank of the river, and led to the tree by which I stood.

20 And I also beheld a [astrait](#note20a) and narrow path, which came along by the rod of iron, even to the tree by which I stood; and it also led by the head of the fountain, unto a large and spacious field, as if it had been a [bworld](#note20b).

21 And I saw numberless concourses of people, many of whom were [apressing](#note21a) forward, that they might obtain the [bpath](#note21b) which led unto the tree by which I stood.

22 And it came to pass that they did come forth, and commence in the path which led to the tree.

23 And it came to pass that there arose a [amist](#note23a) of darkness; yea, even an exceedingly great mist of darkness, insomuch that they who had commenced in the path did lose their way, that they wandered off and were [blost](#note23b).

24 And it came to pass that I beheld others pressing forward, and they came forth and caught hold of the end of the rod of iron; and they did press forward through the mist of darkness, [aclinging](#note24a) to the rod of iron, even until they did come forth and partake of the [bfruit](#note24b) of the tree.

25 And after they had partaken of the fruit of the tree they did cast their eyes about as if they were [aashamed](#note25a).

26 And I also cast my eyes round about, and beheld, on the [aother](#note26a) side of the river of water, a great and [bspacious](#note26b) building; and it stood as it were in the [cair](#note26c), high above the earth.

27 And it was filled with people, both old and young, both male and female; and their manner of dress was exceedingly fine; and they were in the [aattitude](#note27a) of [bmocking](#note27b) and pointing their fingers towards those who had come at and were partaking of the fruit.

28 And after they had [atasted](#note28a) of the fruit they were [bashamed](#note28b), because of those that were [cscoffing](#note28c) at them; and they [dfell away](#note28d) into forbidden paths and were lost.

Chapter 9

Nephi makes two sets of records—Each is called the plates of Nephi—The larger plates contain a secular history; the smaller ones deal primarily with sacred things. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And all these things did my father see, and hear, and speak, as he dwelt in a tent, in the [avalley of Lemuel](#note1a), and also a great many more things, which cannot be written upon these plates.

2 And now, as I have spoken concerning these plates, behold they are not the plates upon which I make a full account of the history of my people; for the [aplates](#note2a) upon which I make a full account of my people I have given the name of Nephi; wherefore, they are called the plates of Nephi, after mine own name; and these plates also are called the plates of Nephi.

3 Nevertheless, I have received a commandment of the Lord that I should make these plates, for the special [apurpose](#note3a) that there should be an account engraven of the [bministry](#note3b) of my people.

4 Upon the other plates should be engraven an account of the reign of the kings, and the wars and contentions of my people; wherefore these plates are for the more part of the ministry; and the [aother plates](#note4a) are for the more part of the reign of the kings and the wars and contentions of my people.

5 Wherefore, the Lord hath commanded me to make these plates for a [awise](#note5a) purpose in him, which purpose I know not.

6 But the Lord [aknoweth](#note6a) all things from the beginning; wherefore, he prepareth a way to accomplish all his works among the children of men; for behold, he hath all [bpower](#note6b) unto the fulfilling of all his words. And thus it is. Amen.

Chapter 10

Lehi predicts that the Jews will be taken captive by the Babylonians—He tells of the coming among the Jews of a Messiah, a Savior, a Redeemer—Lehi tells also of the coming of the one who should baptize the Lamb of God—Lehi tells of the death and resurrection of the Messiah—He compares the scattering and gathering of Israel to an olive tree—Nephi speaks of the Son of God, of the gift of the Holy Ghost, and of the need for righteousness. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And now I, Nephi, proceed to give an account upon [athese plates](#note1a) of my proceedings, and my reign and ministry; wherefore, to proceed with mine account, I must speak somewhat of the things of my father, and also of my brethren.

2 For behold, it came to pass after my father had made an end of speaking the words of his [adream](#note2a), and also of exhorting them to all diligence, he spake unto them concerning the Jews—

3 That after they should be destroyed, even that great city [aJerusalem](#note3a), and many be [bcarried away](#note3b) captive into [cBabylon](#note3c), according to the own due time of the Lord, they should [dreturn](#note3d) again, yea, even be brought back out of captivity; and after they should be brought back out of captivity they should possess again the land of their inheritance.

4 Yea, even [asix hundred](#note4a) years from the time that my father left Jerusalem, a [bprophet](#note4b) would the Lord God raise up among the [cJews](#note4c)—even a [dMessiah](#note4d), or, in other words, a Savior of the world.

5 And he also spake concerning the prophets, how great a number had [atestified](#note5a) of these things, concerning this Messiah, of whom he had spoken, or this Redeemer of the world.

6 Wherefore, all mankind were in a [alost](#note6a) and in a [bfallen](#note6b) state, and ever would be save they should rely on this Redeemer.

7 And he spake also concerning a [aprophet](#note7a) who should come before the Messiah, to prepare the way of the Lord—

8 Yea, even he should go forth and cry in the wilderness: [aPrepare](#note8a) ye the way of the Lord, and make his paths straight; for there standeth one among you whom ye know not; and he is mightier than I, whose shoe’s latchet I am not worthy to unloose. And much spake my father concerning this thing.

9 And my father said he should baptize in [aBethabara](#note9a), beyond Jordan; and he also said he should [bbaptize](#note9b) with water; even that he should baptize the Messiah with water.

10 And after he had baptized the Messiah with water, he should behold and bear record that he had baptized the [aLamb](#note10a) of God, who should take away the sins of the world.

11 And it came to pass after my father had spoken these words he spake unto my brethren concerning the gospel which should be preached among the Jews, and also concerning the [adwindling](#note11a) of the Jews in [bunbelief](#note11b). And after they had [cslain](#note11c) the Messiah, who should come, and after he had been slain he should [drise](#note11d) from the dead, and should make himself [emanifest](#note11e), by the Holy Ghost, unto the Gentiles.

12 Yea, even my father spake much concerning the Gentiles, and also concerning the house of Israel, that they should be compared like unto an [aolive tree](#note12a), whose [bbranches](#note12b) should be broken off and should be [cscattered](#note12c) upon all the face of the earth.

13 Wherefore, he said it must needs be that we should be led with one accord into the [aland of promise](#note13a), unto the fulfilling of the word of the Lord, that we should be scattered upon all the face of the earth.

14 And after the house of [aIsrael](#note14a) should be scattered they should be [bgathered](#note14b) together again; or, in fine, after the [cGentiles](#note14c) had received the fulness of the [dGospel](#note14d), the natural branches of the [eolive tree](#note14e), or the [fremnants](#note14f) of the house of [gIsrael](#note14g), should be grafted in, or [hcome](#note14h) to the knowledge of the true Messiah, their Lord and their Redeemer.

15 And after this manner of language did my father prophesy and speak unto my brethren, and also many more things which I do not write in this book; for I have written as many of them as were expedient for me in mine [aother book](#note15a).

16 And all these things, of which I have spoken, were done as my father dwelt in a [atent](#note16a), in the valley of Lemuel.

17 And it came to pass after I, Nephi, having heard all the [awords](#note17a) of my father, concerning the things which he saw in a [bvision](#note17b), and also the things which he spake by the power of the Holy Ghost, which power he received by faith on the Son of God—and the Son of God was the [cMessiah](#note17c) who should come—I, Nephi, was [ddesirous](#note17d) also that I might see, and hear, and know of these things, by the power of the [eHoly Ghost](#note17e), which is the [fgift](#note17f) of God unto [gall](#note17g) those who diligently seek him, as well in times of [hold](#note17h) as in the time that he should manifest himself unto the children of men.

18 For he is the [asame](#note18a) yesterday, today, and forever; and the way is prepared for all men from the foundation of the world, if it so be that they repent and come unto him.

19 For he that diligently [aseeketh](#note19a) shall find; and the [bmysteries](#note19b) of God shall be unfolded unto them, by the power of the [cHoly Ghost](#note19c), as well in these times as in times of old, and as well in times of old as in times to come; wherefore, the [dcourse](#note19d) of the Lord is one eternal round.

20 Therefore remember, O man, for all thy doings thou shalt be brought into [ajudgment](#note20a).

21 Wherefore, if ye have sought to do [awickedly](#note21a) in the days of your [bprobation](#note21b), then ye are found [cunclean](#note21c) before the judgment-seat of God; and no unclean thing can dwell with God; wherefore, ye must be cast off forever.

22 And the Holy Ghost giveth [aauthority](#note22a) that I should speak these things, and deny them not.

Chapter 11

Nephi sees the Spirit of the Lord and is shown in vision the tree of life—He sees the mother of the Son of God and learns of the condescension of God—He sees the baptism, ministry, and crucifixion of the Lamb of God—He sees also the call and ministry of the Twelve Apostles of the Lamb. About 600–592 B.C.

1 For it came to pass after I had desired to know the things that my father had seen, and believing that the Lord was able to make them known unto me, as I sat [apondering](#note1a) in mine heart I was [bcaught away](#note1b) in the Spirit of the Lord, yea, into an exceedingly high [cmountain](#note1c), which I never had before seen, and upon which I never had before set my foot.

2 And the Spirit said unto me: Behold, what [adesirest](#note2a) thou?

3 And I said: I desire to behold the things which my father [asaw](#note3a).

4 And the Spirit said unto me: [aBelievest](#note4a) thou that thy father saw the [btree](#note4b) of which he hath spoken?

5 And I said: Yea, thou knowest that I [abelieve](#note5a) all the words of my father.

6 And when I had spoken these words, the Spirit cried with a loud voice, saying: Hosanna to the Lord, the most high God; for he is God over all the [aearth](#note6a), yea, even above all. And blessed art thou, Nephi, because thou [bbelievest](#note6b) in the Son of the most high God; wherefore, thou shalt behold the things which thou hast desired.

7 And behold this thing shall be given unto thee for a [asign](#note7a), that after thou hast beheld the tree which bore the fruit which thy father tasted, thou shalt also behold a man descending out of heaven, and him shall ye witness; and after ye have witnessed him ye shall [bbear record](#note7b) that it is the Son of God.

8 And it came to pass that the Spirit said unto me: Look! And I looked and beheld a tree; and it was like unto the [atree](#note8a) which my father had seen; and the [bbeauty](#note8b) thereof was far beyond, yea, exceeding of all beauty; and the [cwhiteness](#note8c) thereof did exceed the whiteness of the driven snow.

9 And it came to pass after I had seen the tree, I said unto the Spirit: I behold thou hast shown unto me the tree which is [aprecious](#note9a) above all.

10 And he said unto me: What desirest thou?

11 And I said unto him: To know the [ainterpretation](#note11a) thereof—for I spake unto him as a man speaketh; for I beheld that he was in the [bform](#note11b) of a man; yet nevertheless, I knew that it was the Spirit of the Lord; and he spake unto me as a man speaketh with another.

12 And it came to pass that he said unto me: Look! And I looked as if to look upon him, and I saw him not; for he had gone from before my presence.

13 And it came to pass that I looked and beheld the great city of Jerusalem, and also other cities. And I beheld the city of Nazareth; and in the city of [aNazareth](#note13a) I beheld a [bvirgin](#note13b), and she was exceedingly fair and white.

14 And it came to pass that I saw the [aheavens](#note14a) open; and an angel came down and stood before me; and he said unto me: Nephi, what beholdest thou?

15 And I said unto him: A virgin, most beautiful and fair above all other virgins.

16 And he said unto me: Knowest thou the [acondescension](#note16a) of God?

17 And I said unto him: I know that he loveth his children; nevertheless, I do not know the meaning of all things.

18 And he said unto me: Behold, the [avirgin](#note18a) whom thou seest is the [bmother](#note18b) of the Son of God, after the manner of the flesh.

19 And it came to pass that I beheld that she was carried away in the Spirit; and after she had been carried away in the [aSpirit](#note19a) for the space of a time the angel spake unto me, saying: Look!

20 And I looked and beheld the virgin again, bearing a [achild](#note20a) in her arms.

21 And the angel said unto me: Behold the [aLamb](#note21a) of God, yea, even the [bSon](#note21b) of the Eternal [cFather](#note21c)! Knowest thou the meaning of the [dtree](#note21d) which thy father saw?

22 And I answered him, saying: Yea, it is the [alove](#note22a) of God, which [bsheddeth](#note22b) itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the [cmost](#note22c) desirable above all things.

23 And he spake unto me, saying: Yea, and the most [ajoyous](#note23a) to the soul.

24 And after he had said these words, he said unto me: Look! And I looked, and I beheld the Son of God [agoing](#note24a) forth among the children of men; and I saw many fall down at his feet and worship him.

25 And it came to pass that I beheld that the [arod of iron](#note25a), which my father had seen, was the [bword](#note25b) of God, which [cled](#note25c) to the fountain of [dliving waters](#note25d), or to the [etree of life](#note25e); which waters are a representation of the love of God; and I also beheld that the tree of life was a representation of the love of God.

26 And the angel said unto me again: Look and behold the [acondescension](#note26a) of God!

27 And I looked and [abeheld](#note27a) the Redeemer of the world, of whom my father had spoken; and I also beheld the [bprophet](#note27b) who should prepare the way before him. And the Lamb of God went forth and was [cbaptized](#note27c) of him; and after he was baptized, I beheld the heavens open, and the Holy Ghost come down out of heaven and abide upon him in the form of a [ddove](#note27d).

28 And I beheld that he went forth ministering unto the people, in [apower](#note28a) and great glory; and the multitudes were gathered together to hear him; and I beheld that they cast him out from among them.

29 And I also beheld [atwelve](#note29a) others following him. And it came to pass that they were [bcarried away](#note29b) in the Spirit from before my face, and I saw them not.

30 And it came to pass that the angel spake unto me again, saying: Look! And I looked, and I beheld the heavens open again, and I saw [aangels](#note30a) descending upon the children of men; and they did minister unto them.

31 And he spake unto me again, saying: Look! And I looked, and I beheld the Lamb of God going forth among the children of men. And I beheld multitudes of people who were [asick](#note31a), and who were afflicted with all manner of diseases, and with [bdevils](#note31b) and [cunclean spirits](#note31c); and the angel spake and showed all these things unto me. And they were [dhealed](#note31d) by the power of the Lamb of God; and the devils and the unclean spirits were cast out.

32 And it came to pass that the angel spake unto me again, saying: Look! And I looked and beheld the Lamb of God, that he was [ataken](#note32a) by the people; yea, the Son of the everlasting God was [bjudged](#note32b) of the world; and I saw and bear record.

33 And I, Nephi, saw that he was [alifted up](#note33a) upon the cross and [bslain](#note33b) for the sins of the world.

34 And after he was slain I saw the multitudes of the earth, that they were gathered together to [afight](#note34a) against the apostles of the Lamb; for thus were the twelve called by the angel of the Lord.

35 And the multitude of the earth was gathered together; and I beheld that they were in a large and spacious [abuilding](#note35a), like unto the building which my father saw. And the angel of the Lord spake unto me again, saying: Behold the world and the wisdom thereof; yea, behold the house of Israel hath gathered together to [bfight](#note35b) against the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

36 And it came to pass that I saw and bear record, that the great and spacious building was the [apride](#note36a) of the world; and it [bfell](#note36b), and the fall thereof was exceedingly great. And the angel of the Lord spake unto me again, saying: Thus shall be the destruction of all nations, kindreds, tongues, and people, that shall fight against the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

Chapter 12

Nephi sees in vision the land of promise; the righteousness, iniquity, and downfall of its inhabitants; the coming of the Lamb of God among them; how the Twelve Disciples and the Twelve Apostles will judge Israel; and the loathsome and filthy state of those who dwindle in unbelief. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that the angel said unto me: Look, and behold thy seed, and also the seed of thy brethren. And I looked and beheld the [aland of promise](#note1a); and I beheld multitudes of people, yea, even as it were in number as many as the [bsand](#note1b) of the sea.

2 And it came to pass that I beheld multitudes gathered together to battle, one against the other; and I beheld [awars](#note2a), and rumors of wars, and great slaughters with the sword among my people.

3 And it came to pass that I beheld many generations pass away, after the manner of wars and contentions in the land; and I beheld many cities, yea, even that I did not number them.

4 And it came to pass that I saw a [amist](#note4a) of [bdarkness](#note4b) on the face of the land of promise; and I saw lightnings, and I heard thunderings, and earthquakes, and all manner of tumultuous noises; and I saw the earth and the rocks, that they rent; and I saw mountains tumbling into pieces; and I saw the plains of the earth, that they were [cbroken up](#note4c); and I saw many cities that they were [dsunk](#note4d); and I saw many that they were burned with fire; and I saw many that did tumble to the earth, because of the quaking thereof.

5 And it came to pass after I saw these things, I saw the [avapor](#note5a) of darkness, that it passed from off the face of the earth; and behold, I saw multitudes who had not fallen because of the great and terrible judgments of the Lord.

6 And I saw the heavens open, and the [aLamb](#note6a) of God descending out of heaven; and he came down and [bshowed](#note6b) himself unto them.

7 And I also saw and bear record that the Holy Ghost fell upon [atwelve](#note7a) others; and they were ordained of God, and chosen.

8 And the angel spake unto me, saying: Behold the twelve disciples of the Lamb, who are chosen to minister unto thy seed.

9 And he said unto me: Thou rememberest the [atwelve apostles](#note9a) of the Lamb? Behold they are they who shall [bjudge](#note9b) the twelve tribes of Israel; wherefore, the twelve ministers of thy seed shall be judged of them; for ye are of the house of Israel.

10 And these [atwelve ministers](#note10a) whom thou beholdest shall judge thy seed. And, behold, they are righteous forever; for because of their faith in the Lamb of God their [bgarments](#note10b) are made white in his blood.

11 And the angel said unto me: Look! And I looked, and beheld [athree](#note11a) generations pass away in righteousness; and their garments were white even like unto the Lamb of God. And the angel said unto me: These are made white in the blood of the Lamb, because of their faith in him.

12 And I, Nephi, also saw many of the [afourth](#note12a) generation who passed away in righteousness.

13 And it came to pass that I saw the multitudes of the earth gathered together.

14 And the angel said unto me: Behold thy seed, and also the seed of thy brethren.

15 And it came to pass that I looked and beheld the people of my seed gathered together in multitudes [aagainst](#note15a) the seed of my brethren; and they were gathered together to battle.

16 And the angel spake unto me, saying: Behold the fountain of [afilthy](#note16a) water which thy father saw; yea, even the [briver](#note16b) of which he spake; and the depths thereof are the depths of [chell](#note16c).

17 And the [amists](#note17a) of darkness are the temptations of the devil, which [bblindeth](#note17b) the eyes, and hardeneth the hearts of the children of men, and leadeth them away into [cbroad roads](#note17c), that they perish and are lost.

18 And the large and spacious [abuilding](#note18a), which thy father saw, is vain [bimaginations](#note18b) and the [cpride](#note18c) of the children of men. And a great and a terrible [dgulf](#note18d) divideth them; yea, even the word of the [ejustice](#note18e) of the Eternal God, and the Messiah who is the Lamb of God, of whom the Holy Ghost beareth record, from the beginning of the world until this time, and from this time henceforth and forever.

19 And while the angel spake these words, I beheld and saw that the seed of my brethren did contend against my seed, according to the word of the angel; and because of the pride of my seed, and the [atemptations](#note19a) of the devil, I beheld that the seed of my brethren did [boverpower](#note19b) the people of my seed.

20 And it came to pass that I beheld, and saw the people of the seed of my brethren that they had overcome my seed; and they went forth in multitudes upon the face of the land.

21 And I saw them gathered together in multitudes; and I saw [awars](#note21a) and rumors of wars among them; and in wars and rumors of wars I saw [bmany generations](#note21b) pass away.

22 And the angel said unto me: Behold these shall [adwindle](#note22a) in unbelief.

23 And it came to pass that I beheld, after they had dwindled in unbelief they became a [adark](#note23a), and loathsome, and a [bfilthy](#note23b) people, full of [cidleness](#note23c) and all manner of abominations.

Chapter 13

Nephi sees in vision the church of the devil set up among the Gentiles, the discovery and colonizing of America, the loss of many plain and precious parts of the Bible, the resultant state of gentile apostasy, the restoration of the gospel, the coming forth of latter-day scripture, and the building up of Zion. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that the angel spake unto me, saying: Look! And I looked and beheld many nations and kingdoms.

2 And the angel said unto me: What beholdest thou? And I said: I behold many [anations](#note2a) and kingdoms.

3 And he said unto me: These are the nations and kingdoms of the Gentiles.

4 And it came to pass that I saw among the nations of the [aGentiles](#note4a) the formation of a [bgreat church](#note4b).

5 And the angel said unto me: Behold the formation of a [achurch](#note5a) which is most abominable above all other churches, which [bslayeth](#note5b) the saints of God, yea, and tortureth them and bindeth them down, and yoketh them with a [cyoke](#note5c) of iron, and bringeth them down into captivity.

6 And it came to pass that I beheld this [agreat](#note6a) and [babominable](#note6b) church; and I saw the [cdevil](#note6c) that he was the founder of it.

7 And I also saw [agold](#note7a), and silver, and silks, and scarlets, and fine-twined [blinen](#note7b), and all manner of precious clothing; and I saw many harlots.

8 And the angel spake unto me, saying: Behold the gold, and the silver, and the silks, and the scarlets, and the fine-twined linen, and the precious clothing, and the harlots, are the [adesires](#note8a) of this great and abominable church.

9 And also for the [apraise](#note9a) of the world do they [bdestroy](#note9b) the saints of God, and bring them down into captivity.

10 And it came to pass that I looked and beheld many waters; and they divided the Gentiles from the seed of my brethren.

11 And it came to pass that the angel said unto me: Behold the wrath of God is upon the seed of thy brethren.

12 And I looked and beheld a man among the Gentiles, who was separated from the seed of my brethren by the many waters; and I beheld the Spirit of God, that it came down and [awrought](#note12a) upon the man; and he went forth upon the many waters, even unto the seed of my brethren, who were in the promised land.

13 And it came to pass that I beheld the Spirit of God, that it wrought upon other Gentiles; and they went forth out of captivity, upon the many waters.

14 And it came to pass that I beheld many [amultitudes](#note14a) of the Gentiles upon the [bland of promise](#note14b); and I beheld the wrath of God, that it was upon the seed of my brethren; and they were [cscattered](#note14c) before the Gentiles and were smitten.

15 And I beheld the Spirit of the Lord, that it was upon the Gentiles, and they did prosper and [aobtain](#note15a) the [bland](#note15b) for their inheritance; and I beheld that they were white, and exceedingly fair and [cbeautiful](#note15c), like unto my people before they were [dslain](#note15d).

16 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, beheld that the Gentiles who had gone forth out of captivity did humble themselves before the Lord; and the power of the Lord was [awith them](#note16a).

17 And I beheld that their mother Gentiles were gathered together upon the waters, and upon the land also, to battle against them.

18 And I beheld that the power of God was with them, and also that the wrath of God was upon all those that were gathered together [aagainst](#note18a) them to battle.

19 And I, Nephi, beheld that the Gentiles that had gone out of captivity were [adelivered](#note19a) by the power of God out of the hands of all other nations.

20 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, beheld that they did prosper in the land; and I beheld a [abook](#note20a), and it was carried forth among them.

21 And the angel said unto me: Knowest thou the meaning of the book?

22 And I said unto him: I know not.

23 And he said: Behold it proceedeth out of the mouth of a Jew. And I, Nephi, beheld it; and he said unto me: The [abook](#note23a) that thou beholdest is a [brecord](#note23b) of the [cJews](#note23c), which contains the covenants of the Lord, which he hath made unto the house of Israel; and it also containeth many of the prophecies of the holy prophets; and it is a record like unto the engravings which are upon the [dplates of brass](#note23d), save there are not so many; nevertheless, they contain the covenants of the Lord, which he hath made unto the house of Israel; wherefore, they are of great worth unto the Gentiles.

24 And the angel of the Lord said unto me: Thou hast beheld that the [abook](#note24a) proceeded forth from the mouth of a Jew; and when it proceeded forth from the mouth of a Jew it contained the fulness of the gospel of the Lord, of whom the twelve apostles bear record; and they bear record according to the truth which is in the Lamb of God.

25 Wherefore, these things go forth from the [aJews](#note25a) in purity unto the [bGentiles](#note25b), according to the truth which is in God.

26 And after they go forth by the [ahand](#note26a) of the twelve apostles of the Lamb, from the Jews [bunto](#note26b) the Gentiles, thou seest the formation of that [cgreat](#note26c) and abominable [dchurch](#note26d), which is most abominable above all other churches; for behold, they have [etaken](#note26e) away from the gospel of the Lamb many parts which are [fplain](#note26f) and most precious; and also many covenants of the Lord have they taken away.

27 And all this have they done that they might pervert the right ways of the Lord, that they might blind the eyes and harden the hearts of the children of men.

28 Wherefore, thou seest that after the book hath gone forth through the hands of the great and abominable church, that there are many plain and [aprecious](#note28a) things taken away from the book, which is the book of the Lamb of God.

29 And after these plain and precious things were [ataken](#note29a) away it goeth forth unto all the nations of the Gentiles; and after it goeth forth unto all the nations of the Gentiles, yea, even across the many waters which thou hast seen with the Gentiles which have gone forth out of captivity, thou seest—because of the many plain and precious things which have been taken out of the book, which were plain unto the understanding of the children of men, according to the plainness which is in the Lamb of God—because of these things which are taken away out of the gospel of the Lamb, an exceedingly great many do stumble, yea, insomuch that Satan hath great power over them.

30 Nevertheless, thou beholdest that the Gentiles who have gone forth out of captivity, and have been lifted up by the power of God above all other nations, upon the face of the land which is choice above all other lands, which is the land that the Lord God hath covenanted with thy father that his seed should have for the [aland](#note30a) of their inheritance; wherefore, thou seest that the Lord God will not suffer that the Gentiles will utterly destroy the [bmixture](#note30b) of thy [cseed](#note30c), which are among thy brethren.

31 Neither will he suffer that the Gentiles shall [adestroy](#note31a) the seed of thy brethren.

32 Neither will the Lord God suffer that the Gentiles shall forever remain in that awful state of blindness, which thou beholdest they are in, because of the plain and most precious parts of the gospel of the Lamb which have been kept back by that [aabominable](#note32a) church, whose formation thou hast seen.

33 Wherefore saith the Lamb of God: I will be [amerciful](#note33a) unto the Gentiles, unto the visiting of the remnant of the house of Israel in great judgment.

34 And it came to pass that the angel of the Lord spake unto me, saying: Behold, saith the Lamb of God, after I have [avisited](#note34a) the [bremnant](#note34b) of the house of Israel—and this remnant of whom I speak is the seed of thy father—wherefore, after I have visited them in judgment, and smitten them by the hand of the Gentiles, and after the Gentiles do [cstumble](#note34c) exceedingly, because of the most plain and precious parts of the [dgospel](#note34d) of the Lamb which have been kept back by that abominable church, which is the mother of harlots, saith the Lamb—I will be merciful unto the [eGentiles](#note34e) in that day, insomuch that I will [fbring forth](#note34f) unto them, in mine own power, much of my [ggospel](#note34g), which shall be plain and precious, saith the Lamb.

35 For, behold, saith the Lamb: I will manifest myself unto thy seed, that they shall write many things which I shall minister unto them, which shall be plain and precious; and after thy seed shall be destroyed, and dwindle in unbelief, and also the seed of thy brethren, behold, [athese things](#note35a) shall be hid up, to come forth unto the Gentiles, by the gift and power of the Lamb.

36 And in them shall be written my [agospel](#note36a), saith the Lamb, and my [brock](#note36b) and my salvation.

37 And [ablessed](#note37a) are they who shall seek to bring forth my [bZion](#note37b) at that day, for they shall have the [cgift](#note37c) and the [dpower](#note37d) of the Holy Ghost; and if they [eendure](#note37e) unto the end they shall be [flifted up](#note37f) at the last day, and shall be saved in the everlasting [gkingdom](#note37g) of the Lamb; and whoso shall [hpublish](#note37h) peace, yea, tidings of great joy, how beautiful upon the mountains shall they be.

38 And it came to pass that I beheld the remnant of the seed of my brethren, and also the [abook](#note38a) of the Lamb of God, which had proceeded forth from the mouth of the Jew, that it came forth from the Gentiles [bunto](#note38b) the remnant of the seed of my brethren.

39 And after it had come forth unto them I beheld [aother](#note39a) [bbooks](#note39b), which came forth by the power of the Lamb, from the Gentiles unto them, unto the [cconvincing](#note39c) of the Gentiles and the remnant of the seed of my brethren, and also the Jews who were scattered upon all the face of the earth, that the records of the prophets and of the twelve apostles of the Lamb are [dtrue](#note39d).

40 And the angel spake unto me, saying: These [alast records](#note40a), which thou hast seen among the Gentiles, shall [bestablish](#note40b) the truth of the [cfirst](#note40c), which are of the [dtwelve apostles](#note40d) of the Lamb, and shall make known the plain and precious things which have been taken away from them; and shall make known to all kindreds, tongues, and people, that the Lamb of God is the Son of the Eternal Father, and the [eSavior](#note40e) of the world; and that all men must come unto him, or they cannot be saved.

41 And they must come according to the words which shall be established by the mouth of the Lamb; and the words of the Lamb shall be made known in the records of thy seed, as well as in the [arecords](#note41a) of the twelve apostles of the Lamb; wherefore they both shall be established in [bone](#note41b); for there is [cone God](#note41c) and one [dShepherd](#note41d) over all the earth.

42 And the time cometh that he shall manifest himself unto all nations, both unto the [aJews](#note42a) and also unto the Gentiles; and after he has manifested himself unto the Jews and also unto the Gentiles, then he shall manifest himself unto the Gentiles and also unto the Jews, and the [blast](#note42b) shall be first, and the [cfirst](#note42c) shall be last.

Chapter 14

An angel tells Nephi of the blessings and cursings to fall upon the Gentiles—There are only two churches: the Church of the Lamb of God and the church of the devil—The Saints of God in all nations are persecuted by the great and abominable church—The Apostle John will write concerning the end of the world. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it shall come to pass, that if the [aGentiles](#note1a) shall hearken unto the Lamb of God in that day that he shall manifest himself unto them in word, and also in [bpower](#note1b), in very deed, unto the [ctaking](#note1c) away of their [dstumbling blocks](#note1d)—

2 And harden not their hearts against the Lamb of God, they shall be numbered among the seed of thy father; yea, they shall be [anumbered](#note2a) among the house of Israel; and they shall be a [bblessed](#note2b) people upon the [cpromised land](#note2c) forever; they shall be no more brought down into captivity; and the house of Israel shall no more be confounded.

3 And that great [apit](#note3a), which hath been digged for them by that great and abominable church, which was founded by the devil and his children, that he might lead away the souls of men down to hell—yea, that great pit which hath been digged for the destruction of men shall be filled by those who digged it, unto their utter destruction, saith the Lamb of God; not the destruction of the soul, save it be the casting of it into that [bhell](#note3b) which hath no end.

4 For behold, this is according to the [acaptivity](#note4a) of the devil, and also according to the justice of God, upon all those who will work wickedness and abomination before him.

5 And it came to pass that the angel spake unto me, Nephi, saying: Thou hast beheld that if the Gentiles repent it shall be [awell](#note5a) with them; and thou also knowest concerning the covenants of the Lord unto the house of Israel; and thou also hast heard that whoso [brepenteth](#note5b) not must perish.

6 Therefore, [awo](#note6a) be unto the Gentiles if it so be that they harden their hearts against the Lamb of God.

7 For the time cometh, saith the Lamb of God, that I will work a great and a [amarvelous work](#note7a) among the children of men; a [bwork](#note7b) which shall be everlasting, either on the one hand or on the other—either to the convincing of them unto [cpeace](#note7c) and [dlife eternal](#note7d), or unto the deliverance of them to the hardness of their hearts and the blindness of their minds unto their being brought down into captivity, and also into destruction, both temporally and spiritually, according to the [ecaptivity](#note7e) of the devil, of which I have spoken.

8 And it came to pass that when the angel had spoken these words, he said unto me: Rememberest thou the [acovenants](#note8a) of the Father unto the house of Israel? I said unto him, Yea.

9 And it came to pass that he said unto me: Look, and behold that great and abominable church, which is the mother of abominations, whose founder is the [adevil](#note9a).

10 And he said unto me: Behold there are save [atwo](#note10a) churches only; the one is the church of the Lamb of God, and the [bother](#note10b) is the church of the [cdevil](#note10c); wherefore, [dwhoso](#note10d) belongeth not to the church of the Lamb of God belongeth to that great church, which is the mother of abominations; and she is the [ewhore](#note10e) of all the earth.

11 And it came to pass that I looked and beheld the whore of all the earth, and she sat upon many [awaters](#note11a); and she had dominion over [ball](#note11b) the earth, among all nations, kindreds, tongues, and people.

12 And it came to pass that I beheld the church of the Lamb of God, and its numbers were [afew](#note12a), because of the wickedness and abominations of the whore who sat upon many waters; nevertheless, I beheld that the church of the Lamb, who were the saints of God, were also upon [ball](#note12b) the face of the earth; and their dominions upon the face of the earth were small, because of the wickedness of the great whore whom I saw.

13 And it came to pass that I beheld that the great mother of abominations did gather together multitudes upon the face of all the earth, among all the nations of the Gentiles, to [afight](#note13a) against the Lamb of God.

14 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, beheld the power of the Lamb of God, that it descended upon the saints of the church of the Lamb, and upon the covenant people of the Lord, who were scattered upon all the face of the earth; and they were [aarmed](#note14a) with [brighteousness](#note14b) and with the [cpower](#note14c) of God in great glory.

15 And it came to pass that I beheld that the wrath of God was [apoured](#note15a) out upon that great and abominable church, insomuch that there were wars and rumors of wars among all the [bnations](#note15b) and kindreds of the earth.

16 And as there began to be [awars](#note16a) and rumors of wars among all the nations which belonged to the mother of abominations, the angel spake unto me, saying: Behold, the wrath of God is upon the mother of harlots; and behold, thou seest all these things—

17 And when the [aday](#note17a) cometh that the [bwrath](#note17b) of God is poured out upon the mother of harlots, which is the great and abominable church of all the earth, whose founder is the devil, then, at that day, the [cwork](#note17c) of the Father shall commence, in preparing the way for the fulfilling of his [dcovenants](#note17d), which he hath made to his people who are of the house of Israel.

18 And it came to pass that the angel spake unto me, saying: Look!

19 And I looked and beheld a man, and he was dressed in a white robe.

20 And the angel said unto me: Behold [aone](#note20a) of the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

21 Behold, he shall [asee](#note21a) and [bwrite](#note21b) the [cremainder](#note21c) of these things; yea, and also many things which have been.

22 And he shall also write concerning the end of the world.

23 Wherefore, the things which he shall write are just and true; and behold they are written in the [abook](#note23a) which thou beheld proceeding out of the mouth of the Jew; and at the time they proceeded out of the mouth of the Jew, or, at the time the book proceeded out of the mouth of the Jew, the things which were written were plain and pure, and most [bprecious](#note23b) and easy to the understanding of all men.

24 And behold, the things which this [aapostle](#note24a) of the Lamb shall write are many things which thou hast seen; and behold, the remainder shalt thou see.

26 And also others who have been, to them hath he shown all things, and they have [awritten](#note26a) them; and they are [bsealed](#note26b) up to come forth in their purity, according to the truth which is in the Lamb, in the own due time of the Lord, unto the house of Israel.

28 And behold, I, Nephi, am forbidden that I should write the remainder of the things which I saw and heard; wherefore the things which I have written sufficeth me; and I have written but a small part of the things which I saw.

29 And I bear record that I saw the things which my [afather](#note29a) saw, and the angel of the Lord did make them known unto me.

30 And now I make an end of speaking concerning the things which I saw while I was [acarried away](#note30a) in the Spirit; and if all the things which I saw are not written, the things which I have written are [btrue](#note30b). And thus it is. Amen.

Chapter 15

Lehi’s seed are to receive the gospel from the Gentiles in the latter days—The gathering of Israel is likened unto an olive tree whose natural branches will be grafted in again—Nephi interprets the vision of the tree of life and speaks of the justice of God in dividing the wicked from the righteous. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that after I, Nephi, had been carried away in the Spirit, and seen all these things, I returned to the tent of my father.

2 And it came to pass that I beheld my brethren, and they were disputing one with another concerning the things which my father had spoken unto them.

3 For he truly spake many great things unto them, which were hard to be [aunderstood](#note3a), save a man should inquire of the Lord; and they being hard in their hearts, therefore they did not look unto the Lord as they ought.

4 And now I, Nephi, was grieved because of the hardness of their hearts, and also, because of the things which I had seen, and knew they must unavoidably come to pass because of the great wickedness of the children of men.

5 And it came to pass that I was overcome because of my afflictions, for I considered that mine [aafflictions](#note5a) were great above all, because of the [bdestruction](#note5b) of my people, for I had beheld their fall.

6 And it came to pass that after I had received [astrength](#note6a) I spake unto my brethren, desiring to know of them the cause of their disputations.

7 And they said: Behold, we cannot understand the words which our father hath spoken concerning the natural branches of the [aolive tree](#note7a), and also concerning the Gentiles.

8 And I said unto them: Have ye [ainquired](#note8a) of the Lord?

9 And they said unto me: [aWe](#note9a) have not; for the Lord maketh no such thing known unto us.

10 Behold, I said unto them: How is it that ye do not keep the commandments of the Lord? How is it that ye will [aperish](#note10a), because of the hardness of your hearts?

11 Do ye not remember the things which the Lord hath said?—If ye will not harden your hearts, and [aask](#note11a) me in [bfaith](#note11b), believing that ye shall receive, with diligence in keeping my commandments, surely these things shall be made known unto you.

12 Behold, I say unto you, that the house of Israel was compared unto an olive tree, by the Spirit of the Lord which was in our father; and behold are we not broken off from the house of Israel, and are we not a [abranch](#note12a) of the house of Israel?

13 And now, the thing which our father meaneth concerning the grafting in of the natural branches through the fulness of the Gentiles, is, that in the latter days, when our seed shall have [adwindled](#note13a) in unbelief, yea, for the space of many years, and many generations after the [bMessiah](#note13b) shall be manifested in body unto the children of men, then shall the fulness of the [cgospel](#note13c) of the Messiah come unto the Gentiles, and from the [dGentiles](#note13d) unto the remnant of our seed—

14 And at that day shall the remnant of our [aseed](#note14a) [bknow](#note14b) that they are of the house of Israel, and that they are the [ccovenant](#note14c) people of the Lord; and then shall they know and [dcome](#note14d) to the [eknowledge](#note14e) of their forefathers, and also to the knowledge of the gospel of their Redeemer, which was ministered unto their fathers by him; wherefore, they shall come to the knowledge of their Redeemer and the very points of his doctrine, that they may know how to come unto him and be saved.

15 And then at that day will they not rejoice and give praise unto their everlasting God, their [arock](#note15a) and their salvation? Yea, at that day, will they not receive the strength and nourishment from the true [bvine](#note15b)? Yea, will they not come unto the true fold of God?

16 Behold, I say unto you, Yea; they shall be remembered again among the house of Israel; they shall be [agrafted](#note16a) in, being a natural branch of the olive tree, into the true olive tree.

17 And this is what our father meaneth; and he meaneth that it will not come to pass until after they are scattered by the Gentiles; and he meaneth that it shall come by way of the Gentiles, that the Lord may show his power unto the Gentiles, for the very cause that he shall be [arejected](#note17a) of the Jews, or of the house of Israel.

18 Wherefore, our father hath not spoken of our seed alone, but also of all the house of Israel, pointing to the covenant which should be fulfilled in the latter days; which covenant the Lord made to our father Abraham, saying: In thy [aseed](#note18a) shall all the kindreds of the earth be [bblessed](#note18b).

19 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, spake much unto them concerning these things; yea, I spake unto them concerning the [arestoration](#note19a) of the Jews in the latter days.

20 And I did rehearse unto them the words of [aIsaiah](#note20a), who spake [bconcerning](#note20b) the [crestoration](#note20c) of the Jews, or of the house of Israel; and after they were restored they should no more be confounded, neither should they be scattered again. And it came to pass that I did speak many words unto my brethren, that they were pacified and did [dhumble](#note20d) themselves before the Lord.

21 And it came to pass that they did speak unto me again, saying: What meaneth this thing which our father saw in a dream? What meaneth the [atree](#note21a) which he saw?

22 And I said unto them: It was a representation of the [atree of life](#note22a).

23 And they said unto me: What meaneth the [arod of iron](#note23a) which our father saw, that led to the tree?

24 And I said unto them that it was the [aword](#note24a) of God; and whoso would hearken unto the word of God, and would [bhold](#note24b) fast unto it, they would never perish; neither could the [ctemptations](#note24c) and the fiery [ddarts](#note24d) of the [eadversary](#note24e) overpower them unto blindness, to lead them away to destruction.

25 Wherefore, I, Nephi, did exhort them to give [aheed](#note25a) unto the word of the Lord; yea, I did exhort them with all the energies of my soul, and with all the [bfaculty](#note25b) which I possessed, that they would give heed to the word of God and remember to keep his commandments always in all things.

26 And they said unto me: What meaneth the [ariver](#note26a) of water which our father saw?

27 And I said unto them that the [awater](#note27a) which my father saw was [bfilthiness](#note27b); and so much was his mind swallowed up in other things that he beheld not the filthiness of the water.

28 And I said unto them that it was an awful [agulf](#note28a), which separated the wicked from the tree of life, and also from the saints of God.

29 And I said unto them that it was a representation of that awful [ahell](#note29a), which the angel said unto me was prepared for the wicked.

30 And I said unto them that our father also saw that the [ajustice](#note30a) of God did also divide the wicked from the righteous; and the brightness thereof was like unto the brightness of a flaming [bfire](#note30b), which ascendeth up unto God forever and ever, and hath no end.

31 And they said unto me: Doth this thing mean the torment of the body in the days of [aprobation](#note31a), or doth it mean the final state of the soul after the [bdeath](#note31b) of the temporal body, or doth it speak of the things which are temporal?

32 And it came to pass that I said unto them that it was a representation of things both temporal and spiritual; for the day should come that they must be judged of their [aworks](#note32a), yea, even the works which were done by the temporal body in their days of [bprobation](#note32b).

33 Wherefore, if they should [adie](#note33a) in their wickedness they must be [bcast off](#note33b) also, as to the things which are spiritual, which are pertaining to righteousness; wherefore, they must be brought to stand before God, to be [cjudged](#note33c) of their [dworks](#note33d); and if their works have been filthiness they must needs be [efilthy](#note33e); and if they be filthy it must needs be that they cannot [fdwell](#note33f) in the kingdom of God; if so, the kingdom of God must be filthy also.

34 But behold, I say unto you, the kingdom of God is not filthy, and there cannot any unclean thing enter into the kingdom of God; wherefore there must needs be a place of [afilthiness](#note34a) prepared for that which is filthy.

35 And there is a place prepared, yea, even that [aawful](#note35a) [bhell](#note35b) of which I have spoken, and the [cdevil](#note35c) is the preparator of it; wherefore the final state of the souls of men is to dwell in the kingdom of God, or to be cast out because of that [djustice](#note35d) of which I have spoken.

36 Wherefore, the wicked are rejected from the righteous, and also from that [atree of life](#note36a), whose fruit is most precious and most [bdesirable](#note36b) above all other fruits; yea, and it is the [cgreatest](#note36c) of all the [dgifts](#note36d) of God. And thus I spake unto my brethren. Amen.

Chapter 16

The wicked take the truth to be hard—Lehi’s sons marry the daughters of Ishmael—The Liahona guides their course in the wilderness—Messages from the Lord are written on the Liahona from time to time—Ishmael dies; his family murmurs because of afflictions. About 600–592 B.C.

1 And now it came to pass that after I, Nephi, had made an end of speaking to my brethren, behold they said unto me: Thou hast declared unto us hard things, more than we are able to bear.

2 And it came to pass that I said unto them that I knew that I had spoken [ahard things](#note2a) against the wicked, according to the truth; and the righteous have I justified, and testified that they should be lifted up at the last day; wherefore, the [bguilty](#note2b) taketh the [ctruth](#note2c) to be hard, for it [dcutteth](#note2d) them to the very center.

3 And now my brethren, if ye were righteous and were willing to hearken to the truth, and give heed unto it, that ye might [awalk](#note3a) uprightly before God, then ye would not murmur because of the truth, and say: Thou speakest hard things against us.

4 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did exhort my brethren, with all diligence, to keep the commandments of the Lord.

5 And it came to pass that they did [ahumble](#note5a) themselves before the Lord; insomuch that I had joy and great hopes of them, that they would walk in the paths of righteousness.

6 Now, all these things were said and done as my father dwelt in a tent in the [avalley](#note6a) which he called Lemuel.

7 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, took one of the [adaughters](#note7a) of Ishmael to [bwife](#note7b); and also, my brethren took of the [cdaughters](#note7c) of Ishmael to wife; and also [dZoram](#note7d) took the eldest daughter of Ishmael to wife.

8 And thus my father had fulfilled all the [acommandments](#note8a) of the Lord which had been given unto him. And also, I, Nephi, had been blessed of the Lord exceedingly.

9 And it came to pass that the voice of the Lord spake unto my father by night, and commanded him that on the morrow he should take his [ajourney](#note9a) into the wilderness.

10 And it came to pass that as my father arose in the morning, and went forth to the tent door, to his great astonishment he beheld upon the ground a round [aball](#note10a) of curious workmanship; and it was of fine brass. And within the ball were two spindles; and the one [bpointed](#note10b) the way whither we should go into the wilderness.

11 And it came to pass that we did gather together whatsoever things we should carry into the wilderness, and all the remainder of our provisions which the Lord had given unto us; and we did take [aseed](#note11a) of every kind that we might carry into the wilderness.

12 And it came to pass that we did take our tents and depart into the wilderness, across the river Laman.

13 And it came to pass that we traveled for the space of four days, nearly a south-southeast direction, and we did pitch our tents again; and we did call the name of the place [aShazer](#note13a).

14 And it came to pass that we did take our bows and our arrows, and go forth into the wilderness to slay food for our families; and after we had slain food for our families we did return again to our families in the wilderness, to the place of Shazer. And we did go forth again in the wilderness, following the same direction, keeping in the most fertile parts of the wilderness, which were in the borders near the [aRed Sea](#note14a).

15 And it came to pass that we did travel for the space of many days, [aslaying](#note15a) food by the way, with our bows and our arrows and our stones and our slings.

16 And we did follow the [adirections](#note16a) of the ball, which led us in the more fertile parts of the wilderness.

17 And after we had traveled for the space of many days, we did pitch our tents for the space of a time, that we might again rest ourselves and obtain food for our families.

18 And it came to pass that as I, Nephi, went forth to slay food, behold, I did break my bow, which was made of fine [asteel](#note18a); and after I did break my bow, behold, my brethren were angry with me because of the loss of my bow, for we did obtain no food.

19 And it came to pass that we did return without food to our families, and being much fatigued, because of their journeying, they did suffer much for the want of food.

20 And it came to pass that Laman and Lemuel and the sons of Ishmael did begin to murmur exceedingly, because of their sufferings and afflictions in the wilderness; and also my father began to murmur against the Lord his God; yea, and they were all exceedingly sorrowful, even that they did [amurmur](#note20a) against the Lord.

21 Now it came to pass that I, Nephi, having been afflicted with my brethren because of the loss of my bow, and their bows having lost their [asprings](#note21a), it began to be exceedingly difficult, yea, insomuch that we could obtain no food.

22 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did speak much unto my brethren, because they had hardened their hearts again, even unto [acomplaining](#note22a) against the Lord their God.

23 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did [amake](#note23a) out of wood a bow, and out of a straight stick, an arrow; wherefore, I did arm myself with a bow and an arrow, with a sling and with stones. And I said unto my [bfather](#note23b): Whither shall I go to obtain food?

24 And it came to pass that he did [ainquire](#note24a) of the Lord, for they had [bhumbled](#note24b) themselves because of my words; for I did say many things unto them in the energy of my soul.

25 And it came to pass that the voice of the Lord came unto my father; and he was truly [achastened](#note25a) because of his murmuring against the Lord, insomuch that he was brought down into the depths of sorrow.

26 And it came to pass that the voice of the Lord said unto him: Look upon the ball, and behold the things which are written.

27 And it came to pass that when my father beheld the things which were [awritten](#note27a) upon the ball, he did fear and tremble exceedingly, and also my brethren and the sons of Ishmael and our wives.

28 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, beheld the pointers which were in the ball, that they did work according to the [afaith](#note28a) and diligence and heed which we did give unto them.

29 And there was also written upon them a new writing, which was plain to be read, which did give us [aunderstanding](#note29a) concerning the ways of the Lord; and it was written and changed from time to time, according to the faith and diligence which we gave unto it. And thus we see that by [bsmall](#note29b) means the Lord can bring about great things.

30 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did go forth up into the top of the mountain, according to the [adirections](#note30a) which were given upon the ball.

31 And it came to pass that I did slay wild [abeasts](#note31a), insomuch that I did obtain food for our families.

32 And it came to pass that I did return to our tents, bearing the beasts which I had slain; and now when they beheld that I had obtained [afood](#note32a), how great was their joy! And it came to pass that they did humble themselves before the Lord, and did give thanks unto him.

33 And it came to pass that we did again take our journey, traveling nearly the same course as in the beginning; and after we had traveled for the space of many days we did pitch our tents again, that we might tarry for the space of a time.

34 And it came to pass that [aIshmael](#note34a) died, and was buried in the place which was called [bNahom](#note34b).

35 And it came to pass that the daughters of Ishmael did [amourn](#note35a) exceedingly, because of the loss of their father, and because of their [bafflictions](#note35b) in the wilderness; and they did [cmurmur](#note35c) against my father, because he had brought them out of the land of Jerusalem, saying: Our father is dead; yea, and we have wandered much in the wilderness, and we have suffered much affliction, hunger, thirst, and fatigue; and after all these sufferings we must perish in the wilderness with hunger.

36 And thus they did murmur against my father, and also against me; and they were desirous to [areturn](#note36a) again to Jerusalem.

37 And Laman said unto Lemuel and also unto the sons of Ishmael: Behold, let us [aslay](#note37a) our father, and also our brother Nephi, who has taken it upon him to be our [bruler](#note37b) and our teacher, who are his elder brethren.

38 Now, he says that the Lord has talked with him, and also that [aangels](#note38a) have ministered unto him. But behold, we know that he lies unto us; and he tells us these things, and he worketh many things by his cunning arts, that he may deceive our eyes, thinking, perhaps, that he may lead us away into some strange wilderness; and after he has led us away, he has thought to make himself a king and a ruler over us, that he may do with us according to his will and pleasure. And after this manner did my brother Laman [bstir up](#note38b) their hearts to [canger](#note38c).

39 And it came to pass that the Lord was with us, yea, even the voice of the Lord came and did speak many words unto them, and did [achasten](#note39a) them exceedingly; and after they were chastened by the voice of the Lord they did turn away their anger, and did repent of their sins, insomuch that the Lord did bless us again with food, that we did not perish.

Chapter 17

Nephi is commanded to build a ship—His brethren oppose him—He exhorts them by recounting the history of God’s dealings with Israel—Nephi is filled with the power of God—His brethren are forbidden to touch him, lest they wither as a dried reed. About 592–591 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that we did again take our journey in the wilderness; and we did travel nearly eastward from that time forth. And we did travel and [awade](#note1a) through much affliction in the wilderness; and our [bwomen](#note1b) did bear children in the wilderness.

2 And so great were the [ablessings](#note2a) of the Lord upon us, that while we did live upon [braw](#note2b) [cmeat](#note2c) in the wilderness, our women did give plenty of suck for their children, and were strong, yea, even like unto the men; and they began to bear their journeyings without murmurings.

3 And thus we see that the commandments of God must be fulfilled. And if it so be that the children of men keep the commandments of God he doth nourish them, and [astrengthen](#note3a) them, and provide means whereby they can accomplish the thing which he has commanded them; wherefore, he did [bprovide](#note3b) means for us while we did sojourn in the wilderness.

4 And we did sojourn for the space of many years, yea, even eight years in the wilderness.

5 And we did come to the land which we called [aBountiful](#note5a), because of its much fruit and also wild honey; and all these things were prepared of the Lord that we might not perish. And we beheld the sea, which we called Irreantum, which, being interpreted, is many waters.

6 And it came to pass that we did pitch our tents by the seashore; and notwithstanding we had suffered many [aafflictions](#note6a) and much difficulty, yea, even so much that we cannot write them all, we were exceedingly rejoiced when we came to the seashore; and we called the place Bountiful, because of its much fruit.

7 And it came to pass that after I, Nephi, had been in the land of Bountiful for the space of many days, the voice of the Lord came unto me, saying: [aArise](#note7a), and get thee into the mountain. And it came to pass that I arose and went up into the mountain, and cried unto the Lord.

8 And it came to pass that the Lord spake unto me, saying: Thou shalt [aconstruct](#note8a) a ship, after the [bmanner](#note8b) which I shall show thee, that I may carry thy people across these waters.

9 And I said: Lord, whither shall I go that I may find ore to molten, that I may make [atools](#note9a) to construct the ship after the manner which thou hast shown unto me?

10 And it came to pass that the Lord told me whither I should go to find ore, that I might make tools.

11 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did make a bellows wherewith to [ablow](#note11a) the fire, of the skins of beasts; and after I had made a bellows, that I might have wherewith to blow the fire, I did smite two stones together that I might make fire.

12 For the Lord had not hitherto suffered that we should make much fire, as we journeyed in the wilderness; for he said: I will make thy food become sweet, that ye [acook](#note12a) it not;

13 And I will also be your [alight](#note13a) in the wilderness; and I will prepare the way before you, if it so be that ye shall keep my commandments; wherefore, inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall be led towards the [bpromised land](#note13b); and ye shall [cknow](#note13c) that it is by me that ye are led.

14 Yea, and the Lord said also that: After ye have arrived in the promised land, ye shall [aknow](#note14a) that I, the Lord, am [bGod](#note14b); and that I, the Lord, did [cdeliver](#note14c) you from destruction; yea, that I did bring you out of the land of Jerusalem.

15 Wherefore, I, Nephi, did strive to keep the [acommandments](#note15a) of the Lord, and I did [bexhort](#note15b) my brethren to faithfulness and diligence.

16 And it came to pass that I did [amake](#note16a) tools of the ore which I did molten out of the rock.

17 And when my brethren saw that I was about to [abuild](#note17a) a ship, they began to [bmurmur](#note17b) against me, saying: Our brother is a fool, for he thinketh that he can build a ship; yea, and he also thinketh that he can cross these great waters.

18 And thus my brethren did [acomplain](#note18a) against me, and were desirous that they might not labor, for they did not [bbelieve](#note18b) that I could build a ship; neither would they believe that I was instructed of the Lord.

19 And now it came to pass that I, Nephi, was exceedingly sorrowful because of the hardness of their hearts; and now when they saw that I began to be sorrowful they were glad in their hearts, insomuch that they did [arejoice](#note19a) over me, saying: We knew that ye could not construct a ship, for we knew that ye were lacking in judgment; wherefore, thou canst not accomplish so great a work.

20 And thou art like unto our father, led away by the foolish [aimaginations](#note20a) of his heart; yea, he hath led us out of the land of Jerusalem, and we have wandered in the wilderness for these many years; and our women have toiled, being big with child; and they have borne children in the wilderness and suffered all things, save it were death; and it would have been better that they had died before they came out of Jerusalem than to have suffered these afflictions.

21 Behold, these many years we have suffered in the wilderness, which time we might have enjoyed our possessions and the land of our inheritance; yea, and we might have been happy.

22 And we know that the people who were in the land of Jerusalem were a [arighteous](#note22a) people; for they kept the statutes and judgments of the Lord, and all his commandments, according to the law of Moses; wherefore, we know that they are a righteous people; and our father hath judged them, and hath led us away because we would hearken unto his words; yea, and our brother is like unto him. And after this manner of language did my brethren murmur and complain against us.

23 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, spake unto them, saying: Do ye believe that our fathers, who were the children of Israel, would have been led away out of the hands of the [aEgyptians](#note23a) if they had not hearkened unto the words of the Lord?

24 Yea, do ye suppose that they would have been led out of bondage, if the Lord had not commanded Moses that he should [alead](#note24a) them out of bondage?

25 Now ye know that the children of Israel were in [abondage](#note25a); and ye know that they were laden with [btasks](#note25b), which were grievous to be borne; wherefore, ye know that it must needs be a good thing for them, that they should be [cbrought](#note25c) out of bondage.

26 Now ye know that [aMoses](#note26a) was commanded of the Lord to do that great work; and ye know that by his [bword](#note26b) the waters of the Red Sea were divided hither and thither, and they passed through on dry ground.

27 But ye know that the Egyptians were [adrowned](#note27a) in the Red Sea, who were the armies of Pharaoh.

28 And ye also know that they were fed with [amanna](#note28a) in the wilderness.

29 Yea, and ye also know that Moses, by his word according to the power of God which was in him, [asmote](#note29a) the rock, and there came forth water, that the children of Israel might quench their thirst.

30 And notwithstanding they being led, the Lord their God, their Redeemer, going before them, [aleading](#note30a) them by day and giving light unto them by night, and doing all things for them which were [bexpedient](#note30b) for man to receive, they hardened their hearts and blinded their minds, and [creviled](#note30c) against Moses and against the true and living God.

31 And it came to pass that according to his word he did [adestroy](#note31a) them; and according to his word he did [blead](#note31b) them; and according to his word he did do all things for them; and there was not any thing done save it were by his word.

32 And after they had crossed the river Jordan he did make them mighty unto the [adriving](#note32a) out of the children of the land, yea, unto the scattering them to destruction.

33 And now, do ye suppose that the children of this land, who were in the land of promise, who were driven out by our fathers, do ye suppose that they were righteous? Behold, I say unto you, Nay.

34 Do ye suppose that our fathers would have been more choice than they if they had been righteous? I say unto you, Nay.

35 Behold, the Lord esteemeth all [aflesh](#note35a) in one; he that is [brighteous](#note35b) is [cfavored](#note35c) of God. But behold, this [dpeople](#note35d) had rejected every word of God, and they were ripe in iniquity; and the fulness of the wrath of God was upon them; and the Lord did curse the land against them, and bless it unto our fathers; yea, he did curse it against them unto their destruction, and he did bless it unto our fathers unto their obtaining power over it.

36 Behold, the Lord hath created the [aearth](#note36a) that it should be [binhabited](#note36b); and he hath created his children that they should possess it.

37 And he [araiseth](#note37a) up a righteous nation, and destroyeth the nations of the wicked.

38 And he leadeth away the righteous into precious [alands](#note38a), and the wicked he [bdestroyeth](#note38b), and curseth the land unto them for their sakes.

39 He ruleth high in the heavens, for it is his throne, and this earth is his [afootstool](#note39a).

40 And he loveth those who will have him to be their God. Behold, he loved our [afathers](#note40a), and he [bcovenanted](#note40b) with them, yea, even Abraham, [cIsaac](#note40c), and [dJacob](#note40d); and he remembered the covenants which he had made; wherefore, he did bring them out of the land of [eEgypt](#note40e).

He sent fiery flying [bserpents](#note41b) among them; and after they were bitten he prepared a way that they might be [chealed](#note41c); and the labor which they had to perform was to look; and because of the [dsimpleness](#note41d) of the way, or the easiness of it, there were many who perished.

42 And they did harden their hearts from time to time, and they did [arevile](#note42a) against [bMoses](#note42b), and also against God; nevertheless, ye know that they were led forth by his matchless power into the land of promise.

44 Wherefore, the Lord [acommanded](#note44a) my father that he should depart into the wilderness; and the Jews also sought to take away his life; yea, and [bye](#note44b) also have sought to take away his life; wherefore, ye are murderers in your hearts and ye are like unto them.

Ye have seen an [bangel](#note45b), and he spake unto you; yea, ye have heard his voice from time to time; and he hath spoken unto you in a still small voice, but ye were [cpast](#note45c) feeling, that ye could not feel his words; wherefore, he has spoken unto you like unto the voice of thunder, which did cause the earth to shake as if it were to divide asunder.

47 Behold, my soul is rent with anguish because of you, and my heart is pained; I fear lest ye shall be cast off forever. Behold, I am [afull](#note47a) of the Spirit of God, insomuch that my frame has [bno](#note47b) strength.

48 And now it came to pass that when I had spoken these words they were angry with me, and were desirous to throw me into the depths of the sea; and as they came forth to lay their hands upon me I spake unto them, saying: In the name of the Almighty God, I command you that ye [atouch](#note48a) me not, for I am filled with the [bpower](#note48b) of God, even unto the consuming of my flesh; and whoso shall lay his hands upon me shall [cwither](#note48c) even as a dried reed; and he shall be as naught before the power of God, for God shall smite him.

49 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, said unto them that they should murmur no more against their father; neither should they withhold their labor from me, for God had commanded me that I should [abuild](#note49a) a ship.

50 And I said unto them: [aIf](#note50a) God had commanded me to do all things I could do them. If he should command me that I should say unto this water, be thou earth, it should be earth; and if I should say it, it would be done.

51 And now, if the Lord has such great power, and has wrought so many miracles among the children of men, how is it that he cannot [ainstruct](#note51a) me, that I should build a ship?

52 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, said many things unto my brethren, insomuch that they were [aconfounded](#note52a) and could not contend against me; neither durst they lay their hands upon me nor touch me with their fingers, even for the space of many days. Now they durst not do this lest they should wither before me, so powerful was the [bSpirit](#note52b) of God; and thus it had wrought upon them.

54 And it came to pass that I stretched forth my hand unto my brethren, and they did not wither before me; but the Lord did shake them, even according to the word which he had spoken.

55 And now, they said: We know of a surety that the Lord is [awith thee](#note55a), for we know that it is the power of the Lord that has shaken us. And they fell down before me, and were about to [bworship](#note55b) me, but I would not suffer them, saying: I am thy brother, yea, even thy younger brother; wherefore, worship the Lord thy God, and honor thy father and thy mother, that thy [cdays](#note55c) may be long in the land which the Lord thy God shall give thee.

Chapter 18

The ship is finished—The births of Jacob and Joseph are mentioned—The company embarks for the promised land—The sons of Ishmael and their wives join in revelry and rebellion—Nephi is bound, and the ship is driven back by a terrible tempest—Nephi is freed, and by his prayer the storm ceases—The people arrive in the promised land. About 591–589 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that they did [aworship](#note1a) the Lord, and did go forth with me; and we did work timbers of curious [bworkmanship](#note1b). And the Lord did show me from time to time after what manner I should work the timbers of the [cship](#note1c).

2 Now I, Nephi, did not work the timbers after the manner which was learned by men, neither did I build the ship after the manner of men; but I did build it after the manner which the Lord had shown unto me; wherefore, it was not after the manner of men.

3 And I, Nephi, did go into the mount oft, and I did [apray](#note3a) oft unto the Lord; wherefore the Lord [bshowed](#note3b) unto me [cgreat](#note3c) things.

4 And it came to pass that after I had finished the ship, according to the word of the Lord, my brethren beheld that it was good, and that the workmanship thereof was exceedingly fine; wherefore, they did [ahumble](#note4a) themselves again before the Lord.

5 And it came to pass that the voice of the Lord came unto my father, that we should arise and go down into the ship.

6 And it came to pass that on the morrow, after we had prepared all things, much fruits and [ameat](#note6a) from the wilderness, and honey in abundance, and provisions according to that which the Lord had commanded us, we did go down into the ship, with all our loading and our [bseeds](#note6b), and whatsoever thing we had brought with us, every one according to his age; wherefore, we did all go down into the [cship](#note6c), with our wives and our children.

7 And now, my father had begat two sons in the wilderness; the elder was called [aJacob](#note7a) and the younger [bJoseph](#note7b).

8 And it came to pass after we had all gone down into the ship, and had taken with us our provisions and things which had been commanded us, we did put forth into the [asea](#note8a) and were driven forth before the wind towards the [bpromised land](#note8b).

9 And after we had been [adriven](#note9a) forth before the wind for the space of many days, behold, my brethren and the sons of Ishmael and also their wives began to make themselves merry, insomuch that they began to dance, and to sing, and to speak with much [brudeness](#note9b), yea, even that they did forget by what power they had been brought thither; yea, they were lifted up unto exceeding rudeness.

10 And I, Nephi, began to fear exceedingly lest the Lord should be angry with us, and smite us because of our iniquity, that we should be swallowed up in the depths of the sea; wherefore, I, Nephi, began to speak to them with much soberness; but behold they were [aangry](#note10a) with me, saying: We will not that our younger brother shall be a [bruler](#note10b) over us.

11 And it came to pass that Laman and Lemuel did take me and [abind](#note11a) me with cords, and they did treat me with much harshness; nevertheless, the Lord did suffer it that he might show forth his power, unto the fulfilling of his word which he had [bspoken](#note11b) concerning the wicked.

12 And it came to pass that after they had bound me insomuch that I could not move, the [acompass](#note12a), which had been prepared of the Lord, did cease to work.

13 Wherefore, they knew not whither they should steer the ship, insomuch that there arose a great [astorm](#note13a), yea, a great and terrible tempest, and we were [bdriven](#note13b) back upon the waters for the space of three days; and they began to be frightened exceedingly lest they should be drowned in the sea; nevertheless they did not loose me.

14 And on the fourth day, which we had been driven back, the tempest began to be exceedingly sore.

15 And it came to pass that we were about to be swallowed up in the depths of the sea. And after we had been driven back upon the waters for the space of four days, my brethren began to [asee](#note15a) that the judgments of God were upon them, and that they must perish save that they should repent of their iniquities; wherefore, they came unto me, and loosed the bands which were upon my wrists, and behold they had swollen exceedingly; and also mine ankles were much swollen, and great was the soreness thereof.

16 Nevertheless, I did look unto my God, and I did [apraise](#note16a) him all the day long; and I did not murmur against the Lord because of mine afflictions.

17 Now my father, Lehi, had said many things unto them, and also unto the sons of [aIshmael](#note17a); but, behold, they did breathe out much threatenings against anyone that should speak for me; and my parents being [bstricken](#note17b) in years, and having [csuffered](#note17c) much grief because of their [dchildren](#note17d), they were brought down, yea, even upon their sick-beds.

18 Because of their grief and much sorrow, and the iniquity of my brethren, they were brought near even to be carried out of this time to meet their God; yea, their [agrey hairs](#note18a) were about to be brought down to lie low in the dust; yea, even they were near to be cast with sorrow into a watery grave.

19 And Jacob and Joseph also, being young, having need of much nourishment, were grieved because of the afflictions of their mother; and also [amy wife](#note19a) with her tears and prayers, and also my children, did not soften the hearts of my brethren that they would loose me.

20 And there was nothing save it were the power of God, which threatened them with destruction, could soften their [ahearts](#note20a); wherefore, when they saw that they were about to be swallowed up in the depths of the sea they repented of the thing which they had done, insomuch that they loosed me.

21 And it came to pass after they had loosed me, behold, I took the compass, and it did work whither I desired it. And it came to pass that I [aprayed](#note21a) unto the Lord; and after I had prayed the winds did cease, and the storm did cease, and there was a great calm.

22 And it came to pass that I, Nephi, did guide the ship, that we sailed again towards the promised land.

23 And it came to pass that after we had sailed for the space of many days we did arrive at the [apromised land](#note23a); and we went forth upon the land, and did pitch our tents; and we did call it the promised land.

24 And it came to pass that we did begin to till the earth, and we began to plant seeds; yea, we did put all our [aseeds](#note24a) into the earth, which we had brought from the land of Jerusalem. And it came to pass that they did grow exceedingly; wherefore, we were blessed in abundance.

25 And it came to pass that we did find upon the land of promise, as we journeyed in the wilderness, that there were [abeasts](#note25a) in the forests of every kind, both the cow and the ox, and the ass and the horse, and the goat and the wild goat, and all manner of wild animals, which were for the use of men. And we did find all manner of [bore](#note25b), both of [cgold](#note25c), and of silver, and of copper.

Chapter 19

Nephi makes plates of ore and records the history of his people—The God of Israel will come six hundred years from the time Lehi left Jerusalem—Nephi tells of His sufferings and crucifixion—The Jews will be despised and scattered until the latter days, when they will return unto the Lord. About 588–570 B.C.

1 And it came to pass that the Lord commanded me, wherefore I did make plates of ore that I might engraven upon them the [arecord](#note1a) of my people. And upon the plates which I made I did [bengraven](#note1b) the record of my [cfather](#note1c), and also our journeyings in the wilderness, and the prophecies of my father; and also many of mine own prophecies have I engraven upon them.

2 And I knew not at the time when I made them that I should be commanded of the Lord to make [athese plates](#note2a); wherefore, the record of my father, and the genealogy of his fathers, and the more part of all our proceedings in the wilderness are engraven upon those first plates of which I have spoken; wherefore, the things which transpired before I made [bthese plates](#note2b) are, of a truth, more particularly made mention upon the first plates.

3 And after I had made these plates by way of commandment, I, Nephi, received a commandment that the ministry and the prophecies, the more plain and precious parts of them, should be written upon [athese plates](#note3a); and that the things which were written should be kept for the instruction of my people, who should possess the land, and also for other [bwise](#note3b) purposes, which purposes are known unto the Lord.

4 Wherefore, I, Nephi, did make a record upon the [aother plates](#note4a), which gives an account, or which gives a greater account of the wars and contentions and destructions of my people. And this have I done, and commanded my people what they should do after I was gone; and that these plates should be handed down from one generation to another, or from one prophet to another, until further commandments of the Lord.

5 And an account of my [amaking](#note5a) these plates shall be given hereafter; and then, behold, I proceed according to that which I have spoken; and this I do that the more sacred things may be [bkept](#note5b) for the knowledge of my people.

6 Nevertheless, I do not [awrite](#note6a) anything upon plates save it be that I think it be [bsacred](#note6b). And now, if I do err, even did they err of old; not that I would excuse myself because of other men, but because of the [cweakness](#note6c) which is in me, according to the flesh, I would excuse myself.

7 For the things which some men esteem to be of great worth, both to the body and soul, others set at [anaught](#note7a) and trample under their feet. Yea, even the very God of Israel do men [btrample](#note7b) under their feet; I say, trample under their feet but I would speak in other words—they set him at naught, and [chearken not](#note7c) to the voice of his counsels.

8 And behold he [acometh](#note8a), according to the words of the angel, in [bsix hundred](#note8b) years from the time my father left Jerusalem.

9 And the world, because of their iniquity, shall judge him to be a thing of naught; wherefore they scourge him, and he suffereth it; and they smite him, and he suffereth it. Yea, they [aspit](#note9a) upon him, and he suffereth it, because of his loving [bkindness](#note9b) and his [clong-suffering](#note9c) towards the children of men.

10 And the [aGod](#note10a) of our fathers, who were [bled](#note10b) out of Egypt, out of bondage, and also were preserved in the wilderness by him, yea, the [cGod](#note10c) of Abraham, and of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, [dyieldeth](#note10d) himself, according to the words of the angel, as a man, into the hands of [ewicked](#note10e) men, to be [flifted up](#note10f), according to the words of [gZenock](#note10g), and to be [hcrucified](#note10h), according to the words of Neum, and to be buried in a [isepulchre](#note10i), according to the words of [jZenos](#note10j), which he spake concerning the three days of [kdarkness](#note10k), which should be a sign given of his death unto those who should inhabit the isles of the sea, more especially given unto those who are of the [lhouse of Israel](#note10l).

11 For thus spake the prophet: The Lord God surely shall [avisit](#note11a) all the house of Israel at that day, some with his [bvoice](#note11b), because of their righteousness, unto their great joy and salvation, and others with the [cthunderings](#note11c) and the lightnings of his power, by tempest, by fire, and by [dsmoke](#note11d), and [evapor](#note11e) of [fdarkness](#note11f), and by the opening of the [gearth](#note11g), and by [hmountains](#note11h) which shall be carried up.

12 And [aall](#note12a) these things must surely come, saith the prophet [bZenos](#note12b). And the [crocks](#note12c) of the earth must rend; and because of the [dgroanings](#note12d) of the earth, many of the kings of the isles of the sea shall be wrought upon by the Spirit of God, to exclaim: The God of nature suffers.

13 And as for those who are at Jerusalem, saith the prophet, they shall be [ascourged](#note13a) by all people, because they crucify the God of Israel, and turn their hearts aside, rejecting signs and wonders, and the power and glory of the God of Israel.

14 And because they turn their hearts aside, saith the prophet, and have [adespised](#note14a) the Holy One of Israel, they shall wander in the flesh, and perish, and become a [bhiss](#note14b) and a [cbyword](#note14c), and be [dhated](#note14d) among all nations.

15 Nevertheless, when that day cometh, saith the prophet, that they [ano more](#note15a) [bturn](#note15b) aside their hearts against the Holy One of Israel, then will he remember the [ccovenants](#note15c) which he made to their fathers.

16 Yea, then will he remember the [aisles](#note16a) of the sea; yea, and all the people who are of the house of Israel, will I [bgather](#note16b) in, saith the Lord, according to the words of the prophet Zenos, from the four quarters of the earth.

17 Yea, and all the earth shall [asee](#note17a) the salvation of the Lord, saith the prophet; every nation, kindred, tongue and people shall be blessed.

18 And I, Nephi, have written these things unto my people, that perhaps I might persuade them that they would [aremember](#note18a) the Lord their Redeemer.

19 Wherefore, I speak unto all the house of Israel, if it so be that they should obtain [athese things](#note19a).

20 For behold, I have workings in the spirit, which doth [aweary](#note20a) me even that all my joints are weak, for those who are at Jerusalem; for had not the Lord been merciful, to show unto me concerning them, even as he had prophets of old, I should have perished also.

21 And he surely did show unto the [aprophets](#note21a) of old all things [bconcerning](#note21b) them; and also he did show unto many concerning us; wherefore, it must needs be that we know concerning them for they are written upon the plates of brass.

22 Now it came to pass that I, Nephi, did teach my brethren these things; and it came to pass that I did read many things to them, which were engraven upon the [aplates of brass](#note22a), that they might know concerning the doings of the Lord in other lands, among people of old.

23 And I did read many things unto them which were written in the [abooks](#note23a) of Moses; but that I might more fully persuade them to believe in the Lord their Redeemer I did read unto them that which was written by the prophet [bIsaiah](#note23b); for I did [cliken](#note23c) all scriptures unto us, that it might be for our [dprofit](#note23d) and learning.

24 Wherefore I spake unto them, saying: Hear ye the words of the prophet, ye who are a [aremnant](#note24a) of the house of Israel, a [bbranch](#note24b) who have been broken off; [chear](#note24c) ye the words of the prophet, which were written unto all the house of Israel, and liken them unto yourselves, that ye may have hope as well as your brethren from whom ye have been broken off; for after this manner has the prophet written.

Chapter 20

The Lord reveals His purposes to Israel—Israel has been chosen in the furnace of affliction and is to go forth from Babylon—Compare Isaiah 48. About 588–570 B.C.

Chapter 22

Israel will be scattered upon all the face of the earth—The Gentiles will nurse and nourish Israel with the gospel in the last days—Israel will be gathered and saved, and the wicked will burn as stubble—The kingdom of the devil will be destroyed, and Satan will be bound. About 588–570 B.C.

1 And now it came to pass that after I, Nephi, had read these things which were engraven upon the [aplates of brass](#note1a), my brethren came unto me and said unto me: What [bmeaneth](#note1b) these things which ye have read? Behold, are they to be understood according to things which are [cspiritual](#note1c), which shall come to pass according to the spirit and not the flesh?

2 And I, Nephi, said unto them: Behold they were [amanifest](#note2a) unto the prophet by the voice of the [bSpirit](#note2b); for by the Spirit are all things made known unto the [cprophets](#note2c), which shall come upon the children of men according to the flesh.

3 Wherefore, the things of which I have read are things pertaining to things both [atemporal](#note3a) and spiritual; for it appears that the house of Israel, sooner or later, will be [bscattered](#note3b) upon all the face of the earth, and also [camong](#note3c) all nations.

4 And behold, there are many who are already lost from the knowledge of those who are at Jerusalem. Yea, the more part of all the [atribes](#note4a) have been [bled away](#note4b); and they are [cscattered](#note4c) to and fro upon the [disles](#note4d) of the sea; and whither they are none of us knoweth, save that we know that they have been led away.

5 And since they have been led away, these things have been prophesied concerning them, and also concerning all those who shall hereafter be scattered and be confounded, because of the Holy One of Israel; for against him will they [aharden](#note5a) their hearts; wherefore, they shall be scattered among all nations and shall be [bhated](#note5b) of all men.

6 Nevertheless, after they shall be [anursed](#note6a) by the [bGentiles](#note6b), and the Lord has lifted up his hand upon the Gentiles and set them up for a standard, and their [cchildren](#note6c) have been carried in their arms, and their daughters have been carried upon their shoulders, behold these things of which are spoken are temporal; for thus are the covenants of the Lord with our fathers; and it meaneth us in the days to come, and also all our brethren who are of the house of Israel.

7 And it meaneth that the time cometh that after all the house of Israel have been scattered and confounded, that the Lord God will raise up a mighty nation among the [aGentiles](#note7a), yea, even upon the face of this land; and by them shall our seed be [bscattered](#note7b).

8 And after our seed is scattered the Lord God will proceed to do a [amarvelous work](#note8a) among the [bGentiles](#note8b), which shall be of great [cworth](#note8c) unto our seed; wherefore, it is likened unto their being nourished by the [dGentiles](#note8d) and being carried in their arms and upon their shoulders.

9 And it shall also be of [aworth](#note9a) unto the Gentiles; and not only unto the Gentiles but [bunto](#note9b) all the [chouse of Israel](#note9c), unto the making known of the [dcovenants](#note9d) of the Father of heaven unto Abraham, saying: In thy [eseed](#note9e) shall all the kindreds of the earth be [fblessed](#note9f).

10 And I would, my brethren, that ye should know that all the kindreds of the earth cannot be blessed unless he shall make [abare](#note10a) his arm in the eyes of the nations.

11 Wherefore, the Lord God will proceed to make bare his arm in the eyes of all the [anations](#note11a), in bringing about his covenants and his gospel unto those who are of the house of Israel.

12 Wherefore, he will [abring](#note12a) them again out of [bcaptivity](#note12b), and they shall be [cgathered](#note12c) together to the lands of their [dinheritance](#note12d); and they shall be [ebrought](#note12e) out of obscurity and out of [fdarkness](#note12f); and they shall know that the [gLord](#note12g) is their [hSavior](#note12h) and their Redeemer, the [iMighty One](#note12i) of Israel.

13 And the blood of that great and [aabominable](#note13a) church, which is the whore of all the earth, shall turn upon their own heads; for they shall [bwar](#note13b) among themselves, and the sword of their [cown](#note13c) hands shall fall upon their own heads, and they shall be drunken with their own blood.

14 And every [anation](#note14a) which shall war against thee, O house of Israel, shall be turned one against another, and they shall [bfall](#note14b) into the pit which they digged to ensnare the people of the Lord. And all that [cfight](#note14c) against Zion shall be destroyed, and that great whore, who hath perverted the right ways of the Lord, yea, that great and abominable church, shall tumble to the [ddust](#note14d) and great shall be the fall of it.

15 For behold, saith the prophet, the time cometh speedily that Satan shall have no more power over the hearts of the children of men; for the day soon cometh that all the proud and they who do wickedly shall be as [astubble](#note15a); and the day cometh that they must be [bburned](#note15b).

16 For the time soon cometh that the fulness of the [awrath](#note16a) of God shall be poured out upon all the children of men; for he will not suffer that the wicked shall destroy the righteous.

17 Wherefore, he will [apreserve](#note17a) the [brighteous](#note17b) by his power, even if it so be that the fulness of his wrath must come, and the righteous be preserved, even unto the destruction of their enemies by fire. Wherefore, the righteous need not fear; for thus saith the prophet, they shall be saved, even if it so be as by fire.

18 Behold, my brethren, I say unto you, that these things must shortly come; yea, even blood, and fire, and vapor of smoke must come; and it must needs be upon the face of this earth; and it cometh unto men according to the flesh if it so be that they will harden their hearts against the Holy One of Israel.

19 For behold, the righteous shall not perish; for the time surely must come that all they who fight against Zion shall be cut off.

20 And the Lord will surely [aprepare](#note20a) a way for his people, unto the fulfilling of the words of Moses, which he spake, saying: A [bprophet](#note20b) shall the Lord your God raise up unto you, like unto me; him shall ye hear in all things whatsoever he shall say unto you. And it shall come to pass that all those who will not hear that prophet shall be [ccut off](#note20c) from among the people.

21 And now I, Nephi, declare unto you, that this [aprophet](#note21a) of whom Moses spake was the Holy One of Israel; wherefore, he shall execute [bjudgment](#note21b) in righteousness.

22 And the righteous need not fear, for they are those who shall not be confounded. But it is the kingdom of the devil, which shall be built up among the children of men, which kingdom is established among them which are in the flesh—

23 For the time speedily shall come that all [achurches](#note23a) which are built up to get gain, and all those who are built up to get power over the flesh, and those who are built up to become [bpopular](#note23b) in the eyes of the world, and those who seek the lusts of the flesh and the things of the world, and to do all manner of iniquity; yea, in fine, all those who belong to the kingdom of the [cdevil](#note23c) are they who need fear, and tremble, and [dquake](#note23d); they are those who must be brought low in the dust; they are those who must be [econsumed](#note23e) as stubble; and this is according to the words of the prophet.

24 And the time cometh speedily that the righteous must be led up as [acalves](#note24a) of the stall, and the Holy One of Israel must reign in dominion, and might, and power, and great [bglory](#note24b).

25 And he [agathereth](#note25a) his children from the four quarters of the earth; and he numbereth his [bsheep](#note25b), and they know him; and there shall be one fold and one shepherd; and he shall feed his sheep, and in him they shall find [cpasture](#note25c).

26 And because of the [arighteousness](#note26a) of his people, [bSatan](#note26b) has no power; wherefore, he cannot be loosed for the space of [cmany years](#note26c); for he hath no power over the hearts of the people, for they dwell in righteousness, and the Holy One of Israel [dreigneth](#note26d).

27 And now behold, I, Nephi, say unto you that all these [athings](#note27a) must come according to the flesh.

28 But, behold, all nations, kindreds, tongues, and people shall dwell safely in the Holy One of Israel if it so be that they will [arepent](#note28a).

29 And now I, Nephi, make an end; for I durst not speak further as yet concerning these things.

30 Wherefore, my brethren, I would that ye should consider that the things which have been written upon the [aplates of brass](#note30a) are true; and they testify that a man must be obedient to the commandments of God.

31 Wherefore, ye need not suppose that I and my father are the only ones that have testified, and also taught them. Wherefore, if ye shall be obedient to the [acommandments](#note31a), and endure to the end, ye shall be saved at the last day. And thus it is. Amen.
