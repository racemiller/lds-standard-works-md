# The Book of Moroni

Chapter 1

Moroni writes for the benefit of the Lamanites—The Nephites who will not deny Christ are put to death. About A.D. 401–21.

1 Now I, Moroni, after having made an end of abridging the account of the people of Jared, I had supposed [anot](#note1a) to have written more, but I have not as yet perished; and I make not myself known to the Lamanites lest they should destroy me.

2 For behold, their [awars](#note2a) are exceedingly fierce among themselves; and because of their [bhatred](#note2b) they [cput to death](#note2c) every Nephite that will not deny the Christ.

3 And I, Moroni, will not [adeny](#note3a) the Christ; wherefore, I wander whithersoever I can for the safety of mine own life.

4 Wherefore, I write a few more things, contrary to that which I had supposed; for I had supposed not to have written any more; but I write a few more things, that perhaps they may be of [aworth](#note4a) unto my brethren, the Lamanites, in some future day, according to the will of the Lord.

Chapter 2

Jesus gave the twelve Nephite disciples power to confer the gift of the Holy Ghost. About A.D. 401–21.

1 The words of Christ, which he spake unto his [adisciples](#note1a), the twelve whom he had chosen, as he laid his hands upon them—

2 And he called them by name, saying: Ye shall [acall](#note2a) on the Father in my name, in mighty prayer; and after ye have done this ye shall have [bpower](#note2b) that to him upon whom ye shall lay your [chands](#note2c), [dye](#note2d) shall give the Holy Ghost; and in my name shall ye give it, for thus do mine apostles.

3 Now Christ spake these words unto them at the time of his first appearing; and the multitude heard it not, but the disciples heard it; and on as many as they [alaid](#note3a) their hands, fell the Holy Ghost.

Chapter 3

Elders ordain priests and teachers by the laying on of hands. About A.D. 401–21.

1 The manner which the disciples, who were called the [aelders](#note1a) of the church, [bordained](#note1b) [cpriests](#note1c) and teachers—

2 After they had prayed unto the Father in the name of Christ, they [alaid](#note2a) their hands upon them, and said:

3 In the name of Jesus Christ I ordain you to be a priest (or if he be a [ateacher](#note3a), I ordain you to be a teacher) to preach repentance and [bremission](#note3b) of sins through Jesus Christ, by the endurance of faith on his name to the end. Amen.

4 And after this manner did they [aordain](#note4a) priests and teachers, according to the [bgifts](#note4b) and callings of God unto men; and they ordained them by the [cpower](#note4c) of the Holy Ghost, which was in them.

Chapter 4

How elders and priests administer the sacramental bread is explained. About A.D. 401–21.

1 The [amanner](#note1a) of their [belders](#note1b) and priests administering the flesh and blood of Christ unto the church; and they administered it [caccording](#note1c) to the commandments of Christ; wherefore we know the manner to be true; and the elder or priest did minister it—

2 And they did kneel down with the [achurch](#note2a), and pray to the Father in the name of Christ, saying:

3 O God, the Eternal Father, we ask thee in the name of thy Son, Jesus Christ, to bless and [asanctify](#note3a) this [bbread](#note3b) to the souls of all those who partake of it; that they may eat in [cremembrance](#note3c) of the body of thy Son, and witness unto thee, O God, the Eternal Father, that they are willing to take upon them the [dname](#note3d) of thy Son, and always remember him, and keep his commandments which he hath given them, that they may always have his [eSpirit](#note3e) to be with them. Amen.

Chapter 6

Repentant persons are baptized and fellowshipped—Church members who repent are forgiven—Meetings are conducted by the power of the Holy Ghost. About A.D. 401–21.

1 And now I speak concerning baptism. Behold, elders, priests, and teachers were baptized; and they were not baptized save they brought forth [afruit](#note1a) meet that they were [bworthy](#note1b) of it.

2 Neither did they receive any unto baptism save they came forth with a [abroken](#note2a) [bheart](#note2b) and a contrite spirit, and witnessed unto the church that they truly repented of all their sins.

3 And none were received unto baptism save they [atook upon](#note3a) them the name of Christ, having a determination to serve him to the end.

4 And after they had been received unto baptism, and were wrought upon and [acleansed](#note4a) by the power of the Holy Ghost, they were numbered among the people of the [bchurch](#note4b) of Christ; and their [cnames](#note4c) were taken, that they might be remembered and nourished by the good word of God, to keep them in the right way, to keep them continually [dwatchful](#note4d) unto prayer, [erelying](#note4e) alone upon the merits of Christ, who was the author and the finisher of their faith.

5 And the [achurch](#note5a) did meet together [boft](#note5b), to [cfast](#note5c) and to pray, and to speak one with another concerning the welfare of their souls.

6 And they did [ameet](#note6a) together oft to partake of bread and wine, in [bremembrance](#note6b) of the Lord Jesus.

7 And they were strict to observe that there should be [ano iniquity](#note7a) among them; and whoso was found to commit iniquity, and [bthree](#note7b) witnesses of the church did condemn them before the [celders](#note7c), and if they repented not, and [dconfessed](#note7d) not, their names were [eblotted out](#note7e), and they were not [fnumbered](#note7f) among the people of Christ.

8 But [aas](#note8a) oft as they repented and sought forgiveness, with real [bintent](#note8b), they were [cforgiven](#note8c).

9 And their meetings were [aconducted](#note9a) by the church after the manner of the workings of the Spirit, and by the [bpower](#note9b) of the Holy Ghost; for as the power of the Holy Ghost led them whether to preach, or to exhort, or to pray, or to supplicate, or to sing, even so it was done.

Chapter 7

An invitation is given to enter into the rest of the Lord—Pray with real intent—The Spirit of Christ enables men to know good from evil—Satan persuades men to deny Christ and do evil—The prophets manifest the coming of Christ—By faith, miracles are wrought and angels minister—Men should hope for eternal life and cleave unto charity. About A.D. 401–21.

1 And now I, Moroni, write a few of the words of my father Mormon, which he spake concerning [afaith](#note1a), hope, and charity; for after this manner did he speak unto the people, as he taught them in the [bsynagogue](#note1b) which they had built for the place of worship.

2 And now I, Mormon, speak unto you, my beloved brethren; and it is by the [agrace](#note2a) of God the Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ, and his holy will, because of the gift of his [bcalling](#note2b) unto me, that I am permitted to speak unto you at this time.

3 Wherefore, I would speak unto you that are of the [achurch](#note3a), that are the [bpeaceable](#note3b) followers of Christ, and that have obtained a sufficient [chope](#note3c) by which ye can enter into the [drest](#note3d) of the Lord, from this time henceforth until ye shall rest with him in heaven.

4 And now my brethren, I judge these things of you because of your peaceable [awalk](#note4a) with the children of men.

5 For I remember the word of God which saith by their [aworks](#note5a) ye shall know them; for if their works be good, then they are good also.

6 For behold, God hath said a man being [aevil](#note6a) cannot do that which is good; for if he [boffereth](#note6b) a gift, or [cprayeth](#note6c) unto God, except he shall do it with real [dintent](#note6d) it profiteth him nothing.

7 For behold, it is not counted unto him for righteousness.

8 For behold, if a man being [aevil](#note8a) giveth a gift, he doeth it [bgrudgingly](#note8b); wherefore it is counted unto him the same as if he had retained the gift; wherefore he is counted evil before God.

9 And likewise also is it counted evil unto a man, if he shall pray and not with [areal intent](#note9a) of heart; yea, and it profiteth him nothing, for God receiveth none such.

10 Wherefore, a man being evil cannot do that which is good; neither will he give a good gift.

11 For behold, a bitter [afountain](#note11a) cannot bring forth good water; neither can a good fountain bring forth bitter water; wherefore, a man being a servant of the devil cannot follow Christ; and if he [bfollow](#note11b) Christ he cannot be a [cservant](#note11c) of the devil.

12 Wherefore, all things which are [agood](#note12a) cometh of God; and that which is [bevil](#note12b) cometh of the devil; for the devil is an enemy unto God, and fighteth against him continually, and inviteth and enticeth to [csin](#note12c), and to do that which is evil continually.

13 But behold, that which is of God inviteth and enticeth to do [agood](#note13a) continually; wherefore, every thing which inviteth and [benticeth](#note13b) to do [cgood](#note13c), and to love God, and to serve him, is [dinspired](#note13d) of God.

14 Wherefore, take heed, my beloved brethren, that ye do not judge that which is [aevil](#note14a) to be of God, or that which is good and of God to be of the devil.

15 For behold, my brethren, it is given unto you to [ajudge](#note15a), that ye may know good from evil; and the way to judge is as plain, that ye may know with a perfect knowledge, as the daylight is from the dark night.

16 For behold, the [aSpirit of Christ](#note16a) is given to every [bman](#note16b), that he may [cknow](#note16c) good from evil; wherefore, I show unto you the way to judge; for every thing which inviteth to do good, and to persuade to believe in Christ, is sent forth by the power and gift of Christ; wherefore ye may know with a perfect knowledge it is of God.

17 But whatsoever thing persuadeth men to do [aevil](#note17a), and believe not in Christ, and deny him, and serve not God, then ye may know with a perfect knowledge it is of the devil; for after this manner doth the devil work, for he persuadeth no man to do good, no, not one; neither do his angels; neither do they who subject themselves unto him.

18 And now, my brethren, seeing that ye know the [alight](#note18a) by which ye may judge, which light is the light of Christ, see that ye do not judge wrongfully; for with that same [bjudgment](#note18b) which ye judge ye shall also be judged.

19 Wherefore, I beseech of you, brethren, that ye should search diligently in the [alight of Christ](#note19a) that ye may know good from evil; and if ye will lay hold upon every good thing, and condemn it not, ye certainly will be a [bchild](#note19b) of Christ.

20 And now, my brethren, how is it possible that ye can lay hold upon every good thing?

21 And now I come to that faith, of which I said I would speak; and I will tell you the way whereby ye may lay hold on every good thing.

22 For behold, God [aknowing](#note22a) all things, being from [beverlasting](#note22b) to everlasting, behold, he sent [cangels](#note22c) to minister unto the children of men, to make manifest concerning the coming of Christ; and in Christ there should come every good thing.

23 And God also declared unto prophets, by his own mouth, that Christ should come.

24 And behold, there were divers ways that he did manifest things unto the children of men, which were good; and all things which are good cometh of Christ; otherwise men were [afallen](#note24a), and there could no good thing come unto them.

25 Wherefore, by the ministering of [aangels](#note25a), and by every word which proceeded forth out of the mouth of God, men began to exercise faith in Christ; and thus by faith, they did lay hold upon every good thing; and thus it was until the coming of Christ.

26 And after that he came men also were [asaved](#note26a) by faith in his name; and by faith, they become the [bsons](#note26b) of God. And as surely as Christ liveth he spake these words unto our fathers, saying: [cWhatsoever](#note26c) thing ye shall ask the Father in my name, which is good, in faith believing that ye shall receive, behold, it shall be done unto you.

27 Wherefore, my beloved brethren, have [amiracles](#note27a) ceased because Christ hath ascended into heaven, and hath sat down on the right hand of God, to [bclaim](#note27b) of the Father his rights of mercy which he hath upon the children of men?

28 For he hath answered the ends of the law, and he claimeth all those who have faith in him; and they who have faith in him will [acleave](#note28a) unto every good thing; wherefore he [badvocateth](#note28b) the cause of the children of men; and he dwelleth eternally in the heavens.

29 And because he hath done this, my beloved brethren, have miracles ceased? Behold I say unto you, Nay; neither have [aangels](#note29a) ceased to minister unto the children of men.

30 For behold, they are subject unto him, to minister according to the word of his command, showing themselves unto them of strong faith and a firm mind in every form of [agodliness](#note30a).

31 And the office of their ministry is to call men unto repentance, and to fulfil and to do the work of the covenants of the Father, which he hath made unto the children of men, to prepare the way among the children of men, by declaring the word of Christ unto the [achosen](#note31a) vessels of the Lord, that they may bear testimony of him.

32 And by so doing, the Lord God prepareth the way that the [aresidue](#note32a) of men may have [bfaith](#note32b) in Christ, that the Holy Ghost may have place in their hearts, according to the power thereof; and after this manner bringeth to pass the Father, the covenants which he hath made unto the children of men.

33 And Christ hath said: [aIf](#note33a) ye will have [bfaith](#note33b) in me ye shall have power to do whatsoever thing is [cexpedient](#note33c) in me.

34 And he hath said: [aRepent](#note34a) all ye ends of the earth, and come unto me, and be baptized in my name, and have faith in me, that ye may be saved.

35 And now, my beloved brethren, if this be the case that these things are true which I have spoken unto you, and God will show unto you, with [apower](#note35a) and great glory at the last [bday](#note35b), that they are true, and if they are true has the day of miracles ceased?

36 Or have angels ceased to appear unto the children of men? Or has he [awithheld](#note36a) the power of the Holy Ghost from them? Or will he, so long as time shall last, or the earth shall stand, or there shall be one man upon the face thereof to be saved?

37 Behold I say unto you, Nay; for it is by faith that [amiracles](#note37a) are wrought; and it is by faith that angels appear and minister unto men; wherefore, if these things have ceased wo be unto the children of men, for it is because of [bunbelief](#note37b), and all is vain.

38 For no man can be saved, according to the words of Christ, save they shall have faith in his name; wherefore, if these things have ceased, then has faith ceased also; and awful is the state of man, for they are as though there had been no redemption made.

39 But behold, my beloved brethren, I judge better things of you, for I judge that ye have faith in Christ because of your meekness; for if ye have not faith in him then ye are not [afit](#note39a) to be numbered among the people of his church.

40 And again, my beloved brethren, I would speak unto you concerning [ahope](#note40a). How is it that ye can attain unto faith, save ye shall have hope?

41 And what is it that ye shall [ahope](#note41a) for? Behold I say unto you that ye shall have [bhope](#note41b) through the atonement of Christ and the power of his resurrection, to be raised unto life [ceternal](#note41c), and this because of your faith in him according to the promise.

42 Wherefore, if a man have [afaith](#note42a) he [bmust](#note42b) needs have hope; for without faith there cannot be any hope.

43 And again, behold I say unto you that he cannot have faith and hope, save he shall be [ameek](#note43a), and lowly of heart.

44 If so, his [afaith](#note44a) and hope is vain, for none is [bacceptable](#note44b) before God, save the [cmeek](#note44c) and lowly in heart; and if a man be meek and lowly in heart, and [dconfesses](#note44d) by the power of the Holy Ghost that Jesus is the Christ, he must needs have charity; for if he have not charity he is nothing; wherefore he must needs have charity.

45 And [acharity](#note45a) suffereth long, and is [bkind](#note45b), and [cenvieth](#note45c) not, and is not puffed up, seeketh not her own, is not easily [dprovoked](#note45d), thinketh no evil, and rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth, beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things.

46 Wherefore, my beloved brethren, if ye have not charity, ye are nothing, for charity never faileth. Wherefore, cleave unto charity, which is the greatest of all, for all things must fail—

47 But [acharity](#note47a) is the pure [blove](#note47b) of Christ, and it endureth [cforever](#note47c); and whoso is found possessed of it at the last day, it shall be well with him.

48 Wherefore, my beloved brethren, [apray](#note48a) unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love, which he hath bestowed upon all who are true [bfollowers](#note48b) of his Son, Jesus Christ; that ye may become the sons of God; that when he shall appear we shall [cbe](#note48c) like him, for we shall see him as he is; that we may have this hope; that we may be [dpurified](#note48d) even as he is pure. Amen.

Chapter 8

The baptism of little children is an evil abomination—Little children are alive in Christ because of the Atonement—Faith, repentance, meekness and lowliness of heart, receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end lead to salvation. About A.D. 401–21.

1 An epistle of my [afather](#note1a) Mormon, written to me, Moroni; and it was written unto me soon after my calling to the ministry. And on this wise did he write unto me, saying:

2 My beloved son, Moroni, I rejoice exceedingly that your Lord Jesus Christ hath been mindful of you, and hath called you to his ministry, and to his holy work.

3 I am mindful of you always in my prayers, continually praying unto God the Father in the name of his Holy Child, Jesus, that he, through his infinite [agoodness](#note3a) and [bgrace](#note3b), will keep you through the endurance of faith on his name to the end.

4 And now, my son, I speak unto you concerning that which grieveth me exceedingly; for it grieveth me that there should [adisputations](#note4a) rise among you.

5 For, if I have learned the truth, there have been disputations among you concerning the baptism of your little children.

6 And now, my son, I desire that ye should labor diligently, that this gross error should be removed from among you; for, for this intent I have written this epistle.

7 For immediately after I had learned these things of you I inquired of the Lord concerning the matter. And the [aword](#note7a) of the Lord came to me by the power of the Holy Ghost, saying:

8 [aListen](#note8a) to the words of Christ, your Redeemer, your Lord and your God. Behold, I came into the world not to call the righteous but sinners to repentance; the [bwhole](#note8b) need no physician, but they that are sick; wherefore, little [cchildren](#note8c) are [dwhole](#note8d), for they are not capable of committing [esin](#note8e); wherefore the curse of [fAdam](#note8f) is taken from them in me, that it hath no power over them; and the law of [gcircumcision](#note8g) is done away in me.

9 And after this manner did the Holy Ghost manifest the word of God unto me; wherefore, my beloved son, I know that it is solemn [amockery](#note9a) before God, that ye should baptize little children.

10 Behold I say unto you that this thing shall ye teach—repentance and baptism unto those who are [aaccountable](#note10a) and capable of committing sin; yea, teach parents that they must repent and be baptized, and humble themselves as their little [bchildren](#note10b), and they shall all be saved with their little children.

11 And their little [achildren](#note11a) need no repentance, neither baptism. Behold, baptism is unto repentance to the fulfilling the commandments unto the [bremission](#note11b) of sins.

12 But little [achildren](#note12a) are alive in Christ, even from the foundation of the world; if not so, God is a partial God, and also a changeable God, and a [brespecter](#note12b) to persons; for how many little children have died without baptism!

13 Wherefore, if little children could not be saved without baptism, these must have gone to an endless hell.

14 Behold I say unto you, that he that supposeth that little children need baptism is in the gall of bitterness and in the bonds of iniquity; for he hath neither [afaith](#note14a), hope, nor charity; wherefore, should he be cut off while in the thought, he must go down to hell.

15 For awful is the wickedness to suppose that God saveth one child because of baptism, and the other must perish because he hath no baptism.

16 Wo be unto them that shall pervert the ways of the Lord after this manner, for they shall perish except they repent. Behold, I speak with boldness, having [aauthority](#note16a) from God; and I fear not what man can do; for [bperfect](#note16b) [clove](#note16c) [dcasteth out](#note16d) all fear.

17 And I am filled with [acharity](#note17a), which is everlasting love; wherefore, all children are alike unto me; wherefore, I love little children with a perfect love; and they are all alike and [bpartakers](#note17b) of salvation.

18 For I know that God is not a partial God, neither a changeable being; but he is [aunchangeable](#note18a) from [ball](#note18b) eternity to all eternity.

19 Little [achildren](#note19a) cannot repent; wherefore, it is awful wickedness to deny the pure mercies of God unto them, for they are all alive in him because of his [bmercy](#note19b).

20 And he that saith that little children need baptism denieth the mercies of Christ, and setteth at naught the [aatonement](#note20a) of him and the power of his redemption.

21 Wo unto such, for they are in danger of death, [ahell](#note21a), and an [bendless](#note21b) torment. I speak it boldly; God hath commanded me. Listen unto them and give heed, or they stand against you at the [cjudgment-seat](#note21c) of Christ.

22 For behold that all little children are [aalive](#note22a) in Christ, and also all they that are without the [blaw](#note22b). For the power of [credemption](#note22c) cometh on all them that have [dno law](#note22d); wherefore, he that is not condemned, or he that is under no condemnation, cannot repent; and unto such baptism availeth nothing—

23 But it is mockery before God, denying the mercies of Christ, and the power of his Holy Spirit, and putting trust in [adead works](#note23a).

24 Behold, my son, this thing ought not to be; for [arepentance](#note24a) is unto them that are under condemnation and under the curse of a broken law.

25 And the first fruits of [arepentance](#note25a) is [bbaptism](#note25b); and baptism cometh by faith unto the fulfilling the commandments; and the fulfilling the commandments bringeth [cremission](#note25c) of sins;

26 And the remission of sins bringeth [ameekness](#note26a), and lowliness of heart; and because of meekness and lowliness of heart cometh the visitation of the [bHoly Ghost](#note26b), which [cComforter](#note26c) [dfilleth](#note26d) with hope and perfect [elove](#note26e), which love endureth by [fdiligence](#note26f) unto [gprayer](#note26g), until the end shall come, when all the [hsaints](#note26h) shall dwell with God.

27 Behold, my son, I will write unto you again if I go not out soon against the Lamanites. Behold, the [apride](#note27a) of this nation, or the people of the Nephites, hath proven their destruction except they should repent.

28 Pray for them, my son, that repentance may come unto them. But behold, I fear lest the Spirit hath [aceased](#note28a) [bstriving](#note28b) with them; and in this part of the land they are also seeking to put down all power and authority which cometh from God; and they are [cdenying](#note28c) the Holy Ghost.

29 And after rejecting so great a knowledge, my son, they must perish soon, unto the fulfilling of the prophecies which were spoken by the prophets, as well as the words of our Savior himself.

30 Farewell, my son, until I shall write unto you, or shall meet you again. Amen.

Chapter 9

Both the Nephites and the Lamanites are depraved and degenerate—They torture and murder each other—Mormon prays that grace and goodness may rest upon Moroni forever. About A.D. 401.

1 My beloved son, I write unto you again that ye may know that I am yet alive; but I write somewhat of that which is grievous.

2 For behold, I have had a sore battle with the Lamanites, in which we did not conquer; and Archeantus has fallen by the sword, and also Luram and Emron; yea, and we have lost a great number of our choice men.

3 And now behold, my son, I fear lest the Lamanites shall destroy this people; for they do not repent, and Satan stirreth them up continually to [aanger](#note3a) one with another.

4 Behold, I am laboring with them continually; and when I speak the word of God with [asharpness](#note4a) they tremble and anger against me; and when I use no sharpness they [bharden](#note4b) their hearts against it; wherefore, I fear lest the Spirit of the Lord hath ceased [cstriving](#note4c) with them.

5 For so exceedingly do they anger that it seemeth me that they have no fear of death; and they have lost their love, one towards another; and they [athirst](#note5a) after blood and revenge continually.

6 And now, my beloved son, notwithstanding their hardness, let us labor [adiligently](#note6a); for if we should cease to [blabor](#note6b), we should be brought under condemnation; for we have a labor to perform whilst in this tabernacle of clay, that we may conquer the enemy of all righteousness, and rest our souls in the kingdom of God.

7 And now I write somewhat concerning the sufferings of this people. For according to the knowledge which I have received from Amoron, behold, the Lamanites have many prisoners, which they took from the tower of Sherrizah; and there were men, women, and children.

8 And the husbands and fathers of those women and children they have slain; and they feed the women upon the [aflesh](#note8a) of their husbands, and the children upon the flesh of their fathers; and no water, save a little, do they give unto them.

9 And notwithstanding this great [aabomination](#note9a) of the Lamanites, it doth not exceed that of our people in Moriantum. For behold, many of the daughters of the Lamanites have they taken prisoners; and after [bdepriving](#note9b) them of that which was most dear and precious above all things, which is [cchastity](#note9c) and [dvirtue](#note9d)—

10 And after they had done this thing, they did murder them in a most [acruel](#note10a) manner, torturing their bodies even unto death; and after they have done this, they devour their flesh like unto wild beasts, because of the hardness of their hearts; and they do it for a token of bravery.

11 O my beloved son, how can a people like this, that are without civilization—

12 (And only a few years have passed away, and they were a civil and a delightsome people)

13 But O my son, how can a people like this, whose [adelight](#note13a) is in so much abomination—

14 How can we expect that God will [astay](#note14a) his hand in judgment against us?

15 Behold, my heart cries: Wo unto this people. Come out in judgment, O God, and hide their sins, and wickedness, and abominations from before thy face!

16 And again, my son, there are many [awidows](#note16a) and their daughters who remain in Sherrizah; and that part of the provisions which the Lamanites did not carry away, behold, the army of Zenephi has carried away, and left them to wander whithersoever they can for food; and many old women do faint by the way and die.

17 And the army which is with me is weak; and the armies of the Lamanites are betwixt Sherrizah and me; and as many as have fled to the army of [aAaron](#note17a) have fallen victims to their awful brutality.

18 O the depravity of my people! They are without [aorder](#note18a) and without mercy. Behold, I am but a man, and I have but the [bstrength](#note18b) of a man, and I cannot any longer enforce my commands.

19 And they have become strong in their perversion; and they are alike brutal, sparing none, neither old nor young; and they delight in everything save that which is good; and the suffering of our women and our children upon all the face of this land doth exceed everything; yea, tongue cannot tell, neither can it be written.

20 And now, my son, I dwell no longer upon this horrible scene. Behold, thou knowest the wickedness of this people; thou knowest that they are without principle, and past feeling; and their wickedness doth [aexceed](#note20a) that of the Lamanites.

21 Behold, my son, I cannot recommend them unto God lest he should smite me.

22 But behold, my son, I recommend thee unto God, and I trust in Christ that thou wilt be saved; and I [apray](#note22a) unto God that he will [bspare](#note22b) thy life, to witness the return of his people unto him, or their utter destruction; for I know that they must perish except they [crepent](#note22c) and return unto him.

23 And if they perish it will be like unto the [aJaredites](#note23a), because of the wilfulness of their hearts, [bseeking](#note23b) for blood and [crevenge](#note23c).

24 And if it so be that they perish, we know that many of our brethren have [adeserted](#note24a) over unto the Lamanites, and many more will also desert over unto them; wherefore, write somewhat a few things, if thou art spared and I shall perish and not see thee; but I trust that I may see thee soon; for I have sacred records that I would [bdeliver](#note24b) up unto thee.

25 My son, be faithful in Christ; and may not the things which I have written grieve thee, to weigh thee down unto [adeath](#note25a); but may Christ lift thee up, and may his sufferings and death, and the showing his body unto our fathers, and his mercy and [blong-suffering](#note25b), and the hope of his glory and of eternal life, rest in your [cmind](#note25c) forever.

26 And may the grace of God the Father, whose throne is high in the heavens, and our Lord Jesus Christ, who sitteth on the [aright hand](#note26a) of his power, until all things shall become subject unto him, be, and abide with you forever. Amen.

Chapter 10

A testimony of the Book of Mormon comes by the power of the Holy Ghost—The gifts of the Spirit are dispensed to the faithful—Spiritual gifts always accompany faith—Moroni’s words speak from the dust—Come unto Christ, be perfected in Him, and sanctify your souls. About A.D. 421.

1 Now I, Moroni, write somewhat as seemeth me good; and I write unto my brethren, the [aLamanites](#note1a); and I would that they should know that more than [bfour hundred and twenty](#note1b) years have passed away since the sign was given of the coming of Christ.

2 And I [aseal](#note2a) up [bthese records](#note2b), after I have spoken a few words by way of exhortation unto you.

3 Behold, I would exhort you that when ye shall read these things, if it be wisdom in God that ye should read them, that ye would remember how [amerciful](#note3a) the Lord hath been unto the children of men, from the creation of Adam even down until the time that ye shall receive these things, and [bponder](#note3b) it in your [chearts](#note3c).

4 And when ye shall receive these things, I would exhort you that ye would [aask](#note4a) God, the Eternal Father, in the name of Christ, if these things are not [btrue](#note4b); and if ye shall ask with a [csincere heart](#note4c), with [dreal intent](#note4d), having [efaith](#note4e) in Christ, he will [fmanifest](#note4f) the [gtruth](#note4g) of it unto you, by the power of the Holy Ghost.

5 And by the power of the Holy Ghost ye may [aknow](#note5a) the [btruth](#note5b) of all things.

6 And whatsoever thing is good is just and true; wherefore, nothing that is good denieth the Christ, but acknowledgeth that he is.

7 And ye may [aknow](#note7a) that he is, by the power of the Holy Ghost; wherefore I would exhort you that ye deny not the power of God; for he worketh by power, [baccording](#note7b) to the faith of the children of men, the same today and tomorrow, and forever.

8 And again, I exhort you, my brethren, that ye deny not the [agifts](#note8a) of God, for they are many; and they come from the same God. And there are [bdifferent](#note8b) ways that these gifts are administered; but it is the same God who worketh all in all; and they are given by the manifestations of the [cSpirit](#note8c) of God unto men, to profit them.

9 [aFor](#note9a) behold, to one is given by the Spirit of God, that he may [bteach](#note9b) the word of wisdom;

10 And to another, that he may [ateach](#note10a) the word of [bknowledge](#note10b) by the same Spirit;

11 And to another, exceedingly great [afaith](#note11a); and to another, the gifts of [bhealing](#note11b) by the same Spirit;

12 And again, to another, that he may work mighty [amiracles](#note12a);

13 And again, to another, that he may prophesy concerning all things;

14 And again, to another, the beholding of angels and ministering spirits;

15 And again, to another, all kinds of tongues;

16 And again, to another, the interpretation of [alanguages](#note16a) and of divers kinds of tongues.

17 And all these gifts come by the Spirit of Christ; and they come unto every man severally, according as he will.

18 And I would exhort you, my beloved brethren, that ye remember that [aevery](#note18a) good [bgift](#note18b) cometh of Christ.

19 And I would exhort you, my beloved brethren, that ye remember that he is the [asame](#note19a) yesterday, today, and forever, and that all these gifts of which I have spoken, which are spiritual, never will be done away, even as long as the world shall stand, only according to the [bunbelief](#note19b) of the children of men.

20 Wherefore, there must be [afaith](#note20a); and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity.

21 And except ye have [acharity](#note21a) ye can in nowise be saved in the kingdom of God; neither can ye be saved in the kingdom of God if ye have not faith; neither can ye if ye have no hope.

22 And if ye have no hope ye must needs be in [adespair](#note22a); and despair cometh because of iniquity.

23 And Christ truly said unto our fathers: [aIf](#note23a) ye have faith ye can do all things which are expedient unto me.

24 And now I speak unto all the ends of the earth—that if the day cometh that the power and gifts of God shall be done away among you, it shall be [abecause](#note24a) of [bunbelief](#note24b).

25 And wo be unto the children of men if this be the case; for there shall be [anone](#note25a) that doeth good among you, no not one. For if there be one among you that doeth good, he shall work by the power and gifts of God.

26 And wo unto them who shall do these things away and die, for they [adie](#note26a) in their [bsins](#note26b), and they cannot be saved in the kingdom of God; and I speak it according to the words of Christ; and I lie not.

27 And I exhort you to remember these things; for the time speedily cometh that ye shall know that I lie not, for ye shall see me at the bar of God; and the Lord God will say unto you: Did I not declare my [awords](#note27a) unto you, which were written by this man, like as one [bcrying](#note27b) from the dead, yea, even as one speaking out of the [cdust](#note27c)?

28 I declare these things unto the fulfilling of the prophecies. And behold, they shall proceed forth out of the mouth of the everlasting God; and his word shall [ahiss](#note28a) forth from generation to generation.

29 And God shall show unto you, that that which I have written is [atrue](#note29a).

30 And again I would exhort you that ye would [acome](#note30a) unto Christ, and lay hold upon every good [bgift](#note30b), and [ctouch not](#note30c) the evil gift, nor the [dunclean thing](#note30d).

31 And [aawake](#note31a), and arise from the dust, O Jerusalem; yea, and put on thy beautiful garments, O daughter of [bZion](#note31b); and [cstrengthen](#note31c) thy [dstakes](#note31d) and enlarge thy borders forever, that thou mayest [eno more](#note31e) be confounded, that the covenants of the Eternal Father which he hath made unto thee, O house of Israel, may be fulfilled.

32 Yea, [acome](#note32a) unto Christ, and be [bperfected](#note32b) in him, and [cdeny](#note32c) yourselves of all ungodliness; and if ye shall deny yourselves of all ungodliness, and [dlove](#note32d) God with all your might, mind and strength, then is his grace sufficient for you, that by his grace ye may be [eperfect](#note32e) in Christ; and if by the grace of God ye are perfect in Christ, ye can in nowise deny the power of God.

33 And again, if ye by the grace of God are perfect in Christ, and deny not his power, then are ye [asanctified](#note33a) in Christ by the grace of God, through the shedding of the [bblood](#note33b) of Christ, which is in the covenant of the Father unto the remission of your [csins](#note33c), that ye become [dholy](#note33d), without spot.

34 And now I bid unto all, farewell. I soon go to [arest](#note34a) in the [bparadise](#note34b) of God, until my [cspirit](#note34c) and body shall again [dreunite](#note34d), and I am brought forth triumphant through the [eair](#note34e), to meet you before the [fpleasing](#note34f) bar of the great [gJehovah](#note34g), the Eternal [hJudge](#note34h) of both quick and dead. Amen.
