# Abraham, Facsimile 1

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 1. The Angel of the Lord.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 2. Abraham fastened upon an altar.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 3. The idolatrous priest of Elkenah attempting to offer up Abraham as a sacrifice.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 4. The altar for sacrifice by the idolatrous priests, standing before the gods of Elkenah, Libnah, Mahmackrah, Korash, and Pharaoh.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 5. The idolatrous god of Elkenah.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 6. The idolatrous god of Libnah.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 7. The idolatrous god of Mahmackrah.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 8. The idolatrous god of Korash.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 9. The idolatrous god of Pharaoh.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 10. Abraham in Egypt.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 11. Designed to represent the pillars of heaven, as understood by the Egyptians.

Abraham, Facsimile 1 — Fig. 12. Raukeeyang, signifying expanse, or the firmament over our heads; but in this case, in relation to this subject, the Egyptians meant it to signify Shaumau, to be high, or the heavens, answering to the Hebrew word, Shaumahyeem.
